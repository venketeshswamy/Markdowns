# Ind AS 1: Presentation of Financial Statements

## Objective and Scope
- Objective (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Prescribe basis for presenting general purpose financial statements (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Ensure comparability with previous periods and other entities (Para 1)
- Scope (Para 2)
  - 🎯 Apply in preparing and presenting general purpose financial statements (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Scope Exclusions (Para 4)
    - 🔗 Does not apply to condensed interim financial statements (Para 4)
    - 🔗 See Ind AS 34 for interim financial statements (Para 4)
    - ⚠️ Exception: Paragraphs 15 to 35 apply to interim statements (Para 4)
  - 🎯 Applies to consolidated and separate financial statements (Para 4)
  - ⚠️ Adaptations for specific entity types (Para 5)
    - 📋 Not for profit Private or Public sector entities (Para 5)
    - 📋 Entities without share capital adapting member interest presentation (Para 6)

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Definition - General Purpose Financial Statements (Para 7)
  - Meets needs of users who cannot demand tailored reports (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Impracticable (Para 7)
  - Cannot apply requirement after making every reasonable effort (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Material (Para 7)
  - Omitting misstating or obscuring influences primary user decisions (Para 7)
  - Materiality depends on nature or magnitude or both (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Notes (Para 7)
  - Additional info to core financial statements and narrative descriptions (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Other Comprehensive Income (Para 7)
  - Income and expense items not recognised in profit or loss (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Profit or Loss (Para 7)
  - Total of income less expenses excluding OCI components (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Reclassification Adjustments (Para 7)
  - Amounts reclassified to profit or loss in current period (Para 7)
- 📌 Definition - Total Comprehensive Income (Para 7)
  - Change in equity from non-owner transactions and events (Para 7)

## Recognition Criteria
- True and Fair View and Compliance
  - 🎯 Shall present true and fair financial position (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Presumption that applying Ind AS achieves fair view (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Explicit unreserved statement of compliance required in notes (Para 16)
  - ⚠️ Rectification of inappropriate policies by disclosure is prohibited (Para 18)
- Departure from Ind AS Requirements
  - ⚠️ Permitted in extremely rare misleading circumstances (Para 19)
  - ⚠️ Prohibited departure requires maximum disclosure of conflict (Para 23)
- Going Concern Gate
  - 🎯 Management shall assess ability to continue as going concern (Para 25)
  - 🎯 Minimum twelve months forward assessment from reporting date (Para 26)
  - ⚠️ Disclosure required if material uncertainties exist (Para 25)
  - ⚠️ Disclosure required if financial statements not prepared on going concern (Para 25)
- Accrual Basis Gate
  - 🎯 Prepare financial statements except cash flows using accrual (Para 27)

## Measurement
- Materiality and Aggregation
  - 🎯 Present separately each material class of similar items (Para 29)
  - 🎯 Present separately dissimilar items unless they are immaterial (Para 29)
  - ⚠️ Immaterial disclosures required by Ind AS need not be provided (Para 31)
- Offsetting Rules
  - ⚠️ Shall not offset assets and liabilities or income and expenses (Para 32)
  - ⚠️ Exception: When required or permitted by an Ind AS (Para 32)
  - ⚠️ Exception: Incidental transactions presenting substance of transaction (Para 34)
  - ⚠️ Exception: Similar group transactions on net basis unless material (Para 35)
- Frequency of Reporting
  - 🎯 Present complete set of financial statements at least annually (Para 36)
- rounding and Rounding Units
  - Presenting info in thousands lakhs millions or crores permitted (Para 53)

## Presentation
- Complete Set of Financial Statements
  - 🎯 Balance sheet as at end of period (Para 10a)
  - 🎯 Statement of profit and loss for period (Para 10b)
  - 🎯 Statement of changes in equity for period (Para 10c)
  - 🎯 Statement of cash flows for period (Para 10d)
  - 🎯 Notes with material accounting policy information (Para 10e)
  - 🎯 Comparative information for preceding period (Para 10ea)
  - 🎯 Balance sheet at start of preceding period for restatements (Para 10f)
  - 🎯 Single statement of profit and loss in two sections (Para 10A)
- Balance Sheet Classification
  - 🎯 Separate current and non-current assets and liabilities (Para 60)
  - ⚠️ Exception: Presentation based on liquidity is more reliable (Para 60)
  - ⚠️ Shall not classify deferred tax assets or liabilities as current (Para 56)
- Current Assets Classification
  - 🎯 Realised or sold or consumed in normal operating cycle (Para 66a)
  - 🎯 Held primarily for purpose of trading (Para 66b)
  - 🎯 Expected to be realised within twelve months (Para 66c)
  - 🎯 Cash or cash equivalent unless restricted for twelve months (Para 66d)
- Current Liabilities Classification
  - 🎯 Expected to be settled in normal operating cycle (Para 69a)
  - 🎯 Held primarily for purpose of trading (Para 69b)
  - 🎯 Due to be settled within twelve months (Para 69c)
  - 🎯 No right to defer settlement for twelve months (Para 69d)
- Statement of Profit and Loss Classification
  - 🎯 Single statement with profit or loss and OCI sections (Para 10A)
  - ⚠️ Extraordinary items shall not be presented anywhere (Para 87)
  - 🎯 Present analysis of expenses using nature of expense method (Para 99)

## Application Guidance
- Obscuring Material Information (Para 7)
  - 📋 Language used is vague or unclear (Para 7a)
  - 📋 Information scattered throughout financial statements (Para 7b)
  - 📋 Dissimilar items inappropriately aggregated (Para 7c)
  - 📋 Similar items inappropriately disaggregated (Para 7d)
  - 📋 Hidden by immaterial info reducing understandability (Para 7e)
- Material Accounting Policy Information
  - 📋 Entity changed its policy during reporting period (Para 117Ba)
  - 📋 Policy chosen from options permitted by Ind ASs (Para 117Bb)
  - 📋 Policy developed in accordance with Ind AS 8 (Para 117Bc)
  - 📋 Policy relates to significant judgements or assumptions (Para 117Bd)
  - 📋 Complex accounting where users need guidance to understand (Para 117Be)
- Management Judgements (Para 122)
  - 📋 When significant risks and rewards are transferred (Para 123b)
  - 📋 Whether sales are financing arrangements not generating revenue (Para 123c)
  - 📋 Whether contractual terms give rise to SPPI (Para 123d)

## Logical Flow Chains
- RAMD Spine for Ind AS 1
  - Recognition: True and fair view gate passed (Para 15)
    - leads to Accrual Basis and Going concern assessment (Para 25)
      - leads to Initial Classification of assets and liabilities (Para 60)
        - leads to Presentation in complete set of statements (Para 10)
          - leads to Notes and exhaustive accounting disclosures (Para 112)
- Debt Covenant Breach and Classification Flow
  - Material loan covenant breached on or before reporting date (Para 74)
    - if Lender agrees by reporting date to provide grace period (Para 75)
      - leads to Non-current liability classification (Para 75)
    - if Lender agrees after reporting date but before approval (Para 74)
      - leads to Current liability classification (Para 74)
      - requires Note disclosure under Ind AS 107 (Para 74)
    - If No breach happens but extension sought before breach for period at least covering the reporting date, = Keep Original Classification itself

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Format of Financial Statements: Schedule III format vs Standard based flexibility
  - Old AS: Detailed guidance given under Schedule VI of Act
  - Ind AS: Ind AS 1 provides structural features with Schedule III
  - Financial Impact: Better alignment of international presentation standards
  - ICAI Exam Note: Often tested with presentation of regulatory lines
- 🔄 Classification of Expenses: Functional vs Nature-wise choice
  - Old AS: Allowed both function and nature of expenses
  - Ind AS: Only nature-wise classification of expenses permitted
  - Financial Impact: Simplified comparative P&L presentation across entities
  - ICAI Exam Note: Standard question on presentation of expenses
- 🔄 Extraordinary Items: Allowed vs Prohibited
  - Old AS: Allowed separate line item for extraordinary items
  - Ind AS: Strict prohibition of extraordinary item classification
  - Financial Impact: Eliminates subjective classifications of non-recurring events
  - ICAI Exam Note: Tested as a direct classification question

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- 🌐 Statement of Profit or Loss Options
  - IFRS: Allows option of single statement or two-statement approach
  - Ind AS: Mandates single statement with P&L and OCI sections
  - Reason for carve-out: Standardises financial statement presentations in India
- 🌐 Periodicity of Reporting
  - IFRS: Permits preparation over 52-week period
  - Ind AS: Mandates reporting on calendar-based periods only
  - Reason for carve-out: Strict alignment with Indian Companies Act requirements
- 🌐 Presentation of Expenses
  - IFRS: Choice of nature-wise or function-wise classification
  - Ind AS: Nature-wise classification of expenses is mandatory
  - Reason for carve-out: Standardises comparability for Indian stakeholders
- 🌐 Classification of Liability on Breach Waiver
  - IFRS: Non-current if waiver obtained before reporting period end
  - Ind AS: Non-current if waiver obtained before approval date (Para 74)
  - Reason for carve-out: To avoid harsh current classification in India

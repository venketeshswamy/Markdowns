# Ind AS 105: Non-current Assets Held for Sale and Discontinued Operations

## Objective and Scope
- 🎯 Objective: Specify accounting for assets held for sale (Para 1)
- 🎯 Objective: Present discontinued operations separately in statement (Para 1)
- 🎯 Measurement: Lower of carrying amount and fair value (Para 1)
- 🎯 Presentation: Held for sale assets presented separately (Para 1)
- Scope applies to all recognised non-current assets (Para 2)
- Scope applies to all disposal groups of an entity (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Scope measurement exclusions (Para 5)
    - 🔗 Deferred tax assets under Ind AS 12
    - 🔗 Employee benefits under Ind AS 19
    - 🔗 Financial assets under Ind AS 109
    - 🔗 Investment property at fair value under Ind AS 40
    - 🔗 Agriculture at fair value under Ind AS 41
    - 🔗 Insurance contracts under Ind AS 117

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Non-current assets: assets that do not meet the definition of current assets (App A)
- 📌 Current asset: entity classifies an asset as current when (App A)
  - expects to realise or consume in normal operating cycle
  - holds asset primarily for purpose of trading
  - expects to realise within twelve months
  - asset is cash or cash equivalent unless restricted
- 📌 Disposal group: group of assets to be disposed of together (App A)
  - ⚠️ Includes liabilities directly associated with those assets
- 📌 Cash-generating unit: smallest identifiable group generating independent cash inflows (App A)
- 📌 Discontinued operation: component of entity disposed or held for sale (App A)
  - 🎯 Represents separate major line of business or geographical area
  - 🎯 Is part of single coordinated disposal plan
  - 🎯 Is subsidiary acquired exclusively with view to resale

## Recognition Criteria
- 🎯 General Principle: Carrying amount recovered principally through sale (Para 6)
  - 🎯 Criteria 1: Available for immediate sale in present condition (Para 7)
  - 🎯 Criteria 2: Sale must be highly probable (Para 7)
- 🎯 Highly Probable Sale Conditions (Para 8)
  - Management must be committed to a plan to sell
  - Active programme to locate a buyer must be initiated
  - Marketed at price reasonable in relation to fair value
  - Sale expected to be completed within one year
  - Significant changes to or withdrawal from plan unlikely
- 🎯 Classification as Held for Distribution to Owners (Para 12A)
  - Entity is committed to distribute asset to owners
  - Must be available for immediate distribution
  - Distribution must be highly probable
- ⚠️ Assets to be Abandoned (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Shall not classify as held for sale
  - ⚠️ Carrying amount recovered principally through continuing use

## Measurement
- Initial and Subsequent Measurement
  - 🎯 Measure at lower of carrying amount and fair value (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Less costs to sell or costs to distribute (Para 15A)
  - 🎯 Depreciation and amortization shall be immediately stopped (Para 25)
  - 🎯 Interest and expenses of liabilities shall continue (Para 25)
- Measurement of sale expected beyond one year
  - 🧮 Measure costs to sell at their present value (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Present value increase presented as financing cost (Para 17)
- Impairment Losses and Reversals
  - 🎯 Recognise impairment loss for initial or subsequent write-down (Para 20)
  - 🎯 Recognise gain for subsequent increase in fair value (Para 21)
  - ⚠️ Gain shall not exceed cumulative impairment loss recognised (Para 21)
  - 🎯 Allocation of impairment loss to disposal group (Para 23)
    - Reduce carrying amount of goodwill first
    - Then other assets pro rata based on carrying amount
- Changes to a plan of sale (Para 27)
  - 🎯 Measure at lower of carrying amount before classification
  - 🎯 Adjusted for depreciation had it not been classified
  - 🎯 And its recoverable amount at date of decision

## Presentation
- Balance Sheet Presentation
  - 🎯 Present non-current asset classified as held for sale separately (Para 38)
  - 🎯 Present liabilities of disposal group separately (Para 38)
  - ⚠️ Assets and liabilities shall not be offset (Para 38)
- Income Statement Presentation
  - 🎯 Discontinued operations presented as a single amount (Para 33)
  - 🎯 Any required adjustment included in continuing operations (Para 28)

## Disclosure Requirements
- 🎯 Single amount in P&L comprising total of (Para 33)
  - Post-tax profit or loss of discontinued operations
  - Post-tax gain or loss on fair value measurement
- 🎯 Analysis of the single amount into (Para 33)
  - Revenue, expenses and pre-tax profit of discontinued operations
  - Related income tax expense as per Ind AS 12
- 🎯 Disclosures in statement of cash flows (Para 33)
  - Net cash flows attributable to operating activities
  - Net cash flows attributable to investing activities
  - Net cash flows attributable to financing activities
- 🎯 Additional disclosures for held for sale assets (Para 41)
  - Description of the non-current asset or disposal group
  - Facts and circumstances leading to expected disposal
  - Expected manner and timing of that disposal
  - Gain or loss recognised on measurement or disposal
  - Reportable segment in which presented as per Ind AS 108
- 🎯 Adjustments to amounts previously classified in prior periods (Para 35)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 9 Exception to One-Year Requirement (App B)
  - Delay caused by events beyond entity control (B1)
  - Entity reasonably expects others will impose transfer conditions (B1a)
  - Buyer unexpectedly imposes conditions requiring timely actions (B1b)
  - Unlikely circumstances arise during initial one-year period (B1c)
  - Entity takes necessary action and criteria are met (B1c)

## Illustrative Examples
- IE 1 — Depreciation cessation (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Asset classified as HFS mid-year
  - 💡 Entry: Depreciate until classification date, then stop
  - 💡 Conclusion: Carry at lower of CA and FV-CTS
- IE 3 — Disposal group remeasurement (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Group with inventory and investments
  - 💡 Calculation: Remeasure non-scope assets first per standard
  - 💡 Conclusion: Impairment allocated only to scope-included assets
- IE 4 — Reversal of held for sale (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Property no longer meets criteria
  - 💡 Calculation: CA adjusted for skipped depreciation vs Recoverable amount
  - 💡 Conclusion: Adjustment charged to profit or loss
- IE 5 — Single outlet sale definition (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Retailer sells one of 200 outlets
  - 💡 Conclusion: Disposal group but not discontinued operation
- IE 8 — Regulated unit sale timing (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Approval customary and expected within 1 year
  - 💡 Conclusion: Qualifies for HFS on commitment date
- IE 12 — Shareholder approval uncertainty (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Formal vote outcome is uncertain
  - 💡 Conclusion: Highly probable test not met, no HFS
- IE 16 — Idle manufacturing plant (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Demand declined, kept in workable condition
  - 💡 Conclusion: Neither abandoned nor HFS, continue depreciation
- IE 18 — Impairment reversal limit (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Fair value increases after initial impairment
  - 💡 Calculation: Gain restricted to cumulative impairment recognised
- IE 24 — Disposal group impairment allocation (TB)
  - 💡 Scenario: Group FV drops below CA
  - 💡 Conclusion: Allocate to goodwill first, not to current assets

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- ⚠️ Para 9: Exception to one-year requirement
  - Delay caused by events beyond entity control
  - Entity remains committed to its plan to sell
- ⚠️ Para 8A: Loss of control in subsidiary
  - Classify all assets and liabilities of subsidiary
  - Regardless of retaining non-controlling interest after sale
- ⚠️ Para 11: Acquired exclusively for subsequent disposal
  - Short period to meet criteria, usually three months
- ⚠️ Para 12: Criteria met after reporting period
  - Shall not classify as held for sale in issued statements
  - Must disclose in notes if met before authorisation
- ⚠️ Para 13: Non-current assets to be abandoned
  - Shall not account for temporarily idle asset as abandoned
  - Carrying amount recovered through continuing use

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- Para 7 — Examples of Available for Immediate Sale (TB)
  - 📋 Headquarters needing vacating is usual and customary
  - 📋 Waiting for replacement indicates not available immediately
  - 📋 Clearing backlog of orders indicates not available immediately
  - 📋 Selling facility with uncompleted orders is available immediately
  - 📋 Renovating property prior to sale indicates not available immediately
  - 📋 Buyer undertaking normal legal searches qualifies as available

## Logical Flow Chains
- RAMD Master Spine
  - Recognition: Are held for sale criteria met?
    - Yes: Initial Measurement (Lower of CA and FV-CTS)
      - leads to Subsequent Measurement (Impairment/Reversals)
        - leads to Presentation (Separate in Balance Sheet)
          - leads to Disclosure (Separate in P&L and Notes)
    - No: Continue normal accounting under relevant Ind AS
- Changes to Plan Chain (Para 26)
  - Condition: Held for sale criteria no longer met
    - requires: Cease classification as held for sale
      - leads to: Measure at lower of adjusted CA and recoverable amount
        - results in: Adjustment included in continuing operations P&L

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Scope and Concept
  - Old AS: AS 24 covered only Discontinuing Operations
  - Ind AS: Ind AS 105 covers both Held for Sale and Discontinued
  - Impact: Broader scope and specific measurement rules introduced
  - ICAI exam relevance: Triggers measurement changes not in old GAAP
- 🔄 Measurement Basis
  - Old AS: No explicit held for sale measurement category
  - Ind AS: Requires measurement at lower of CA and FV-CTS
  - Impact: Immediate impairment recognition on classification

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- 🌐 Presentation standard reference
  - IFRS: Refers to IFRS 18 Presentation and Disclosure
  - Ind AS carve-out: Refers to Ind AS 1 Presentation of Financial Statements
  - Reason for carve-out: IFRS 18 is not yet adopted in India
- 🌐 Scope Exclusions
  - IFRS: Mentions IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts
  - Ind AS addition: Uses Ind AS 117 equivalents
- 🌐 Subsidiaries without Public Accountability
  - IFRS: Explicit exemptions under IFRS 19
  - Ind AS carve-out: No equivalent Ind AS 19 standard exists

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 One Year Rule (Para 8)
  - Threshold: Sale must be expected to complete within 12 months
  - Tests: Validity of highly probable sale classification
- 🧮 Three Month Rule (Para 11)
  - Threshold: Criteria met within short period, usually 3 months
  - Tests: Exclusively acquired for subsequent disposal exception
- 🧮 Impairment Allocation Formula (Para 23)
  - Formula: Step 1 reduce Goodwill to zero, Step 2 pro-rata other assets
  - Variables: Carrying amount of scope-included assets only

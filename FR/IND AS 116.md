# Ind AS 116: Leases

## Objective and Scope
- 🎯 Ensure lessees and lessors provide relevant information (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Faithfully represents those lease transactions (Para 1)
  - Gives basis to assess lease financial effects (Para 1)
- Scope applies to all leases except: (Para 3)
  - ⚠️ Leases to explore for non-regenerative resources (Para 3a)
  - ⚠️ Leases of biological assets by lessee (Para 3b)
  - ⚠️ Service concession arrangements (Para 3c)
  - ⚠️ IP licences granted by a lessor (Para 3d)
  - ⚠️ Lessee rights under intangible licensing agreements (Para 3e)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 38 for intangible exclusions (Para 3e)

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Lease (App A): A contract, or part of a contract, that conveys the right to use an asset (the underlying asset) for a period of time in exchange for consideration.
- 📌 Lessee (App A): An entity that obtains the right to use an underlying asset for a period of time in exchange for consideration.
- 📌 Lessor (App A): An entity that provides the right to use an underlying asset for a period of time in exchange for consideration.
- 📌 Right-of-use asset (App A): An asset that represents a lessee’s right to use an underlying asset for the lease term.
- 📌 Initial direct costs (App A): Incremental costs of obtaining a lease that would not have been incurred if the lease had not been obtained, except for such costs incurred by a manufacturer or dealer lessor in connection with a finance lease.
- 📌 Interest rate implicit in the lease (App A): The rate of interest that causes the present value of (a) the lease payments and (b) the unguaranteed residual value to equal the sum of (i) the fair value of the underlying asset and (ii) any initial direct costs of the lessor.
- 📌 Lessee’s incremental borrowing rate (App A): The rate of interest that a lessee would have to pay to borrow over a similar term, and with a similar security, the funds necessary to obtain an asset of a similar value to the right-of-use asset in a similar economic environment.
- 📌 Lease term (App A): The non-cancellable period for which a lessee has the right to use an underlying asset, together with periods covered by an option to extend or terminate if reasonably certain.

## Recognition Criteria
- Identifying a Lease
  - 🎯 Contract conveys right to control identified asset use (Para 9)
  - 🎯 Must exchange for consideration over period of time (Para 9)
- Separating Components
  - 🎯 Account for each lease component separately (Para 12)
  - ⚠️ Practical expedient allows combining lease and non-lease (Para 15)
- Lessee Recognition
  - 🎯 Recognise right-of-use asset and lease liability (Para 22)
  - ⚠️ Exception for short-term leases (Para 5a)
  - ⚠️ Exception for low-value underlying assets (Para 5b)
- Lessor Recognition
  - 🎯 Classify each lease as operating or finance (Para 61)
  - 🎯 Finance lease: transfers substantially all risks and rewards (Para 62)
  - 🎯 Operating lease: does not transfer substantially all risks (Para 62)

## Measurement
- Lessee Initial Measurement
  - 🎯 Measure right-of-use asset at cost (Para 23)
  - 📋 Components of ROU asset cost (Para 24)
    - Amount of initial measurement of lease liability
    - Lease payments made before commencement less incentives
    - Initial direct costs incurred by lessee
    - Estimate of dismantling and restoring costs
  - 🎯 Measure lease liability at present value of unpaid payments (Para 26)
    - 🧮 Discount using interest rate implicit in lease (Para 26)
    - ⚠️ Use incremental borrowing rate if implicit unknown (Para 26)
- Lessee Subsequent Measurement
  - 🎯 Apply cost model to right-of-use asset (Para 29)
    - Cost less accumulated depreciation and impairment (Para 30a)
    - Adjusted for lease liability remeasurement (Para 30b)
  - 🎯 Subsequent measurement of lease liability (Para 36)
    - Increase carrying amount to reflect interest (Para 36a)
    - Reduce carrying amount to reflect payments made (Para 36b)
    - Remeasure to reflect reassessments or modifications (Para 36c)
- Lessor Initial Measurement (Finance Lease)
  - 🎯 Recognise asset as receivable equal to net investment (Para 67)
  - 🧮 Measure net investment using implicit interest rate (Para 68)
  - 📋 Lease payments included in measurement (Para 70)
    - Fixed payments less lease incentives payable
    - Variable payments depending on index or rate
    - Residual value guarantees provided to lessor
    - Purchase option exercise price if reasonably certain
    - Termination penalties if term reflects exercise
- Lessor Subsequent Measurement
  - 🎯 Finance Lease: Recognise constant periodic rate of return (Para 75)
  - 🎯 Operating Lease: Recognise income on straight-line basis (Para 81)
- Sale and Leaseback Measurement
  - 🎯 Seller-lessee measures ROU asset at retained proportion (Para 100a)
  - 🎯 Recognise gain or loss only on transferred rights (Para 100a)

## Presentation
- Lessee Presentation
  - 🎯 Present ROU assets separately in balance sheet (Para 47a)
  - 🎯 Present lease liabilities separately in balance sheet (Para 47b)
  - 🎯 Present interest expense separately from depreciation (Para 49)
  - 🎯 Principal portion of liability in financing cash flows (Para 50a)
  - 🎯 Interest portion applying Ind AS 7 cash flows (Para 50b)
  - 🎯 Short-term and low-value payments in operating flows (Para 50c)
- Lessor Presentation
  - 🎯 Finance Lease: Present assets as receivable (Para 67)
  - 🎯 Operating Lease: Present underlying assets by nature (Para 88)

## Disclosure Requirements
- Lessee Disclosures (Para 53)
  - 🎯 Depreciation charge for ROU assets by class (Para 53a)
  - 🎯 Interest expense on lease liabilities (Para 53b)
  - 🎯 Expense relating to short-term leases (Para 53c)
  - 🎯 Expense relating to low-value asset leases (Para 53d)
  - 🎯 Expense relating to variable lease payments (Para 53e)
  - 🎯 Income from subleasing right-of-use assets (Para 53f)
  - 🎯 Total cash outflow for leases (Para 53g)
  - 🎯 Additions to right-of-use assets (Para 53h)
  - 🎯 Gains or losses from sale and leaseback (Para 53i)
  - 🎯 End of period carrying amount of ROU assets (Para 53j)
  - 🎯 Maturity analysis of lease liabilities (Para 58)
  - 🎯 Additional qualitative and quantitative information (Para 59)
- Lessor Disclosures (Para 90)
  - Finance Leases
    - 🎯 Selling profit or loss (Para 90ai)
    - 🎯 Finance income on the net investment (Para 90aii)
    - 🎯 Income relating to variable lease payments (Para 90aiii)
    - 🎯 Qualitative and quantitative explanation of changes (Para 93)
    - 🎯 Maturity analysis of lease payments receivable (Para 94)
  - Operating Leases
    - 🎯 Lease income separately disclosing variable payments (Para 90b)
    - 🎯 Disaggregate PPE into leased and non-leased assets (Para 95)
    - 🎯 Maturity analysis showing undiscounted payments (Para 97)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Portfolio Application
  - 🎯 May apply standard to portfolio with similar characteristics (B1)
- Explains Combination of Contracts
  - 🎯 Combine if negotiated as package with overall objective (B2a)
- Explains Low-value Asset Assessment
  - 🎯 Assessed based on value when asset is new (B3)
  - 🎯 Independent of lessee size or materiality (B4)
- Explains Identifying a Lease
  - 🎯 Customer must obtain substantially all economic benefits (B9a)
  - 🎯 Customer must have right to direct the use (B9b)
- Explains Lease Term
  - 🎯 Assess if reasonably certain to exercise extension options (B37)

- ⚠️ Para 34: Investment property — Must use fair value if Ind AS 40 allows
- ⚠️ Para B14: Substantive substitution rights — Precludes identified asset control
- ⚠️ Para 101: Sale not at fair value — Adjust as prepayment or financing

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- Para B8 — Examples of low-value underlying assets
  - 📋 tablet and personal computers
  - 📋 small items of office furniture
  - 📋 telephones
- Para 63 — Examples of situations classifying lease as finance lease
  - 📋 lease transfers ownership of the underlying asset
  - 📋 lessee has option to purchase at price lower than fair value
  - 📋 lease term is for major part of economic life
  - 📋 present value of payments amounts to substantially all fair value
  - 📋 underlying asset is of specialised nature
- Para B42(a) — Examples of in-substance fixed lease payments
  - 📋 payments made only if asset is proven capable of operating
  - 📋 payments initially structured as variable but variability resolves
- Para B26 — Examples of decision-making rights to direct use
  - 📋 rights to change the type of output produced
  - 📋 rights to change when the output is produced
  - 📋 rights to change where the output is produced
  - 📋 rights to change whether and quantity of output produced

## Logical Flow Chains
- RAMD Spine for Lessee Accounting (Para 9-60)
  - Recognition: Is contract or contains a lease? (Para 9)
    - Yes: Are recognition exemptions applied? (Para 5)
      - Yes: Expense payments on straight-line basis (Para 6)
      - No: leads to Initial Measurement of ROU Asset (Para 23)
        - leads to Subsequent Measurement via cost model (Para 29)
          - leads to Presentation on Balance Sheet and P&L (Para 47)
            - leads to Disclosure in financial statement Notes (Para 53)
    - No: Apply Ind AS 115 or other applicable standard
- Assessing if asset is identified (Para B13-B20)
  - Explicitly or implicitly specified? (Para B13)
    - Yes: Does supplier have substantive substitution right? (Para B14)
      - Yes: Asset is not identified, thus not a lease
      - No: Asset is identified, proceed to evaluate control

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Lessee Accounting Model (TB)
  - Old AS 19: Dual model classifying Finance vs Operating lease
  - Ind AS 116: Single model recognising ROU Asset and Liability
  - Financial Impact: Significantly increases assets and liabilities on balance sheet
  - ICAI Exam Note: High frequency question on financial ratio impacts
- 🔄 Definition of Lease (TB)
  - Old AS 19: Based on risks and rewards transfer concept
  - Ind AS 116: Based on right to control use of identified asset
  - Financial Impact: Some service contracts may now be capitalised leases
  - ICAI Exam Note: Often asked to evaluate contract under control criteria

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- 🌐 Measurement of ROU Asset for Investment Property (TB)
  - IFRS 16: Requires fair value model if IAS 40 fair value model used
  - Ind AS 116: Excludes this as Ind AS 40 mandates cost model
  - Reason for carve-out: Maintain structural consistency with Ind AS 40

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 Initial Measurement of ROU Asset (Para 24)
  - Formula: Lease Liability + Pre-payments - Incentives + Direct costs + Dismantling
  - Variables: Distinct components forming initial capitalised cost
  - Threshold: Applies universally to all capitalised leases
- 🧮 Lease Liability Discounting (Para 26)
  - Formula: Present Value of unpaid lease payments over lease term
  - Variables: Discounted using implicit rate or incremental borrowing rate
  - Threshold: Implicit rate must be readily determinable to use
- 🧮 Sale and Leaseback ROU retained (Para 100a)
  - Formula: (PV of lease payments / Fair Value of Asset) * Carrying Amount
  - Variables: Determines exact right-of-use proportion retained by seller-lessee
  - Threshold: Transfer must strictly qualify as sale under Ind AS 115

## Transition and Effective Date
- 📅 Effective date: Annual reporting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2019
- 📅 Transition method: Full retrospective or modified retrospective permitted (Para C5)
- ⚠️ Practical expedients available on transition (Para C10)
  - May elect not to reassess if existing contract is lease
  - May apply single discount rate to similar lease portfolio
  - May exclude initial direct costs from ROU measurement
  - May use hindsight for lease term determination
- 🎯 Disclosures required in year of adoption
  - Weighted average lessee borrowing rate applied to liabilities (Para C12a)

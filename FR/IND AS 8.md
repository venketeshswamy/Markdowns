# Ind AS 8: Accounting Policies, Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors

## Objective and Scope
- Objective (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Prescribe selection and change criteria for accounting policies (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Prescribe accounting treatment and disclosure for changes and errors (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Enhance relevance reliability and comparability of financial statements (Para 1)
- Scope of application (Para 3)
  - 🎯 Apply in selecting and applying accounting policies (Para 3)
  - 🎯 Apply in accounting for policy and estimate changes (Para 3)
  - 🎯 Apply in correcting prior period errors (Para 3)
  - ⚠️ Exclusion: Tax effects of adjustments handled under Ind AS 12 (Para 4)

## Key Definitions
- Accounting policies
  - 📌 Specific principles bases conventions rules and practices applied (Para 5)
- Accounting estimates
  - 📌 Monetary amounts subject to measurement uncertainty (Para 5)
- Indian Accounting Standards
  - 📌 Standards prescribed under Section 133 of Companies Act (Para 5)
- Material
  - 📌 Definition aligned with paragraph 7 of Ind AS 1 (Para 5)
- Prior period errors
  - 📌 Omissions and misstatements from failure or misuse of reliable info (Para 5)
- Retrospective application
  - 📌 Applying new policy as if always applied (Para 5)
- Retrospective restatement
  - 📌 Correcting error amounts as if error never occurred (Para 5)
- Impracticable
  - 📌 Cannot apply after making every reasonable effort (Para 5)
- Prospective application
  - 📌 Applying new policy or estimate change from change date forward (Para 5)

## Recognition Criteria
- Selection of policies
  - 🎯 Determine by applying specific applicable Ind AS (Para 7)
  - ⚠️ Policies need not be applied if effect is immaterial (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Immaterial departures to achieve specific presentation are inappropriate (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Management judgment used in absence of specific Ind AS (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Judgment must yield relevant and reliable information (Para 10)
- Changes in accounting policies
  - ⚠️ Permitted only if required by Ind AS (Para 14)
  - ⚠️ Permitted if results in reliable and more relevant info (Para 14)
- Changes in accounting estimates
  - 🎯 Recognise change in estimate prospectively in profit or loss (Para 36)
- Error corrections
  - 🎯 Correct material prior period errors retrospectively upon discovery (Para 42)

## Measurement
- Policy Selection Hierarchy (Para 11)
  - 🎯 First refer to Ind ASs dealing with similar issues (Para 11)
  - 🎯 Second refer to Conceptual Framework definitions and criteria (Para 11)
  - ⚠️ May consider recent IASB or standard-setting pronouncements (Para 12)
- Retrospective Application Mechanics
  - 🎯 Adjust opening balance of affected equity components (Para 22)
  - 🎯 Adjust other comparative amounts for each prior period (Para 22)
- Estimate Change Mechanics
  - 🎯 Recognise in period of change if affecting that only (Para 36)
  - 🎯 Recognise in change and future periods if affecting both (Para 36)
  - 🎯 Adjust carrying amount of related asset liability or equity (Para 37)
- Retrospective Restatement Mechanics
  - 🎯 Restate comparative amounts for prior periods presented (Para 42)
  - 🎯 Restate opening balances if error occurred before earliest period (Para 42)

## Presentation
- Balance Sheet Adjustments
  - 🎯 Retrospective changes adjust opening balance of retained earnings (Para 26)
  - ⚠️ Retrospective changes can adjust other equity if required (Para 26)
- Profit and Loss Presentation
  - ⚠️ Exclude error corrections from current period profit or loss (Para 46)
  - 🎯 Include prospective estimate changes directly in profit or loss (Para 36)

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- Exceptions to Retrospective Application
  - ⚠️ Para 19: Follow transitional provisions instead of retrospective rules (Para 19)
  - ⚠️ Para 23: Do not apply retrospectively if effects are impracticable (Para 23)
  - ⚠️ Para 24: If period-specific effect impracticable adjust earliest practicable equity (Para 24)
  - ⚠️ Para 25: If cumulative effect impracticable apply prospectively (Para 25)
- Exceptions to Retrospective Restatement
  - ⚠️ Para 43: Do not restate errors retrospectively if impracticable (Para 43)
  - ⚠️ Para 44: If period-specific error effect impracticable restate earliest practicable balances (Para 44)
  - ⚠️ Para 45: If cumulative error effect impracticable restate prospectively (Para 45)

## Logical Flow Chains
- Policy Selection RAMD Spine
  - Selection: Standard exists? Yes: Apply standard (Para 7) leads to Measurement and Disclosure
  - Selection: Standard exists? No: Use judgement hierarchy (Para 10-11) leads to policy selection
- Policy Change Flow
  - Change proposed: Allowed under Para 14? Yes: Apply transitional or retrospective rules (Para 19)
  - Retrospective: Practicable? Yes: Adjust opening comparative equity (Para 22) leads to Disclosure (Para 28)
  - Retrospective: Practicable? No: Adjust earliest practicable or prospective (Para 24-25)
- Prior Period Error Flow
  - Error discovered: Material? Yes: Retrospective restatement required (Para 42)
  - Restatement: Practicable? Yes: Restate comparative periods (Para 42) leads to Disclosure (Para 49)
  - Restatement: Practicable? No: Adjust earliest opening balance or prospective (Para 44-45)

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- Treatment of Prior Period Items
  - 🔄 AS 5: Prior period items included in current P and L with separate disclosure
  - 🔄 Ind AS 8: Prior period errors corrected retrospectively by restating comparatives
  - Impact: Current period P and L is not distorted by old errors
  - ICAI Exam Note: High-yield point on comparative financial statement restatement
- Extraordinary Items
  - 🔄 AS 5: Disclose extraordinary items separately in P and L
  - 🔄 Ind AS 8: Does not recognize extraordinary items (aligned with Ind AS 1)
  - Impact: Simpler P and L classification without arbitrary extraordinary splits
  - ICAI Exam Note: Often asked in presentation and classification questions

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- Priority of IASB Pronouncements
  - 🌐 IAS 8: Mentions standard-setters with similar frameworks but not IASB specifically
  - 🌐 Ind AS 8: Explicitly permits first considering most recent IASB pronouncements (Para 12)
  - Reason for carve-out: Facilitate convergence and alignment with international practices (App 1)
- Integral and Non-Integral Guidance
  - 🌐 IAS 8: Contains references to non-integral guidance accompanying the standard
  - 🌐 Ind AS 8: Modifies Para 9 to delete non-integral guidance text (App 1)
  - Reason for carve-out: Non-integral guidance is not notified under Indian law

# Ind AS 103: Business Combinations

## Objective and Scope
- 🎯 Improve relevance, reliability, and comparability of financial statements (Para 1)
- 🎯 Establish principles for recognizing and measuring assets and liabilities (Para 1)
- 🎯 Establish principles for recognizing goodwill or bargain purchase gain (Para 1)
- 🎯 Determine information to disclose for evaluating financial effects (Para 1)
- Scope applies to all business combinations (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Exception 1: Formation of a joint arrangement (Para 2(a))
  - ⚠️ Exception 2: Acquisition of asset or group of assets (Para 2(b))
  - ⚠️ Exception 3: Combinations of entities under common control (Para 2(c))
  - ⚠️ Exception 4: Acquisition by an investment entity (Para 2A)

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Business Combination (App A): A transaction or other event in which an acquirer obtains control of one or more businesses.
- 📌 Business (App A): An integrated set of activities and assets that is capable of being conducted and managed for the purpose of providing goods or services to customers, generating investment income (such as dividends or interest) or generating other income from ordinary activities.
- 📌 Acquirer (App A): The entity that obtains control of the acquiree.
- 📌 Acquiree (App A): The business or businesses that the acquirer obtains control of in a business combination.
- 📌 Contingent Consideration (App A): Usually, an obligation of the acquirer to transfer additional assets or equity interests to the former owners of an acquiree as part of the exchange for control of the acquiree if specified future events occur or conditions are met.

## Recognition Criteria
- General recognition principle
  - 🎯 Recognize identifiable assets acquired and liabilities assumed (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Recognize any non-controlling interest in the acquiree (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Condition 1: Must meet Conceptual Framework asset/liability definitions (Para 11)
  - 🎯 Condition 2: Must be part of exchange for the acquiree (Para 12)
  - 💡 May recognize assets not previously recognized by acquiree (Para 13)
- Recognition exceptions
  - ⚠️ Contingent liabilities: Recognize if present obligation and reliably measurable (Para 23)
  - ⚠️ Contingent assets: Shall not recognize contingent asset at acquisition (Para 23A)
  - ⚠️ Operating leases: Do not recognize asset/liability for favorable/unfavourable terms (B42)

## Measurement
- Initial Measurement Principle
  - 🎯 Measure acquired assets and assumed liabilities at acquisition-date fair value (Para 18)
  - 🎯 Measure non-controlling interest at fair value or proportionate share (Para 19)
- Exceptions to Measurement Principle
  - ⚠️ Reacquired rights: Measure based on remaining contractual term (Para 29)
  - ⚠️ Share-based payments: Measure per Ind AS 102 (Para 30)
  - ⚠️ Assets held for sale: Measure at fair value less costs to sell (Para 31)
- Exceptions to Both Recognition and Measurement
  - ⚠️ Income taxes: Account per Ind AS 12 (Para 24)
  - ⚠️ Employee benefits: Account per Ind AS 19 (Para 26)
  - ⚠️ Indemnification assets: Measure on same basis as indemnified item (Para 27)
  - ⚠️ Leases: Recognize ROU asset and lease liability (Para 28A)
- Measurement of Consideration Transferred
  - 🎯 Measure at acquisition-date fair value of assets transferred (Para 37)
  - 🎯 Include acquisition-date fair value of contingent consideration (Para 39)
  - ⚠️ Acquisition-related costs are expensed, not part of consideration (Para 53)
- Measurement of Goodwill or Bargain Purchase
  - 🎯 Goodwill: Excess of consideration and NCI over net identifiable assets (Para 32)
  - ⚠️ Bargain purchase: Recognize gain in Other Comprehensive Income (Para 34)
- Subsequent Measurement
  - 🎯 Reacquired rights: Amortise over remaining contract period (Para 55)
  - 🎯 Contingent liabilities: Higher of Ind AS 37 amount or initial minus income (Para 56)
  - 🎯 Indemnification assets: Assess collectibility and derecognize when settled (Para 57)
  - 🎯 Contingent consideration equity: Do not remeasure (Para 58)
  - 🎯 Contingent consideration liability: Remeasure at fair value through P&L (Para 58)

## Presentation
- Balance Sheet Classification
  - 🎯 Classify acquired assets and liabilities based on acquisition-date conditions (Para 15)
  - 🔗 Financial instruments classified per Ind AS 109 (Para 16)
  - ⚠️ Leases retain original inception classification unless modified (Para 17)
- Statement of Changes in Equity
  - 🎯 Bargain purchase gain accumulates in equity as capital reserve (TB)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 2: Common Control Combinations
  - Apply pooling of interest method (B1)
  - Assets and liabilities reflected at carrying amounts (TB)
  - No adjustments made to reflect fair values (TB)
- Explains Para 3: Definition of a Business
  - Assess if integrated set is capable of being conducted and managed (B11)
  - If no outputs: acquired process must be critical to develop outputs (B12B)
  - If outputs exist: process must be critical to continue producing outputs (B12C)
- Explains Para 6: Identifying the Acquirer
  - Generally entity transferring cash or other assets (B14)
  - Generally entity issuing equity interests (B15)
  - Consider relative voting rights in combined entity (B15)
  - Consider composition of governing body and senior management (B15)
- Explains Para 33: Mutual Entities
  - Measure goodwill using fair value of acquiree equity interests (B47)
  - Recognize acquiree net assets as direct addition to capital (B47)
- Explains Para 51: Determining What is Part of Combination
  - Consider reasons for the transaction (B50)
  - Consider who initiated the transaction (B50)
  - Consider timing of the transaction (B50)
- Explains Para 52: Pre-existing Relationships
  - Settlement of non-contractual relationship measured at fair value (B52)
  - Settlement of contractual relationship measured at lesser of two amounts (B52)

## Illustrative Examples
- IE1 — 💡 Concentration Test
  - 💡 Scenario: Entity acquires 1000Cr building and 200Cr cash (TB)
  - 💡 Key calculation: 1000/1100 = 91% value in single asset (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Concentration test met, transaction is asset acquisition (TB)
- IE2 — 💡 Development Stage Entity
  - 💡 Scenario: Research engineers developing pending patent with no revenue (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Workforce plus tech constitutes substantive process and business (TB)
- IE3 — 💡 Contingent Consideration Liability
  - 💡 Scenario: 20M payable if specified performance targets met (TB)
  - 💡 Key entry: Initial PV recorded as liability, goodwill increased (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Subsequent fair value changes recorded in P&L (TB)
- IE4 — 💡 Step Acquisition
  - 💡 Scenario: Acquirer holds 30%, buys remaining 70% (TB)
  - 💡 Key calculation: Remeasure 30% holding to fair value on acquisition date (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Gain or loss recognized in P&L, OCI reclassified if applicable (TB)
- IE5 — 💡 Common Control Demerger
  - 💡 Scenario: Division transferred to new company with same shareholders (TB)
  - 💡 Key entry: Assets transferred at book value, difference to Capital Reserve (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: No goodwill recognized in common control pooling of interest (TB)

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- ⚠️ Para 2: Excludes joint ventures, common control, and asset acquisitions
- ⚠️ Para 45: Measurement period adjustments allowed only within one year
- ⚠️ Para 47: Adjustments must reflect facts existing at acquisition date only
- ⚠️ Para 53: Debt or equity issuance costs follow Ind AS 32/109, not expensed as acquisition costs
- ⚠️ Para 28A: Short-term and low-value leases exempt from ROU recognition
- ⚠️ Para 23: Contingent liabilities recognized even if outflow is not probable
- ⚠️ Para 58: Contingent consideration classified as equity is never remeasured
- ⚠️ Para 34: Bargain purchase requires reassessment of all assets and liabilities before recognition

## Logical Flow Chains
- Chain 1 — RAMD Spine
  - Recognition: Does transaction meet definition of business combination?
    - Yes: leads to Identification of Acquirer and Acquisition Date (Para 8)
      - leads to Initial Measurement of identifiable assets and liabilities (Para 18)
        - leads to Measurement of Goodwill or Bargain Purchase Gain (Para 32)
          - leads to Subsequent Measurement of specific items (Para 54)
            - leads to Disclosure of financial effects (Para 59)
    - No: leads to Asset Acquisition Accounting (Para 2)
- Chain 2 — Business Definition Decision Tree
  - Apply Optional Concentration Test (B7A)
    - If substantially all fair value in single asset: leads to Asset Acquisition (B7B)
    - If not met or bypassed: Apply Substantive Process Test (B12)
      - If inputs and substantive process acquired: leads to Business Combination (B12)
      - If missing substantive process: leads to Asset Acquisition (B12)

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Asset Acquisition vs Business Combination Differences (Proxy for AS 14) (TB)
  - Old AS / Asset Acq: Intangibles allocated on relative FV, no goodwill recognized
  - Ind AS Business Comb: Intangibles recognized at FV, goodwill separately recognized
  - Financial Impact: Changes asset carrying values and creates goodwill on balance sheet
  - ICAI Exam Note: High yield for differentiating asset purchase from business purchase
- 🔄 Transaction Costs Treatment (TB)
  - Old AS / Asset Acq: Capitalized as component of the cost of assets acquired
  - Ind AS Business Comb: Expensed immediately in period incurred
  - Financial Impact: Immediate reduction in P&L for business combinations
  - ICAI Exam Note: Frequently tested adjustment in purchase consideration calculations
- 🔄 Deferred Tax Accounting on Initial Recognition (TB)
  - Old AS / Asset Acq: Prohibited for temporary differences on initial asset recognition
  - Ind AS Business Comb: Recorded on temporary differences of assets and liabilities
  - Financial Impact: Creates Deferred Tax Assets/Liabilities which impact goodwill amount
  - ICAI Exam Note: Crucial step in net identifiable asset calculation

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 Goodwill Calculation (Para 32)
  - Formula: (Consideration + NCI + Previously Held Equity) - Net Identifiable Assets
  - Variables: Consideration transferred, Non-controlling interest, Acquisition-date fair values
  - Threshold: If result is negative, it triggers Bargain Purchase reassessment
- 🧮 Measurement Period Limit (Para 45)
  - Formula: Acquisition Date + Maximum 1 Year
  - Variables: Time elapsed since acquisition date
  - Threshold: Hard stop at one year for adjusting provisional amounts
- 🧮 Concentration Test (B7B)
  - Formula: FV of single asset / FV of total gross assets acquired
  - Variables: Gross assets exclude cash, deferred tax assets, and goodwill
  - Threshold: Must be "substantially all" to qualify as asset acquisition
- 🧮 Pre-combination SBP Allocation (B58)
  - Formula: Market measure × (Completed vesting period / Greater vesting period)
  - Variables: Acquiree award market measure, original and total vesting periods
  - Threshold: Determines portion of replacement award allocated to purchase consideration

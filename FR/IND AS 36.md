# Ind AS 36: Impairment of Assets

## Objective and Scope
- 🎯 Ensure assets carried at not more than recoverable amount (Para 1)
- 🎯 Prescribe procedures for impairment recognition and reversal (Para 1)
- Applies to all assets except specific exclusions (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Inventories (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 2
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Deferred tax assets (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 12
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Employee benefit assets (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 19
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Financial assets (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 109
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Investment property at fair value (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 40
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Biological assets at fair value (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 41
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Contract assets (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 115
  - ⚠️ Excluded: Non-current assets held for sale (Para 2)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 105

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Carrying amount: [amount asset is recognised after deducting accumulated depreciation and impairment] (Para 6)
- 📌 Cash-generating unit (CGU): [smallest identifiable group of assets generating independent cash inflows] (Para 6)
- 📌 Corporate assets: [assets other than goodwill contributing to future cash flows of multiple CGUs] (Para 6)
- 📌 Costs of disposal: [incremental costs directly attributable to disposal excluding finance and tax costs] (Para 6)
- 📌 Fair value: [price received to sell asset in orderly transaction at measurement date] (Para 6)
- 📌 Impairment loss: [amount carrying amount exceeds recoverable amount] (Para 6)
- 📌 Recoverable amount: [higher of fair value less costs of disposal and value in use] (Para 6)
- 📌 Value in use: [present value of future cash flows expected to be derived from asset] (Para 6)

## Recognition Criteria
- Assess for indications of impairment at end of each reporting period (Para 9)
  - 🎯 If indication exists, estimate recoverable amount (Para 9)
- Mandatory annual impairment testing (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Intangible asset with indefinite useful life (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Intangible asset not yet available for use (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Goodwill acquired in a business combination (Para 10)
  - ⚠️ Tested annually irrespective of impairment indicators (Para 10)

## Measurement
- Measuring Recoverable Amount
  - 🧮 Recoverable Amount = Higher of FVLCD or Value in use (Para 18)
  - ⚠️ If either exceeds carrying amount, asset is not impaired (Para 19)
  - ⚠️ If FVLCD cannot be measured, use Value in use (Para 20)
- Fair Value Less Costs of Disposal (FVLCD)
  - 🎯 Exclude finance costs and income tax expense from disposal costs (Para 28)
  - ⚠️ Termination benefits and business reorganisation costs excluded (Para 28)
- Value in Use (VIU)
  - 🎯 Estimate future cash inflows and outflows (Para 31)
  - 🎯 Apply appropriate discount rate to cash flows (Para 31)
  - 🎯 Base projections on recent management budgets max 5 years (Para 33)
  - ⚠️ Exclude cash flows from uncommitted future restructurings (Para 44)
  - ⚠️ Exclude cash flows from asset performance enhancements (Para 44)
  - ⚠️ Exclude cash flows from financing activities (Para 50)
  - ⚠️ Exclude income tax receipts or payments (Para 50)
  - 🧮 Discount rate shall be pre-tax reflecting specific asset risks (Para 55)
- Recognising Impairment Loss (Individual Asset)
  - 🎯 Reduce carrying amount to recoverable amount (Para 59)
  - 🎯 Recognise loss immediately in profit or loss (Para 60)
  - ⚠️ Unless carried at revalued amount under another Standard (Para 60)
  - 🎯 Adjust future depreciation over remaining useful life (Para 63)
- CGUs and Goodwill
  - 🎯 Identify smallest group of independent cash-generating assets (Para 68)
  - 🎯 Allocate goodwill to CGUs expected to benefit from synergies (Para 80)
  - 🎯 Allocate corporate assets on reasonable and consistent basis (Para 102)
  - 🎯 Impairment loss allocation order for CGU (Para 104)
    - 1st: Reduce carrying amount of allocated goodwill (Para 104)
    - 2nd: Other assets pro-rata based on carrying amounts (Para 104)
  - ⚠️ Individual asset carrying amount shall not be reduced below highest of:
    - Fair value less costs of disposal (Para 105)
    - Value in use (Para 105)
    - Zero (Para 105)
- Reversing an Impairment Loss
  - 🎯 Assess annually if prior impairment decreased or no longer exists (Para 110)
  - 🎯 If indication exists, estimate recoverable amount (Para 110)
  - 🎯 Reversal limited to carrying amount had no impairment been recognised (Para 117)
  - 🎯 Recognise reversal immediately in profit or loss (Para 119)
  - ⚠️ Reversal for CGU allocated pro-rata excluding goodwill (Para 122)
  - ⚠️ Impairment loss recognised for goodwill shall not be reversed (Para 124)

## Presentation
- Balance Sheet Impact
  - 🎯 Asset carrying amount reduced by impairment loss (Para 59)
- Profit & Loss Impact
  - 🎯 Impairment loss charged to statement of profit and loss (Para 60)
  - 🎯 Reversal of impairment credited to statement of profit and loss (Para 119)
- Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) Impact
  - 🎯 Impairment on revalued asset treated as revaluation decrease in OCI (Para 60)
  - 🎯 Reversal on revalued asset treated as revaluation increase in OCI (Para 119)

## Disclosure Requirements
- Disclose for each class of assets (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Amount of impairment losses recognised in profit or loss (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Line items in statement of profit and loss including losses (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Amount of reversals of impairment losses in profit or loss (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Line items in statement of profit and loss including reversals (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Amount of impairment losses on revalued assets in OCI (Para 126)
  - 🎯 Amount of reversals on revalued assets in OCI (Para 126)
- Disclose for each reportable segment (Para 129)
  - 🎯 Impairment losses recognised in P&L and OCI during period (Para 129)
  - 🎯 Reversals of impairment losses recognised in P&L and OCI (Para 129)
- Disclose for material individual asset or CGU impairments (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Events and circumstances leading to recognition or reversal (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Amount of the impairment loss recognised or reversed (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Nature of individual asset and reportable segment (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Description of CGU and reasons for any aggregation changes (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Recoverable amount and whether it is FVLCD or VIU (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Fair value hierarchy level if based on FVLCD (Para 130)
  - 🎯 Discount rate used if based on VIU (Para 130)
- Disclose estimates for CGUs containing goodwill or indefinite intangibles (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Carrying amount of allocated goodwill (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Carrying amount of allocated indefinite life intangibles (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Basis for determining recoverable amount (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Key assumptions and approach to determining values (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Period of projected cash flows and growth rates used (Para 134)
  - 🎯 Discount rates applied to cash flow projections (Para 134)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 30 Value in Use (App A)
  - 🎯 Present value techniques require estimated cash flows and discount rate (A1)
  - 🎯 Traditional approach uses single expected cash flow and rate (A4)
  - 🎯 Expected cash flow approach uses probability-weighted outcomes (A7)
- Explains Para 80 Testing CGUs with NCI (App C)
  - 🎯 Gross up goodwill if NCI measured at proportionate share (C4)
  - 🎯 Allocate impairment to grossed-up goodwill first (C5)
  - ⚠️ Only parent's share of goodwill impairment recognised in financials (C8)

## Illustrative Examples
- IE1 — Measurement of Recoverable Amount
  - 💡 Scenario: Building with carrying value 300, VIU 250, FVLCD 238 (TB)
  - 💡 Key calculation: Recoverable amount is higher of VIU and FVLCD (250) (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Impairment loss is 50 (300 - 250) (TB)
- IE2 — Future Restructuring Cash Flows
  - 💡 Scenario: VIU with restructuring is 44, without is 36. Carrying value 40 (TB)
  - 💡 Key calculation: Exclude future uncommitted restructuring benefits (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Use VIU 36, recognize impairment of 4 (TB)
- IE3 — Revalued Asset Impairment
  - 💡 Scenario: Asset revalued upwards by 14. Current impairment loss 15.30 (TB)
  - 💡 Key entry: Adjust 14 against Revaluation Surplus in OCI (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Remaining 1.30 charged to Profit and Loss (TB)
- IE4 — Goodwill Impairment Reversal
  - 💡 Scenario: Government reinstates licenses improving cash flows for CGU (TB)
  - 💡 Key rule: Prior impairment on goodwill (80,000) cannot be reversed (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Reverse impairment only for other identifiable assets (TB)
- IE5 — Corporate Asset Allocation
  - 💡 Scenario: HQ building allocated to 3 CGUs based on weighted carrying amounts (TB)
  - 💡 Key calculation: Test individual CGUs first, then total entity including HQ (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Pro-rata allocation of impairment if entity as a whole impaired (TB)

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- ⚠️ Para 2: Exclusions from scope — Applies to other specific Ind AS standards
- ⚠️ Para 10: Mandatory annual test — Applies irrespective of impairment indicators
- ⚠️ Para 19: FVLCD > Carrying Amount — If met, VIU calculation is not required
- ⚠️ Para 44: Future restructuring — Excluded from VIU unless entity is committed
- ⚠️ Para 50: Financing/Tax cash flows — Strictly excluded from VIU cash flow projections
- ⚠️ Para 60: Revaluation model — Impairment charged to OCI up to revaluation surplus
- ⚠️ Para 105: CGU Impairment limits — Asset not reduced below highest of FVLCD, VIU, or 0
- ⚠️ Para 124: Goodwill reversal — Reversal of goodwill impairment is strictly prohibited

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- Para 12 — Examples of external indicators of impairment
  - 📋 observable indications that asset's value has declined significantly
  - 📋 significant changes with adverse effect in technological, market, economic or legal environment
  - 📋 market interest rates or return rates have increased decreasing value in use
  - 📋 carrying amount of net assets is more than market capitalisation
- Para 12 — Examples of internal indicators of impairment
  - 📋 evidence is available of obsolescence or physical damage of asset
  - 📋 significant adverse changes in extent or manner asset is used
  - 📋 evidence from internal reporting that economic performance is worse than expected
- Para 28 — Examples of costs of disposal
  - 📋 legal costs
  - 📋 stamp duty and similar transaction taxes
  - 📋 costs of removing the asset
  - 📋 direct incremental costs to bring asset into condition for sale
- Para 111 — Examples of external indicators for reversal of impairment
  - 📋 observable indications that asset's value has increased significantly
  - 📋 significant changes with favourable effect in technological, market, economic or legal environment
  - 📋 market interest rates or return rates have decreased increasing value in use
- Para 111 — Examples of internal indicators for reversal of impairment
  - 📋 significant favourable changes in extent or manner asset is used
  - 📋 evidence from internal reporting that economic performance is better than expected

## Logical Flow Chains
- Chain 1 — RAMD Spine:
  - Recognition: Is there an indication of impairment? (Para 9)
    - Yes: leads to Initial Measurement of Recoverable Amount (Para 18)
      - leads to Subsequent Measurement (Compare Carrying Amount vs Recoverable Amount)
        - Yes (Impaired): leads to Presentation in Profit & Loss (Para 60)
          - leads to Disclosure of impairment details and assumptions (Para 126)
    - No: No impairment test required (unless mandatory annual test applies)
- Chain 2 — CGU Impairment Allocation:
  - Identify smallest cash-generating unit (Para 68)
    - requires adding allocable corporate assets and goodwill
      - leads to comparing CGU Carrying Amount with CGU Recoverable Amount
        - if impaired: reduce Goodwill to zero first
          - then: reduce other assets pro-rata based on carrying amounts
            - unless: asset hits floor limit (FVLCD, VIU, or zero)

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Impairment of Revalued Assets
  - Old AS: Permitted set off against revaluation reserve per AS 28
  - Ind AS: Treated strictly as revaluation decrease through OCI under Ind AS 16
  - Financial Impact: Impacts OCI presentation directly rather than reserve adjustment
  - ICAI Exam Note: Frequently tested adjustment in comprehensive standalone questions

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 Recoverable Amount (Para 18)
  - Formula: Higher of (Fair Value Less Costs of Disposal) and (Value in Use)
  - Variables: FVLCD = Market price - direct selling costs; VIU = PV of future cash flows
  - Threshold: If RA < Carrying Amount, asset is impaired
- 🧮 Impairment Loss (Para 59)
  - Formula: Carrying Amount - Recoverable Amount
  - Variables: Carrying amount includes accumulated depreciation
- 🧮 Revised Depreciation (Para 63)
  - Formula: (Revised Carrying Amount - Residual Value) / Remaining Useful Life
  - Variables: Revised carrying amount is post-impairment value

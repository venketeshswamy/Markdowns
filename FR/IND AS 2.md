# Ind AS 2: Inventories

## Objective and Scope
- 🎯 Prescribe the accounting treatment for inventories (Para 1)
- 🎯 Determine cost recognized as asset until revenue recognized (Para 1)
- 🎯 Provide guidance on cost determination and subsequent expense recognition (Para 1)
- 🎯 Provide guidance on any write-down to net realisable value (NRV) (Para 1)
- Scope applies to all inventories except:
  - ⚠️ Financial instruments (Para 2b)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 32 and Ind AS 109
  - ⚠️ Biological assets at point of harvest (Para 2c)
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 41
- Measurement requirements do NOT apply to:
  - ⚠️ Agricultural and forest products measured at NRV (Para 3a)
  - ⚠️ Minerals and mineral products measured at NRV (Para 3a)
  - ⚠️ Commodity broker-traders measuring inventories at fair value less costs to sell (Para 3b)
  - 🎯 Changes in these values recognized in profit or loss in period of change (Para 3)

## Key Definitions
- 📌 Inventories (Para 6): Assets:
  - Held for sale in the ordinary course of business
  - In the process of production for such sale
  - In the form of materials or supplies to be consumed in production or rendering services
- 📌 Net realisable value (NRV) (Para 6):
  - Estimated selling price in the ordinary course of business
  - Less: Estimated costs of completion
  - Less: Estimated costs necessary to make the sale
- 📌 Fair value (Para 6):
  - Price received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability
  - In an orderly transaction between market participants at measurement date
  - 🔗 See Ind AS 113 Fair Value Measurement
- ⚠️ Entity-specific vs Market-based (Para 7):
  - NRV is an entity-specific value
  - Fair value is not entity-specific
  - ⚠️ NRV may not equal fair value less costs to sell

## Recognition Criteria
- 🎯 Carrying amount recognized as expense when related revenue is recognized (Para 34)
- 🎯 Write-down to NRV recognized as expense in period write-down occurs (Para 34)
- 🎯 All inventory losses recognized as expense in period loss occurs (Para 34)
- 🎯 Reversal of write-down recognized as reduction in inventory expense (Para 34)
- ⚠️ Inventory allocated to other assets (e.g., self-constructed PPE) (Para 35):
  - 🔗 Recognized as expense during useful life of that asset (Ind AS 16)

## Measurement
- 🎯 Initial and Subsequent Base Rule:
  - 🎯 Shall be measured at the lower of cost and net realisable value (Para 9)
- Cost of Inventories:
  - 🎯 Comprises all costs of purchase, conversion, and other costs incurred (Para 10)
  - Costs of Purchase (Para 11):
    - 📋 Purchase price
    - 📋 Import duties and non-recoverable taxes
    - 📋 Transport and handling costs
    - ⚠️ Trade discounts, rebates, and similar items must be deducted
  - Costs of Conversion (Para 12-14):
    - 📋 Direct labour and costs directly related to units of production
    - 🎯 Systematic allocation of fixed and variable production overheads
    - ⚠️ Fixed overhead allocation based on normal capacity (Para 13)
    - ⚠️ Unallocated fixed overheads expensed in period incurred (Para 13)
    - 🎯 Variable overheads allocated based on actual use of facilities (Para 13)
  - Joint Products and By-products (Para 14):
    - 🎯 Allocate joint costs on a rational and consistent basis (e.g., relative sales value)
    - ⚠️ Immaterial by-products measured at NRV and deducted from main product cost
  - Other Costs (Para 15):
    - 🎯 Included only to extent incurred in bringing inventory to present location/condition
    - 📋 E.g., non-production overheads or specific customer design costs
  - ⚠️ Excluded Costs (Expensed immediately) (Para 16):
    - 📋 Abnormal amounts of wasted materials, labour, or other production costs
    - 📋 Storage costs (unless necessary before further production stage)
    - 📋 Administrative overheads not contributing to present location/condition
    - 📋 Selling and distribution costs
  - Deferred Settlement (Para 18):
    - ⚠️ Difference between normal credit price and amount paid recognized as interest expense
- Techniques for Measurement of Cost (Para 21-22):
  - Standard Cost Method: May be used if results approximate actual cost
  - Retail Method: Used for large numbers of rapidly changing items with similar margins
- Cost Formulas (Para 23-27):
  - 🎯 Specific Identification Method:
    - ⚠️ Mandatory for items not ordinarily interchangeable
    - ⚠️ Mandatory for goods/services produced for specific projects
  - 🎯 First-In, First-Out (FIFO) or Weighted Average Cost:
    - 🎯 Used for ordinarily interchangeable items
    - 🎯 Entity shall use same cost formula for all inventories of similar nature and use
    - ⚠️ Difference in geographical location alone does not justify different formulas (Para 26)
- Net Realisable Value (NRV) Assessment (Para 28-33):
  - 🎯 Inventories usually written down to NRV item by item (Para 29)
  - ⚠️ Grouping similar/related items permitted if same product line/market (Para 29)
  - 🎯 Estimates based on most reliable evidence at time of estimate (Para 30)
  - ⚠️ Firm sales contracts: NRV based on contract price for contracted quantity (Para 31)
  - ⚠️ Raw Materials Exception (Para 32):
    - 🎯 Not written down if finished goods will be sold at or above cost
    - ⚠️ If finished goods will be sold below cost, materials written down to NRV
    - 🎯 Replacement cost may be best available measure of material NRV
  - Reversal of Write-down (Para 33):
    - 🎯 New assessment of NRV made in each subsequent period
    - 🎯 Reversal limited to the amount of the original write-down

## Presentation
- 🎯 Present carrying amounts in classifications appropriate to the entity (Para 37)
- 📋 Common classifications: merchandise, production supplies, materials, WIP, finished goods (Para 37)
- ⚠️ Classification by nature of expense (Para 39):
  - If expenses classified by nature, present costs recognized as expense for raw materials
  - Present net change in inventories for the period

## Disclosure Requirements
- 🎯 Accounting policies adopted in measuring inventories (Para 36a)
- 🎯 The cost formula used (Para 36a)
- 🎯 Total carrying amount of inventories (Para 36b)
- 🎯 Carrying amount in classifications appropriate to the entity (Para 36b)
- 🎯 Carrying amount of inventories carried at fair value less costs to sell (Para 36c)
- 🎯 Amount of inventories recognised as an expense during the period (Para 36d)
- 🎯 Amount of any write-down of inventories recognised as an expense (Para 36e)
- 🎯 Amount of any reversal of any write-down recognised as a reduction in expense (Para 36f)
- 🎯 Circumstances or events that led to the reversal of a write-down (Para 36g)
- 🎯 Carrying amount of inventories pledged as security for liabilities (Para 36h)

## Application Guidance
- 💡 Selling and Distribution Costs (TB):
  - Included under single umbrella terms but incurred to effect sale/complete transaction
  - Since not related to bringing goods to present location/condition, strictly excluded
- 💡 Cash Discounts (TB):
  - Incurred to recover sale proceeds early, not to make the sale itself
  - ⚠️ Cash discounts are ignored when determining Net Realisable Value

## Illustrative Examples
- 💡 IE1 — Allowable vs Excluded Costs (TB):
  - Scenario: Venus Trading imports cars with various handling/admin costs
  - Key calculation: Include trade discounts, handling, import duties, freight, indenting commission
  - Conclusion: Exclude accounting salaries, sales commission, after-sales warranty (expensed)
- 💡 IE2 — By-Product NRV Deduction (TB):
  - Scenario: Mars Ltd produces joint products MP1, MP2 and a by-product BP
  - Key calculation: NRV of BP (Sales less processing/packing) deducted from total joint cost
  - Conclusion: Remaining joint cost allocated to MP1 and MP2 based on sales value ratio
- 💡 IE3 — Cost Measurement Techniques (TB):
  - Scenario: Mars Fashions sells retail cosmetics and custom handbags
  - Conclusion: Cosmetics use Retail Method (Sales less 65% margin); Handbags use Standard Cost
- 💡 IE4 — NRV Calculation components (TB):
  - Scenario: Determining what deductions apply to selling price for NRV
  - Conclusion: Deduct completion costs and trade discounts. Do NOT deduct cash discounts
- 💡 IE5 — Item-by-Item vs Group Basis (TB):
  - Scenario: Sun Pharma has 4 inventory items with varying Cost and NRV
  - Key calculation: Compare Cost vs NRV for each individual item, sum the lower values
  - Conclusion: Value is 14,290 (item-by-item), grouping not permitted for unrelated items
- 💡 IE6 — Raw Material Write-down Exception (TB):
  - Scenario: UA Ltd finished goods sold below cost; raw material cost 400, replacement 300
  - Conclusion: Since FG is loss-making, RM is written down to replacement cost (NRV) of 300
- 💡 IE7 — Cancelled Order WIP Valuation (TB):
  - Scenario: Sun Ltd custom solar panels order cancelled by customer facing winding up
  - Conclusion: Retain in inventory at lower of cost or NRV; test NRV in open market despite custom design
- 💡 IE8 — Fixed Overhead Absorption at Low Capacity (TB):
  - Scenario: XYZ Ltd normal capacity 10L units, actual 7.5L units, fixed OH 30L
  - Key calculation: Absorption rate = 30L / 10L = 3 per unit (based on normal capacity)
  - Conclusion: Absorbed in inventory = 22.5L. Unabsorbed 7.5L transferred directly to P&L
- 💡 IE9 — Adjusting Event Post Balance Sheet (TB):
  - Scenario: ABC Spare parts cost 10M, BS date NRV 11.5M, April 15 NRV drops to 7.5M
  - Conclusion: Price drop is adjusting event (Ind AS 10), value inventory at lower 7.5M as of BS date
- 💡 IE10 — Reclassification from Investment Property (TB):
  - Scenario: X Ltd converts leased property (Ind AS 40) into flats for sale (Ind AS 2)
  - Key calculation: Fair value at date of change in use becomes cost basis for Ind AS 2
  - Conclusion: Add further conversion costs to this base to determine final inventory cost
- 💡 IE11 — Custom Corporate Gifts Modification (TB):
  - Scenario: Entity manufactures custom gifts, incurring specific design and loan fees
  - Key calculation: Include material, labor, design, testing. Exclude loan raising fee (borrowing cost)
  - Conclusion: Specific customer design costs are capitalized into inventory cost

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- ⚠️ Para 2/3: Financial instruments and biological assets at harvest excluded from scope
- ⚠️ Para 11: Trade discounts and rebates must be deducted from purchase cost
- ⚠️ Para 13: Fixed overhead rate shall NOT be increased due to low production/idle plant
- ⚠️ Para 13: In abnormally high production, fixed OH rate is decreased so inventory not measured above cost
- ⚠️ Para 14: By-products must be deducted at NRV from main product cost if immaterial
- ⚠️ Para 23: Specific identification mandatory for non-interchangeable or project-specific goods
- ⚠️ Para 29: Writing down on a class basis (e.g., all finished goods) is NOT appropriate
- ⚠️ Para 32: Raw materials not written down below cost if finished product is profitable
- ⚠️ Para 33: Write-down reversal strictly limited to the amount of the original write-down

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- 📋 Para 16 — Examples of costs excluded from inventory:
  - Abnormal amounts of wasted materials, labour or other production costs
  - Storage costs, unless necessary in production process before further stage
  - Administrative overheads that do not contribute to bringing to present location/condition
  - Selling costs
- 📋 Para 37 — Common classifications of inventories:
  - Merchandise

  - Materials
  - Work in progress
  - Finished goods

## Logical Flow Chains
- 🎯 RAMD Spine: Cost Accumulation and Valuation Flow
  - Recognition: Are items held for sale, in production, or materials to be consumed? (Para 6)
    - Yes: Proceed to Initial Measurement
    - No: Apply other Ind AS (e.g., PPE, Investment Property)
  - Initial Measurement: Accumulate Cost (Para 10)
    - Add: Purchase + Conversion + Location/Condition costs
    - Deduct: Trade discounts and rebates
    - Leads to: Total Cost of Inventory
  - Subsequent Measurement: NRV Test at Reporting Date (Para 9)
    - Is NRV < Cost?
      - Yes: Write down to NRV. Recognize write-down as expense (Para 34)
      - No: Retain at Cost
  - Presentation & Derecognition (Para 34):
    - Are goods sold?
      - Yes: Recognize carrying amount as COGS expense matching revenue
      - No: Retain on Balance Sheet
  - Disclosure: Report accounting policy, cost formula, and required amounts (Para 36)
- 🎯 Raw Material NRV Test Flow (Para 32):
  - Is finished good expected to be sold at or above cost?
    - Yes: Keep raw material at Cost (no write-down permitted)
    - No: Does RM replacement cost indicate its NRV is below cost?
      - Yes: Write down RM to replacement cost (NRV)

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 Fixed Overhead Absorption Rate (Para 13)
  - Formula: Total Fixed Production Overheads / Normal Capacity Units
  - Threshold test: If actual production << normal, use normal capacity. If actual >> normal, use actual capacity.
  - Objective: Prevent carrying idle capacity costs as assets.
- 🧮 Net Realisable Value Formula (Para 6)
  - Formula: Estimated Selling Price - Estimated Costs of Completion - Estimated Costs to Sell
  - Variable condition: Ignore cash discounts; deduct trade discounts.
- 🧮 Retail Method Formula (Para 22)
  - Formula: Sales Value of Inventory - Appropriate Percentage Gross Margin
  - Variable condition: Must factor in inventory marked down below original selling price.
- 🧮 Net Worth Calculation for Ind AS Roadmap (TB)
  - Formula: Paid-up Share Capital + All Reserves (out of profit/security premium) - Accumulated Losses - Deferred/Misc Expenditure not written off
  - Exclusions: Reserves created out of revaluation of assets and amalgamation
  - Threshold: Phase I >= 500 Cr, Phase II >= 250 Cr

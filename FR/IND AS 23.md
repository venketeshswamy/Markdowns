


# Ind AS 23: Borrowing Costs

## Objective and Scope
- Objective Principle
  - 🎯 Prescribe accounting treatment for borrowing costs (Para 1)
- Scope and Application
  - 🎯 Apply to borrowing costs accounting (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Excludes actual or imputed cost of equity (Para 3)
  - ⚠️ Excludes preference share capital not classified as liability (Para 3)
- Measurement Exemptions
  - ⚠️ Need not apply to assets measured at fair value (Para 4)
  - ⚠️ Need not apply to repetitive large-quantity inventories (Para 4)

## Key Definitions
- Borrowing Costs
  - 📌 Definition — Borrowing costs: interest and other costs that an entity incurs in connection with the borrowing of funds. (Para 5)
  - 🔗 Computed using effective interest method under Ind AS 109 (Para 6a)
- Qualifying Asset
  - 📌 Definition — Qualifying asset: an asset that necessarily takes a substantial period of time to get ready for its intended use or sale. (Para 5)
  - 💡 Substantial period generally interpreted as 12 months or more (TB)

## Recognition Criteria
- General Principle
  - 🎯 Capitalise if directly attributable to qualifying asset (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Expense all other borrowing costs in P&L (Para 8)
- Additional Criteria
  - 🎯 Probable future economic benefit condition (Para 9)
  - 🎯 Costs must be measured reliably (Para 9)
- Derecognition and Impairment
  - ⚠️ Write down if carrying amount exceeds recoverable amount (Para 16)

## Measurement and Presentation
- Initial Measurement of Specific Borrowings
  - 🎯 Based on actual borrowing costs incurred (Para 12)
  - 🧮 Deduct investment income from temporary investment of funds (Para 12)
- Initial Measurement of General Borrowings
  - 🎯 Determine costs applying capitalisation rate to expenditures (Para 14)
  - ⚠️ Capitalised amount shall not exceed total borrowing costs incurred (Para 14)
  - ⚠️ May include borrowings of parent and subsidiaries if appropriate (Para 15)
- Period of Capitalisation: Commencement
  - 🎯 Must meet three conditions simultaneously (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Condition 1: Incurs expenditures for the asset (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Condition 2: Incurs borrowing costs (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Condition 3: Undertakes necessary preparation activities (Para 17)
  - 💡 Expenditures reduced by progress payments and grants (Para 18)
- Period of Capitalisation: Suspension
  - 🎯 Suspend during extended periods of interrupted active development (Para 20)
  - ⚠️ Exception: Do not suspend if substantial technical/admin work continues (Para 21)
  - ⚠️ Exception: Do not suspend if delay is necessary process step (Para 21)
- Period of Capitalisation: Cessation
  - 🎯 Cease when substantially all preparation activities are complete (Para 22)
  - 💡 Routine admin work or minor modifications mean substantial completion (Para 23)
  - 🎯 For multi-part assets usable independently, cease upon part completion (Para 24)
  - ⚠️ If parts cannot be used independently, cease upon total completion (Para 25)

## Disclosure Requirements
- Disclosure of Capitalised Costs
  - 🎯 the amount of borrowing costs capitalised during the period (Para 26a)
  - 🎯 the capitalisation rate used to determine the amount of borrowing costs eligible for capitalisation (Para 26b)

## Illustrative Examples
- IE1 — Temporary Investment Income
  - 💡 Scenario: Specific borrowing of 10M, 2M invested temporarily (TB)
  - 💡 Calculation: Interest incurred minus interest earned on 2M (TB)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Net amount capitalised to qualifying asset (TB)
- IE2 — Suspension Exception
  - 💡 Scenario: Bridge construction halted due to high water levels (TB)
  - 💡 Principle: High water is necessary natural delay (Para 21)
  - 💡 Conclusion: Borrowing costs continue to be capitalised (TB)

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- Scope Exceptions
  - ⚠️ Para 3: Excludes equity cost — treated via Statement of Changes in Equity
  - ⚠️ Para 4: Excludes fair value assets — measured per Ind AS 41
  - ⚠️ Para 4: Excludes repetitive inventories — measured per Ind AS 2
- Measurement Exceptions
  - ⚠️ Para 12: Specific borrowing interest — reduced by temporary investment income
  - ⚠️ Para 14: General borrowing limit — cannot exceed total incurred costs
  - ⚠️ Para 16: Impairment — write down asset if cost exceeds recoverable amount
  - ⚠️ Para 21: Suspension exception — necessary delays do not trigger suspension

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- Para 6 — Examples of borrowing costs
  - 📋 interest expense calculated using the effective interest method as described in Ind AS 109
  - 📋 interest in respect of lease liabilities recognised in accordance with Ind AS 116
  - 📋 exchange differences arising from foreign currency borrowings to the extent that they are regarded as an adjustment to interest costs
- Para 7 — Examples of qualifying assets
  - 📋 inventories
  - 📋 manufacturing plants
  - 📋 power generation facilities
  - 📋 intangible assets
  - 📋 investment properties
  - 📋 bearer plants
- Para 7 — Examples of non-qualifying assets
  - 📋 financial assets
  - 📋 inventories that are manufactured, or otherwise produced, over a short period of time
  - 📋 assets that are ready for their intended use or sale when acquired
- Para 19 — Examples of necessary preparation activities
  - 📋 technical and administrative work prior to the commencement of physical construction
  - 📋 obtaining permits prior to the commencement of physical construction
- Para 24 — Examples of assets completed in parts
  - 📋 A business park comprising several buildings, each of which can be used individually
- Para 25 — Examples of assets needing full completion
  - 📋 An industrial plant involving several processes which are carried out in sequence at different parts of the same site

## Logical Flow Chains
- RAMD Spine
  - Recognition: Is it a qualifying asset? (Para 8)
    - Yes: leads to Initial Measurement (Para 10, 14)
      - leads to Period of Capitalisation Measurement (Para 17)
        - leads to Asset Presentation and Disclosure (Para 26)
    - No: expensed in Statement of Profit and Loss (Para 8)
- Capitalisation Period Chain
  - Commencement: All 3 conditions met? (Para 17)
    - Yes: Begin capitalisation
      - Is active development suspended? (Para 20)
        - Yes: Are delays necessary or administrative? (Para 21)
          - Yes: Continue capitalisation
          - No: Suspend capitalisation
        - No: Continue capitalisation
          - Is asset substantially complete? (Para 22)
            - Yes: Cease capitalisation

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 Definition of Borrowing Costs
  - Old AS: Interest calculated via straight-line or other methods
  - Ind AS: Interest calculated using Effective Interest Rate method
  - Impact: Changes interest amount charged in P&L or capitalised
  - ICAI exam relevance: High yield for numerical EIR adjustments
- 🔄 Exchange Differences
  - Old AS: Para 4(e) has specific formula-based limits
  - Ind AS: Para 6(e) relies on Ind AS 21 assessment
  - Impact: Affects forex loss classified as borrowing cost
  - ICAI exam relevance: Often tested as combined Ind AS 21/23 question
- 🔄 Qualifying Asset Inclusions
  - Old AS: Rigid 12-month criterion normally applies
  - Ind AS: Substantial period judgement; fair value assets excluded
  - Impact: Wider discretion for management judgement under Ind AS
  - ICAI exam relevance: Case study testing management judgement

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- 🌐 Transition Provisions
  - IFRS: Allows early adoption optional transition
  - Ind AS carve-out: Scopes out IAS 23 transitional provisions
  - Reason for carve-out: Ind AS 101 dictates transition for first-time adopters

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 Specific Borrowing Cost (Para 12)
  - Formula: Actual Interest Incurred minus Investment Income on temporary investments
  - Variables: Actual interest = EIR based; Investment income = actual yield
  - Threshold: Result must be directly attributable to asset
- 🧮 General Borrowing Capitalisation Rate (Para 14)
  - Formula: Total General Borrowing Costs divided by Total General Borrowings
  - Variables: Weighted average over the period
  - Threshold: Capitalised amount cannot exceed total general borrowing costs incurred
  - Example: Average rate applied to expenditure limits total capitalisation

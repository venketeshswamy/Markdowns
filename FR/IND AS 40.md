# Ind AS 40: Investment Property

## Objective and Scope
- **Objective**
  - 🎯 Prescribe accounting treatment for investment property (Para 1)
  - 🎯 Prescribe related disclosure requirements (Para 1)
- **Scope**
  - 🎯 Applied in recognition, measurement, and disclosure of IP (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ **Exclusions from Scope** (Para 4)
    - Biological assets related to agricultural activity (See Ind AS 41)
    - Mineral rights and reserves (oil, gas, non-regenerative resources)
- **Matters NOT covered** (Textbook 3.1)
  - Sale in ordinary course of business (See Ind AS 2)
  - Owner-occupied property (See Ind AS 16)
  - Property leased to another entity under Finance Lease (See Ind AS 116)

## Key Definitions
- 📌 **Investment Property (IP)** (Para 5)
  - Property (land or building or part thereof) held to earn rentals or capital appreciation or both
  - Held by owner or by lessee as a right-of-use asset
  - ⚠️ Rather than for:
    - Use in production/supply of goods/services or administrative purposes
    - Sale in the ordinary course of business
- 📌 **Owner-occupied Property (OOP)** (Para 5)
  - Property held for use in production/supply of goods/services or administrative purposes
  - Includes right-of-use assets held by lessee for such purposes
- 📌 **Cost** (Para 5)
  - Amount of cash/equivalents paid or fair value of consideration given to acquire asset
  - Includes amount attributed at initial recognition per other Ind AS
- 📌 **Fair Value** (Para 5)
  - Price received to sell asset in orderly transaction between market participants
- 📌 **Carrying Amount** (Para 5)
  - Amount at which asset is recognised in Balance Sheet

## Recognition Criteria
- 🎯 **General Principle** (Para 16)
  - Recognise as asset when and only when:
    - (a) Probable that future economic benefits associated will flow to entity
    - (b) Cost of the investment property can be measured reliably
- ⚠️ **Replacement Parts** (Para 19)
  - Recognise cost of replacing part in carrying amount if criteria met
  - Derecognise carrying amount of replaced parts
  - 💡 Illustration: Wall replacement (Textbook 3.4, Q1)
- ⚠️ **Day-to-day Servicing** (Para 18)
  - 🎯 Do NOT recognise in carrying amount
  - Recognise in P&L as incurred (Repairs and Maintenance)

## Measurement
- **Measurement at Recognition (Initial)**
  - 🎯 Basis: Measure initially at **Cost** (Para 20)
  - 📋 **Inclusions in Cost** (Para 21)
    - Purchase price
    - Directly attributable expenditures
    - Professional fees (legal services)
    - Property transfer taxes
    - Transaction costs
    - 💡 Club joining fee for office space (Textbook 3.10, Q11)
  - ⚠️ **Exclusions from Cost** (Para 23)
    - Start-up costs (unless necessary to bring to condition)
    - Operating losses before planned occupancy
    - Abnormal waste of material/labour/resources
  - ⚠️ **Deferred Payment** (Para 24)
    - Cost = Cash price equivalent
    - Difference = Interest expense over credit period
    - 💡 Illustration: Land on credit (Textbook 3.5, Q4)
  - ⚠️ **Exchange of Assets** (Para 27)
    - Measure at Fair Value unless:
      - Transaction lacks commercial substance
      - Fair value of neither asset is reliably measurable
    - If exception applies: Measure at carrying amount of asset given up
    - 💡 Illustration: Exchange of warehouse (Textbook 3.4, Q2)
- **Measurement after Recognition**
  - 🎯 **Ind AS 40 Mandatory Policy** (Textbook 3.3)
    - **Cost Model** only (Same as Ind AS 16)
    - ⚠️ **Crucial Difference:** IAS 40 allows Fair Value Model choice; Ind AS 40 does NOT
  - 🎯 **Requirements under Cost Model** (Para 56)
    - Measure in accordance with Ind AS 16 (Property, Plant and Equipment)
    - Asset held for sale: Measure per Ind AS 105
    - Lessee ROU Asset: Measure per Ind AS 116
  - 🧮 **Depreciation**
    - Must depreciate even if Fair Value > Carrying Amount (Textbook 3.9)
    - 💡 Illustration: Stars Ltd factory vs IP (Textbook 3.8, Q10)

## ICAI Listed Examples and Inclusions
- 📋 **Examples of Investment Property** (Para 8, Textbook 3.1)
  - Land held for long-term capital appreciation
  - Land held for currently undetermined future use
  - Building owned (or ROU asset) leased out under operating leases
  - Vacant building held to be leased out under operating leases
  - Property being constructed/developed for future use as IP
- 📋 **Examples of Items NOT IP** (Para 9, Textbook 3.1)
  - Property intended for sale in ordinary course (Inventory - Ind AS 2)
  - Owner-occupied property (Ind AS 16)
  - Property occupied by employees (whether or not they pay market rent)
  - Owner-occupied property awaiting disposal
  - Property leased to another entity under Finance Lease

## Key Exceptions and Conditions
- ⚠️ **Partial Own Use** (Para 10)
  - Property held partly for rentals/appreciation and partly for own use
  - **Test:** Can portions be sold or leased separately (finance lease)?
    - ✅ **Yes:** Account separately (IP vs OOP)
    - ❌ **No:** Classify as IP **only if** own-use portion is **insignificant**
  - 💡 **Illustrations (Textbook 3.2):**
    - Ex 1: 10 floors leased, 5 used, separate title possible -> Split accounting
    - Ex 2: 35% own use, cannot split -> Whole asset is OOP (35% significant)
- ⚠️ **Ancillary Services** (Para 11-13)
  - **Test:** Are services insignificant to arrangement as a whole?
    - ✅ **Insignificant:** Treat as IP (e.g., security/maintenance for office building)
    - ❌ **Significant:** Treat as OOP (e.g., owner-managed hotel)
  - 💡 **Illustrations (Textbook 3.2):**
    - Ex 3: Hotel with separate fitness centre (sold separately) -> Hotel is PPE, Centre is IP
    - Ex 4: Fitness centre not sold separately -> Whole property is PPE
- ⚠️ **Intragroup Leases** (Para 15)
  - Property leased to parent/subsidiary
  - **Consolidated FS:** Owner-occupied (eliminated as inter-company) -> Ind AS 16
  - **Separate FS of Lessor:** Investment Property -> Ind AS 40

## Logical Flow Chains
- **RAMD Spine (Ind AS 40)**
  - Recognition: Probable benefits AND Reliable cost (Para 16)
    - leads to Initial Measurement: Cost + Direct transaction costs (Para 20)
      - leads to Subsequent Measurement: Cost Model (Ind AS 16 rules) (Para 56)
        - leads to Disclosure: Depreciation methods + Fair Value (for info only) (Para 79)
- **Transfer Logic (Para 57)**
  - Trigger: Change in use evidenced by specific events
  - **Commencement of owner-occupation:** Transfer IP to PPE
  - **Commencement of development for sale:** Transfer IP to Inventory
  - **End of owner-occupation:** Transfer PPE to IP
  - **Inception of operating lease:** Transfer Inventory to IP
  - 💡 **Measurement on Transfer (Ind AS 40/Cost Model):**
    - Transfers between IP, PPE, and Inventory made at **Carrying Amount**
    - No profit/loss on transfer date (Para 59)

## Disclosure Requirements
- 🎯 **General Disclosures** (Para 75)
  - Accounting policy for measurement (Cost Model stated)
  - Criteria used to distinguish IP from OOP/Inventory when difficult
  - Extent of reliance on independent valuer
  - Amounts recognised in P&L for:
    - Rental income
    - Direct operating expenses (generating rental income)
    - Direct operating expenses (vacant property)
  - Restrictions on realisability or remittance of income
  - Contractual obligations to purchase/construct/repair
- 🎯 **Cost Model Specific Disclosures** (Para 79)
  - Depreciation methods used
  - Useful lives or depreciation rates
  - Gross carrying amount and accumulated depreciation (start & end)
  - **Reconciliation of Carrying Amount:**
    - Additions (acquisitions, subsequent expenditure)
    - Additions from business combinations
    - Assets classified as held for sale
    - Depreciation charge
    - Impairment losses recognised/reversed
    - Net exchange differences
    - Transfers to/from inventories or OOP
  - 🎯 **Fair Value Disclosure (Mandatory in Ind AS)**
    - Fair value of IP must be disclosed in notes
    - If FV not reliably measurable, disclose description and explanation why

## Illustrative Examples
- 💡 **Example 1 - Split Title (Textbook 3.2)**
  - Scenario: 15 floors (5 own, 10 leased), can sell title separately.
  - Conclusion: 10 floors = IP, 5 floors = PPE.
- 💡 **Example 2 - Significant Own Use (Textbook 3.2)**
  - Scenario: 35% own use, 65% leased, cannot sell separately.
  - Conclusion: Whole asset = PPE (35% is significant).
- 💡 **Example 3 - Hotel & Fitness Centre (Textbook 3.2)**
  - Scenario: Separate fitness building, can be sold/finance leased.
  - Conclusion: Hotel = PPE, Fitness Centre = IP.
- 💡 **Example 4 - Ancillary Services (Textbook 3.2)**
  - Scenario: Security/Maintenance provided to tenants.
  - Conclusion: Services insignificant -> IP.
- 💡 **Q5 - Change in Use (Textbook 3.5)**
  - Scenario: Factory (PPE) to Rental (IP). Cost model used.
  - Entry: Transfer at Carrying Amount (Cost - Accum Dep). No Gain/Loss.
- 💡 **Q6 - Disposal (Textbook 3.5)**
  - Case A (Sale): Bank Dr vs IP Cr -> Gain to P&L.
  - Case B (Finance Lease): Lease Rec Dr vs IP Cr -> Gain to P&L.
- 💡 **Q8 - Impairment on Transfer (Textbook 3.6)**
  - Scenario: IP transfer to PPE. Recoverable amt < Carrying amt.
  - Treatment: Transfer at Carrying Amt, then recognise Impairment Loss.
- 💡 **Q9 - Composite Property (Textbook 3.7)**
  - Scenario: 6 houses bought (1 own, 5 rented).
  - Treatment: Split cost 1/6 PPE, 5/6 IP. Repairs expensed.
- 💡 **Q10 - Factory vs Let-out (Textbook 3.8)**
  - Issue: Factory 1&2 (PPE) and Let-out Prop 3 (IP) all classified as PPE.
  - Correction: Prop 3 must be IP. All measured at Cost Model (Ind AS).
  - Note: Prop 3 Depreciation mandatory even if FV > Cost.

## AS vs Ind AS Differences
- 🔄 **Subsequent Measurement**
  - Old AS (AS 13): Carried at Cost less depreciation OR Fair Value (if revalued).
  - Ind AS 40: Cost Model is **Mandatory**. Fair Value Model not permitted (only for disclosure).
- 🔄 **Scope - Investment Property**
  - Old AS (AS 13): Covered generally under Investments.
  - Ind AS 40: Dedicated standard defining specific criteria for IP.

## Ind AS vs IFRS Differences
- 🌐 **Measurement Model**
  - IFRS (IAS 40): Choice between Cost Model and Fair Value Model.
  - Ind AS 40: **Cost Model only**. Fair Value Model removed (only for disclosure).
  - Reason: To prevent subjective fair valuation impacts on P&L in Indian context.

## Numerical Thresholds and Formulas
- 🧮 **Cost of Deferred Payment Asset** (Para 24)
  - `Cost = Cash Price Equivalent`
  - `Interest = Total Payments - Cash Price Equivalent`
- 🧮 **Disposal Gain/Loss** (Para 69)
  - `Gain/Loss = Net Disposal Proceeds - Carrying Amount`
- 🧮 **Impairment Testing**
  - Apply **Ind AS 36** if indicators of impairment exist.

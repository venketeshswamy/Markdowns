# Strategic Cost & Performance Management (SPOM SET B)

## Part A - Strategic Cost Management

### Chapter 1 - Introduction to Strategic Cost Management

#### Managing Cost Strategically
- Traditional Cost Management
  - *Limitations: Ignores competition, market growth, customer requirements*
  - *Focus: Only quantitative factors inside organization*
  - *Excessive focus on cost reduction ignores strategic importance*
  - *Reactive approach - corrective rather than preventive*
  - *Short-term outlook: year, quarter, or month*
- Strategic Cost Management (SCM)
  - *Definition: Implementation of cost management techniques to sustain and improve strategic position while reducing costs*
  - *Use of cost information in developing and deploying strategy*
  - *Applies to service, manufacturing, and not-for-profit settings*
  - *Integrates quantitative and qualitative information*

#### Three Pillars of SCM
- Value Chain Analysis
  - Primary Activities
    - *Inbound logistics: receiving, storing, handling raw materials*
    - *Operations: transformation of raw materials into finished goods*
    - *Outbound logistics: storing, distributing, delivering finished goods*
    - *Marketing and sales: 7Ps - Product, Price, Place, Promotion, People, Process, Physical evidence*
    - *After-sales service: installation, training, repair*
  - Support Activities
    - *Firm infrastructure: organization structure, legal, finance, accounting*
    - *Technology development: R&D, IT management, cybersecurity*
    - *Human resource management: hiring, training, culture*
    - *Procurement: finding vendors, negotiating prices*
  - *Margin: excess of customer willingness to pay over cost incurred*
  - Value Shop Model (Service Value Chain)
    - *Problem Finding and Acquisition*
    - *Problem Solving*
    - *Choice*
    - *Execution*
    - *Control and Evaluation*
    - *Cyclic nature: runs until solution reached*
  - Conducting Value Chain Analysis
    - *Identify value chain activities*
    - *Determine cost and value of activities*
    - *Assess competitive advantage opportunities*
    - *Internal cost analysis: understand cost sources*
    - *Internal differentiation analysis: enhance customer value*
    - *Vertical linkage analysis: extend across suppliers and users*
  - Strategic Framework Requirements
    - *Industry structure analysis: profitability and competition basis*
    - *Core competencies analysis: key success factors*
    - *Segmentation analysis: understand customers and markets*
- Strategic Positioning Analysis
  - Components
    - *Culture, beliefs, assumptions: Vision and Values*
    - *Stakeholders' influence and expectations: Mission and Objectives*
    - *Task environment capabilities: Strengths and Weaknesses*
    - *Macro environment: Opportunities and Threats*
  - Strategic Position
    - *Premium price (differentiation) OR lower cost (cost leadership)*
    - *"Stuck in the middle" = strategic mistake (Porter)*
  - Tools
    - *SWOT Analysis*
    - *PESTEL Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal)*
    - *STEEPLE (adds Ethical aspects)*
    - *Porter's Five Forces*
    - *Mendelow's Matrix for stakeholders*
      - High Interest High Power: actively engage
      - High Interest Low Power: keep informed
      - Low Interest High Power: keep satisfied
      - Low Interest Low Power: minimal effort
- Cost Driver Analysis
  - *Cost driver: unit of activity that causes costs to be incurred*
  - *More frequency/runs = more cost*
  - Structural Cost Drivers
    - *Scale: size of investment in manufacturing, R&D*
    - *Scope: degree of vertical integration*
    - *Experience: times firm has done before*
    - *Technology: technologies used in value chain*
    - *Complexity: breadth of product line*
  - Executional Cost Drivers
    - *Workforce involvement*
    - *Total quality management*
    - *Capacity utilization*
    - *Plant layout efficiency*
    - *Product configuration*
    - *Linkages with suppliers/customers*
  - *Process: Examine, quantify, explain monetary effects*

#### Total Productive Maintenance (TPM)
- Foundation and 8 Pillars
  - Foundation: 5S
  - Pillar 1: Autonomous Maintenance
    - *Operator responsibility*
    - *Cleaning, lubricating, inspection*
  - Pillar 2: Focused Improvement (Kaizen)
    - *Reduce losses*
    - *Small group activities*
  - Pillar 3: Planned Maintenance
    - *Preventive maintenance*
    - *Predictive maintenance*
    - *Corrective maintenance*
    - *Maintenance prevention*
  - Pillar 4: Early Management
    - *Shorter product development time*
    - *Design for maintainability*
  - Pillar 5: Quality Maintenance
    - *Defect-free production*
    - *Root cause analysis*
  - Pillar 6: Education and Training
    - *Skill development*
    - *Multi-skilling*
  - Pillar 7: Office TPM
    - *Administrative efficiency*
    - *Process automation*
  - Pillar 8: Safety, Health, Environment
    - *Zero accidents*
    - *Zero health damages*
- Performance Measurement: OEE
  - Overall Equipment Effectiveness
  - *OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality*
  - Availability Ratio
    - *= Operating Time / Planned Production Time*
    - *Ideal: > 90%*
    - *Accounts for: breakdowns, setups*
  - Performance Ratio
    - *= (Ideal Cycle Time × Total Count) / Operating Time*
    - *Ideal: > 95%*
    - *Accounts for: minor stops, reduced speed*
  - Quality Ratio
    - *= Good Count / Total Count*
    - *Ideal: > 99%*
    - *Accounts for: defects, rework*
  - *World Class OEE: ≈ 85%*
  - *Realistic target: > 50% (Kotze)*
- Six Big Losses
  - *Equipment failure/breakdown*
  - *Setup/adjustments*
  - *Idling and minor stoppages*
  - *Reduced speed*
  - *Reduced yield*
  - *Quality defects and rework*
- Connection TQM and TPM
  - *Both reduce costs*
  - *Both improve customer satisfaction*
  - *Both require worker involvement*
  - *Both need top management commitment*
  - *Both take long time for benefits*

- Cost Control
  - *Maintaining status quo*
  - *Standards-based*
  - *Variance analysis*
  - *Corrective action*
- Cost Reduction
  - *Permanent genuine saving*
  - Scope
    - *Product design*
    - *Process improvement*
    - *Material substitution*
    - *Labor efficiency*
  - Tools and Techniques
    - *Value analysis*
    - *Work study*
    - *Standardization*
    - *Variety reduction*
- Difference
  - *Control: prevent increase; Reduction: decrease level*
  - *Control: temporary; Reduction: permanent*

#### Theory of Constraints (TOC)
- Operational Measures
  - Throughput
    - *Sales revenue - totally variable costs*
    - *Rate of generating money*
  - Investment (Inventory)
    - *Money tied up in system*
  - Operating Expense
    - *Money spent converting inventory to throughput*
- Goldratt's Five-Step Method
  - *Identify the constraint*
  - *Exploit the constraint*
  - *Subordinate everything else*
  - *Elevate the constraint*
  - *Repeat: find next constraint*

#### Throughput Accounting
- *Extension of TOC*
- *Focus: maximize throughput per unit of constraint*
- Key Metrics
  - *Throughput per unit*
  - *Throughput per constraint unit*
  - *TA Ratio = Return per factory hour / Cost per factory hour*
- Decision Rule
  - *TA Ratio > 1: Accept*
  - *TA Ratio < 1: Reject*
- Advantages
  - *Simple calculations*
  - *Focus on constraint*
  - *Short-term decisions*
- Disadvantages
  - *Ignores fixed costs*
  - *Short-term focus*
  - *Not for long-term decisions*

  - Classification 3: Hansen and Mendoza
    - *Environmental prevention costs*
    - *Environmental detection costs*
    - *Environmental internal failure costs*
    - *Environmental external failure costs*
  - Classification 4: UN Division
    - *Operating costs*
    - *Upstream/downstream costs*
    - *Administrative costs*
    - *Research and development costs*
    - *Less tangible costs*
- EMA Methodology
  - Identification and Allocation
    - *Input/output analysis*
    - *Flow cost accounting*
    - *Activity-based costing*
  - Controlling Environmental Costs
    - *Prevention better than cure*
    - *Cost-benefit analysis*
    - *Continuous improvement*

#### Formulae

##### Sales Variances (Absorption)
- Sales Volume Profit Variance
  - *= (Actual Sales Qty - Budget Sales Qty) × Standard Profit per unit*
- Sales Volume Margin Variance
  - *= (Actual Sales Qty - Budget Sales Qty) × Standard Margin per unit*
- Sales Mix Profit Variance
  - *= (Actual Mix Qty - Budgeted Mix in Actual Total) × Standard Profit*
- Sales Quantity Profit Variance
  - *= (Actual Total Qty - Budgeted Total Qty) × Weighted Average Standard Profit*

##### Sales Variances (Marginal)
- Sales Volume Contribution Variance
  - *= (Actual Sales Qty - Budget Sales Qty) × Standard Contribution per unit*
- Sales Mix Contribution Variance
  - *= (Actual Mix - Standard Mix in Actual Total) × Standard Contribution*
- Sales Quantity Contribution Variance
  - *= (Actual Total - Budgeted Total) × Weighted Average Contribution*

##### Sales Variances (Turnover)
- Sales Price Variance
  - *= (Actual Price - Standard Price) × Actual Qty*
- Sales Volume Variance (Value)
  - *= (Actual Qty - Budgeted Qty) × Standard Price*

##### Direct Material Variances
- Material Price Variance
  - *= (Actual Price - Standard Price) × Actual Qty Purchased*
- Material Usage Variance
  - *= (Actual Qty Used - Standard Qty) × Standard Price*
- Material Mix Variance
  - *= (Actual Mix - Standard Mix in Actual Total) × Standard Price*
- Material Yield Variance
  - *= (Actual Total Input - Standard Total Input) × Standard Weighted Average Price*

##### Direct Labor Variances
- Labor Rate Variance
  - *= (Actual Rate - Standard Rate) × Actual Hours*
- Labor Efficiency Variance
  - *= (Actual Hours - Standard Hours) × Standard Rate*
- Labor Mix Variance
  - *= (Actual Mix - Standard Mix in Actual Total) × Standard Rate*
- Labor Yield Variance
  - *= (Actual Total Hours - Standard Total Hours) × Standard Weighted Average Rate*

##### Fixed Production Overhead Variances
- Total Fixed OH Variance
  - *= Absorbed OH - Actual OH*
- Expenditure Variance
  - *= Budgeted OH - Actual OH*
- Volume Variance
  - *= (Actual Production - Budgeted Production) × Standard OH Rate*
- Efficiency Variance
  - *= (Standard Hours - Actual Hours) × Standard OH Rate per Hour*
- Capacity Variance
  - *= (Actual Hours - Budgeted Hours) × Standard OH Rate per Hour*

# Inspection
## Sec 206: Call for Info & Inquiry (Registrar)
### ✅ Compliance
- **RoC Powers**:
  - Call for info/explanation
  - Call for production of documents
- **Company Duty**:
  - Furnish info within specified time
  - Past officers also liable to furnish info
- **Inquiry**:
  - If info inadequate/unsatisfactory state of affairs
  - 📝 Written Order by RoC informing allegations
  - Inquiry carried out after reasonable opportunity
- **CG Direction**:
  - Direct RoC/Inspector to inquire if circumstances warrant

### 📉 Scope
- **Scrutiny of Documents**: Filed with Registrar
- **Representation**: Business conducted for fraudulent/unlawful purpose
- **Grievances**: Investors' grievances not addressed

### 🚔 Liability (Non-Compliance)
- **Company & Officer**:
  - 💰 Fine: Up to ₹1 Lakh
  - 💰 Continuing: ₹500 / day

## Sec 207: Conduct of Inspection/Inquiry
### ✅ Compliance
- **Duty of Officers/Employees**:
  - Preserve & produce books/papers
  - Render assistance to RoC/Inspector
- **Powers of RoC/Inspector**:
  - Same as Civil Court (CPC, 1908)
  - Discovery/Production of books
  - Summoning & Enforcing attendance
  - Examining on Oath
  - Inspection of books at any place
  - Make copies / Place ID marks

### 🚔 Liability
- **Disobeying Direction**:
  - 💰 Fine: ₹25,000 - ₹1 Lakh
  - Imprisonment: Up to 1 year
- **Conviction Effect**:
  - Deemed vacation of office
  - Disqualification from holding office in any company

## Sec 208: Report on Inspection
### ✅ Compliance
- **Inspector/RoC**:
  - Submit report in writing to CG
  - Can recommend further investigation (with reasons)
    - Government can close or take further action as necessary
    - Report is submitted to Regional Director 
      - If less than 2 year Imprisonment 
        - He can himself issue order
      - Else 
        - Tell CG to take action

## Sec 209: Search & Seizure (Registrar/Inspector)
### ✅ Compliance
- **Condition**: Reasonable ground to believe books likely to be destroyed/mutilated/falsified/secreted
- **Process**:
  - Obtain Order from Special Court
  - Enter & Search premises
  - Seize books/papers
- **Return**:
  - ⏳ Return within 180 days
  - Can call again for further 180 days (Order in writing)
  - Can take copies/extracts before return
# Investigation
## Sec 210: Investigation into Affairs (CG)
### ✅ Compliance
- **CG Opinion based on** :(May order)
  - Report u/s 208 by Registrar
  - 🗳️ Special Resolution (SR) passed by company
  - Public Interest
- **Tribunal/Court Order**:
  - CG *shall* order investigation

### 📉 Scope (Security for Costs)
- **Applicant must pay security (Rule 5)**:
  - Turnover < ₹50 Cr: 💰 ₹10,000
  - Turnover ₹50 Cr - ₹200 Cr: 💰 ₹15,000
  - Turnover > ₹200 Cr: 💰 ₹25,000
- **Refund**: If investigation results in prosecution

## Sec 211: Establishment of SFIO
### ✅ Compliance
- **Setup**: By CG via Notification
- **Composition**:
  - Director (Rank: Joint secretary to GOI) 
  - Experts: Banking, Tax, Forensic Audit, Cyber Forensics, Law, etc.

## Sec 212: Investigation by SFIO
### ✅ Compliance
- **Triggers**:
  - Report u/s 208 
  - Spec Resolution
  - Public Interest 
  - Request by Dept of SG or CG
- **Effect of Assignment**:
  - Other agencies stop investigation
  - Transfer records to SFIO
- **Arrest Power**:
  - Director/Addl/Asst Director (Auth by CG)
  - Reason to believe guilty (Recorded in writing)
    - after arrest
      - , take to Judicial Magistrate - within 24 hours 
      - SFIO officers give report to SFIO
- **Reports**:
  - Interim: If directed by CG
  - Final: On completion
- Others 
  - Investigating officer has powers just like the inspector. 
  - 447 Offence - Non bailable

### 📉 Scope (Bail Restrictions)
- **Offences u/s 447 (Cognizable & Non-bailable)**
- **Bail Conditions**:
  - Public Prosecutor given opportunity to oppose
  - Court satisfied person is not guilty + won't commit crime
- **⚠️ Exceptions (Bail allowed)**:
  - Age < 16 years
  - Woman
  - Sick / Infirm

### 🚔 Liability (Disgorgement)
- **Fraud Reported**:
  - CG files application to Tribunal
  - Disgorgement of assets/cash
  - Personal liability of directors/officers

## Sec 213: Investigation in Other Cases (Tribunal)
### 📉 Scope (Who can apply?)
- **Company with Share Capital**:
  - 📉 ≥ 100 members OR
  - 📉 ≥ 1/10th Voting Power
- **No Share Capital**:
  - 📉 ≥ 1/5th members on register
- **Others**: Any person (if circumstances suggest fraud/misfeasance)

### ✅ Compliance
- **Grounds**:
  - Fraud/Unlawful purpose
  - Oppression
  - Misfeasance/Misconduct
  - Withholding info on affairs
- **Order**: 
  - Tribunal orders after OOBH -> CG appoints Inspector
## Sec 214: Security for payment of costs of investigation
- Refer Section 210 (Security for costs)
## Sec 215 : Only individuals can be inspectors
- No body corporates or firm or association can be appointed as inspector
## Sec 216: Investigation of Ownership
### ✅ Compliance
- **Purpose**: Determine true persons financially interested / controlling policy / beneficial owners
- **Appointment**:
  - CG *may* appoint (suo motu)
  - CG *shall* appoint (if Tribunal directs)
  - During investigation, NCLT can impose restriction on the shares up to 3 years

## Sec 217: Procedure & Powers (Inspector)
### ✅ Compliance
- **Custody of Books**:
  - Max ⏳ 180 days
  - Return + Call again for 180 days
- **Examination**:
  - Officers/Employees/Agents (Present & Past)
  - Other persons (Prior CG/SFIO Director approval)
  - Notes signed by person who was investigated -> Used as evidence (read the notes to him, ask him to sign it, then can be used as evidence)

### 🚔 Liability (Refusal to assist)
- **Failure to produce/furnish info/sign notes**:
  - Imprisonment: Up to 6 months
  - 💰 Fine: ₹25,000 - ₹1 Lakh
  - 💰 Continuing: ₹2,000 / day
### Others
  - Reciprocal arrangement with foreign govt can be Made 
  - If inspetor says evidence is outside india, 
    - court may issue request to competent authority of other country through Central Government
    - Report to be submitted within 30 days

## Sec 218: Protection of Employees
### ✅ Compliance
- **Protection**: Against dismissal/discharge/change in terms during investigation - protect employee who disclosed stuffs during investigation
- **Procedure if need to take action against employee**:
  - Company applies to Tribunal
  - Wait ⏳ 30 days for objection
  - No objection -> Proceed
- **Appeal**:
  - If Tribunal objects -> Appeal to Appellate Tribunal
  - Timeline: ⏳ 30 days
  - Decision: Final & Binding

## Sec 219: Related Companies
### 📉 Scope
- Inspector can investigate:
  - Subsidiary / Holding / Associate
  - Managed by same MD/Manager
  - Board accustomed to act on directions of MD/Director
- **Condition**: Prior CG Approval required

## Sec 220: Seizure by Inspector
### ✅ Compliance
- Similar to S. 209
- Reasonable ground: Destruction/falsification
- Keep till conclusion of investigation -> Return to company

## Sec 221: Freezing of Assets
### ✅ Compliance
- **Order by Tribunal**
- **Grounds**: Removal/Transfer/Disposal prejudicial to company/public interest/Creditor having more than 1 lakh outstanding from company
- **Period**: Max ⏳ 3 years

### 🚔 Liability (Contravention)
- **Company**: 💰 Fine ₹1 Lakh - ₹25 Lakh
- **Officer of co**:
  - Imprisonment: Up to 3 years OR
  - 💰 Fine: ₹50,000 - ₹5 Lakh OR Both

## Sec 222: Restrictions on Securities
### ✅ Compliance
- **Context**: Investigation u/s 216
- **Order by Tribunal**: Restrictions on transfer/issue
- **Period**: Max ⏳ 3 years

### 🚔 Liability (Contravention)
- **Company**: 💰 Fine ₹1 Lakh - ₹25 Lakh
- **Officer**:
  - Imprisonment: Up to 6 months OR
  - 💰 Fine: ₹25,000 - ₹5 Lakh OR Both

## Sec 223: Inspector's Report
### ✅ Compliance
- **Types**: Interim (if directed) & Final
- Report to be in writing-
- **Authentication**: Seal of Co OR Certificate of Public Officer
- **Access**: Members/Creditors can obtain copy by asking CG
- Not Applicable to SFIO Reports

## Sec 224: Action on Inspector's Report
- **224(1) Criminal Liability**
    - If guilty of offence -> **Prosecute**
    - Duty of officers to assist Govt
- **224(2) Winding Up / Relief**
    - If expedient -> File Petition to Tribunal
    - For **Winding Up** (Just & Equitable)
    - Or **Sec 241** (Oppression/Mismanagement)
- **224(3) Recovery Proceedings**
    - If in Public Interest
    - Govt sues **in Company's Name**
    - To recover:
        - Damages (Fraud/Misconduct)
        - Property (Misapplied/Retained)
- **224(5) Disgorgement**
    - If Undue Benefit/Advantage taken
    - **Orders:**
        - Disgorgement (Return asset/cash)
        - **Unlimited Personal Liability** (Director/KMP/Officer)
## Sec 225: Expenses of Investigation

### 1. Who Pays Initially?
- **Central Government**
    - (Defrays expenses first)

### 2. Reimbursement (Who pays back?)
- **A. Convicts / Offenders**
    - Those ordered to pay damages/restore property
    - *Extent*: As specified by **Court**
- **B. Company (The Beneficiary)**
    - If proceedings brought in its name
    - *Extent*: Up to value of **recovered** sums/property
- **C. If NO Prosecution instituted**
    - 1. Company, MD, or Manager (named in report)
    - 2. Applicants (who asked for investigation)
    - *Extent*: As directed by **Central Govt**

### 3. Priority of Payment
- **First Charge**
    - Govt expenses are deducted **first** from any recovered assets
## Sec 227: Legal Advisers & Bankers
### 📉 Scope (Non-Disclosure)
- **Legal Adviser**: Privileged communication exempt (Except client name/address)
- **Bankers**: Only customer information to be disclosed

## Sec 226: Investigation vs. Winding Up

### Core Rule: Investigation Continues
- Cannot be stopped/suspended due to:
    - 1. Sec 241 Application (Oppression)
    - 2. Voluntary Winding Up (Resolution passed)
    - 3. Pending Winding Up Petition

### If Winding Up Order Passed
- **Inspector** -> Informs Tribunal about investigation
- **Tribunal** -> Passes appropriate order

### Director/Employee Status
- **No Immunity**
    - Must participate in proceedings
    - Remains liable for findings
## Sec 227: Non-Disclosure Protections

### 1. Legal Advisers
- **Protected:** Privileged communication made in professional capacity.
- **NOT Protected (Exception):** Name and Address of the client.

### 2. Bankers
- **Protected:** Affairs of *other* customers (unrelated to the investigation).
- **NOT Protected:** Affairs of the specific Company/Person under investigation.
## Sec 228: Foreign Companies
- Provisions of the act equally applies to foreign companies
## Sec 229: Penalty for False Statement
### 🚔 Liability
- **Applicability**: Inspection/Inquiry/Investigation
- **Offence**:
  - Destroy/Mutilate/Falsify records
  - Make false entries
  - Furnish false explanation
- **Penalty**: 💰 Punishment for Fraud (u/s 447)

## NCLT & NCLAT (Sec 407 - 434)
### Constitution
*   **NCLT (Sec 408):** President + Judicial/Technical Members
*   **NCLAT (Sec 410):** Chairperson + Judicial/Technical Members
*   **Objective:** Single window for company disputes (Replaced CLB, BIFR)

### Sec 420: Orders of Tribunal
*   ✅ **Compliance**
    *   Give reasonable opportunity of being heard
    *   Pass orders
    *   Send copy to parties (all parties concerned)
*   📉 **Rectification**
    *   Mistake apparent from record
    *   Time: ⏳ Within 2 years of order
    *   ⚠️ **Exception:** No amendment if appeal preferred

### Sec 421: Appeal to NCLAT
*   ✅ **Procedure**
    *   Aggrieved person may appeal
    *   Filing Period: ⏳ 45 Days
    *   Condonation: ⏳ +45 Days (Sufficient cause)
*   ⚠️ **Exception**
    *   No appeal on consent orders
*   Copy of order to be sent to
    - NCLT
    - Parties of appeal

### Sec 422: Expeditious Disposal
*   ✅ **Timeline**
    *   Disposal target: ⏳ 3 Months (NCLT & NCLAT)
    *   Extension: ⏳ up to +90 Days
*   📝 **Condition**
    *   Must record reasons for delay
    *   *Case Law:* Zee Entertainment v. Invesco (Reasonable opportunity mandatory)

### Sec 423: Appeal to Supreme Court
*   📉 **Scope**
    *   Only on **Question of Law**
*   ✅ **Timeline**
    *   Filing: ⏳ 60 Days
    *   Condonation: (discretion of court)⏳ +60 Days

### Sec 424: Procedure
*   ✅ **Powers**
    *   Guided by **Principles of Natural Justice**
    *   NOT bound by CPC, 1908
    *   Power to regulate own procedure
    *   **Civil Court Powers:**
        *   Summoning/Enforcing attendance
        *   Discovery/Production of docs
        *   Evidence on affidavits
        *   Commission for witness exam
*   🚔 **Enforcement**
    *   Executed as a Decree just like a civil court
    *   Sent to local court where Reg. Office is / Person Resides
### Sec 425: Power to punish for Comtempt of Court
- Instead of HC, NCLT/NCLAT can do it as per Contempt of court act
- Contempt of court
    - merely not following the terms agreed upon between two parties (Consent Terms) is not a ground for initiating contempt proceedings. 
    - Contempt is reserved for willful disobedience of a direct order or undertaking given to the Court.

### Sec 426: Delegation of powers
- Who Can? - NCLT/NCLAT
- To Whom: Any Officer, Employee or Any Person
- They can inquire into matter relating to proceeding or appeal and report back to tribunal
- An order should be passed for the same
### Sec 428: Protection of Action taken in good faith
- Who is protected? - NCLT/NCLAT Officers, Employees and members
- The Act should be done in good faith
### Sec 430: Civil Court Bar
*   📉 **Jurisdiction**
    *   No Civil Court jurisdiction on NCLT empowered matters
    *   No Injunctions allowed
### Sec 431: Vacancy in NCLT/NCLAT not to invalidate its acts
### Sec 432: Right to representation
- A party of appeal can authorise CA/CS/ Practitioners
### Sec 433: Limitation Act will be applicable to these tribunals
### Sec 434: Transfer of Proceedings to NCLT
*   ✅ **Transition**
    *   Pending CLB matters -> Transferred to NCLT
    *   Winding up (HC) -> Transferred to NCLT (except if orders reserved)
    *   *Case Law:* Union Bank v. Nilkamal (Obligatory to transfer pending winding up to NCLT)

## Special Courts (Sec 435 - 438)
### Sec 435: Establishment
*   ✅ **Objective**
    *   Speedy trial of offences
*   📉 **Composition**
    - Composition
        *   **Judge (Session/Addl Session):** Offences punishable with imprisonment ⏳ ≥ 2 Years
        *   **Metropolitan/Judicial Magistrate (1st Class):** Other offences
    - Appointed by
        - CG with concorrence of Chief justice of that jurisdiction     

### Sec 436: Offences Triable
*   📉 **Jurisdiction**
    *   Court where Reg. Office is situated
*   ✅ **Procedure**
    *   Person forwarded to Magistrate
    *   Detention: ⏳ 15 Days (Judicial Mag) / ⏳ 7 Days (Exec Mag)
    *   **Summary Trial:**
        *   Offence punishable with Imprisonment ⏳ ≤ 3 Years
        *   Conviction in summary trial: Max Imprisonment ⏳ 1 Year
        *   If > 1 Year needed -> Switch to Regular Trial
### Sec 437: Appealing of Special Court Order -> To High Court
### Sec 438: CrPC shall apply to the Special Court and has powers just like the normal court
### Sec 439: Non-Cognizable Offences (Cognizabl means arrest without warrant)
*   📉 **General Rule**
    *   All Act offences are **Non-Cognizable**
    *   ⚠️ **Exception:** Sec 212(6) (Fraud/SFIO) = Cognizable
*   ✅ **Cognizance**
    *   Only on written complaint by:
        *   Registrar (ROC)
        *   Shareholder/Member
        *   CG Authorized person
## Compounding (Sec 441)
### 📉 Scope & Authority
*   **Compoundable:** NOT punishable with Imprisonment only OR Imprisonment + Fine
*   **Authority:**
    *   **Regional Director (RD):** Fine 💰 ≤ 25 Lakhs
    *   **NCLT:** Other cases
*   **Exclusions (Cannot Compound):**
    *   Investigation initiated/pending
    *   Similar offence compounded within ⏳ 3 Years
*   Prosecution - started - can i coumpound?
    - Yes - Provided that in writing it is to be given by registrar

### ✅ Procedure
*   Apply to ROC -> Foward to NCLT/RD
*   Order passed
*   Intimate ROC within ⏳ 7 Days
*   Discharge of offender (if prosecution pending)

### 🚔 Liability (Contravention)
*   Officer/Employee failing to comply with order
*   Penalty: 💰 Max 2x the fine provided for the offence

## Mediation & Conciliation (Sec 442)
### ✅ Panel
*   Maintained by Central Govt (Experts)
*   Referral: By NCLT/NCLAT/CG (Suo moto or Application)
*   Disposal of the matter: ⏳ 3 Months
*   Objection: Aggrieved party can object to recommendations

## Adjudication of Penalties (Sec 454)

### 1. The Adjudicator
- **Who**: Officer appointed by Central Govt
- **Rank**: Not below Registrar (ROC)
- **Jurisdiction**: Specified in appointment order

### 2. The Process (Rule 3)
- **Step 1: Notice**
    - To Company, Officer in Default, or Person
    - Indicate nature of default + Max penalty
    - **Time to Reply**: 15 - 30 Days
    - **Extension**: Up to 15 Days (if sufficient cause shown)
- **Step 2: Reply**
    - Mode: Electronic only
    - **Oral Hearing**: Must be specifically requested in reply
- **Step 3: Hearing**
    - Only if AO thinks necessary OR Party requested
    - Notice for hearing: Within 10 working days of reply
    - Powers of AO:
        - Summon persons
        - Order production of documents
- **Step 4: The Order**
    - Must be dated and signed
    - **Timeline to Pass**:
        - No Hearing: Within 30 Days (of reply expiry)
        - Hearing Held: Within 90 Days (of first notice)
    - **Delayed Order**: Valid, but reasons must be recorded
    - **Copy Sent To**: Parties + CG + Uploaded to Website

### 3. Quantum of Penalty
- **Mandatory Minimum**: Cannot be less than Act's minimum
- **Fixed Penalty**: If Act says fixed sum, AO must impose that
- **Factors to Consider**:
    - Size of Company
    - Nature of Business
    - Injury to Public Interest
    - Nature & Repetition of default
    - Disproportionate Gain (if quantifiable)
    - Loss to Investors/Creditors

### 4. The "Amnesty" Rule (Rectification)
- **Applicable Sections**:
    - Sec 92 (Annual Return)
    - Sec 137 (Financial Statements)
- **Condition**:
    - Default rectified BEFORE notice
    - OR within 30 days of notice
- **Result**: NO Penalty imposed; proceedings concluded

### 5. Appeal Mechanism
- **Authority**: Regional Director (RD)
- **Timeline**: Within 60 Days of receipt of order
- **Form**: Form ADJ (as per Rules)

### 6. Post-Order Compliance
- **Payment Mode**: MCA Portal Only
- **Destination**: Consolidated Fund of India
- **Consequences of Non-Compliance** (after 90 days):
    - **Company**: Fine ₹25k - ₹5 Lakhs
    - **Officer/Person**:
        - Imprisonment up to 6 Months
        - OR Fine ₹25k - ₹1 Lakh
        - OR Both

## Miscellaneous Penalties & Provisions
### Sec 446A: Punishment Factors
*   Size of company
*   Nature of business
*   Public interest injury
*   Nature/Repetition of default

### Sec 446B: Lesser Penalties
*   📉 **Applicability**
    *   OPC
    *   Small Company
    *   Start-up
    *   Producer Company
*   💰 **Liability**
    *   50% of specified penalty
    *   **Max Cap:** 💰 ₹2 Lakh (Company) / 💰 ₹1 Lakh (Officer)

### Sec 454A: Repeated Default
*   📉 **Scope**
    *   Second/Subsequent default within ⏳ 3 Years
*   🚔 **Liability**
    *   Amount: 💰 2x the Penalty provided in Act
### Sec 443: CG can appoint company prosecutors (Just like Public Prosecutor)
### Sec 444 : Govt can appeal against acquitting (Not guilty verdict) of a person
### Sec 445 : Compensation as per CRPC if complaint without reasonable cause
### Sec 446 : Fine money can be used to pay legal costs, rest goes to govt

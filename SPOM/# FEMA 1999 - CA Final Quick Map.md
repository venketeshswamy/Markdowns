# FEMA 1999 - CA Final Quick Map
## 🎯 Core Objective
- Facilitate external trade & payments
- Promote orderly FX market development
- Effective: **June 1, 2000**

## 👤 Residential Status `Sec 2(v)`
### Individual Resident in India (PRII)
- **>182 days** in preceding FY
- ⚠️ **Exceptions (becomes PROI)**:
  - Left India for employment/business/uncertain period abroad
  - Came to India NOT for employment/business/uncertain period
- 📅 Status changes from specific date, not yearly

### Entity Resident in India
- Company/LLP registered in India
- Office/branch in India owned by PROI
- Office/branch outside India owned by PRII

### Person Resident Outside India (PROI) `Sec 2(w)`
- Anyone NOT meeting PRII criteria

## 🚫 Core Restrictions `Sec 3-4`

### Sec 3: Prohibited Dealings (unless permitted)
- Deal FX with non-AP (Authorised person)
- Pay/receive for PROI credit except via AP
- Financial transactions for acquiring assets outside India

### Sec 4: Holding Foreign Assets
- PRII cannot hold FX/foreign security/immovable property abroad
- ⚠️ **Exceptions** `Sec 6(4)`:
  - Acquired when resident outside India
  - Inherited from PROI
  - Income from such assets

## 💳 Current Account Transactions `Sec 5`

### Definition `Sec 2(j)`
- All transactions EXCEPT capital account
- Trade payments, interest, remittances, travel, education, medical

### General Rule
- ✅ Freely permitted unless specifically restricted
- Via Authorized Person only

### CAT Rules 2000 - 3 Schedules

#### Schedule I: 🚫 PROHIBITED
- Lottery winnings remittance
- Racing/hobby income
- Banned magazines, sweepstakes
- Dividend with balancing requirement
- Call-back telephone services

#### Schedule II: 🏛️ Govt Approval Required
- Cultural tours → **Min. HRD**
- Ad in foreign media >**USD 10,000** → **Min. Finance**
- Vessel freight (PSU) → **Min. Surface Transport**
- Transponder hiring → **Min. I&B / IT**
- Sports sponsorship >**USD 100,000** → **Min. HRD Sports**

#### Schedule III: 🏦 RBI Approval if Exceeds Limits

##### Individuals: 💰 **USD 250,000/FY** (LRS)
- Private visits, gifts, employment, emigration
- Business travel, medical treatment, studies
- ⚠️ **No RBI approval needed** if:
  - Medical: based on hospital estimate
  - Studies: based on university estimate
  - Emigration: as per country requirement
- 📝 If LRS used in FY, deduct from **USD 250,000**

##### Non-Individuals: Higher Limits
- Donations: **1% of FX earnings (3 FYs)** or **USD 5M** (lower)
- Agent commission (property sale): **USD 25,000** or **5% inward** (higher)
- Consultancy (infra): **USD 10M/project**
- Consultancy (other): **USD 1M/project**
- Pre-incorporation expenses: **5% investment** or **USD 100,000** (higher)

##### ✅ Exemptions from Schedule Limits
- Payment from RFC/EEFC Account (most cases)
- International Credit Card use abroad

### Import of Goods & Services
#### Master Direction - Import
- Regulated by **DGFT** (Foreign Trade Policy)
- Payment via banking channels only
- 📅 Settlement: **within 6 months** from shipment
- 📝 Upload in **IDPMS** (Import Data Processing System)
- Extension: AD can grant **up to 6 months at a time** (max **3 years**)
  - If >**USD 1M** or **>10% avg imports (2 FYs)**: specific conditions

#### Guarantees for Imports (by AD Banks)
- Service importer (non-PSU): **≤USD 500,000**
- PSU/Govt dept: **>USD 100,000** needs **Min. Finance** approval

#### Other stuffs
- You cannot hold more than 25K of INR outside India 
- But in Nepal and Bhutan of 100 rs denomination or lower- unlimited money can be held
- Foreign money in hand cannot be more than 2K USD (while in india) - if you have, give them back (or use RFC)
## 💼 Capital Account Transactions `Sec 6`

### Definition `Sec 2(e)`
- Alters assets/liabilities (incl. contingent) outside India of PRII
- Or assets/liabilities in India of PROI

### General Rule
- 🚫 Prohibited unless specifically permitted
- Powers: **RBI** (debt instruments), **Central Govt** (non-debt) `Sec 6(2), 6(2A)`

### Permissible CAT Regulations 2000

#### Schedule I: PRII Permitted
- Investment in foreign securities (ODI/OPI)
- Raising ECB/FCY loans
- Transfer immovable property abroad
- Issue guarantees to PROI
- Import/export/hold currency notes
- Borrowings from PROI
- RFC/EEFC accounts
- Insurance from foreign companies
- Loans to PROI
- Derivative contracts

#### Schedule II: PROI Permitted (in India)
- Issue/investment in securities of Indian entity
- Contribution to capital of firm/proprietorship
- Acquire/transfer immovable property in India
- Guarantee for PRII
- Deposits between PRII & PROI
- Foreign currency accounts in India
- Remit capital assets outside India

### 🚫 Prohibited CAT
#### PROI Cannot Invest in India:
- Chit funds (except NRI subscription via RBI)
- Nidhi companies
- Agricultural/plantation activities
- Real estate business/farm houses
  - ⚠️ **Exception**: Townships, residential/commercial premises, roads, bridges, REITs
- Trading in TDRs (Transferable Development Rights)

#### PRII Cannot Make ODI in:
- Real estate activity (except townships, construction for sale/lease)
- Gambling
- Financial products linked to INR (without RBI approval)

#### Special Prohibitions:
- 🚫 **No investment** in/from **North Korea** (without Central Govt approval)
- 🚫 **LRS remittance** to **FATF non-cooperative countries**

### Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)
- 💰 **USD 250,000/FY** per resident individual
- For any permissible current or capital account transaction
- Includes minors (guardian countersigns)
- 🚫 NOT available to: corporates, firms, HUF, trusts
- Family consolidation allowed (except capital account co-ownership)

## 💵 External Commercial Borrowings (ECB)

### ECB Framework `Sec 6(3)(d)` + Master Direction
- Commercial loans by PRII from recognized non-resident entities
- **Two tracks**: FCY-denominated, INR-denominated

### Comparison Table

| Parameter | FCY ECB | INR ECB |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Currency** | Any freely convertible FX | Indian Rupee |
| **Forms** | Loans, bonds/debentures (not FCCB), FCCBs, FCEBs, trade credits >**3 yrs**, financial lease | Loans, bonds/debentures (not FCCB), pref shares (not FCCB), trade credits >**3 yrs**, financial lease, plain vanilla INR bonds overseas |
| **Eligible Borrowers** | All FDI-eligible entities, Port Trusts, SEZ units, SIDBI, EXIM Bank | FCY borrowers + Microfinance entities (registered not-for-profit, societies, trusts, coops, NGOs) |
| **Recognized Lenders** | Resident of **FATF/IOSCO compliant** country; <br> Multilateral/Regional FIs (India member); <br> Individuals (only if foreign equity holders or for listed bonds/debentures); <br> Foreign branches/subsidiaries of Indian banks (only FCY ECB, not FCCBs/FCEBs) | Same as FCY |
| **MAMP <br> (Min Avg Maturity Pd)** | **3 years** (general); **1 year** (manufacturing, up to **USD 50M/FY**); <br> **5 years** (from foreign equity holder for WC/GCP/Rupee loan repayment); <br> **10 years** (from non-equity holder for WC/GCP, on-lending by NBFC for WC/GCP); <br> **7 years** (for capex Rupee loan repayment, on-lending by NBFC) | Same as FCY |
| **All-in-Cost** | Benchmark + **500 bps** | Benchmark + **450 bps** |
| **End-uses (Negative List)** | Real estate , <br> capital market investment , <br> equity investment ,<br>  WC (except specific cases),<br> GCP (except specific cases), <br> Rupee loan repayment (except specific cases), <br> on-lending (except NBFC specific cases) <br> | Same as FCY |
| **Hedging** | 📝 Infrastructure cos: **70% mandatory** if MAMP <**5 yrs** (principal + coupon); <br> Natural hedge: only if offsetting cash flows within same FY | Overseas investors: hedge via AD-I banks or foreign branches of Indian banks (back-to-back) |

### Key ECB Limits & Conditions
- 💰 **Automatic Route**: Up to **USD 750M/FY**
- **Leverage**: ECB liability-equity ratio ≤**7:1** (if outstanding ECB >**USD 5M**)
- 📝 **Parking**: 
  - Abroad: in deposits/CDs/treasury bills (rated ≥**AA-**), or foreign branches of Indian banks (for FX expenditure)
  - India: Term deposits with AD-I banks (max **12 months cumulative**, unencumbered)
- **Prepayment**: Charge/penal interest ≤**2%** over contracted rate (outside all-in-cost)
- **Refinancing**: Allowed if weighted maturity not reduced & all-in-cost lowered; Indian banks can refinance only **AAA corporates** or **Maharatna/Navratna** PSUs

### ECB Procedure
- **Automatic Route**: Apply to AD-I bank with *Form ECB*
- **Approval Route**: Via AD-I bank to RBI (above thresholds → Empowered Committee)
- 📝 **LRN (Loan Registration Number)**: Obtain before any drawdown (submit *Form ECB* in duplicate via AD-I)
- 📝 **Monthly Reporting**: *Form ECB 2* within **7 working days** of month-end
- **Changes in T&Cs**: Report to DSIM via revised *Form ECB* within **7 days**
- **LSF (Late Submission Fee)**: For delay in LRN or ECB 2 filing

### Special ECB Windows
- **PSU OMCs**: **USD 10B** aggregate for WC, MAMP **3 yrs**, no hedging/individual limit
- **Startups** (recognized by Central Govt):
  - 💰 **USD 3M/FY**
  - MAMP **3 years**
  - Forms: Loans, non-/optionally/partially convertible pref shares
  - Currency: FCY or INR (or both)
  - All-in-cost: mutually agreed
  - End-use: any business expenditure
  - From own funds (entity) or individual funds (resident individual)
  - 🚫 No leverage ratio, but no guarantee by Indian banks/FIs/NBFCs

### ECB Conversions
- **Into Equity**: 
  - Automatic if FDI policy compliant, sector cap OK, pricing OK, partial conversion reports in *Form FC-GPR* + *Form ECB 2*
  - Exchange rate: on agreement date or lower (with lender consent)
- **Currency Change**: FCY to FCY or FCY to INR freely; INR to FCY 🚫 not allowed

### ECB Security `AD-I Bank Powers`
- **Immovable**: Charge on property (sale proceeds repatriated if enforced)
- **Movable**: Charge on assets (NOC from domestic lenders; can be taken abroad)
- **Financial Securities**: Pledge of shares (promoter's, borrower's, domestic associates), bonds, debentures, GSecs, deposits; also loan assets, current assets, cash, Rupee accounts (incl. escrow/DSRA); ⚠️ Transfer on invocation per FDI/FII norms
- **Guarantees**: Corporate (Indian entity, group company ≥**51% stake**), personal (promoter individual), bank (backed by counter-guarantee/collateral)
  - Performance guarantee: **50%** counted toward financial commitment
  - Joint & several: **100%** for each entity

### ECB Under Investigation
- Entity under FEMA investigation/adjudication/appeal: can raise ECB if otherwise eligible
- Inform AD-I bank/RBI; approval letter copy to agencies


## 🏢 Overseas Direct Investment (ODI)

### FEM (Overseas Investment) Rules 2022 `Notif. 22-Aug-2022`
- Replaced ODI Regulations 2004 & Immovable Property Regulations 2015
- **OI = ODI + OPI + Financial Commitment**

### Key Definitions
- **ODI**: Unlisted equity, or ≥**10%** listed equity, or <**10%** with control
- **OPI**: Investment other than ODI (not unlisted debt, not security by PRII in India)
- **Control**: Right to appoint majority directors, or ≥**10% voting** rights
- **Financial Commitment**: ODI + debt + guarantees + pledges
- **Foreign Entity**: Limited liability entity abroad (⚠️ except strategic sectors)

### ODI by Indian Entity `Schedule I`

#### Eligibility
- Any Indian entity for bona fide business
- ⚠️ **Financial Services ODI**:
  - If Indian entity in financial services: must be registered with regulator + **net profit (3 FYs)**
  - If NOT in financial services (except banking/insurance): **net profit (3 FYs)**
  - COVID relief: Exclude FY 2020-21, 2021-22 from 3-year count

#### 💰 Limits
- **Financial Commitment**: ≤**400% of net worth** (last audited balance sheet)
  - Financial Commitment -PG and CG
    - PG - 50% of PG is counted
    - CG - 100% counted 
    - If jointly held, both companies have 100% . No splitting.
- ⚠️ **Exception**: Maharatna/Navratna/Miniratna PSUs in strategic sectors: **no limit**

#### Modes of ODI
- Subscription/purchase of equity (listed/unlisted)
- Rights issue, bonus shares
- Capitalization of dues (within realization period)
- Swap of securities
- Merger/demerger/amalgamation

#### Pricing
- Arm's length basis (internationally accepted valuation)

### OPI by Indian Entity `Schedule II`
- **Listed Indian company**: freely (including reinvestment)
- **Unlisted Indian entity**: only via rights, bonus, swap, merger, capitalization
- 💰 Limit: ≤**50% of net worth**

### OI by Resident Individual `Schedule III`
- **ODI**: In operating foreign entity (not financial services, no subsidiary) where individual has **control**
- **OPI**: Freely (including reinvestment)
- 💰 Within **LRS USD 250,000/FY**
- ⚠️ **No LRS limit** for:
  - Gift from relative in India holding foreign securities
  - Inheritance from PRII/PROI
  - ESOP/EBS from employer's overseas parent
  - Sweat equity, minimum qualification shares

### OI by Other Entities `Schedule IV`
- Trusts/Societies (education/hospital): RBI prior approval
- Mutual Funds/VCF/AIF: as per **SEBI** limits
- Clearing corporations: for foreign collateral
- Domestic depositories: for IDR underlying
- AD banks: in normal banking business

### OI in IFSC `Schedule V`
- Same rules as overseas
- ⚠️ **Relaxations**:
  - Non-financial services entity: ODI in IFSC financial services OK (except banking/insurance)
  - Regulator approval deemed in **45 days** if no response
  - Resident individual: ODI in financial services (except banking/insurance) if no step-down outside IFSC

### Overseas Immovable Property
#### PRII Can Acquire:
- ✅ Inheritance/gift from PRII with valid FX holding
- ✅ Inheritance from PROI
- ✅ Purchase from PROI via RFC/LRS/jointly with relative PROI
- ✅ Out of income/sale proceeds of overseas assets (other than ODI)
- ✅ Indian entity: for business/staff residence (as per RBI directions)

#### PRII Can Transfer:
- ✅ Gift to eligible PRII or sale
- ✅ Charge as per FEMA

### NOC Requirement `Rule 8`
- If PRII is **NPA/wilful defaulter/under investigation** (CBI/ED/SFIO/regulator):
  - Obtain **NOC** from lender/regulator/agency before ODI/disinvestment
  - If no response in **60 days**: deemed NOC

### Restructuring `Rule 13`
- If foreign entity has losses (**2 consecutive years**):
  - Diminution ≤ proportionate accumulated losses
  - If original investment >**USD 10M** or diminution >**20%** dues: need **registered valuer certificate** (within **6 months** of transaction)

### Reporting (FEM OI Regulations) `Reg 10`
- **APR (Annual Performance Report)**: By **31st December** each year
  - If FY ends 31st Dec: by 31st Dec of next year
  - ⚠️ **No APR** if: <**10%** equity without control + no other financial commitment, OR foreign entity under liquidation
- **Financial Commitment/Disinvestment**: Within **30 days** of remittance/receipt
- **OPI (non-individual)**: Within **60 days** of half-year end (Sept/Mar)
- **ESOP**: Reported by Indian employer
- **Annual Return on Foreign Liabilities & Assets**: To DSIM, RBI (as per RBI timelines)

### Late Submission Fee `Reg 11`
- If evidence of investment not submitted within **6 months** (Reg 9(1))
- Or reporting not done in time (Reg 10)
- Can regularize within **max 3 years** from due date with LSF

### Restrictions on Further Transactions `Reg 12`
- 🚫 No further financial commitment/transfer till reporting regularized


## 📤 Export of Goods & Services `Sec 7`

### Obligations of Exporter
- 📝 **Declaration**: Furnish to specified authority (Customs/SOFTEX official) with **true export value**
- If value not ascertainable: expected value per market conditions
- 📝 **Forms**: *EDF* (goods via customs), *SOFTEX* (software via STPI/FTZ/SEZ)
- Submit export docs to AD within **21 days** of export/SOFTEX certification

### Exemptions from Declaration `Reg 4`
- Trade samples, publicity material (free)
- Personal effects, ship's stores, trans-shipment
- Gifts ≤**₹5 lacs**
- Military supplies (Govt orders)
- Aircraft/engines for repair abroad (re-import within **6 months**)
- Re-export of leased aircraft (de-registered by DGCA)
- Imported goods (defective, loan-based, free of cost surplus) for re-export
- Replacement goods (per FTP)
- Goods for testing (re-import)
- Defective goods for repair (no FX transaction)

### Realization & Repatriation `Sec 8 + Reg 9`
- 📅 **9 months** from export date (or SOFTEX date for software)
- For **SEZ/EOU/EHTP/STP/BTP/Status Holder**: also **9 months**
- **Warehouse exports**: **15 months** from shipment
- ⚠️ **Extension**: RBI or AD can grant for sufficient cause
- Payment via **Authorized Person** in specified manner
- Re-import of goods = deemed realization

### Advance Payment for Exports `Reg 15`
- 📅 Shipment within **1 year** from advance receipt
- Interest (if any): ≤**LIBOR/benchmark + 100 bps**
- Docs via same AD through whom advance received
- ⚠️ If can't ship: refund within 1 year (or RBI approval for extension)
- **Exception**: If export agreement itself provides shipment >**1 year**

### Project Exports `Reg 17`
- Deferred payment/turnkey/civil construction: **prior approval** from **EXIM Bank or AD**
- Pre-award guarantee: AD bank/PRII exporting company can issue (if contract stipulates)

## 🔐 Authorized Persons `Sec 10-12`

### Appointment `Sec 10(1)`
- By **RBI** (in writing) on application
- Types: Authorized Dealer, Money Changer, Off-shore Banking Unit, Other

### Duties `Sec 10(5)`
- Before transaction: obtain **declaration & information** from person
- Ensure no contravention/evasion of FEMA
- If suspicious: refuse in writing & report to RBI

### Powers of RBI
- **Directions** `Sec 11`: On payments, acts relating to FX/foreign security, furnishing info
- **Revocation** `Sec 10(3)`: If public interest, or AP failed conditions, or contravened FEMA (after opportunity)
- **Inspection** `Sec 12`: RBI officer can inspect AP's business for verification/obtaining info/securing compliance

### Penalty on AP `Sec 11`
- Contravention of RBI direction or failure to file return:
  - Up to **₹10,000**
  - Continuing: additional **₹2,000/day**
- After reasonable opportunity of being heard

## ⚖️ Contraventions & Penalties

### Contravention `Sec 13(1)`
- Of any FEMA provision, rule, regulation, notification, direction, order, or authorization condition

### Penalty `Sec 13(1)`
- 💰 Up to **3 times the sum** involved (if quantifiable)
- Or up to **₹2 lacs** (if not quantifiable)
- **Continuing**: further **₹5,000/day** after first day

### Enhanced Penalty `Sec 13(1A)`
- If foreign exchange/security/immovable property aggregate value >**threshold under Sec 37A proviso**:
  - Up to **3 times the sum** involved
  - **Confiscation** of equivalent value of foreign assets in India

### Imprisonment `Sec 13(1C)`
- For contravention under **Sec 13(1A)**:
  - Up to **5 years** + fine
  - In addition to penalty under 13(1A)

### Confiscation `Sec 13(2)`
- Adjudicating Authority may direct:
  - Confiscate currency/security/money/property to Central Govt
  - Bring back FX holdings to India or retain abroad per directions

### Civil Imprisonment `Sec 14`
- If penalty not paid within **90 days** of notice:
  - Where demand >**₹1 crore**: up to **3 years**
  - Other cases: up to **6 months**
- After notice to show cause & satisfaction by AA that defaulter:
  - Transferred/concealed property dishonestly to obstruct recovery, OR
  - Has/had means to pay but refuses/neglects
- HUF: Karta deemed defaulter
- Released on payment of arrears

### Recovery of Arrears `Sec 14A`
- By Officer of Enforcement (≥Asst Director)
- Powers of Income-tax authority (Second Schedule of IT Act)

### Compounding `Sec 15`
- Application to **Director of Enforcement** or authorized officers (ED/RBI)
- Within **180 days** of application receipt
- Once compounded: no further proceeding under Sec 13

## 🏛️ Adjudication & Appeal

### Adjudicating Authority `Sec 16`
- Appointed by **Central Government** (officers of Central Govt)
- Complaint by authorized officer
- 📅 Endeavour to dispose within **1 year** of complaint receipt
- If delay: record reasons periodically

### Appeal to Special Director (Appeals) `Sec 17`
- Against orders of AA (Asst/Deputy Director of Enforcement)
- 📅 Within **45 days** of order receipt (can be extended for sufficient cause)

### Appeal to Appellate Tribunal `Sec 18-19`
- Same as SAFEMA Tribunal
- Against orders of other AAs or Special Director (Appeals)
- By Central Govt or aggrieved person
- 📅 Within **45 days** of order receipt (can be extended)
- 📅 Try to dispose within **180 days** of appeal receipt (if not, record reasons)

### Appeal to High Court `Sec 35`
- On question of law
- 📅 Within **60 days** of AT order communication (can be extended up to **further 60 days** for sufficient cause)
- HC where aggrieved party resides/works, or if Central Govt is appellant, where respondent resides

## 🕵️ Directorate of Enforcement `Sec 36-38`

### Establishment `Sec 36`
- By **Central Government**
- Director + other officers (Officers of Enforcement)
- Powers exercised subject to Central Govt conditions/limitations

### Powers `Sec 37`
- **Search & Seizure**: Director & officers ≥**Asst Director**
- Investigate contraventions under **Sec 13**
- Like powers of Income-tax authorities `Sec 38(2)`

### Empowering Other Officers `Sec 38(1)`
- Central Govt can authorize: Customs, Central Excise, Police, other Central/State Govt officers
- Subject to conditions/limitations

## 📋 Miscellaneous Provisions

### Presumption of Documents `Sec 39`
- If doc produced/seized/received from abroad (authenticated):
  - Presume signature, handwriting, execution, attestation (unless contrary proved)
  - Admit in evidence even if not duly stamped
  - Presume truth of contents (for docs in clause (i))

### Suspension/Relaxation `Sec 40`
- Central Govt can suspend/relax FEMA provisions (indefinitely or for period)
- If circumstances necessitate or in public interest
- Removal of suspension: by notification
- Laid before Parliament (**30 days**)

### Directions by Central Govt `Sec 41`
- Central Govt may give general/special directions to RBI
- RBI must comply

### Contravention by Companies `Sec 42`
- Person in charge + company deemed guilty
- If with consent/connivance/neglect of director/manager/secretary/officer: also guilty
- **Defence**: contravention without knowledge or due diligence exercised

### Death or Insolvency `Sec 43`
- Rights/obligations under **Sec 13** devolve on legal representative/official receiver/official assignee
- Legal representative liable only to extent of inheritance/estate

### Bar on Legal Proceedings `Sec 44`
- No suit/prosecution against Central Govt/RBI/officers for anything done in **good faith** under FEMA

## 📚 Key Compliance Forms

### Export
- 📝 *EDF*: Export Declaration Form (goods via customs)
- 📝 *SOFTEX*: Software export (via STPI/FTZ/SEZ)

### ODI/ECB
- 📝 *Form ECB*: For obtaining LRN & approval (duplicate via AD-I to DSIM)
- 📝 *Form ECB 2*: Monthly actual transaction return (within **7 days** of month-end)
- 📝 *APR*: Annual Performance Report (by **31st Dec**, based on audited financials)
- 📝 *Form FC-GPR*: FDI reporting (for ECB conversion to equity)
- 📝 *UIN*: Unique Identification Number (for foreign entity with ODI, before remittance/acquisition)

### Import
- 📝 *IDPMS*: Import Data Processing & Monitoring System (upload import remittances)
- 📝 *Bill of Entry*: Evidence of import (customs document)

### CAT
- 📝 *CDF*: Currency Declaration Form (if bringing FX >**USD 10,000** total or >**USD 5,000** cash)

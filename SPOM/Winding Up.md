# Chapter 7: Winding Up

## Introduction
### Definition (Sec 2(94A))
- Winding up = Under Companies Act, 2013 OR Liquidation under IBC, 2016

### Modes (Sec 270)
- Part I: Winding up by Tribunal (Sec 271-303)
- Part II: Voluntary Winding Up (Sec 304-323) ⚠️ OMITTED (IBC 2016)
- Part III: Common Provisions (Sec 324-358)

## Part I: Winding Up by Tribunal

### Sec 271: Circumstances for Winding Up
#### 📋 Compliance
- Petition under Sec 272 to Tribunal
- Grounds for winding up:
  - (a) Special Resolution passed
  - (b) Acts against national interest (sovereignty/security/public order)
  - (c) Fraudulent formation/management (on Registrar/CG application)
  - (d) 📊 Default in filing FS/Annual Returns for **5 consecutive FYs**
  - (e) Just & Equitable ground

#### 🔍 Scope
- Applies: Any company under jurisdiction
- ⚠️ Exception: Inability to pay debts (provision omitted)

### Sec 272: Petition for Winding Up
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Who can file:**
  - Company itself
  - Contributory (shares held **6 months in last 18 months**)
  - Registrar (with CG sanction)
  - Person authorized by CG
  - CG/SG (for Sec 271(b) cases)
- **By Company:** Attach Statement of Affairs
- **Copy to Registrar:** Within filing
- ⏱️ Registrar submits views within **60 days**

#### 🔍 Scope
- Contributory rights:
  - Can file even if fully paid-up shares
  - Can file even if company has no assets
  - Can file even if no surplus for distribution

#### ⚖️ Liability
- Registrar needs CG sanction (except Sec 271(a))
- Company must get reasonable opportunity before CG sanction

### Sec 273: Powers of Tribunal
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Tribunal can:**
  - (a) Dismiss with/without costs
  - (b) Pass interim order
  - (c) Appoint Provisional Liquidator (PL)
  - (d) Pass winding up order
  - (e) Any other fit order
- ⏱️ Order within **90 days** from petition date
- Notice to company before appointing PL

#### 🔍 Scope
- Cannot refuse winding up only because:
  - Assets mortgaged = value of assets
  - Company has no assets
- On just & equitable ground: Can refuse if alternative remedy available

### Sec 274: Statement of Affairs
#### 📋 Compliance
- If petition by person other than company:
  - Tribunal directs company to file objections + Statement of Affairs
  - ⏱️ Within **30 days** (extend by **30 days** max)
- On winding up order:
  - Directors submit audited books to Liquidator
  - ⏱️ Within **30 days** of order

#### 🔍 Scope
- Tribunal may direct petitioner deposit security for costs

#### ⚖️ Liability
- Non-filing = Company loses right to oppose
- 💰 Officers: Imprisonment **up to 6 months** OR Fine **₹25,000 to ₹5 lakh** OR Both
- Complaint by: Registrar/PL/CL/authorized person

### Sec 275: Company Liquidator Appointment
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal appoints at winding up order:
  - Official Liquidator OR
  - From panel (Insolvency Professionals under IBC 2016)
- PL/CL from IBC registered professionals
- ⏱️ File Declaration within **7 days** (conflict of interest disclosure)
- Terms/fees specified by Tribunal (based on task/experience/company size)

#### 🔍 Scope
- Tribunal can limit PL powers
- PL can be appointed as CL in final order

### Sec 276: Removal of Liquidator
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Grounds for removal:**
  - Misconduct
  - Fraud/misfeasance
  - Professional incompetence
  - Inability to act
  - Conflict of interest/lack of independence
- Reasonable opportunity before removal

#### 🔍 Scope
- On death/resignation/removal: Work transferred to new CL

#### ⚖️ Liability
- Loss due to fraud/misfeasance: Tribunal recovers from Liquidator

### Sec 277: Intimation of Order
#### 📋 Compliance
- ⏱️ Tribunal intimates within **7 days** to:
  - CL/PL
  - Registrar
- **Registrar actions:**
  - Endorses records
  - Notifies in Official Gazette
  - For listed company: Informs stock exchange(s)
- ⏱️ Within **3 weeks**: CL applies for Winding Up Committee

#### 🔍 Scope
- Winding up order = notice of discharge (officers/employees/workmen)
- ⚠️ Exception: If business continues

#### 📋 Compliance - Winding Up Committee
- **Composition:**
  - Official Liquidator (Tribunal-attached)
  - Nominee of secured creditors
  - Professional nominated by Tribunal
- **Functions:**
  - Take over assets
  - Examine statement of affairs
  - Recover property/cash/assets
  - Review audit reports
  - Sale of assets
  - Finalize creditors/contributories list
  - Compromise/settlement
  - Payment of dividends
  - Other Tribunal-directed functions
- ⏱️ Monthly: CL submits report + minutes to Tribunal
- CL prepares draft final report → Committee approves → Submit to Tribunal

### Sec 278: Effect of Winding Up Order
#### 🔍 Scope
- Operates in favor of:
  - All creditors AND
  - All contributories
- As if joint petition filed

### Sec 279: Stay of Suits
#### 📋 Compliance
- After winding up order/PL appointment:
  - No new suit/proceeding without Tribunal leave
  - Pending suits proceed only with Tribunal leave
- ⏱️ Application for leave disposed within **60 days**

#### 🔍 Scope
- ⚠️ Exception: Appeals before Supreme Court/High Court (no leave needed)

### Sec 280: Jurisdiction of Tribunal
#### 🔍 Scope
- **Tribunal has jurisdiction over:**
  - Any suit by/against company
  - Claims by/against company (including branches)
  - Fast track merger applications (Sec 233)
  - Priority questions
  - Questions of law/fact (assets/business/rights/duties)
- Before OR after winding up order

### Sec 281: CL's Report
#### 📋 Compliance
- ⏱️ Within **60 days** of order
- **Report contents:**
  - Assets (nature/location/value) - use Registered Valuers
  - Cash/bank balance
  - Negotiable securities
  - Capital (issued/subscribed/paid-up)
  - Liabilities (existing/contingent)
  - Creditors details (secured/unsecured debts)
  - Debtors & realization prospects
  - Guarantees extended
  - Contributories list & unpaid calls
  - Trademarks/IP
  - Contracts/JVs/collaborations
  - Holding/subsidiary companies
  - Legal cases
  - Formation manner & fraud (if any)
  - Business viability report
  - Steps to maximize asset value
- Creditor/contributory can inspect (on payment of fees)

### Sec 282: Tribunal Directions
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal after report:
  - Fixes time limit for completion & dissolution
  - Can revise time limit (after hearing CL/creditors/contributories)
  - Can order sale as going concern/assets
  - Can appoint Sale Committee (creditors/promoters/officers)
- If fraud reported:
  - Order investigation (Sec 210)
  - Pass orders (Sec 339-342)
  - Direct CL to file criminal complaint

#### 🔍 Scope
- Tribunal can order steps to protect/preserve/enhance asset value

### Sec 283: Custody of Property
#### 📋 Compliance
- On winding up order/PL appointment:
  - CL/PL takes custody of all property/effects/actionable claims
  - Property deemed in Tribunal custody from order date
- Tribunal can order contributory/trustee/banker/agent/officer to:
  - Pay/deliver/surrender/transfer
  - Money/property/books/papers to CL/PL

#### ⚖️ Liability
- 💰 Non-appearance on summons: Appropriate cost imposed
- Order executed as civil decree

### Sec 284: Cooperation with CL
#### 📋 Compliance
- Promoters/directors/officers/employees must cooperate with CL
- If non-cooperation:
  - CL applies to Tribunal
  - Tribunal orders compliance

### Sec 285: List of Contributories
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal after winding up order:
  - Settles contributories list
  - Rectifies register of members
  - Applies assets for discharge
- Distinguish: Own right vs. representatives
- **Contributory liable if:**
  - Member now OR within ⏱️ **1 year** before winding up
  - For debts/liabilities/costs/expenses

#### 🔍 Scope
- ⚠️ Exception: No calls needed if unnecessary
- **Past member NOT liable if:**
  - Ceased ⏱️ **1 year+** before commencement
  - For debts after ceasing to be member
- **Liability extent:**
  - Limited by shares: Unpaid amount only
  - Limited by guarantee: Unpaid shares (if company has share capital)
- Present members unable to pay → Past members liable

### Sec 286: Directors/Managers Obligations
#### 🔍 Scope
- **Unlimited liability Director/Manager:**
  - Additional contribution (as if unlimited company member)
- ⚠️ Exceptions:
  - Ceased ⏱️ **1 year+** before winding up
  - For debts after ceasing office
  - Unless necessary for debts/liabilities/costs

### Sec 287: Advisory Committee
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal may direct appointment
- 📊 Max **12 members** (creditors/contributories/others)
- ⏱️ Within **30 days**: CL convenes meeting to determine members
- Functions:
  - Advise CL
  - Report to Tribunal
  - Right to inspect books/documents/assets
- CL = Chairperson

#### 🔍 Scope
- Proportion decided by Tribunal per company circumstances

### Sec 288: Periodical Reports
#### 📋 Compliance
- CL submits to Tribunal:
  - Periodical reports
  - ⏱️ End of each **quarter** (minimum)
- Tribunal can review orders on CL application

### Sec 290: Powers of CL
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Subject to Tribunal directions:**
  - (a) Carry on business (for beneficial winding up)
  - (b) Execute deeds/receipts/documents (use company seal)
  - (c) Sell immovable/movable property (auction/private contract/parcels)
  - (d) Sell undertaking as going concern
  - (e) Raise money on asset security
  - (f) Institute/defend suits/prosecutions
  - (g) Invite/settle claims (creditors/employees/others)
  - (h) Inspect Registrar's records
  - (i) Prove/rank/claim in contributory insolvency
  - (j) Draw/accept/endorse negotiable instruments
  - (k) Take letters of administration (deceased contributory)
  - (l) Obtain professional assistance/appoint agent
  - (m) Sign/execute/verify papers for: winding up/asset distribution/duties
  - (n) Apply to Tribunal for orders/directions

#### 🔍 Scope
- Under overall Tribunal control
- Other duties as Tribunal specifies

### Sec 291: Professional Assistance
#### 📋 Compliance
- With Tribunal sanction:
  - Appoint CA/CS/CMA/Legal Practitioners/other professionals
- Appointee files declaration (conflict of interest/independence)

### Sec 292: Exercise & Control of Powers
#### 📋 Compliance
- CL follows directions from:
  - Creditors/contributories resolution (general meeting)
  - Advisory Committee
- Creditors/contributories directions override Committee
- **CL summons meetings:**
  - Whenever fit (to ascertain wishes)
  - When directed by resolution
  - ⏱️ When requested by **1/10th value** creditors/contributories

#### ⚖️ Liability
- Aggrieved person applies to Tribunal
- Tribunal can confirm/reverse/modify CL act/decision

### Sec 293: Books by CL
#### 📋 Compliance
- Maintain proper books for:
  - Proceedings at meetings
  - Other prescribed matters
- Creditor/contributory can inspect (through agent, subject to Tribunal control)

### Sec 294: Audit of CL Accounts
#### 📋 Compliance
- Maintain proper books (receipts/payments)
- ⏱️ Present to Tribunal **twice/year** (minimum):
  - Receipts/payments account
  - Declaration verified
- Tribunal causes audit
- CL provides vouchers/information
- **After audit:**
  - 1 copy filed with Tribunal
  - 1 copy to Registrar (open to inspection)
- For Govt Company:
  - Copy to CG (if CG member)
  - Copy to SG (if SG member)
  - Copy to both (if both members)
- Send printed copy/summary to creditors/contributories (Tribunal may dispense)

### Sec 295: Payment by Contributory
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal can order contributory pay:
  - Money due from him/estate (excluding calls)
- **Tribunal may allow set-off:**
  - Unlimited company: Money due to contributory (independent dealing, NOT dividend)
  - Limited company: To director/manager (unlimited liability) or estate
  - When creditors paid in full: Any money due against subsequent call

### Sec 296: Power to Make Calls
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal can (before/after ascertaining asset sufficiency):
  - Make calls on contributories (to extent of liability)
  - For debts/liabilities/costs/expenses
  - Order payment of calls

### Sec 297: Adjustment of Rights
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal adjusts contributories rights
- Distributes surplus to entitled persons

### Sec 298: Power to Order Costs
#### 🔍 Scope
- If assets insufficient:
  - Tribunal orders payment of costs/charges/expenses
  - In priority order inter se as Tribunal thinks just

### Sec 299: Summon Persons
#### 📋 Compliance
- After PL appointment/winding up order:
- **Tribunal summons:**
  - Company officer
  - Person with company property/books/papers
  - Person indebted to company
  - Person capable of giving info (promotion/formation/trade/dealings/affairs)
- **Examination:**
  - On oath
  - Orally/written interrogatories/affidavit
  - Answers reduced to writing & signed
- **Production:**
  - Books/papers (without prejudice to lien)
  - Tribunal determines lien questions
- CL files report on debt/property
- **If Tribunal finds:**
  - Person indebted: Order payment to PL/CL (with/without costs)
  - Person possesses property: Order delivery to PL/CL

#### ⚖️ Liability
- 💰 Fail to appear without reasonable cause: Appropriate cost
- Order executed as civil decree
- Payment/delivery discharges liability

### Sec 300: Examination of Promoters/Directors
#### 📋 Compliance
- On CL report (fraud since formation):
  - Tribunal directs person/officer attend & be examined
  - On oath (promotion/formation/business/conduct/dealings)
- CL participates (may employ legal assistance)
- **Person examined:**
  - Gets copy of CL report (at own cost)
  - Can employ CA/CS/CMA/Legal practitioners
- **If application to be exculpated:**
  - CL appears & highlights relevant matters
  - Tribunal may order payment of costs
- Notes of examination:
  - Taken in writing
  - Read over & signed
  - Copy to examined person
  - Usable as evidence
  - Open to creditor/contributory inspection

#### 🔍 Scope
- Tribunal can adjourn examination
- Examination before authorized person/authority (if Tribunal directs)
- Powers exercisable by authorized person (except costs)

### Sec 301: Arrest of Absconding Person
#### 📋 Compliance
- Before/after winding up order:
- **If Tribunal satisfied (contributory/person with property/accounts/papers):**
  - About to leave India/abscond
  - About to remove/conceal property
  - To evade calls/avoid examination
- **Tribunal causes:**
  - Contributory detained (till Tribunal orders)
  - Books/papers/movable property seized & kept

### Sec 302: Dissolution by Tribunal
#### 📋 Compliance
- When affairs completely wound up:
  - CL applies for dissolution
- Tribunal orders dissolution (if just & reasonable)
- Company dissolved from order date
- ⏱️ Within **30 days**: Tribunal forwards copy to Registrar
- Registrar records dissolution minute

### Sec 303: Appeals from Old Orders
#### 🔍 Scope
- Nothing affects orders before Act commencement
- Appeals to competent authority before commencement

## Part III: Common Provisions

### Sec 324: Debts Admitted to Proof
#### 🔍 Scope
- **In every winding up:**
  - Contingent debts admitted
  - All claims (present/future/certain/contingent/damages-only)
  - Just estimate of value made

### Sec 326: Overriding Preferential Payments
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Paid in priority:**
  - (a) Workmen's dues
  - (b) Secured creditor shortfall OR workmen's portion (whichever less) - pari passu with workmen
- ⏱️ For **2 years** before order (Sec 2(94A)(i)(ii)):
  - Paid within **30 days** of asset sale
  - Priority over ALL debts (including secured)
  - Paid in full first
- Then other preferential debts paid in full (or abate equally if insufficient)

#### 🔍 Scope
- **Workmen** = employees under Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 Sec 2(s)
- **Workmen's Dues:**
  - Wages/salary (time/piece work/commission)
  - Compensation under Industrial Disputes Act
  - Accrued holiday remuneration
  - ⚠️ Unless: Reconstruction/amalgamation OR insurer rights transferable
  - Workmen's Compensation Act amounts (death/disablement)
  - PF/Pension/Gratuity/welfare funds
- **Workmen's Portion** = (Dues/Total dues+Secured debt) × Security value

### Sec 327: Preferential Payments
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Subject to Sec 326, priority order:**
  - (a) Govt taxes/cesses/rates (⏱️ **12 months** before relevant date)
  - (b) Wages/salary (⏱️ **4 months in last 12 months**, subject to notified cap)
  - (c) Accrued holiday remuneration
  - (d) ESI Act contributions (⏱️ **12 months**, unless reconstruction/amalgamation)
  - (e) Workmen's Compensation Act amounts (unless insurer rights transferable)
  - (f) PF/Pension/Gratuity funds
  - (g) Investigation expenses (Sec 213/216)
- **Person advancing money** for wages/salary/holiday pay:
  - Gets priority to extent of advance
- Debts rank equally (abate if insufficient)
- Have priority over floating charge holders
- ⏱️ Discharged forthwith (if assets sufficient)
- Formal proof not needed (except as prescribed)
- Landlord distress (⏱️ **3 months** before order): First charge on goods/proceeds

#### 🔍 Scope
- ⚠️ Sections 326 & 327 NOT applicable in IBC liquidation
- **Relevant Date:**
  - PL appointment date OR
  - Winding up order date OR
  - Voluntary winding commencement (if earlier)
- Holiday remuneration = amounts payable if employment continued
- Employee ≠ workman

### Sec 328: Fraudulent Preference
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Transaction within ⏱️ 6 months before petition:**
  - Preference to creditor/surety/guarantor
  - Puts person in better position
- Tribunal can:
  - Order restoration
  - Declare transaction invalid
- Applies to:
  - Property transfer
  - Goods delivery
  - Payment/execution

#### 🔍 Scope
- If fraudulent preference proved

### Sec 329: Transfers Not in Good Faith
#### 🔍 Scope
- **Void against CL if within ⏱️ 1 year before petition:**
  - NOT in ordinary business course
  - NOT to good faith purchaser/encumbrancer for value

### Sec 330: Certain Transfers Void
#### 🔍 Scope
- **Void:**
  - Transfer of ALL properties/assets to trustees for all creditors

### Sec 331: Rights of Fraudulently Preferred
#### 📋 Compliance
- Person preferred (property mortgaged/charged):
  - Liable as surety to extent of:
    - Mortgage/charge OR
    - Value of interest (whichever less)
- Value determined at transaction date
- On application re payment:
  - Tribunal determines payment questions
  - Between payee & surety/guarantor
  - Grant relief (even if not needed for winding up)
  - Give leave to bring surety/guarantor as third party

#### 🔍 Scope
- Applies mutatis mutandis to non-payment transactions

### Sec 332: Floating Charge
#### 🔍 Scope
- **Floating charge within ⏱️ 12 months before commencement:**
  - INVALID
  - ⚠️ Exception: Company solvent after creation
  - Valid for: Cash paid + interest @**5%** p.a. (or notified rate)

### Sec 333: Disclaimer of Onerous Property
#### 📋 Compliance
- CL may disclaim (with Tribunal leave):
  - Land with onerous covenants
  - Shares/stocks in companies
  - Property not saleable (onerous act/payment required)
  - Unprofitable contracts
- ⏱️ Within **12 months** from commencement (or extended period)
- If unaware: ⏱️ Within **12 months** after awareness
- Disclaimer determines company rights/liabilities from date
- Tribunal can:
  - Require notices to interested persons
  - Impose terms
- **CL NOT entitled to disclaim if:**
  - Application by interested person
  - ⏱️ CL doesn't decide/give notice within **28 days** (or extended)
  - For contracts: Deemed adopted if no disclaimer

#### 🔍 Scope
- Tribunal can (on application):
  - Rescind contract (with damages)
  - Vest property in entitled person/trustee
  - For leasehold: Vesting subject to same liabilities (or as Tribunal directs)
- Affected person = creditor for compensation/damages

### Sec 334: Transfers After Commencement
#### 🔍 Scope
- **After commencement, VOID (unless Tribunal orders):**
  - Property disposition (including actionable claims)
  - Share transfer
  - Member status alteration

### Sec 335: Attachments/Executions Void
#### 🔍 Scope
- **After commencement, VOID (without Tribunal leave):**
  - Attachment/distress/execution against estate/effects
  - Sale of properties/effects
- ⚠️ Exception: Tax/impost/govt dues recovery proceedings

### Sec 336: Offences by Officers
#### ⚖️ Liability
- **Officer of company being wound up:**
- 💰 Imprisonment **3-5 years** + Fine **₹1-3 lakh** if:
  - (a) Doesn't disclose property/disposal to CL
  - (b) Doesn't deliver property to CL
  - (c) Doesn't deliver books/papers to CL
  - (d) Within ⏱️ **12 months** before/after commencement:
    - (i) Conceals property **≥₹1,000** / debt
    - (ii) Fraudulently removes property **≥₹1,000**
    - (iii) Conceals/destroys/mutilates/falsifies books/papers
    - (iv) Makes false entry
    - (v) Fraudulently parts with/alters/omits books/papers
    - (vi) False representation to obtain property on credit
    - (vii) False pretense company running to obtain credit
    - (viii) Pawns/pledges unpaid credit property (not in ordinary business)
  - (e) Material omission in statement
  - (f) Knows false debt proved, ⏱️ doesn't inform CL for **1 month**
  - (g) After commencement, prevents book/paper production
  - (h) Accounts for property by fictitious losses/expenses (at creditors meeting ⏱️ **12 months** before)
  - (i) False representation/fraud for creditors' consent
- Defence: No intent to defraud/conceal/defeat law

#### ⚖️ Liability - Receiver of Pawned Property
- 💰 Imprisonment **3-5 years** + Fine **₹3-5 lakh**
- If knowingly receives property pawned under Sec 336(1)(d)(viii)

#### 🔍 Scope
- Officer includes: Person directors act per directions/instructions

### Sec 344: Statement of Liquidation
#### 📋 Compliance
- When being wound up (Tribunal/voluntary):
- **Every invoice/order/business letter must state:**
  - "Company is being wound up"
- Issued by/on behalf of:
  - Company
  - CL
  - Receiver
  - Manager

#### ⚖️ Liability
- 💰 Company & every officer/CL/receiver/manager: Fine **₹50,000 to ₹3 lakh**
- If willfully authorizes/permits non-compliance

### Sec 345: Books as Evidence
#### 🔍 Scope
- When being wound up:
  - Company books/papers
  - CL books/papers
- Prima facie evidence of truth (between contributories)

### Sec 346: Inspection by Creditors/Contributories
#### 📋 Compliance
- After winding up order:
  - Creditor/contributory can inspect books/papers
  - Per prescribed rules only

#### 🔍 Scope
- ⚠️ Doesn't restrict rights of:
  - CG/SG
  - Authority/officer thereof
  - Person acting under their authority

### Sec 347: Disposal of Books/Papers
#### 📋 Compliance
- When affairs completely wound up (before dissolution):
  - Books/papers disposed per Tribunal directions
- ⏱️ After **5 years** from dissolution:
  - No responsibility on company/CL/custodian
- CG can (by rules):
  - Prevent destruction for prescribed period
  - Enable creditor/contributory representations to CG
  - Enable appeal to Tribunal from CG order

#### ⚖️ Liability
- 💰 Contravene rule/order: Fine **up to ₹50,000**

### Sec 352: Liquidation Dividend Account
#### 📋 Compliance
- **CL has unpaid money representing:**
  - (a) Dividends unpaid ⏱️ **6 months** after declaration
  - (b) Assets undistributed ⏱️ **6 months** after refundable
- CL deposits forthwith into:
  - **Company Liquidation Dividend & Undistributed Assets Account**
  - In scheduled bank
- On dissolution:
  - Pay unpaid dividends/undistributed assets into Account
- **Furnish to Registrar:**
  - Statement (prescribed form)
  - Nature of sums
  - Names/addresses
  - Amount entitled
  - Claim nature
- CL gets receipt (effectual discharge)

#### 📋 Compliance - Voluntary Winding Up
- When filing Sec 348 statement:
  - Indicate sum payable (preceding ⏱️ **6 months**)
  - ⏱️ Within **14 days**: Pay into Account

#### 📋 Compliance - Claims
- Person claims entitled:
  - Apply to Registrar for payment
  - ⏱️ Registrar settles within **60 days** (or report to RD)
- Unclaimed ⏱️ **15 years**:
  - Transfer to CG General Revenue Account
  - Claim still valid (treated as refund)

#### ⚖️ Liability - CL Retention
- 💰 Interest @**12%** p.a. + penalty (determined by Registrar)
- Liable for default expenses
- If Tribunal winding up:
  - Remuneration disallowed (as Tribunal considers)
  - Removal from office

#### 🔍 Scope
- CG may remit interest (in proper cases)

### Sec 353: CL Returns
#### 📋 Compliance
- If CL defaults in filing/delivering/making return/account/document/notice:
  - ⏱️ Doesn't make good within **14 days** after notice
- Tribunal can (on application by contributory/creditor/Registrar):
  - Order CL make good default (within specified time)
  - Order costs borne by CL

#### 🔍 Scope
- Doesn't prejudice other penalty enactments

### Sec 354: Meetings for Wishes
#### 📋 Compliance
- Tribunal may:
  - Have regard to creditors/contributories wishes (by evidence)
  - Direct meetings to ascertain wishes
  - Appoint Chairman to report result
- **Ascertaining wishes:**
  - Creditors: By debt value
  - Contributories: By vote numbers

### Sec 355: Affidavit Oath
#### 📋 Compliance
- **Affidavit sworn:**
  - In India: Before Court/Tribunal/Judge/authorized person
  - Other country: Before Court/Judge/authorized person OR Indian Diplomatic/Consular Officer
- Courts/Tribunals/Judges/Justices/Commissioners:
  - Take judicial notice of seal/stamp/signature

### Sec 356: Power to Declare Dissolution Void
#### 📋 Compliance
- **After dissolution:**
  - ⏱️ Within **2 years** from dissolution date
  - On CL/interested person application
  - Tribunal can declare dissolution void (on fit terms)
- ⏱️ Within **30 days** from order:
  - Tribunal forwards certified copy to Registrar OR
  - Directs CL/applicant file certified copy with Registrar
- Registrar records

### Sec 357: Commencement of Winding Up
#### 🔍 Scope
- Winding up by Tribunal:
  - Commences at petition presentation time

### Sec 358: Exclusion of Time
#### 🔍 Scope
- Computing limitation for suit/application in company name:

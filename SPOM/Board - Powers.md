# Chapter 3: Meetings of Board and Its Powers (Sec 173-193)

## Section 173: Meetings of Board
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Every company must hold regular Board Meetings (BM) to exercise collective powers.
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **First Meeting:** ⏳ Within 30 days of incorporation.
- **Frequency:** Minimum 4 meetings every year.
- **Gap:** ⏳ Max 120 days between two consecutive meetings.
- **Video Conferencing (VC):** Allowed (Rule 3).
    - ⚠️ Must record/recognize participation.
    - ⚠️ Store proceedings with date/time.
    - 📝 Roll call required.
- **Notice:**
    - ⏳ 7 Days in writing (Hand/Post/Electronic).
    - ⚠️ Shorter Notice: Allowed if 1 Independent Director (ID) present or decision ratified by 1 ID.
    - After the Receipt
        - If intending to attend via VC means
            - Intimate Chairman or CS 
            - If not intimated, assumed attending directly
- If the director absents himself from BM for 12 months without permission -> Deemed to have vacated the office
### 🚧 Restrictions & Limits
- **OPC / Small / Dormant / Pvt (Startup):**
    - 📉 1 meeting in each half of calendar year.
    - ⏳ Min gap of 90 days.
    - ⚠️ Exemption: OPC with only 1 Director (No meeting required).
### 🚔 Consequences
- **Officer in Default:** 💰 Penalty ₹25,000.

## Section 174: Quorum for Board Meetings
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Minimum number of directors required to transact business validly.
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Limit:** Higher of:
    - 1/3rd of Total Strength (Fraction = 1).
    - 2 Directors.
- **Interested Directors:**
    - ⚠️ Excluded from Quorum.
    - If Interested Directors ≥ 2/3rd of strength ➡ Quorum = Remaining Non-interested Directors (Min 2).
        - Not Applicable to Private company which has not defaulted in filing its financial and Annual Retuns with ROC
- **Vacancy in board**
    - If quorum cannot be fulfilled,
        - Remaining director can hold a valid board meeting but can only ACT follows
            - Summoning a General Meeting
            - Appointment of a new director
- **Adjournment (No Quorum):**
    - ⏳ Same day, next week, same time/place. (Even it is Sunday, it should happen)
    - If National Holiday ➡ Next succeeding day (non-holiday).
### 🚧 Restrictions & Limits
- **Section 8 Company:** Quorum = 8 members or 25% of strength (whichever is less), min 2.
- Quorum not applicable to OPC

## Section 175: Resolution by Circulation
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Decisions taken without physical meeting by circulating draft resolution.
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Process:**
    - Circulate draft + papers to ALL directors at registered address.
    - 🗳️ Approved by Majority of directors entitled to vote.
- **Exception:**
    - If ≥ 1/3rd Directors require meeting ➡ Chairperson MUST call a physical/VC meeting.
- **Recording:** Note in minutes of next Board Meeting.

## Section 177: Audit Committee
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Committee to oversee financial reporting and internal controls.
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Composition:** 
    - Min 3 Directors (Majority should be Independent Directors).
        - Majority Independent directors criteria not applicable in case of section 8 companies - Not defaulted in annual return filing
- **Functions:**
    - Auditor 
        - Appointment and Remuneration
        - Monitoring independence 
    - Internal controls evaluation
    - Financial Oversights
        - Examination of FS and Audit report
        - Scrutiny of inter-corporate loans
        - Monitoring the end usage of the funds
    - Valuation of undertakings or assets of the company
    - ⚠️ Related Party Transactions (Omnibus approval allowed).
        - Omnibus approval
            - Criteria for omnibus approval shall be already present containing
                - Per transaction limit
                - overall txn limit
                - Justifications 
                - Transactions excluded from omnibus approval
            - Requires annual re-approval - Valid only for 1 FY
        - Voidable transaction
            - Transaction less than 1 Cr
            - Entered into by director or company officer without AC approval
            - Ratification can be done within 3 months of DOT 
            - Voidable at option of Audit Commitee
                - If made void, loss made by company shall be indemnified by the officer or director
            - Not applicable for WOS and Holding company txns
- Rights of Audit Committee  
    - Call for comments of auditors about
        - IC Systems
        - Scope of Audit, observations of auditors, 
        - Review of FS before submission to the board
    - Discuss any related issues with Stat or Internal auditors
    - Right to investigate 
        - Power to seek professional advice 
        - Access to information in the records of the company
- **Vigil Mechanism:** 
    - Applicability
        - Listed company
        - Any Company
            - Accepting Deposit from public
            - Borrowing from Banks and PFI > 50 Crore
    - Mechanism
        - Who Does?
            - If AC Exists - AC
            - If does not exists - Appoint a director for Vigil Mechanism
        - Rules
            - Adequate safeguards against victimization 
            - direct access to director in case of exceptional cases
            - Action against frivolous repeated complaints
        - Disclosure
            - In board report
            - in website of company

### 🚧 Restrictions & Limits
- **📉 Applicability of Audit Commitee(Public Cos):**
    - Paid-up Capital ≥ ₹10 Cr.
    - Turnover ≥ ₹100 Cr.
        - Leave compliance only if 3 consecutive years not fulfilled
    - O/s Loans/Deposits > ₹50 Cr.
- **Non Applicability**
    - JV
    - WOS
    - Dormant Companies
### Penalty
    - Company - 5L
    - OID - 1L

## Section 179: Powers of Board
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Board exercises all powers of company except those reserved for General Meeting (GM).
- Any act done by board subsequently invalidated in the GN does not invalidate the acts made earlier
    - Viz, only prospective changes can be made
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Powers via Board Meeting Only (Rule 8):**
    - Fund Related
        - Make calls on unpaid shares.
        - Buy-back securities.
        - Issue securities/debentures.
        - Borrow monies.
            - Exception for banking companies
        - Invest funds.
        - Grant loans/guarantees.
            - Exception for banking companies
    - Approve Financial Statements/Board Report.
    - Diversify business.
    - Amalgamation/Merger/Takeover.
    - Appointment of KMPs, Internal auditor and secretarial auditor
- Delegation 
    - Only possible for
        - Borrow
        - Invest
        - Grant Loan 
    - To
        - Other directors
        - MD or Manager
        - Principal officer of the company
- Imposing restrictions on board by Shareholders
    - Pass resolution in GM
    - Default empowerment of shareholders in following cases
        - Directors are acting malafide
        - All directors are interested in a transaction
        - Deadlock in the management

## Section 180: Restrictions on Board Powers
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Board needs Shareholder approval (SR) for critical financial decisions.
    - If a buyer/lessee acts in good faith, their title is valid even if the Board failed to get the special resolution
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Requires Special Resolution (SR) from Shareholders for:**
    - 1. Sale/Lease of Undertaking (>20% of Net Worth/Income).
    - 2. Investment of Merger/Amalgamation compensation (Non-Trust securities).
    - 3. Borrowing > (Paid-up Capital + Free Reserves + Sec. Premium).
        - Temp loans excluded (repayable on demand or less than 6 months)
        - If the limit is exceeded, the debt is not valid unless the lender acted in good faith and without knowledge of the breach.
    - 4. Remitting debt of a Director or giving more time.
### 🚧 Exceptions
- ⚠️ Exemption: Pvt Companies not defaulted in annual return filing.

### ⚙️ Mechanics

## Section 185: Loans to Directors 

### 1. General Prohibition (Sec 185(1))
- **Rule**: Company CANNOT advance loan, guarantee, or security to:
    - **Prohibited Recipients**:
        - Any **Director** of Company or Holding Co.
        - Any **Partner** or **Relative** of such Director
        - Any **Firm** where Director/Relative is a partner

### 2. Restricted Permissions (Sec 185(2))
- **Applicability**: Loans/Guarantee/Security to "Person in whom Director is interested"
- **Who is this Person?**
  - **Private Co**: Where Director is Director or Member
  - **Body Corporate**: Director(s) control **>= 25% voting power**
  - **Body Corporate**: Board/MD/Manager accustomed to act on Director's instructions
- **Mandatory Conditions**:
  - **Special Resolution** passed in General Meeting
  - Explanatory statement must disclose particulars
  - Loan utilized for **Principal Business Activities**

### 3. Exceptions (Sec 185(3))
- **Managing / Whole Time Director**
  - As part of service conditions (for all employees)
  - OR Approved by Special Resolution
- **Ordinary Course of Business**
  - Company strictly in business of lending
  - **Condition**: Interest rate >= Govt Security Yield (1,3,5,10 yr tenor)
- **Holding to Subsidiary**
  - **To Wholly Owned Subsidiary (WOS)**: Loan, Guarantee, or Security allowed
  - **To Subsidiary (Any)**: Guarantee/Security allowed for loan from Bank/FI
  - **Condition**: Funds used for Principal Business Activities

### 4. Penalties (Sec 185(4))
- **Company**: Fine ₹5L - ₹25L
- **Officer in Default**: Imprisonment <= 6 months OR Fine ₹5L - ₹25L
- **Recipient**: Imprisonment <= 6 months OR Fine ₹5L - ₹25L OR Both

### 5. Exemptions (Conditional)
- *Pre-requisite*: No default in filing S.137 (Financials) or S.92 (Annual Return)
- **Government Company**: Approval from Ministry/State Govt
- **Private Company**:
  - No Body Corporate invested in share capital
  - Borrowings < (2x Paid-up Capital OR ₹50 Cr)
  - No default in borrowing repayment
- **Nidhi Company**: Loan to Director/Relative in capacity as member

- Exception
    - A foreign company is acquired and that company has more than 2 layers + NOt prohibited in that country
    - Subsidiary having an investment subsidiary due to statutory requirements

    - Give loan

    - higher of

- To exceed limit
    - Get SR in GM (Actually Postal ballot reqd)
- Exceptions
    - To WOS

- Non Applicability

        - funding is for Industrial research stuffs

- Should not be lower than prevailing yield of G-SECS

## Section 187: Investments in Company's Own Name
- **General Rule [Sec 187(1)]**
  - All investments (property, security, assets)
  - Must be held in Company's **own name**
  - **Exception (Proviso)**
    - Shares in **Subsidiary Company**
    - Can be held by **Nominees**
    - *Purpose:* To meet statutory minimum members (Pvt: 2, Public: 7)
- **Exemptions [Sec 187(2)]**
  - **Collection**
    - Deposited with Bankers
    - Purpose: Collecting dividend/interest
  - **Facilitating Transfer**
    - Deposited/Transferred to Bankers (SBI/Scheduled)
    - *Condition:* If not transferred within **6 months**, must restore to company name
  - **Security for Loan**
    - Deposited with **Any Person**
    - Purpose: Security for loan repayment or obligation
  - **Depository (Demat)**
    - Held in name of Depository
    - Company is **Beneficial Owner**
- **Register Requirements [Sec 187(3) & Rule 14]**
  - **When to Maintain?**
    - If securities are NOT held in company's name
  - **Form MBP-3**
    - Maintained from date of registration
    - Entered Chronologically
  - **Content**
    - Particulars of investment
    - **Reasons** for not holding in own name
    - Relationship/Contract with the holder
  - **Logistics**
    - Location: **Registered Office**
    - Preservation: **Permanently**
    - Custody: Company Secretary (or authorized Director/Officer)
  - **Inspection**
    - Who: Members & Debenture holders
    - Cost: **Free** (during business hours)
- Penalty
    - Company - 5L
    - OID - 50K
## Section 188: Related Party Transactions (RPT)
### Related Party and Relative
- Related Party
    - Individual
        - Directors, KMP and Relatives
    - Entities
        - Firm - Above individuals are Partners
        - Pvt Company - Individual is Member or Director
        - Public Co  - Individual is Director or has more than 2% voting power
        - Groups
            - C H A S + Fellow Subsidiary
        - Advisors - Persons whose instructions Board is supposed to act upon
Relatives (Includes Step Relative)
    - Father + Mother 
    - Brother + Sister
    - Son + Daughter + their married partner
### 🎯 Core Provision
- Transactions with Relate  d Parties require approval to prevent conflict of interest.
### Royalty is not coming under the transactions referred under this section
### ⚙️ Mechanics
- **Board Approval:** Required for all RPTs (Resolution).
    - Except
        - Arms Length txns
        - WOS Transactions 
            - Provided CFS is given to the shareholders
- **Shareholder Approval (OR):** If transaction exceeds prescribed thresholds (Individually or in aggregate) - TO and N/W of PY 
    - Sale or Purchase - 10% or more of the TO of company
    - Property Sale or Purchase - 10% of Net Worth
    - Leasing - 10% of TO
    - Services - 10% of TO
    - appointment of place or place of profit - Monthly exceeding 2.5 L
    - Underwriting commission more than 1% of net worth
- **Register:** 📝 Form MBP-4 (Sec 189).
### 🚧 Restrictions & Limits
- ⚠️ No vote by interested member in GM.
    - But if the 90% of members are related parties, this abstinence is not required (related party to that transaction)
    - Exceptions
        - Pvt company not defaulted in annual return filing - abstain not reqd
### 🚔 Consequences
- **Voidable:** At option of Board/Shareholders.
    - Ratification to be done within 3 months
    - if not company may proceed with recovery of losses 
- **Penalty:**
    - Listed Co: 💰 ₹25 Lakh.
    - Other Co: 💰 ₹5 Lakh.
### Disclosure of RPT in Board's Report

## Section 192: Non-Cash Transactions with Directors
- **The Restriction [Sec 192(1)]**
  - **Scope:** Arrangements for "Consideration other than Cash" (Asset Swaps)
  - **Parties Involved:**
    - Company AND
    - Director (of Co, Holding, Subsidiary, Associate) OR
    - Connected Person
  - **Transaction Types:**
    - Director acquires asset FROM Company
    - Company acquires asset FROM Director
- **Conditions for Approval [Sec 192(2)]**
  - **Prior Approval:** Resolution in General Meeting
  - **Valuation:** Notice must include asset value by **Registered Valuer**
  - **Special Case (Holding Co Director):**
    - Need Approval from **Company's GM** AND
    - Need Approval from **Holding Company's GM**
- **Consequences of Default [Sec 192(3)]**
  - **Status:** **Voidable** at option of the Company
  - **Exceptions (Cannot be Voided if):**
    - **Restitution Impossible:** Assets can't be returned + Company is indemnified
    - **Innocent Third Party:** Rights acquired bona fide for value without notice
## Section 193: Contracts by One Person Company (OPC)
- **Applicability**
  - **Entity:** OPC (Limited by Shares or Guarantee)
  - **Parties:** Contract between **OPC** AND its **Sole Member** (who is also Director)
- **Documentation Requirements**
  - **Scenario A:** Contract is in Writing (Preferred)
  - **Scenario B:** Contract is NOT in Writing
    - Terms must be contained in a **Memorandum** OR
    - Recorded in **Minutes** of the **Next Board Meeting**
- **Exception [Sec 193(2)]**
  - Section DOES NOT apply if contract is in the **Ordinary Course of Business**
- **Filing Requirement**
  - **Who:** Inform the **Registrar (ROC)**
  - **What:** Particulars of contract/minutes
  - **Timeline:** Within **15 days** of Board approval

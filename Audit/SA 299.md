# SA 299(Revised): Joint Audit of Financial Statements

## Objective
- Lay down broad principles for conducting joint audit (Para 4a)
- Provide uniform approach to process of joint audit (Para 4b)
- Identify distinct areas of work and coverage (Para 4c)
- Identify individual and joint responsibility of joint auditors (Para 4d)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audit by more than one auditor (Para 1)
- Does NOT apply to: Principal and component auditor relationship (Para 2)
- Effective from: April 1, 2018 (Para 3)

## Definitions
- 📌 Joint Audit (Para 5): An audit of financial statements of an entity by two or more auditors appointed with the objective of issuing the audit report.
- 📌 Joint Auditors (Para 5): Such auditors are described as joint auditors.

## Requirements
- Planning and Allocation of Work
  - 🎯 Involve engagement partner and key members in planning (Para 6)
  - 🎯 Jointly establish overall audit strategy (Para 7)
  - 🎯 Discuss and develop joint audit plan before audit (Para 8)
    - Identify division and common audit areas (Para 8a)
    - Ascertain reporting objectives for timing and communications (Para 8b)
    - Consider and communicate significant factors directing efforts (Para 8c)
    - Consider results of preliminary engagement activities (Para 8d)
    - Ascertain nature, timing and extent of resources necessary (Para 8e)
  - 🎯 Consider, assess and communicate material misstatement risks (Para 9)
  - 🎯 Discuss and document procedures for common and specific areas (Para 10)
    - Communicate same to those charged with governance (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Obtain common engagement and management representation letters (Para 11)
  - 🎯 Sign work allocation document by all joint auditors (Para 12)
    - Communicate allocation to those charged with governance (Para 12)
- Responsibility and Co-ordination
  - 🎯 Each joint auditor shall be responsible only for allocated work (Para 13)
  - 🎯 All joint auditors shall be jointly and severally responsible for: (Para 14)
    - Undivided audit work carried out by all (Para 14a)
    - Decisions concerning nature, timing, extent of common procedures (Para 14b)
    - Matters brought to notice with agreement among all (Para 14c)
    - Examining financial statements comply with relevant statutes (Para 14d)
    - Presentation and disclosure per applicable framework (Para 14e)
    - Ensuring audit report complies with statutes and SAs (Para 14f)
  - 🎯 Communicate relevant matters to other joint auditors (Para 15)
    - ⚠️ If requiring attention, disclosure, or discussion (Para 15)
    - Must communicate in writing prior to completion (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Determine nature, timing, extent of procedures for allocated areas (Para 16)
    - Individually evaluate internal control and assess risk (Para 16)
- Audit Conclusion and Reporting
  - 🎯 Issue common audit report (Para 17)
    - ⚠️ Unless auditors disagree regarding opinion or matters (Para 17)
    - 🎯 Express opinion in separate audit report if disagreeing (Para 17)
    - 🎯 Shall not be bound by majority views (Para 17)
    - 🎯 Make reference to separate reports in Other Matter paragraph (Para 17)
    - 🔗 See SA 706(Revised) (Para 17)
  - Each joint auditor is entitled to assume: (Para 18)
    - Others performed work in accordance with SAs (Para 18a)
    - ⚠️ Not necessary to review work of other joint auditors (Para 18a)
    - Others brought notice of framework departures or observations (Para 18b)
  - Entitled to proceed assuming branch audited by another complies with laws (Para 19)
  - 🎯 Discuss and communicate conclusions before finalizing report (Para 20)
- Communication with TCWG
  - 🎯 Communicate expected modification circumstances and wording (Para 21)
    - 🔗 See SA 705(Revised) (Para 21)
  - 🎯 Communicate expectation and wording of EOM or OM paragraph (Para 21)
    - 🔗 See SA 706(Revised) (Para 21)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 8(a): Division of Work
  - Divide work by mutual discussion (Para A1)
  - Usually divided by identifiable units or specified areas (Para A1)
  - If impossible, divide by assets, liabilities, income, expenditure (Para A1)
  - Important areas often remain undivided and covered by all (Para A1)
- Explains Para 12: Allocation Documentation
  - Documentation avoids disputes among joint auditors (Para A2)
  - Communication avoids disputes with the entity (Para A2)
- Explains Para 13 and 14: Responsibility Sharing
  - Responsibility shared for obtaining and evaluating information (Para A3)
  - ⚠️ Unless specific distribution pattern agreed upon (Para A3)
  - Specific unit allocation means separate responsibility to evaluate it (Para A3)
  - Proper coordination and rationality required (Para A3)
- Explains Para 14(b): Common Decisions
  - Joint responsibility only for appropriateness of agreed audit procedures (Para A4)
  - Proper execution remains individual responsibility of concerned auditor (Para A4)
- Explains Para 17: Disagreement on Reporting
  - Each disagreeing auditor must issue separate report (Para A5)
  - Must cross-reference other reports in respective OM paragraph (Para A5)
- Explains Para 19: Branch Audits Review
  - Specific responsibility to review allocated branch returns (Para A6)
  - Separate responsibility to exercise judgement on visiting allocated branches (Para A6)
  - Agree among themselves for review of unassigned branches (Para A6)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 17: Disagreement trigger — Issues separate audit report
  - If condition met: Use Other Matter paragraph to cross-reference
  - If condition not met: Issue common audit report
- ⚠️ Para 18(a): Reviewing other's work — Not necessary
  - Entitled to assume compliance with ICAI SAs
- ⚠️ Para 15: Discovering relevant matters — Communication mandate
  - Must be in writing prior to audit completion
- ⚠️ Para A1: Indivisible work areas — Certain important areas
  - Remain undivided and must be covered by all joint auditors

## Logical Flow Chains
- Work Allocation Process (Paras 7-12)
  - Jointly establish overall strategy (Para 7)
    - leads to: Develop joint audit plan (Para 8)
      - leads to: Identify division of areas (Para 8a)
        - leads to: Sign work allocation document (Para 12)
          - leads to: Communicate to TCWG (Para 12)
- Audit Reporting Decision (Paras 17-20)
  - Discuss conclusions before finalizing report (Para 20)
    - if agreement: Issue common audit report (Para 17)
    - ⚠️ unless: Disagreement regarding opinion or matters (Para 17)
      - leads to: Issue separate audit report (Para 17)
        - requires: Reference other reports in OM paragraph (Para 17)

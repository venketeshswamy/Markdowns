# SA 505: External Confirmations

## Objective
- 🎯 Objective (Para 5)
  - Design and perform external confirmation procedures
  - Obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence

## Scope
- Applies to (Para 1)
  - Auditor's use of external confirmation procedures
  - Obtaining audit evidence per SA 330 and SA 500
- Does NOT apply to (Para 1)
  - Inquiries regarding litigation and claims
  - 🔗 See SA 501 for litigation inquiries
- Effective Date (Para 4)
  - Audits of financial statements for periods beginning on/after April 1, 2010

## Definitions
- 📌 External confirmation (Para 6a)
  - Audit evidence obtained as a direct written response to the auditor from a third party (the confirming party), in paper form, or by electronic or other medium.
- 📌 Positive confirmation request (Para 6b)
  - A request that the confirming party respond directly to the auditor indicating whether the confirming party agrees or disagrees with the information in the request, or providing the requested information.
- 📌 Negative confirmation request (Para 6c)
  - A request that the confirming party respond directly to the auditor only if the confirming party disagrees with the information provided in the request.
- 📌 Non-response (Para 6d)
  - A failure of the confirming party to respond, or fully respond, to a positive confirmation request, or a confirmation request returned undelivered.
- 📌 Exception (Para 6e)
  - A response that indicates a difference between information requested to be confirmed, or contained in the entity’s records, and information provided by the confirming party.

## Requirements
- External Confirmation Procedures (Para 7)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall maintain control over requests, including:
    - (a) Determining information to be confirmed (Ref: A1)
    - (b) Selecting appropriate confirming party (Ref: A2)
    - (c) Designing confirmation requests (Ref: A3-A6)
      - Ensure properly addressed
      - Ensure return information directs to auditor
    - (d) Sending requests and follow-ups (Ref: A7)
- Management’s Refusal (Para 8, 9)
  - 🎯 If management refuses permission to send:
    - (a) Inquire reasons and seek evidence of validity/reasonableness (Ref: A8)
    - (b) Evaluate implications on risk assessment and other procedures (Ref: A9)
      - Includes fraud risk
    - (c) Perform alternative audit procedures (Ref: A10)
  - 🎯 If refusal unreasonable OR unable to obtain evidence:
    - Communicate with TCWG (SA 260)
    - Determine implications for opinion (SA 705)
- Reliability of Responses (Para 10, 11)
  - 🎯 If doubts about reliability arise:
    - Obtain further audit evidence to resolve doubts (Ref: A11-A16)
  - 🎯 If response determined unreliable:
    - Evaluate implications on risk assessment
    - Modify related nature/timing/extent of procedures (Ref: A17)
- Non-Responses (Para 12)
  - 🎯 In case of non-response:
    - Perform alternative audit procedures to obtain evidence (Ref: A18-A19)
- Mandatory Positive Confirmations (Para 13)
  - 🎯 If positive confirmation determined necessary:
    - Alternative procedures will NOT provide sufficient evidence
  - 🎯 If such confirmation not obtained:
    - Determine implications for opinion (SA 705) (Ref: A20)
- Exceptions (Para 14)
  - 🎯 Investigate exceptions to determine if misstatements (Ref: A21-A22)
- Negative Confirmations (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Shall NOT use as sole substantive procedure UNLESS ALL 4 present:
    - (a) Risk of material misstatement is low AND controls tested
    - (b) Population is large number of small, homogeneous items
    - (c) Very low exception rate expected
    - (d) Auditor not aware of conditions causing disregard of requests
- Evaluating Evidence (Para 16)
  - 🎯 Evaluate if results provide relevant/reliable evidence
  - Determine if further procedures necessary (Ref: A24-A25)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 7 (Procedures)
  - Info to confirm (A1)
    - Account balances, terms of agreements, side agreements
  - Confirming party (A2)
    - Knowledgeable parties provide more reliable evidence
  - Designing requests (A3-A6)
    - Design affects response rate and reliability
    - Blank confirmation requests reduce risk of rote agreement (A5)
    - Validating addresses is part of proper addressing (A6)
  - Follow-up (A7)
    - Send additional requests if no reply within reasonable time
- Explains Para 8 (Refusal)
  - Reasonableness (A8)
    - Common reason: Legal dispute or ongoing negotiation
    - Risk: Management concealing fraud/error
  - Risk Implications (A9)
    - Unreasonable refusal may indicate fraud risk factor (SA 240)
  - Alternative Procedures (A10)
    - Similar to non-response procedures (A18-A19)
- Explains Para 10-11 (Reliability)
  - Reliability Factors (A11)
    - Risks: Interception, alteration, fraud
  - Electronic Responses (A12)
    - Risks: Proof of origin, authority, alteration
    - Mitigation: Encryption, digital signatures, website authenticity
  - Third Party Intermediaries (A13)
    - Risks: Integrity of transmission, authorization
  - Oral Responses (A15)
    - NOT an external confirmation
    - Request written confirmation; if fails, treat as non-response
  - Restrictive Language (A16)
    - Does not necessarily invalidate reliability
- Explains Para 12 (Non-Responses)
  - Alternative Procedures (A18)
    - Examining specific subsequent cash receipts
    - Shipping docs/sales near period-end
  - Risk Assessment (A19)
    - Non-response may indicate unidentified risk/fraud
- Explains Para 13 (Mandatory Positive)
  - Circumstances (A20)
    - Info only available outside entity
    - Fraud risks preventing reliance on internal evidence
- Explains Para 14 (Exceptions)
  - Indicative of Misstatements (A21)
    - May indicate fraud or control deficiencies
  - Non-Misstatements (A22)
    - Timing differences, measurement errors, clerical errors
- Explains Para 15 (Negative)
  - Persuasiveness (A23)
    - Less persuasive than positive
    - Failure to respond != verification of accuracy
    - More likely to respond if disagreement favors them
- Explains Para 16 (Evaluation)
  - Categorization (A24)
    - Agreement, Unreliable, Non-response, Exception
  - Conclusion (A25)
    - Sufficiency of evidence per SA 330

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Oral Responses (Para A15)
  - Status: Does NOT meet definition of external confirmation
  - Action: Must request written response
  - If no written response: Treat as non-response (Para 12 applies)
- ⚠️ Restrictive Language (Para A16)
  - Effect: Does NOT necessarily invalidate reliability
- ⚠️ Negative Confirmation Usage (Para 15)
  - Condition: Prohibited as sole procedure UNLESS ALL 4 conditions met
  - Impact: Failure to meet even one requires positive confirmation or other substantives
- ⚠️ "Blank" Confirmation Requests (Para A5)
  - Benefit: Reduces risk of respondent replying without verification
  - Drawback: May result in lower response rates due to effort required

## ICAI Listed Examples
- 📋 Factors in Designing Requests (Para A4)
  - Assertions being addressed
  - Specific identified risks (including fraud)
  - Layout and presentation
  - Prior experience on engagement
  - Method of communication (paper/electronic)
  - Management's authorization/encouragement
  - Ability of party to confirm (e.g., invoice vs balance)
- 📋 Reliability Doubts - Indicators (Para A11)
  - Received by auditor indirectly
  - Appeared not to come from intended party
- 📋 Risks with Third Party Intermediaries (Para A13)
  - Response not from proper source
  - Respondent not authorized
  - Integrity of transmission compromised
- 📋 Alternative Procedures - Receivables (Para A18)
  - Examining specific subsequent cash receipts
  - Examining shipping documentation
  - Examining sales near period-end
- 📋 Alternative Procedures - Payables (Para A18)
  - Examining subsequent cash disbursements
  - Correspondence from third parties
  - Other records (e.g., goods received notes)
- 📋 Circumstances Requiring Positive Confirmation (Para A20)
  - Info available only outside entity
  - Specific fraud risks (e.g., management override/collusion)
- 📋 Categorization of Results (Para A24)
  - Response indicating agreement
  - Response deemed unreliable
  - Non-response
  - Response indicating exception

## Logical Flow Chains
- Management Refusal Process (Para 8-9)
  - Auditor requests permission
  - If Refused leads to Inquire Reasons (8a)
    - AND Seek evidence of validity (8a)
    - AND Evaluate risk implications (8b)
    - AND Perform alternative procedures (8c)
      - If refusal unreasonable OR evidence unobtainable (Para 9)
        - leads to Communicate with TCWG
        - AND Modify Opinion (SA 705)
- Negative Confirmation Logic (Para 15)
  - Evaluate 4 conditions (Low risk, Large pop/small bal, Low exception rate, No disregard)
    - If ALL present leads to May use Negative Conf as sole procedure
    - If ANY missing leads to Must NOT use as sole procedure
- Oral Response Handling (Para A15)
  - Oral response received
    - leads to Request written confirmation
      - If received leads to Valid Audit Evidence
      - If NOT received leads to Treat as Non-Response (Para 12)

# SRE 2410: Review of Interim Financial Information Performed by the Independent Auditor of the Entity

## Objective
- To enable auditor to express a conclusion on IFI (Para 7)
  - Whether anything causes belief IFI is not per AFRF (Para 7)
  - Reduce to moderate level the risk of inappropriate conclusion (Para 7)

## Scope
- Applies to: Review of IFI by entity's independent auditor (Para 1)
  - Adapted for other historical financial info reviews (Para 3a)
- Does NOT apply to: Reviews by non-auditor practitioner (Para 3)
  - 🔗 See SA 2400 for non-auditor reviews (Para 3)
- Effective from: Periods beginning on or after April 1, 2010 (Para 65)

## Definitions
- 📌 Interim financial information (Para 2): Financial information that is prepared and presented in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework and comprises either a complete or a condensed set of financial statements for a period that is shorter than the entity's financial year.

## Requirements
- General Principles
  - 🎯 The auditor should comply with ethical requirements relevant to audit (Para 4)
  - 🎯 The auditor should implement quality control procedures for engagement (Para 5)
  - 🎯 The auditor should plan and perform with professional skepticism (Para 6)
- Agreeing Terms
  - 🎯 The auditor and client should agree on engagement terms (Para 10)
- Understanding Entity and Internal Control
  - 🎯 The auditor should have sufficient understanding of entity and environment (Para 12)
    - To identify types of potential material misstatements (Para 12a)
    - To select inquiries and analytical procedures (Para 12b)
  - 🎯 A recently appointed auditor should obtain understanding of entity (Para 17)
- Procedures and Inquiries
  - 🎯 The auditor should make inquiries and perform analytical procedures (Para 19)
  - 🎯 The auditor should obtain evidence IFI reconciles with accounting records (Para 25)
  - 🎯 The auditor should inquire about subsequent events requiring adjustment (Para 26)
  - 🎯 The auditor should inquire whether management changed going concern assessment (Para 27)
  - 🎯 The auditor should make additional inquiries if questioning material adjustment (Para 29)
- Evaluating Misstatements
  - 🎯 The auditor should evaluate uncorrected misstatements individually and aggregate (Para 30)
- Management Representations
  - 🎯 The auditor should obtain written representation from management (Para 34)
- Accompanying Information
  - 🎯 The auditor should read accompanying other information for material inconsistency (Para 36)
  - 🎯 The auditor should discuss material misstatement of fact with management (Para 37)
- Communication
  - 🎯 The auditor should communicate material adjustment matters to management (Para 38)
  - 🎯 The auditor should inform TCWG if management does not respond appropriately (Para 39)
  - 🎯 The auditor should consider withdrawing if TCWG does not respond appropriately (Para 40)
  - 🎯 The auditor should communicate fraud or noncompliance to management (Para 41)
  - 🎯 The auditor should communicate relevant governance matters to TCWG (Para 42)
- Reporting
  - 🎯 The auditor should issue a written report containing specific elements (Para 43)
  - 🎯 The auditor should express qualified or adverse conclusion for AFRF departure (Para 45)
  - 🎯 The auditor should communicate in writing if unable to complete review (Para 49)
  - 🎯 The auditor should add emphasis of matter for disclosed going concern uncertainty (Para 56)
  - 🎯 The auditor should express qualified or adverse conclusion if going concern undisclosed (Para 59)
  - 🎯 The auditor should consider modifying report for significant non-going-concern uncertainty (Para 60)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 The auditor should prepare sufficient appropriate review documentation (Para 64)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Non-Auditor Exception: If practitioner is not entity auditor — SA 2400 applies (Para 3)
- ⚠️ Corroboration Exception: Review ordinarily does not require corroborating litigation inquiries (Para 24)
- ⚠️ Legal Wording Exception: If law prescribes report wording — auditor responsibilities remain unchanged (Para 44)
- ⚠️ Pre-Acceptance Limitation: If management imposes scope limit upfront — do not accept engagement (Para 50)
- ⚠️ Post-Acceptance Limitation: If management limits scope and refuses removal — communicate and disclaim (Para 51)
- ⚠️ Prior Audit Modification: If prior audit limited — consider if scope limitation still exists (Para 54)
- ⚠️ Undisclosed Uncertainty: If going concern doubt inadequately disclosed — express qualified or adverse conclusion (Para 59)
- ⚠️ India Specific Modification: Do not access predecessor working papers — peruse audited statements instead (Material Modifications)
- ⚠️ India Specific Modification: Place of signature replaces practitioner jurisdiction location (Material Modifications)

## Logical Flow Chains
- Going Concern Assessment Process
  - 🎯 Inquire regarding going concern changes (Para 27)
  - Leads to: Awareness of events casting significant doubt (Para 27)
    - 🎯 Require inquiring management on future action plans (Para 27a)
    - 🎯 Require considering adequacy of disclosure (Para 27b)
  - Results in: Adequate disclosure present (Para 56)
    - 🎯 Add emphasis of matter paragraph to report (Para 56)
  - ⚠️ Unless: Material uncertainty inadequately disclosed (Para 59)
    - 🎯 Express qualified or adverse conclusion (Para 59)
- Uncorrected Misstatements Evaluation
  - 🎯 Evaluate misstatements individually and aggregate (Para 30)
  - Leads to: Assessing if material adjustment required (Para 31)
    - Consider nature, cause, amount, and potential effect (Para 32)
    - May designate amount below which not aggregated (Para 33)
- Resolving Suspicious Matters
  - Matter causes question on material adjustment (Para 29)
  - Leads to: 🎯 Make additional inquiries or procedures (Para 29)
  - Results in: Concluding if AFRF departure exists (Para 46)
    - If management does not correct departure (Para 46)
    - 🎯 Express qualified conclusion (Para 46)
    - ⚠️ Unless: Departure is material and pervasive (Para 47)
      - 🎯 Express adverse conclusion (Para 47)
- Handling Scope Limitations
  - Management imposes limitation after acceptance (Para 51)
  - Leads to: Auditor requests removal (Para 51)
    - If management refuses (Para 51)
    - 🎯 Communicate why review cannot be completed (Para 51)
    - Consider withdrawing or resigning from annual audit (Para 40)

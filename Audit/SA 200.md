# SA 200: Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor and the Conduct of an Audit in Accordance with Standards on Auditing

## Objective
- Obtain reasonable assurance and report on financial statements (Para 11)
  - Obtain reasonable assurance financial statements are free from material misstatement (Para 11a)
  - Report on financial statements and communicate as required by SAs (Para 11b)
- 🎯 Disclaim opinion or withdraw if reasonable assurance cannot be obtained (Para 12)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audits of financial statements in accordance with SAs (Para 1)
- To be adapted for audits of other historical financial information (Para 2)
- Effective from: April 1, 2010 (Para 10)

## Definitions
- 📌 Applicable financial reporting framework (Para 13a): Framework adopted by management for financial statements
  - 📌 Fair presentation framework (Para 13a): Requires compliance and acknowledges need for additional disclosures or departures
  - 📌 Compliance framework (Para 13a): Requires compliance without fair presentation acknowledgements
- 📌 Audit evidence (Para 13b): Information used for auditor's opinion
  - 📌 Sufficiency (Para 13b(i)): Measure of quantity of audit evidence
  - 📌 Appropriateness (Para 13b(ii)): Measure of quality - relevance and reliability
- 📌 Audit risk (Para 13c): Risk of inappropriate opinion when financial statements are materially misstated
- 📌 Auditor (Para 13d): Person or persons conducting the audit
- 📌 Detection risk (Para 13e): Risk auditor's procedures will not detect material misstatement
- 📌 Financial statements (Para 13f): Structured representation of historical financial information
- 📌 Historical financial information (Para 13g): Financial information about past economic events
- 📌 Management (Para 13h): Person(s) with executive responsibility for entity's operations
- 📌 Misstatement (Para 13i): Difference between reported amount and required amount per framework
- 📌 Premise (Para 13j): Management and TCWG responsibilities fundamental to audit conduct
- 📌 Professional judgment (Para 13k): Application of training and knowledge in audit decisions
- 📌 Professional skepticism (Para 13l): Attitude including questioning mind and critical assessment
- 📌 Reasonable assurance (Para 13m): High but not absolute level of assurance
- 📌 Risk of material misstatement (Para 13n): Risk financial statements are materially misstated prior to audit
  - 📌 Inherent risk (Para 13n(i)): Susceptibility to misstatement before considering controls
  - 📌 Control risk (Para 13n(ii)): Risk misstatement won't be prevented or detected by internal control
- 📌 Those charged with governance (Para 13o): Person(s) overseeing strategic direction and accountability

## Requirements
- Ethical Requirements
  - 🎯 Comply with relevant ethical requirements including independence (Para 14)
- Professional Skepticism
  - 🎯 Plan and perform audit with professional skepticism (Para 15)
- Professional Judgment
  - 🎯 Exercise professional judgment in planning and performing audit (Para 16)
- Sufficient Appropriate Audit Evidence and Audit Risk
  - 🎯 Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to reduce audit risk to acceptably low level (Para 17)
- Conduct of Audit in Accordance with SAs
  - Complying with SAs Relevant to Audit
    - 🎯 Comply with all SAs relevant to audit (Para 18)
    - 🎯 Understand entire text of SA including application material (Para 19)
    - 🎯 Do not represent compliance unless complied with all relevant SAs (Para 20)
  - Objectives Stated in Individual SAs
    - 🎯 Use objectives in planning and performing audit (Para 21)
      - Determine if additional procedures necessary (Para 21a)
      - Evaluate if sufficient appropriate evidence obtained (Para 21b)
  - Complying with Relevant Requirements
    - 🎯 Comply with each requirement unless SA not relevant or conditional requirement not met (Para 22)
    - 🎯 In exceptional circumstances, may depart from requirement but perform alternative procedures (Para 23)
  - Failure to Achieve an Objective
    - 🎯 Evaluate if failure to achieve objective prevents overall objectives achievement (Para 24)
    - 🎯 Document significant matters when objective not achieved (Para 24)

## Application Guidance
- An Audit of Financial Statements
  - Explains Para 3: Scope of Audit
    - Auditor's opinion does not assure future viability or management efficiency (A1)
    - Laws may require opinions on other matters like internal control (A1)
  - Explains Para 4: Preparation of Financial Statements
    - Management and TCWG responsibilities for preparation and presentation (A2)
    - Responsibilities include identifying framework and making judgments (A3)
    - Financial statements may be general or special purpose (A4)
    - Framework sources include standards, laws, interpretations (A5)
    - Framework determines form and content of financial statements (A6)
    - Frameworks may be fair presentation or compliance frameworks (A7)
    - Framework determines complete set of financial statements (A8)
    - SA 210 and SA 800 provide additional guidance (A9)
    - Auditor must obtain agreement on management responsibilities (A10)
    - Government entities may have broader mandates (A11)
  - Explains Para 8: Form of Auditor's Opinion
    - Opinion depends on applicable framework and laws (A12)
    - Different opinions for fair presentation vs compliance frameworks (A13)
- Ethical Requirements Relating to Audit
  - Explains Para 14: Ethical Requirements
    - Auditor subject to Code of Ethics issued by ICAI (A14)
    - Fundamental principles: integrity, objectivity, competence, confidentiality, behavior (A15)
    - Independence required comprising mind and appearance (A16)
    - SQC 1 and SA 220 set out firm and engagement partner responsibilities (A17)
- Professional Skepticism
  - Explains Para 15: Professional Skepticism
    - Includes alertness to contradictory evidence and possible fraud (A18)
    - Necessary to reduce risks of overlooking unusual circumstances (A19)
    - Critical assessment of audit evidence including reliability (A20)
    - May accept documents as genuine unless reason to believe otherwise (A21)
    - Past experience of honesty does not relieve need for skepticism (A22)
- Professional Judgment
  - Explains Para 16: Professional Judgment
    - Essential for interpreting requirements and making decisions (A23)
    - Based on training, knowledge and experience (A24)
    - Consultation assists in making reasonable judgments (A25)
    - Evaluated based on competent application of principles (A26)
    - Needs to be exercised throughout audit and documented (A27)
- Sufficient Appropriate Audit Evidence and Audit Risk
  - Explains Paras 5 and 17: Audit Evidence and Risk
    - Audit evidence cumulative from procedures and other sources (A28)
    - Sufficiency and appropriateness interrelated (A29-A30)
    - Professional judgment determines if sufficient appropriate evidence obtained (A31)
    - Audit risk function of risks of material misstatement and detection risk (A32)
    - Audit risk does not include business risks like litigation (A33)
    - Risks of material misstatement at financial statement and assertion levels (A34-A35)
    - Risk assessment approaches may use mathematical models (A36)
    - Inherent risk and control risk are entity's risks (A37)
    - Inherent risk higher for complex calculations or estimates (A38)
    - Control risk function of internal control effectiveness (A39)
    - Risks of material misstatement may be assessed separately or combined (A40)
    - SA 315 provides guidance on risk assessment (A41)
    - Detection risk inversely related to risks of material misstatement (A42)
    - Detection risk affected by nature, timing, extent of procedures (A43)
    - SA 300 and SA 330 provide guidance on planning and responses (A44)
    - Inherent limitations arise from nature of reporting, procedures, timeliness (A45)
    - Financial reporting involves management judgment and estimates (A46)
    - Practical limitations on obtaining audit evidence (A47)
    - Balance between benefit and cost in audit (A48)
    - Audit directed to areas with highest risks (A49)
    - SAs require risk assessment procedures and testing (A50)
    - Particular challenges for fraud, related parties, non-compliance, going concern (A51)
    - Subsequent discovery of misstatement does not necessarily indicate audit failure (A52)
    - Limitations relevant even when reporting frauds to government (A53)
- Conduct of Audit in Accordance with SAs
  - Explains Paras 18-24: Nature and Application of SAs
    - SAs provide standards for auditor's work (A54)
    - Effective dates and limitations specified in each SA (A55)
    - SAs do not override laws and regulations (A56)
    - SAs relevant to government entities with possible broader mandates (A57)
    - SAs contain objectives, requirements, application material (A58)
    - Application material explains requirements and provides guidance (A59)
    - Appendices form part of application material (A60)
    - Introductory material provides context (A61)
    - Definitions assist consistent application (A62)
    - Additional considerations for smaller entities and government entities (A63)
    - Smaller entity characteristics: concentration of ownership, straightforward transactions (A64)
    - Considerations developed for unlisted entities but may help listed entities (A65)
    - Owner-manager term used for smaller entities (A66)
    - Objectives link requirements to overall objectives (A67)
    - Objectives subject to inherent limitations (A68)
    - Interrelationships among SAs must be considered (A69)
    - Auditor determines additional procedures if needed (A70)
    - Evaluate evidence using objectives; extend work if insufficient (A71)
    - SA may not be relevant if circumstances don't exist (A72)
    - Conditional requirements apply when condition exists (A73)
    - SA 230 documents departures from requirements (A74)
    - Professional judgment determines if objective achieved (A75)
    - Documentation provides evidence of objective achievement (A76)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 12: Unless reasonable assurance obtained, disclaim opinion or withdraw
- ⚠️ Para 22: Requirement not applicable if SA not relevant or conditional requirement condition not met
- ⚠️ Para 23: In exceptional circumstances, may depart from requirement with alternative procedures
- ⚠️ Para A1: Auditor's opinion does not assure future viability or management efficiency
- ⚠️ Para A21: Auditor may accept documents as genuine unless reason to believe otherwise
- ⚠️ Para A22: Past honesty experience does not relieve need for professional skepticism
- ⚠️ Para A39: Internal control can reduce but not eliminate risks of material misstatement
- ⚠️ Para A44: Detection risk can be reduced but not eliminated
- ⚠️ Para A45: Auditor cannot obtain absolute assurance due to inherent limitations
- ⚠️ Para A48: Difficulty, time, or cost not valid basis to omit necessary audit procedure
- ⚠️ Para A52: Subsequent discovery of material misstatement does not necessarily indicate audit failure

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A8: Examples of single financial statements
  - 📋 Balance sheet
  - 📋 Statement of profit & loss
  - 📋 Statement of cash flows
  - 📋 Statement of operations by product lines
- Para A15: Fundamental principles of professional ethics
  - 📋 Integrity
  - 📋 Objectivity
  - 📋 Professional competence and due care
  - 📋 Confidentiality
  - 📋 Professional behavior
- Para A18: Examples requiring professional skepticism
  - 📋 Audit evidence that contradicts other audit evidence
  - 📋 Information questioning reliability of documents and inquiries
  - 📋 Conditions indicating possible fraud
  - 📋 Circumstances suggesting need for additional procedures
- Para A23: Examples requiring professional judgment
  - 📋 Materiality and audit risk
  - 📋 Nature, timing, and extent of audit procedures
  - 📋 Evaluating sufficiency of audit evidence
  - 📋 Evaluating management's judgments
  - 📋 Drawing conclusions based on audit evidence
- Para A38: Examples of higher inherent risk
  - 📋 Complex calculations
  - 📋 Accounts with significant estimation uncertainty
  - 📋 Technological developments making products obsolete
  - 📋 Lack of sufficient working capital
  - 📋 Declining industry with many business failures
- Para A47: Examples of practical limitations
  - 📋 Management may not provide complete information
  - 📋 Fraud involving sophisticated concealment schemes
  - 📋 Auditor not having legal powers like search
- Para A51: Examples of particularly significant inherent limitations
  - 📋 Fraud involving senior management or collusion
  - 📋 Existence and completeness of related party relationships
  - 📋 Non-compliance with laws and regulations
  - 📋 Future events affecting going concern
- Para A64: Characteristics of smaller entities
  - 📋 Concentration of ownership and management
  - 📋 Straightforward or uncomplicated transactions
  - 📋 Simple record-keeping
  - 📋 Few lines of business and products
  - 📋 Few internal controls
  - 📋 Few levels of management
  - 📋 Few personnel with wide range of duties

## Logical Flow Chains
- Audit Process Flow
  - Understand management responsibilities and obtain agreement (Para 4, A10)
  - Plan audit with professional skepticism and judgment (Paras 15-16)
  - Assess risks of material misstatement (Para 7)
  - Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 17)
  - Evaluate evidence against objectives (Para 21)
  - Form opinion based on conclusions (Para 7)
  - Report on financial statements (Para 11)
- Risk Assessment Flow
  - Identify risks at financial statement and assertion levels (A34)
  - Assess inherent risk and control risk (A37-A40)
  - Determine detection risk level (A42)
  - Design further audit procedures responsive to risks (A43)
- Evidence Evaluation Flow
  - Obtain audit evidence from various procedures (A28)
  - Assess sufficiency (quantity) and appropriateness (quality) (A29-A30)
  - Use objectives to evaluate if evidence sufficient (Para 21b)
  - If insufficient: extend work or perform additional procedures (A71)

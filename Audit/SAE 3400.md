# SAE 3400: The Examination of Prospective Financial Information

## Objective
- Establish standards on prospective financial information (Para 1)
  - Examine best-estimate and hypothetical assumptions (Para 1)
- Provide moderate level of assurance (Para 9)
  - Due to speculative nature of future events (Para 8)

## Scope
- Applies to: Examination of prospective financial information (Para 1)
- Does NOT apply to: General or narrative terms (Para 1)
- Effective from: April 1, 2007 (Para 35)

## Definitions
- 📌 Prospective financial information (Para 3): financial information based on assumptions about events that may occur in the future and possible actions by an entity.
- 📌 Forecast (Para 4): prospective financial information prepared on the basis of assumptions as to future events which management expects to take place and the actions management expects to take as of the date the information is prepared (best-estimate assumptions).
- 📌 Projection (Para 5): prospective financial information prepared on the basis of hypothetical assumptions about future events and management actions which are not necessarily expected to take place, or a mixture of best-estimate and hypothetical assumptions.

## Requirements
- Evidence and Preparation
  - 🎯 Auditor should obtain sufficient appropriate evidence (Para 2)
    - Best-estimate assumptions are not unreasonable (Para 2)
    - Hypothetical assumptions are consistent with purpose (Para 2)
    - Information is properly prepared on assumptions (Para 2)
    - Proper presentation and adequate disclosure (Para 2)
    - Consistent basis with historical financial statements (Para 2)
- Engagement Acceptance
  - 🎯 Auditor should not accept or should withdraw (Para 11)
    - ⚠️ When assumptions are clearly unrealistic (Para 11)
    - ⚠️ When information inappropriate for intended use (Para 11)
  - 🎯 Auditor and client should agree on terms (Para 12)
    - 🔗 See SA 210 for engagement letters (Para 12)
- Knowledge of Business
  - 🎯 Auditor should obtain sufficient knowledge level (Para 13)
    - To evaluate if significant assumptions identified (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Auditor should consider extent of historical reliance (Para 14)
    - To assess consistent basis preparation (Para 14)
- Period Covered
  - 🎯 Auditor should consider period of time covered (Para 16)
    - Must not extend beyond reasonable basis timeframe (Para 16)
- Examination Procedures
  - 🎯 Auditor should consider matters for procedure extent (Para 17)
- Written Representations
  - 🎯 Auditor should obtain written representations from management (Para 25)
    - Regarding intended use of information (Para 25)
    - Completeness of significant management assumptions (Para 25)
    - Management acceptance of its responsibility (Para 25)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 Auditor should document important evidential matters (Para 27)
    - Including sources, assumptions, and audit plan (Para 27)
- Reporting
  - 🎯 Report should contain specific formal elements (Para 28)
  - 🎯 Auditor should express qualified or adverse opinion (Para 32)
    - ⚠️ When presentation and disclosure is not adequate (Para 32)
  - 🎯 Auditor should express adverse opinion or withdraw (Para 33)
    - ⚠️ When significant assumptions lack reasonable basis (Para 33)
  - 🎯 Auditor should withdraw or disclaim opinion (Para 34)
    - ⚠️ When examination affected by scope limitations (Para 34)

## Application Guidance
- Explains General Nature
  - Information illustrates possible consequences of actions (Para 5)
  - Can include financial statements or elements thereof (Para 6)
  - Management is responsible for preparation and presentation (Para 7)
  - Difficult to obtain positive opinion level satisfaction (Para 9)
- Explains Business Knowledge
  - Auditor needs to establish if historical info audited (Para 14)
  - ⚠️ Adjust approach if prior report modified (Para 15)
  - ⚠️ Adjust approach if entity in start-up phase (Para 15)
- Explains Examination Procedures
  - Assess source and reliability of internal and external evidence (Para 18)
  - Ensure hypothetical assumptions cover all implications (Para 19)
  - Satisfy that hypothetical assumptions are consistent and realistic (Para 20)
  - Verify mathematical accuracy via recomputation checks (Para 21)
  - Focus on areas highly sensitive to material variation (Para 22)
  - Consider interrelationship of components in statements (Para 23)
  - Apply procedures to elapsed historical portion (Para 24)
- Explains Presentation and Disclosure
  - Ensure presentation is informative and not misleading (Para 26)
  - Ensure accounting policies clearly disclosed in notes (Para 26)
  - Indicate clearly if assumptions are best-estimates or hypothetical (Para 26)
- Explains Reporting Outcomes
  - State negative assurance regarding reasonable basis (Para 29)
  - Express opinion on proper preparation and framework (Para 29)
  - Insert caveat that actual results will likely differ (Para 29)
  - Restrict use of projection to intended purpose (Para 29)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 11: Unrealistic Assumptions — Should not accept or should withdraw from engagement
- ⚠️ Para 15: Prior report modified or Start-up phase — Auditor must consider facts and effect on examination
- ⚠️ Para 32: Inadequate presentation or disclosure — Auditor should express qualified or adverse opinion or withdraw
- ⚠️ Para 33: Assumptions lack reasonable basis — Auditor should express adverse opinion or withdraw
- ⚠️ Para 34: Necessary procedures precluded — Auditor should withdraw or disclaim opinion

## Logical Flow Chains
- Engagement Acceptance Process
  - Evaluate intended use and assumptions (Para 10)
    - leads to: Assessment of realism (Para 11)
      - if clearly unrealistic: ⚠️ Withdraw or do not accept (Para 11)
      - if acceptable: Agree on terms via engagement letter (Para 12)
- Reporting Modification Process
  - Evaluate presentation and evidence (Para 32)
    - if presentation inadequate: 🎯 Express qualified or adverse opinion (Para 32)
    - if assumptions lack basis: 🎯 Express adverse opinion (Para 33)
    - if scope is limited: 🎯 Disclaim opinion or withdraw (Para 34)

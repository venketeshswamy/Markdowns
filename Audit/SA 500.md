# SA 500: Audit Evidence

## Objective
- 🎯 To design and perform audit procedures (Para 4)
  - to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 4)
  - to draw reasonable conclusions for opinion (Para 4)

## Scope
- Explains what constitutes audit evidence (Para 1)
- Deals with auditor responsibility to design procedures (Para 1)
- Applies to all audit evidence obtained (Para 2)
- Effective from April 1, 2009 (Para 3)

## Definitions
- 📌 Accounting records (Para 5a)
  - The records of initial accounting entries and supporting records, such as checks and records of electronic fund transfers; invoices; contracts; the general and subsidiary ledgers, journal entries and other adjustments to the financial statements that are not reflected in journal entries; and records such as work sheets and spreadsheets supporting cost allocations, computations, reconciliations and disclosures.
- 📌 Appropriateness (of audit evidence) (Para 5b)
  - The measure of the quality of audit evidence; that is, its relevance and its reliability in providing support for the conclusions on which the auditor’s opinion is based.
- 📌 Audit evidence (Para 5c)
  - Information used by the auditor in arriving at the conclusions on which the auditor’s opinion is based. Audit evidence includes both information contained in the accounting records underlying the financial statements and information obtained from other sources.
- 📌 Management’s expert (Para 5d)
  - An individual or organisation possessing expertise in a field other than accounting or auditing, whose work in that field is used by the entity to assist the entity in preparing the financial statements.
- 📌 Sufficiency (of audit evidence) (Para 5e)
  - The measure of the quantity of audit evidence. The quantity of the audit evidence needed is affected by the auditor’s assessment of the risks of material misstatement and also by the quality of such audit evidence.

## Requirements
- Sufficient Appropriate Audit Evidence
  - 🎯 The auditor shall design and perform appropriate procedures (Para 6)
  - 🎯 to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 6)
- Information Used as Evidence
  - 🎯 The auditor shall consider relevance and reliability (Para 7)
- Work of Management's Expert
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate expert work to necessary extent (Para 8)
    - 🎯 Evaluate competence, capabilities and objectivity (Para 8a)
    - 🎯 Obtain an understanding of that expert's work (Para 8b)
    - 🎯 Evaluate appropriateness of work for assertion (Para 8c)
- Information Produced by Entity
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate if sufficiently reliable (Para 9)
    - 🎯 Obtaining evidence about accuracy and completeness (Para 9a)
    - 🎯 Evaluating if sufficiently precise and detailed (Para 9b)
- Selecting Items for Testing
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine effective selection means (Para 10)
- Inconsistency or Doubts
  - ⚠️ If evidence is inconsistent or reliability is doubted: (Para 11)
    - 🎯 The auditor shall determine necessary modifications (Para 11)
    - 🎯 and shall consider effect on other aspects (Para 11)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Sufficient Appropriate Evidence
  - Evidence is cumulative and primarily from audit procedures (A1)
  - ⚠️ Inquiry alone ordinarily does not provide sufficient evidence (A2)
  - Sufficiency and appropriateness are highly interrelated (A4)
  - ⚠️ Obtaining more evidence may not compensate poor quality (A4)
  - Professional judgment determines evidence sufficiency (A6)
- Explains Sources and Procedures
  - Corroborating independent information increases assurance (A8)
  - Risk assessment and further procedures provide evidence (A10)
  - ⚠️ Electronic info may require timely audit procedures (A13)
  - Inspection provides varying reliability degrees (A14)
  - Observation is limited to the exact time observed (A17)
  - External confirmations address assertions and absent conditions (A18)
  - Recalculation checks mathematical accuracy (A19)
  - Reperformance is independent execution of controls (A20)
  - Analytical procedures study plausible relationships (A21)
  - Evaluating responses to inquiry is integral (A22)
  - ⚠️ Corroboration of management intent inquiry is vital (A24)
- Explains Relevance and Reliability
  - Relevance is logical connection to audit procedure purpose (A27)
  - Direction of testing affects relevance (A27)
  - Reliability is influenced by source and nature (A31)
  - Reliability increases if from independent external sources (A31)
  - Reliability increases if related internal controls are effective (A31)
  - Direct evidence is more reliable than indirect (A31)
  - Documentary evidence is more reliable than oral (A31)
  - Original documents are more reliable than photocopies (A31)
- Explains Work of Management's Expert
  - Failure to use necessary experts increases misstatement risks (A34)
  - Competence is nature and level of expertise (A37)
  - Capability is ability to exercise competence (A37)
  - Objectivity relates to bias or conflict effects (A37)
  - ⚠️ Employee experts face inherent objectivity threats (A42)
  - Understanding includes expert's field and methods (A44)
  - Agreement with expert clarifies roles and scope (A46)
  - Evaluation includes findings, assumptions, and source data (A48)
- Explains Information Produced by Entity
  - Needs to be sufficiently complete and accurate (A49)
  - ⚠️ Evidence may be obtained concurrently with procedure (A50)
  - Must be precise enough for auditor's analytical purposes (A51)
- Explains Selecting Items
  - Means depend on circumstances and efficiency needs (A52)
  - 100% examination is common for tests of details (A53)
  - ⚠️ Selective examination does not constitute audit sampling (A55)
- Explains Inconsistency
  - Inconsistency indicates specific evidence may lack reliability (A57)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para A2: Inquiry alone — does not provide sufficient evidence
  - requires corroboration and additional procedures
- ⚠️ Para A4: Obtaining more evidence — may not compensate poor quality
  - quality dictates the volume needed
- ⚠️ Para A13: Electronic information — may not be retrievable later
  - auditor must request retention or perform timely tests
- ⚠️ Para A17: Observation — limited to point in time observed
  - act of observation may affect how process is performed
- ⚠️ Para A31: External sources — reliability subject to important exceptions
  - unreliable if source lacks knowledge or objectivity
- ⚠️ Para A42: Expert employed by entity — objectivity threat always present
  - employed expert is inherently less objective than engaged expert
- ⚠️ Para A55: Selective examination — does not constitute audit sampling
  - cannot project test results to entire population
- ⚠️ Para 11: Inconsistent evidence — triggers need for modified procedures
  - requires resolving doubts and assessing impact on audit

  - 📋 Selecting all items (100% examination)

  - 📋 Audit sampling

  - 📋 Population constitutes small number of large value items

  - 📋 High value or key items
  - 📋 All items over a certain amount
  - 📋 Items to obtain information

## Logical Flow Chains
- Evidence Evaluation Process (Para 7)
  - Auditor considers relevance and reliability (Para 7)
  - leads to using management expert work (Para 8)
    - requires evaluating competence and objectivity (Para 8a)
    - requires obtaining understanding of work (Para 8b)
    - requires evaluating appropriateness as evidence (Para 8c)
  - leads to using entity produced information (Para 9)
    - requires evaluating accuracy and completeness (Para 9a)
    - requires evaluating precision and detail (Para 9b)
  - leads to designing test selection means (Para 10)
  - ⚠️ if evidence is inconsistent or unreliable (Para 11)
    - requires modifying or adding audit procedures (Para 11)

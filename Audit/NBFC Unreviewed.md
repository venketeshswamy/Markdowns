# Audit of NBFCs (Ch. 14 – Unit 2)

## 1. Definition & Principal Business Criteria
### Sec 45-I(f) RBI (Amendment) Act, 1997
- Financial institution which is a company
- Non-banking institution (company) — principal business: receiving deposits / lending
- Such other institution as RBI may, with Central Govt approval, notify
### 📌 Principal Business Test (both must be satisfied)
- 🔢 Financial assets > 50% of total assets (net of intangibles)
- 🔢 Income from financial assets > 50% of gross income
- ⚠️ Exception: if only asset test met but NOT income test → NOT an NBFC

## 2. Registration & Regulation
### Sec 45-IA RBI Act 1934 — Mandatory conditions
- Certificate of Registration (CoR) from RBI
- 🔢 Net Owned Fund (NOF) — minimum thresholds
  - General (new applicants): ₹10 crore
  - 📌 Proviso: Previously ₹2 crore; raised for new registrations
  - ❌ Exception: NBFC-P2P, NBFC-AA, NBFCs with no public funds & no customer interface → NOF = ₹2 crore
  - ❌ Exception: NBFC-IFC & IDF-NBFC → NOF = ₹300 crore
  - 🕐 Glide path for existing NBFCs (NBFC-ICC, MFI, Factor)
    - NBFC-ICC: ₹2Cr → ₹5Cr (Mar 2025) → ₹10Cr (Mar 2027)
    - NBFC-MFI: ₹5Cr → ₹7Cr (Mar 2025) → ₹10Cr (Mar 2027)
    - ⚠️ Exception NE Region MFI: ₹2Cr → ₹5Cr (Mar 2025) → ₹10Cr (Mar 2027)
    - NBFC-Factor: ₹5Cr → ₹7Cr (Mar 2025) → ₹10Cr (Mar 2027)
### ❌ Exempted from RBI Registration (regulated by other regulators)
- Housing Finance Institutions (NHB)
- Merchant Banking Cos (SEBI)
- Stock Exchanges (SEBI)
- Stock-broking/sub-broking Cos (SEBI)
- Venture Capital Fund Cos (SEBI)
- Nidhi Companies (MCA)
- Insurance Companies (IRDAI)
- Chit Companies (Chit Funds Act 1982)
- Specified Micro Finance Companies
- Securitisation & Reconstruction Companies
- Mutual Benefit Companies
- Core Investment Companies (CICs)
  - ✅ Inclusion: CIC asset size ≥ ₹100 crore AND accessing public funds → must register
  - ❌ Exclusion: CIC asset size < ₹100 crore → exempt
  - ❌ Exclusion: CIC ≥ ₹100 crore but NOT accessing public funds → exempt
- Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) Companies
### RBI Directions Issued to NBFCs
- Public deposits acceptance
- Prudential norms (capital adequacy, income recognition, asset classification, provisioning)
- Risk exposure norms
- Fair Practices Code (non-coercive recovery, adequate loan disclosures)
- Auditor reporting directions

## 3. Types of NBFCs
### By Deposit Acceptance
- NBFC-D: Deposit-taking
- NBFC-ND: Non-deposit taking (suffix ND)
### By Activity
- ICC: Investment & Credit Company
- IFC: Infrastructure Finance Company
- CIC: Core Investment Company
- IDF-NBFC: Infrastructure Debt Fund
- NBFC-MFI: Micro Finance Institution
- NBFC-Factors
- NOFHC: Non-Operative Financial Holding Company
- NBFC-P2P: Peer to Peer Lending Platform
- NBFC-AA: Account Aggregator
- NBFC-MGC: Mortgage Guarantee Company
- Asset Finance Co., Investment Co., Loan Co.

## 4. Scale-Based Regulation (SBR) — effective 01 Oct 2022
### Four Layers
- **NBFC-BL (Base Layer)**
  - Non-deposit taking NBFCs with asset size < ₹1000 crore
  - NBFC-P2P, NBFC-AA, NOFHC
  - NBFCs with no public funds and no customer interface
  - ⚠️ Exception: Govt-owned NBFCs → placed in BL or ML only (NOT UL till further notice)
  - ❌ NBFC-BL NOT eligible to include perpetual debt in Tier 1 or Tier 2 capital
- **NBFC-ML (Middle Layer)**
  - All NBFC-Ds (irrespective of asset size)
  - Non-deposit NBFCs with asset size ≥ ₹1000 crore
  - SPDs, IDF-NBFCs (always ML), CICs, HFCs, NBFC-IFCs
  - 📌 NBFC-D, CIC, IFC, HFC → ML or UL only (NOT BL)
- **NBFC-UL (Upper Layer)**
  - Specifically identified by RBI via parameters & scoring
  - 🔢 Top 10 NBFCs by asset size → always UL
  - Enhanced regulatory requirements apply
- **NBFC-TL (Top Layer)**
  - Ideally remains empty
  - Populated only if RBI sees substantial systemic risk from specific UL NBFCs
### Layer Assignment Rules
- NBFC-ICC, NBFC-MFI, NBFC-Factor, NBFC-MGC → any layer (depends on parameters)
- SPD, IDF-NBFC → always ML
- NBFC-P2P, NBFC-AA, NOFHC, NBFCs without public funds & customer interface → always BL
### Reference Mapping (from Oct 2022)
- NBFC-ND → NBFC-BL
- NBFC-D & NBFC-ND-SI → NBFC-ML or NBFC-UL
- ⚠️ Exception: Existing NBFC-ND-SI with assets ₹500Cr–<₹1000Cr (not mandatorily ML) → NBFC-BL

## 5. Banks vs NBFCs
- ❌ NBFC cannot accept demand deposits (some can accept term deposits)
- ❌ Not part of payment & settlement system; cannot issue cheques on itself
- ❌ DICGC deposit insurance NOT available to NBFC depositors
- ❌ No minimum Priority Sector lending requirement

## 6. Prudential Norms
### 6.1 Capital Requirements
#### CRAR — Minimum Capital Ratio
- 🔢 Minimum CRAR: 15% of aggregate risk-weighted assets (on+off balance sheet)
- 🔢 Tier 1 minimum: 10% (except NBFC-MFI and gold-lending NBFCs)
- 🔢 Gold-lending NBFC (gold loans ≥ 50% of financial assets) → min Tier 1: 12%
#### Tier 1 Capital (except NBFC-BL)
- Owned fund reduced by excess group/subsidiary investments > 10% of owned fund
- Perpetual debt instruments (non-deposit taking NBFCs) ≤ 15% of aggregate Tier 1 (prev yr Mar 31)
- ❌ NBFC-BL: perpetual debt NOT eligible in Tier 1
#### Tier 2 Capital (except NBFC-BL)
- Preference shares (non-compulsorily convertible)
- Revaluation reserves @ 55% discount
- General provisions & loss reserves ≤ 1.25% of risk-weighted assets
- Hybrid debt capital instruments
- Subordinated debt
- Perpetual debt in excess of Tier 1 eligibility
- 📌 Proviso: Aggregate Tier 2 must not exceed Tier 1
- ❌ NBFC-BL: perpetual debt NOT eligible in Tier 2
#### Risk Weights (On-Balance Sheet)

##### 0% (The "Safe" Zone)
- Cash & bank balances (incl. FDs with banks)
- Approved securities
- Loans fully secured against deposits held
- Loans to staff
- Income tax deducted at source (net)
- Advance tax paid (net)
- Interest due on Govt securities
- Central Govt fund-based claims
- Central Govt guaranteed claims
- State Govt securities / direct exposure

##### 20% (High-Grade Institutions)
- Bonds of public sector banks
- State Govt guaranteed claims (not in default / default ≤ 90 days)

##### 50% (Stable Infrastructure)
- PPP/post-COD infra projects (>1 yr commercial ops)

##### 100% (Standard Commercial Risk)
- FDs/bonds of public financial institutions
- Shares, debentures, CPs, MF units
- Stock on hire (NBV)
- Intercorporate loans/deposits
- Other secured loans (good)
- Bills purchased/discounted
- Fixed assets (leased out, premises, furniture)
- State Govt guaranteed claims (default > 90 days)

##### 125% (High-Risk Retail)
- Consumer credit (retail loans excl. housing, education, vehicle, gold, microfinance/SHG)
- Credit Card Receivables

### 6.2 Income Recognition
- Based on recognised accounting principles
- NPA income (interest/discount/hire charges/lease rentals) → recognised only on actual realisation
- Income recognised before NPA status and remaining unrealised → must be reversed
#### Asset Classification (applies to all NBFCs except NBFC-MFIs)
- **Standard Asset**: no default perceived, no abnormal risk
- **Sub-Standard Asset**
  - NPA for ≤18 months (NBFC-BL)
  - NPA for ≤12 months (NBFC-ML and above)
  - Renegotiated/rescheduled/restructured account → sub-standard till 1 yr satisfactory performance
  - 📌 Proviso: Infrastructure loans → classified per Para 17 of Master Direction 2023
  - ⚠️ Class NOT upgraded merely due to rescheduling
- **Doubtful Asset**
  - Sub-standard for >18 months (NBFC-BL)
  - Sub-standard for >12 months (NBFC-ML and above)
  - Applies to: term loan, lease asset, hire purchase asset, any other asset
- **Loss Asset**
  - Identified by NBFC / internal or external auditor / RBI inspection, not yet written off
  - Asset with non-recoverability threat: security erosion / security unavailability / borrower fraud
- **NPA Definition** (>180 days overdue rule — glide path applies)
  - Interest overdue >180 days
  - Term loan instalment overdue >180 days
  - Demand/call loan overdue >180 days from date of demand/call
  - Bill overdue >180 days
  - Short-term loan/advance income on receivables overdue >180 days
  - Dues on asset sale/services/expense reimbursement overdue >180 days
  - Lease rental/hire purchase instalment overdue >180 days
  - Any credit facility of same borrower where one facility becomes NPA → entire exposure NPA
  - 📌 Proviso: Lease & hire purchase → classify each account on its own recovery record
#### 🕐 NPA Glide Path (toward 90-day norm)
- >150 days overdue: By 31 Mar 2024
- >120 days overdue: By 31 Mar 2025
- >90 days overdue: By 31 Mar 2026
- ❌ Glide path NOT applicable to NBFCs already on 90-day norm

### 6.3 Provisioning Requirements (except NBFC-MFIs)
- **Loss Assets**: 100% write-off; if retained → 100% provision
- **Doubtful Assets**
  - Unsecured portion: 100%
  - Secured portion (additional provision by age):
    - Up to 1 year doubtful: 20%
    - 1 to 3 years doubtful: 30%
    - >3 years doubtful: 50%
- **Sub-Standard Assets**: 10% of total outstanding
- **Standard Assets — NBFC-BL** (except ML and above): 0.25% of outstanding
  - Not netted from gross advances
  - Shown as 'Contingent Provisions against Standard Assets' in balance sheet
- **Standard Assets — NBFC-ML** (not applicable for NBFC-UL): 0.40% of outstanding
  - Not netted from gross advances
  - Shown as 'Contingent Provisions against Standard Assets' in balance sheet

## 7. Audit Procedures
### Step 1: Ascertain Principal Business
- Review MoA & AoA
- Scan Board/Committee minutes
- Discuss with top management (corporate strategy/plan)
- Corroborate with actual financial results
- 🔢 Determine if financial asset >50% and financial income >50% thresholds met
### Step 2: Evaluate Internal Control System
- Assess adequacy of accounting system and internal controls
- Review recovery system effectiveness
- Verify periodic review of advances in place
- ⚠️ Absence of review system → high NPA risk
### Step 3: Registration with RBI
- Obtain copy of CoR (or application if pending)
- Verify: dual condition — CoR + minimum NOF compliance
- NBFCs incorporated after 9 Jan 1997 → must have CoR before commencing business
- NBFC holding public deposits → verify liquid asset investment (% as specified by RBI)
- Verify quarterly returns on liquid assets filed timely with RBI
### Step 4: Public Deposit Directions (Non-Banking Financial Companies Acceptance of Public Deposits (Reserve Bank) Directions, 2016)
- Verify classification of NBFC
- Credit rating ceiling compliance for public deposits
  - **On downgrading below minimum investment grade** (ICC/Factor):
    - Stop accepting fresh deposits & renewing existing deposits immediately
    - Existing deposits run off to maturity
    - 🕐 Report position to RBI Regional Office within 15 working days
    - 📌 Proviso: matured deposit NOT renewed without depositor's express & voluntary consent
- Test-check interest calculations (no excess payment)
- Test-check brokerage/commission/incentive payments (within specified limits)
- Written application from depositor required (per Advertisement Rules 1977)
- Verify deposit register; check timely payment of deposits; note delays/defaults
- Liquid asset investments lodged in safe custody with designated scheduled commercial bank
- Verify prescribed returns filed timely with RBI
- NBFC not accepting deposits → verify Board resolution on non-acceptance
- Group Holding Investment Companies → verify Board resolution on investment & non-acceptance of public deposits
### Step 5: Prudential Norms Compliance
- Check income recognition, asset classification, provisioning
- Verify NPA income NOT taken to P&L on accrual basis
- NPA accounts from prior year still treated as NPA (unless regularised per Directions)
- Board policy for demand/call loans in place
- NBFC not exceeded concentration norms (single borrower / group)
- Verify capital adequacy ratio (CRAR) correctly computed

## 8. Audit Checklist (NBFC-ICC — Illustrative)
- Physically verify shares & securities; certificate from custodian bank/institution
- No loans against security of NBFC's own shares
- Dividend income: recognised on cash basis (default); accrual basis option available if AGM declared
- ⚠️ Exception: Bonds/debentures of corporates → accrual basis only if interest predetermined & not in arrears
- Test-check broker bills vs stock market quotations on trade dates
- Board minutes for investment purchases/sales; current vs long-term classification
- Investments valued per NBFC Prudential Norms; provision for market value fall
- Subsidiary/group company investments — verify price & valuation (prudential norms basis)
- Unquoted debentures/bonds → treat as term loans/credit facilities (NOT investments) for income recognition & asset classification
- AS 13 (or applicable Ind AS) compliance to extent not inconsistent with Directions
- Depository confirmation for securities held in demat
- Securities lending: same type/class returned + all corporate benefits (dividend, rights, bonus, interest)
- Charges on securities lent/borrowed verified
- Approved intermediary confirmation on year-end securities
- Every loan sanctioned properly; conditions verified (limit, security, interest, repayment terms)
- Security & agreements for advances verified; net worth of borrower/guarantor assessed
- Balance confirmations from counterparties obtained
- Bill discounting: proper records for every bill; discounting charges accounted for
- Concentration limits — NBFC not lent/invested in excess per Prudential Norms
- Loan appraisal & follow-up system adequate; NPA trend analysed
- Classification of loans into Standard/Sub-Standard/Doubtful/Loss; adequacy of provision

## 9. Classification of Frauds
### Categories (based on IPC)
- Misappropriation & criminal breach of trust
- Fraudulent encashment (forged instruments, fictitious accounts, book manipulation, property conversion)
- Unauthorised credit facilities for reward/gratification
- Negligence & cash shortages
- Cheating & forgery
- Irregularities in foreign exchange transactions
- Any other fraud not in above heads
### 📌 Mandatory reporting as fraud (even without proven fraudulent intent)
- 🔢 Cash shortage > ₹10,000
- 🔢 Cash shortage > ₹5,000 if detected by management/auditor/inspecting officer and NOT self-reported by cashier
### Overseas Branches
- All frauds at overseas branches/offices → reported to RBI per prescribed format (Master Direction — Monitoring of Frauds in NBFCs (RBI) Directions, 2016)

## 10. Auditor's Duty
### 10.1 Additional Report to Board of Directors (RBI Directions 2016)
#### Applicable to ALL NBFCs (Para 3A)
- I. Whether CoR obtained from RBI (if company meets Principal Business Criteria)
- II. Whether company with existing CoR continues to satisfy Principal Business Criteria as at 31 Mar
- III. Whether NOF meets requirements per Master Direction (Scale Based Regulation) Directions 2023 & Deposit-taking Directions 2016
- 📌 Statutory Auditor Certificate (SAC) to be submitted to Regional Office (DNBS) within 5 working days of signing audit report; NOT later than 31 December of same year
#### Additional Matters — NBFCs Accepting/Holding Public Deposits (Para 3B)
- (i) Public deposits + specific borrowings within admissible limits per Directions 2016
- (ii) Excess deposits regularised per Directions 2016
- (iii) Whether accepting deposits without minimum investment grade credit rating
- (iv) CRAR correctly determined; compliant with minimum CRAR
- (v) Credit rating in force for each fixed deposit scheme; aggregate deposits not exceeding rating agency limit
- (vi) No violation of restriction on public deposit acceptance
- (vii) No default in payment of interest/principal to depositors
- (viii) Compliance with prudential norms (income recognition, accounting standards, asset classification, provisioning, concentration norms) — Master Direction 2023
- (ix) Liquid assets requirement complied with (Sec 45-IB); designated bank communicated to RBI (NBS-3)
- (x) Return on deposits (DNBS-01) filed within stipulated period — Master Direction (Filing of Supervisory Returns) Directions 2024
- (xi) Quarterly return on prudential norms filed within stipulated period
- (xii) New branch/closure/agent appointment — compliance with Directions 2016
#### Additional Matters — NBFCs NOT Accepting Public Deposits (Para 3C)
- (i) Board resolution for non-acceptance of public deposits passed
- (ii) No public deposits accepted during the year
- (iii) Prudential norms compliance (income recognition, accounting standards, asset classification, provisioning) — Master Direction 2023
- (iv)(a) CRAR in NBS-7 correctly determined; meets minimum CRAR
- (iv)(b) NBS-7 (annual capital funds & risk asset statement) filed within stipulated period
- (v) Whether correctly classified as NBFC-MFI per Master Direction 2023
#### NBFCs Not Required to Hold CoR (Para 3D)
- ⚠️ Apart from Para 3A(I), auditor states company is complying with RBI-stipulated conditions
#### Qualified/Unfavourable Statements (Para 4)
- Auditor must state reasons for any qualified or unfavourable statement
- If unable to express opinion → state fact + reasons
### 10.2 Exception Report to RBI (Para 5)
#### Trigger Conditions (any one)
- Statement in Para 3 is unfavourable or qualified, OR
- Non-compliance with Chapter IIIB of RBI Act 1934, OR
- Non-compliance with Public Deposits Directions 2016, OR
- Non-compliance with Master Direction (Scale Based Regulation) Directions 2023
#### Obligation
- Submit exception report to concerned Regional Office, DNBS, RBI (jurisdiction of registered office)
- Report contains ONLY contraventions — NOT compliance statements
### 10.3 Compliance with CARO 2020
- Para 3(iii): Loans, guarantees, security — terms not prejudicial; repayment schedule; >90 days overdue amount; demand loans/loans without terms (amount, % of total, promoter/related party portion)
- Para 3(xvi)(a): Registration required under Sec 45-IA; whether obtained
- Para 3(xvi)(b): Non-Banking Financial / Housing Finance activities without valid CoR
- Para 3(xvi)(c): CIC — continues to fulfil CIC criteria; exempted/unregistered CIC criteria compliance
- Para 3(xvi)(d): Group having >1 CIC — number of CICs in group
### 10.4 Sec 45MA RBI Act 1934
- Auditor of NBFC (or non-banking miscellaneous company) accepting public deposits → inquire whether RBI statements/information (Chapter IIIB) furnished
- If not satisfied → duty to report to RBI with aggregate deposits amount
- Such report also incorporated in statutory report under Sec 143 Companies Act 2013

## 11. Ind AS Applicability & Financial Statement Format
### Applicability
- Per Rule 4(1)(iv) Companies (Indian Accounting Standards) Rules, 2015 (amended 2023)
### Division III of Schedule III — Balance Sheet
- Financial Assets: Cash, Bank balance, Derivatives, Receivables (Trade + Other), Loans, Investments, Other financial assets
- Non-Financial Assets: Inventories, Current tax (net), DTA, Investment property, Biological assets, PPE, CWIP, Intangibles under dev., Goodwill, Other intangibles, Other non-financial assets
- Financial Liabilities: Derivatives, Payables (Trade: MSME + Others; Other Payables: MSME + Others), Debt Securities, Borrowings (other than debt securities), Deposits, Subordinated liabilities, Other financial liabilities
- Non-Financial Liabilities: Current tax (net), Provisions, DTL, Other non-financial liabilities
- Non-Financial Capital: Equity share capital, Other equity
### Division III — P&L
- Revenue: Interest income, Dividend income, Rental income, Fee & commission, Net gain on FV changes, Net gain on derecognition (amortised category), Sale of products, Sale of services, Others
- Expenses: Finance costs, Fee & commission expense, Net loss on FV changes, Net loss on derecognition, Impairment on financial instruments, Cost of material, Stock-in-trade purchases, Inventory changes, Employee benefits, D&A & impairment, Other expenses
- Profit/loss → continuing operations, discontinued operations
- OCI: Items not reclassified to P&L; items reclassified to P&L
- EPS: Basic & Diluted (continuing / discontinued / combined)
### Division III vs Division II — Key Differences
- NBFCs may present balance sheet in order of liquidity (Division II — NOT allowed)
- NBFC 'other income/expenditure' disclosure threshold: >1% of total income (Division II: >1% of revenue from ops OR ₹10 lakhs, whichever higher)
- NBFCs: separate disclosure under 'receivables' of dues from LLPs where director is partner/member
- Revenue from operations and OCI items disclosed on face of P&L (Division II — notes only)
- Separate disclosure of trade receivables with significant credit risk increase & credit-impaired
- Statutory reserve distribution restrictions/conditions → separately disclosed in notes

# SRE 2400 (Revised): Engagements to Review Historical Financial Statements

## Objective
- Objectives of practitioner in a review engagement (Para 14)
  - 🎯 Obtain limited assurance primarily by inquiry and analytical procedures (Para 14a)
  - 🎯 Conclude if financial statements are free from material misstatement (Para 14a)
  - 🎯 Report on the financial statements as a whole (Para 14b)
  - 🎯 Communicate as required by this SRE (Para 14b)
  - 🎯 Disclaim or withdraw if limited assurance cannot be obtained (Para 15)

## Scope
- Applies to
  - Practitioner reviewing historical financial statements (Para 1a)
  - When practitioner is not the independent auditor (Para 1a)
- Does NOT apply to
  - Reviews performed by entity's independent auditor (Para 2)
- Effective from
  - Reviews for periods beginning on or after April 1, 2016 (Para 13)

## Definitions
- 📌 Analytical procedures (Para 17a): Evaluations of financial information through analysis of plausible relationships among both financial and non-financial data. Analytical procedures also encompass such investigation as is necessary of identified fluctuations or relationships that are inconsistent with other relevant information or that differ from expected values by a significant amount.
- 📌 Engagement risk (Para 17b): The risk that the practitioner expresses an inappropriate conclusion when the financial statements are materially misstated.
- 📌 General purpose financial statements (Para 17c): Financial statements prepared in accordance with a general purpose framework.
- 📌 General purpose framework (Para 17d): A financial reporting framework designed to meet the common financial information needs of a wide range of users. The financial reporting framework may be a fair presentation framework or a compliance framework.
- 📌 Inquiry (Para 17e): Inquiry consists of seeking information of knowledgeable persons, both financial and non-financial, within the entity or outside the entity.
- 📌 Limited assurance (Para 17f): The level of assurance obtained where engagement risk is reduced to a level that is acceptable in the circumstances of the engagement, but where that risk is greater than for a reasonable assurance engagement, as the basis for expressing a conclusion in accordance with this SRE. The combination of the nature, timing and extent of evidence gathering procedures is at least sufficient for the practitioner to obtain a meaningful level of assurance. To be meaningful, the level of assurance obtained by the practitioner is likely to enhance the intended users’ confidence about the financial statements.
- 📌 Practitioner (Para 17g): A professional accountant in public practice. The term includes the engagement partner or other members of the engagement team, or, as applicable, the firm. Where this SRE expressly intends that a requirement or responsibility be fulfilled by the engagement partner, the term “engagement partner” rather than “practitioner” is used.
- 📌 Professional judgment (Para 17h): The application of relevant training, knowledge and experience, within the context provided by assurance, accounting and ethical standards, in making informed decisions about the courses of action that are appropriate in the circumstances of the review engagement.
- 📌 Relevant ethical requirements (Para 17i): Ethical requirements to which the engagement team is subject, which ordinarily comprise the Code of Ethics issued by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) together with other relevant pronouncements issued by the ICAI.
- 📌 Special purpose financial statements (Para 17j): Financial statements prepared in accordance with a special purpose framework.
- 📌 Special purpose framework (Para 17k): A financial reporting framework designed to meet the financial information needs of specific users. The financial reporting framework may be a fair presentation framework or a compliance framework.

## Requirements
- Conduct of Review
  - 🎯 Have understanding of entire SRE text (Para 18)
  - 🎯 Comply with each requirement of this SRE (Para 19)
    - ⚠️ Unless requirement is not relevant to engagement (Para 19)
  - 🎯 Shall not represent compliance in report (Para 20)
    - ⚠️ Unless complied with all relevant requirements (Para 20)
- Ethical and Professional Standards
  - 🎯 Comply with relevant ethical requirements and independence (Para 21)
  - 🎯 Plan and perform with professional skepticism (Para 22)
  - 🎯 Exercise professional judgment in conducting review (Para 23)
- Quality Control
  - 🎯 Engagement partner shall possess appropriate competence (Para 24)
  - 🎯 Take responsibility for overall engagement quality (Para 25a)
  - 🎯 Take responsibility for direction and supervision (Para 25b)
  - 🎯 Ensure report is appropriate in circumstances (Para 25c)
  - 🎯 Ensure engagement performed per firm quality policies (Para 25d)
  - 🎯 Communicate information causing firm to decline engagement (Para 26)
  - 🎯 Remain alert for ethical non-compliance by team (Para 27)
  - 🎯 Consider results of firm monitoring process (Para 28)
- Acceptance and Continuance
  - 🎯 Shall not accept review engagement if conditions exist (Para 29)
    - ⚠️ Unless required by law or regulation (Para 29)
    - Practitioner not satisfied rational purpose exists (Para 29a)
    - Reason to believe ethical requirements not satisfied (Para 29b)
    - Information needed likely unavailable or unreliable (Para 29c)
    - Cause to doubt management integrity affects performance (Para 29d)
    - Imposed scope limitation leads to disclaiming conclusion (Para 29e)
  - 🎯 Determine if financial reporting framework is acceptable (Para 30a)
  - 🎯 Obtain management agreement of its responsibilities (Para 30b)
  - 🎯 Discuss with management if preconditions not met (Para 31)
  - 🎯 Determine action if preconditions fail post-acceptance (Para 32)
  - 🎯 Refer to SRE only if report complies fully (Para 33)
  - 🎯 Evaluate if prescribed wording misunderstands assurance (Para 34)
  - 🎯 Do not accept if explanation cannot mitigate misunderstanding (Para 35)
  - 🎯 Agree terms of engagement with management prior (Para 36)
  - 🎯 Record agreed terms in an engagement letter (Para 37)
  - 🎯 Evaluate need to revise terms on recurring engagements (Para 38)
  - 🎯 Not agree to change terms without reasonable justification (Para 39)
  - 🎯 Determine justification if changing to no assurance (Para 40)
  - 🎯 Record new terms if changed during engagement (Para 41)
- Communication
  - 🎯 Communicate matters of sufficient importance timely (Para 42)
- Performing the Engagement
  - 🎯 Determine materiality for financial statements as a whole (Para 43)
  - 🎯 Revise materiality if new information emerges (Para 44)
  - 🎯 Obtain understanding of entity and environment (Para 45)
  - 🎯 Include industry, operations, systems, and policies (Para 46)
  - 🎯 Design and perform inquiry and analytical procedures (Para 47)
  - 🎯 Make specific inquiries of management and others (Para 48)
  - 🎯 Consider adequacy of data for analytical procedures (Para 49)
- Specific Circumstances
  - 🎯 Remain alert for undisclosed related party transactions (Para 50)
  - 🎯 Inquire on significant unusual transactions outside normal course (Para 51)
  - 🎯 Communicate and evaluate fraud or non-compliance (Para 52)
  - 🎯 Consider management assessment of going concern ability (Para 53)
  - 🎯 Inquire on future plans if going concern doubt arises (Para 54)
  - 🎯 Take steps to satisfy adequacy if using others' work (Para 55)
  - 🎯 Obtain evidence reconciling statements to accounting records (Para 56)
  - 🎯 Design additional procedures if material misstatement likely (Para 57)
- Subsequent Events
  - 🎯 Request management correct statements for subsequent events (Para 58)
  - 🎯 Discuss and determine amendment if fact known post-report (Para 59)
  - 🎯 Notify management not to issue if amendment refused (Para 60)
- Written Representations
  - 🎯 Request written representation on management responsibilities (Para 61)
  - 🎯 Request representation on related parties, fraud, going concern (Para 62)
  - 🎯 Discuss, re-evaluate, take action if not provided (Para 63)
  - 🎯 Disclaim or withdraw if representations are unreliable (Para 64)
  - 🎯 Date representations as near as practicable to report (Para 65)
- Evaluating Evidence
  - 🎯 Evaluate whether sufficient appropriate evidence obtained (Para 66)
  - 🎯 Discuss effects of scope limitations on review (Para 67)
  - 🎯 Evaluate evidence to determine effect on report (Para 68)
- Forming the Conclusion
  - 🎯 Evaluate if statements adequately refer to framework (Para 69)
  - 🎯 Consider impact of uncorrected misstatements and bias (Para 70)
  - 🎯 Consider overall presentation if fair presentation framework (Para 71)
  - 🎯 Express conclusion in appropriate form based on framework (Para 72)
  - 🎯 Express unmodified conclusion if limited assurance obtained (Para 73)
  - 🎯 Use specified phrases for unmodified conclusion (Para 74)
    - ⚠️ Unless otherwise required by law or regulation (Para 74)
  - 🎯 Express modified conclusion if materially misstated or unable (Para 75)
  - 🎯 Use appropriate heading for modified conclusion (Para 76)
  - 🎯 Express qualified or adverse conclusion for material misstatements (Para 77)
  - 🎯 Use specified phrases for qualified conclusion due to misstatement (Para 78)
  - 🎯 Use specified phrases for adverse conclusion (Para 79)
  - 🎯 Describe and quantify financial effects in basis paragraph (Para 80)
    - ⚠️ Unless impracticable to quantify effects (Para 80a)
    - ⚠️ Unless prohibited by law to include omitted disclosures (Para 80c)
  - 🎯 Express qualified or disclaimer if unable to obtain evidence (Para 81)
  - 🎯 Withdraw if inability to obtain evidence is pervasive (Para 82)
  - 🎯 Use specified phrases for qualified conclusion due to inability (Para 83)
  - 🎯 State inability to obtain evidence when disclaiming conclusion (Para 84)
  - 🎯 Include reasons for inability in basis for conclusion paragraph (Para 85)
- The Practitioner's Report
  - 🎯 Ensure report is in writing with specific elements (Para 86)
  - 🎯 Include Emphasis of Matter if fundamental to understanding (Para 87)
    - ⚠️ Provided evidence confirms matter not materially misstated (Para 87)
  - 🎯 Alert users if prepared under special purpose framework (Para 88)
  - 🎯 Place Emphasis of Matter immediately after conclusion (Para 89)
  - 🎯 Include Other Matter paragraph for relevant matters (Para 90)
    - ⚠️ Unless prohibited by law or regulation (Para 90)
  - 🎯 Address other reporting responsibilities in separate section (Para 91)
  - 🎯 Date report no earlier than sufficient evidence obtained (Para 92)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 Document nature, timing, extent, results, and significant matters (Para 93)
  - 🎯 Record who performed work and who reviewed it (Para 94)
  - 🎯 Document discussions with management on significant matters (Para 95)
  - 🎯 Document how inconsistencies with findings were addressed (Para 96)

- ⚠️ Para 15: Unless qualified conclusion is sufficient — practitioner must disclaim or withdraw

- ⚠️ Para 20: Unless complied with all requirements — practitioner shall not represent compliance
- ⚠️ Para 29: Unless required by law or regulation — practitioner shall not accept engagement if preconditions fail
- ⚠️ Para 31: Unless required by law or regulation — do not accept if preconditions remain unsatisfied
- ⚠️ Para 35: Unless required by law or regulation — do not accept if explanation cannot mitigate misunderstanding
- ⚠️ Para 74: Unless otherwise required by law — use specified phrases for unmodified conclusion
- ⚠️ Para 78: Unless otherwise required by law — use specified phrases for qualified conclusion
- ⚠️ Para 79: Unless otherwise required by law — use specified phrases for adverse conclusion
- ⚠️ Para 80: Unless impracticable — practitioner shall describe and quantify misstatement effects
- ⚠️ Para 80: Unless prohibited by law — practitioner shall include omitted disclosures
- ⚠️ Para 83: Unless otherwise required by law — use specified phrases for qualified conclusion due to inability
- ⚠️ Para 87: Provided evidence confirms matter not misstated — practitioner shall include Emphasis of Matter
- ⚠️ Para 90: If not prohibited by law or regulation — practitioner shall include Other Matter paragraph

## Logical Flow Chains
- Engagement Acceptance Process
  - 🎯 Assess preconditions for review engagement (Para 30)
    - if met: 🎯 Agree terms and record in engagement letter (Para 36)
    - if not met: 🎯 Discuss with management/TCWG (Para 31)
      - leads to: Resolve issues and accept engagement
      - ⚠️ unless: Cannot be resolved (Para 31)
        - leads to: 🎯 Do not accept engagement
- Suspected Material Misstatement Process
  - 🎯 Become aware of matter causing belief of material misstatement (Para 57)
    - requires: 🎯 Design and perform additional procedures
      - leads to: Conclude matter not likely to cause material misstatement
      - or leads to: Determine matter causes statements to be materially misstated
      - or leads to: Unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence (Scope limitation)
- Going Concern Doubt Evaluation
  - 🎯 Become aware of events casting doubt on going concern (Para 54)
    - requires: 🎯 Inquire about future plans and feasibility (Para 54a)
      - leads to: 🎯 Evaluate if responses provide sufficient basis (Para 54b)
        - to conclude: Financial statements can continue on going concern basis
        - or to conclude: Financial statements are materially misstated
- Modifying the Conclusion Process
  - 🎯 Evaluate evidence obtained (Para 75)
    - if financial statements are materially misstated:
      - and effects are material but not pervasive: Express Qualified Conclusion (Para 77a)
      - and effects are material and pervasive: Express Adverse Conclusion (Para 77b)
    - if unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence:
      - and effects are material but not pervasive: Express Qualified Conclusion (Para 81a)
      - and effects are material and pervasive: Withdraw from engagement (Para 82)
        - ⚠️ unless: Withdrawal not possible, then Express Disclaimer of Conclusion (Para 81b)

# SA 520: Analytical Procedures

## Objective
- 🎯 To obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence (Para 3a)
  - When using substantive analytical procedures (Para 3a)
- 🎯 To design and perform procedures near audit end (Para 3b)
  - Assist forming overall conclusion on financial statements (Para 3b)
  - Assess consistency with auditor's entity understanding (Para 3b)

## Scope
- Applies to: substantive analytical procedures (Para 1)
- Applies to: analytical procedures for overall conclusion (Para 1)
- Does NOT apply to: risk assessment procedures (Para 1)
  - 🔗 See SA 315: Risk assessment procedures (Para 1)
- Effective from: April 1, 2010 (Para 2)

## Definitions
- 📌 Analytical procedures (Para 4): evaluations of financial information through analysis of plausible relationships among both financial and non-financial data. Analytical procedures also encompass such investigation as is necessary of identified fluctuations or relationships that are inconsistent with other relevant information or that differ from expected values by a significant amount.

## Requirements
- Substantive Analytical Procedures (Para 5)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine suitability for given assertions (Para 5a)
    - Take account of assessed misstatement risks (Para 5a)
    - Take account of tests of details if any (Para 5a)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate reliability of data (Para 5b)
    - Take account of source and comparability (Para 5b)
    - Take account of nature and preparation controls (Para 5b)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall develop an expectation (Para 5c)
    - Evaluate if expectation is sufficiently precise (Para 5c)
    - Must identify potentially material misstatements (Para 5c)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine acceptable difference amount (Para 5d)
    - Acceptable difference without further investigation (Para 5d)
- Overall Conclusion Procedures (Para 6)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall design and perform analytical procedures (Para 6)
    - Near the end of the audit (Para 6)
    - To confirm consistency with auditor understanding (Para 6)
- Investigating Results (Para 7)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall investigate inconsistent fluctuations (Para 7)
    - ⚠️ If procedures identify differing expected values (Para 7)
    - By inquiring of management (Para 7a)
    - By obtaining appropriate audit evidence for responses (Para 7a)
    - By performing other necessary audit procedures (Para 7b)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 4 (Nature of Procedures)
  - Range from simple comparisons to complex statistical analyses (A3)
  - Apply to consolidated statements, components, or individual elements (A3)
- Explains Para 5 (Substantive Analytical Procedures)
  - Decision based on expected effectiveness and efficiency (A4)
  - May inquire of management regarding data availability (A5)
  - May use analytical data prepared by management (A5)
    - ⚠️ Provided auditor is satisfied data is properly prepared (A5)
- Explains Para 5a (Suitability for Assertions)
  - More applicable to large volumes of predictable transactions (A6)
  - Depends on assessment of effectiveness in detecting misstatements (A6)
  - Different procedure types provide different assurance levels (A8)
  - Influenced by assertion nature and assessed misstatement risk (A9)
  - May be used alongside tests of details for same assertion (A10)
- Explains Para 5b (Data Reliability)
  - Influenced by source, nature, and obtaining circumstances (A12)
  - May test operating effectiveness of preparation controls (A13)
    - Effective controls provide greater confidence in results (A13)
    - May be tested in conjunction with other control tests (A13)
  - Relevant for period end and interim date procedures (A14)
- Explains Para 5c (Precision of Expectation)
  - Accuracy of expected results prediction matters (A15)
  - Degree of potential information disaggregation matters (A15)
  - Availability of both financial and non-financial data matters (A15)
- Explains Para 5d (Acceptable Difference Amount)
  - Influenced by materiality and desired level of assurance (A16)
  - ⚠️ As assessed risk increases, acceptable uninvestigated difference decreases (A16)
- Explains Para 6 (Overall Conclusion)
  - Corroborates conclusions formed during individual component audits (A17)
  - May identify previously unrecognised material misstatement risk (A18)
    - 🔗 Requires auditor to revise risk assessment under SA 315 (A18)
  - May be similar to risk assessment procedures used earlier (A19)
- Explains Para 7 (Investigating Results)
  - Evaluate responses against auditor understanding of entity (A20)
  - ⚠️ Unless management explanation is adequate, perform other procedures (A21)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 7: If procedures identify significant fluctuations — auditor must investigate
- ⚠️ A5: If using management data — provided auditor is satisfied it is properly prepared
- ⚠️ A11: Public Sector Entities — traditional business relationships may not apply
- ⚠️ A16: As assessed risk increases — acceptable uninvestigated difference decreases
- ⚠️ A21: If management explanation is inadequate — auditor must perform other procedures

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A1 — Examples of financial information comparisons
  - 📋 Comparable information for prior periods
  - 📋 Anticipated results of the entity such as budgets
  - 📋 Anticipated results of the entity such as forecasts
  - 📋 Expectations of the auditor such as depreciation estimation
  - 📋 Similar industry information averages
  - 📋 Other entities of comparable size in same industry
- Para A2 — Examples of considered relationships
  - 📋 Elements conforming to predictable patterns like gross margin percentages
  - 📋 Between financial and non-financial information like payroll to employees
- Para A7 — Examples of predictive models
  - 📋 Estimating total payroll costs using fixed pay rates and employee numbers
  - 📋 Widely recognised trade ratios like profit margins for retail
- Para A8 — Examples of assurance levels
  - 📋 Persuasive evidence: predicting rental income via apartments, rates, and vacancy
  - 📋 Corroborative evidence: gross margin percentages confirming revenue figures
- Para A11 — Examples in public sector entities
  - 📋 Little direct relationship between revenue and expenditure
  - 📋 No relationship between expenditures on inventories and reported fixed assets
  - 📋 Variations in cost per kilometer of road construction
  - 📋 Number of vehicles acquired compared with vehicles retired
- Para A12 — Examples of factors determining data reliability
  - 📋 Information obtained from independent sources outside the entity
  - 📋 Broad industry data supplemented for specialised products
  - 📋 Budgets established as expected results rather than achievable goals
  - 📋 Controls over the preparation, review and maintenance of budgets
- Para A13 — Examples of joint control testing
  - 📋 Testing controls over unit sales recording alongside sales invoice processing
- Para A15 — Examples evaluating expectation precision
  - 📋 Greater consistency expecting gross profit margins versus discretionary expenses
  - 📋 Disaggregating financial info on individual sections of an operation
  - 📋 Disaggregating financial statements of components of a diversified entity
  - 📋 Using available non-financial info like number of units produced
- App 1 — Examples of Reasonableness tested accounts
  - 📋 Interest expense against interest bearing obligations
  - 📋 Raw Material Consumption to Production quantity
  - 📋 Wastage and Scrap percentage against production and raw material consumption
  - 📋 Work-in-Progress based on issued materials and Sales quantity
  - 📋 Sales discounts and commissions against sales volume
  - 📋 Rental revenues based on occupancy of premises
- App 1 — Examples of Commonly used Ratios
  - 📋 Elements of income or loss as a percentage of sales
  - 📋 Gross profit turnover
  - 📋 Accounts receivable turnover
  - 📋 Inventory turnover
  - 📋 Profitability, leverage, and liquidity
- App 1 — Examples of Sources of information
  - 📋 Interim financial information
  - 📋 Budgets
  - 📋 Management accounts
  - 📋 Non-financial information
  - 📋 Bank and cash records
  - 📋 VAT returns
  - 📋 Board minutes
  - 📋 Discussion or correspondence with the client at year-end

## Logical Flow Chains
- Substantive Analytical Execution (Para 5)
  - Determine suitability for assertion (Para 5a)
    - leads to: Evaluate reliability of data (Para 5b)
      - leads to: Develop precise expectation (Para 5c)
        - leads to: Determine acceptable difference (Para 5d)
          - if recorded amount > acceptable difference: Investigate (Para 7)
- Investigating Differences (Para 7)
  - Identify inconsistent fluctuations (Para 7)
    - leads to: Inquire of management (Para 7a)
      - if explanation adequate: Obtain corroborative audit evidence (Para 7a)
      - ⚠️ unless explanation inadequate: Perform other audit procedures (Para 7b)

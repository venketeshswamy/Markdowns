# SA 402: Audit Considerations Relating to an Entity Using a Service Organisation

## Objective
- 🎯 To obtain an understanding of service nature and significance (Para 7(a))
  - Sufficient to identify and assess material misstatement risks (Para 7(a))
  - Understanding their effect on user entity’s internal control (Para 7(a))
- 🎯 To design and perform responsive audit procedures (Para 7(b))

## Scope
- Applies to user auditor obtaining sufficient appropriate evidence (Para 1)
- Applies when service organisation is part of information system (Para 3)
- ⚠️ Does NOT apply to limited financial institution processing (Para 5)
  - 📋 Examples: Bank checking accounts or broker securities (Para 5)
- ⚠️ Does NOT apply to proprietary financial interests audit (Para 5)
- Effective for audits beginning on or after April 1, 2010 (Para 6)

## Definitions
- 📌 Complementary user entity controls (Para 8(a))
  - Controls that the service organisation assumes, in the design of its service, will be implemented by user entities, and which, if necessary to achieve control objectives, are identified in the description of its system.
- 📌 Report on the description and design of controls (Type 1 report) (Para 8(b))
  - A report that comprises a description, prepared by management of the service organisation, of the service organisation’s system, control objectives and related controls that have been designed and implemented as at a specified date; and a report by the service auditor with the objective of conveying reasonable assurance that includes the service auditor’s opinion on the description of the service organisation’s system, control objectives and related controls and the suitability of the design of the controls to achieve the specified control objectives.
- 📌 Report on the description, design, and operating effectiveness (Type 2 report) (Para 8(c))
  - A report that comprises a description, prepared by management of the service organisation, of the service organisation’s system, control objectives and related controls, their design and implementation as at a specified date or throughout a specified period and, in some cases, their operating effectiveness throughout a specified period; and a report by the service auditor with the objective of conveying reasonable assurance that includes the service auditor’s opinion on the description of the service organisation’s system, control objectives and related controls, the suitability of the design of the controls to achieve the specified control objectives, and the operating effectiveness of the controls; and a description of the service auditor’s tests of the controls and the results thereof.
- 📌 Service auditor (Para 8(d))
  - An auditor who, at the request of the service organisation, provides an assurance report on the controls of a service organisation.
- 📌 Service organisation (Para 8(e))
  - A third-party organisation (or segment of a third-party organisation) that provides services to user entities that are part of those entities’ information systems relevant to financial reporting.
- 📌 Service organisation’s system (Para 8(f))
  - The policies and procedures designed, implemented and maintained by the service organisation to provide user entities with the services covered by the service auditor’s report.
- 📌 Subservice organisation (Para 8(g))
  - A service organisation used by another service organisation to perform some of the services provided to user entities that are part of those user entities’ information systems relevant to financial reporting.
- 📌 User auditor (Para 8(h))
  - An auditor who audits and reports on the financial statements of a user entity.
- 📌 User entity (Para 8(i))
  - An entity that uses a service organisation and whose financial statements are being audited.

## Requirements
- Obtaining Understanding of Services
  - 🎯 The user auditor shall obtain an understanding (Para 9)
    - Of nature and significance of services provided (Para 9(a))
    - Of nature and materiality of transactions processed (Para 9(b))
    - Of degree of interaction between entities (Para 9(c))
    - Of relationship nature and contractual terms (Para 9(d))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate design and implementation (Para 10)
    - Of relevant user entity controls over services (Para 10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine if understanding is sufficient (Para 11)
  - 🎯 If understanding is insufficient, shall perform procedures: (Para 12)
    - Obtain Type 1 or Type 2 report (Para 12(a))
    - Contact service organisation through user entity (Para 12(b))
    - Visit service organisation and perform procedures (Para 12(c))
    - Use another auditor to perform procedures (Para 12(d))
- Using Type 1 or Type 2 Reports
  - 🎯 The auditor shall be satisfied as to: (Para 13)
    - Service auditor’s professional competence (Para 13(a))
      - ⚠️ Except where service auditor is ICAI member (Para 13(a))
    - Service auditor’s independence (Para 13(a))
    - Adequacy of standards under which report issued (Para 13(b))
  - 🎯 If using report for design and implementation, shall: (Para 14)
    - Evaluate appropriateness of date or period (Para 14(a))
    - Evaluate sufficiency and appropriateness of evidence (Para 14(b))
    - Determine relevance of complementary user controls (Para 14(c))
- Responding to Assessed Risks
  - 🎯 The user auditor shall determine evidence availability (Para 15)
    - ⚠️ If insufficient at user entity, shall perform further procedures (Para 15(b))
    - Or use another auditor at service organisation (Para 15(b))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain operating effectiveness evidence if expected (Para 16)
    - By obtaining a Type 2 report (Para 16(a))
    - By performing tests of controls (Para 16(b))
    - By using another auditor for tests (Para 16(c))
  - 🎯 If using Type 2 report for operating effectiveness, shall: (Para 17)
    - Evaluate appropriateness of date or period (Para 17(a))
    - Determine relevance of complementary user controls (Para 17(b))
    - Evaluate adequacy of time period elapsed (Para 17(c))
    - Evaluate relevance of tested controls to assertions (Para 17(d))
- Subservice Organisations
  - 🎯 The auditor shall apply SA requirements to subservice (Para 18)
    - ⚠️ If Type 1 or 2 report excludes subservice services (Para 18)
- Fraud and Non-Compliance
  - 🎯 The auditor shall inquire of user entity management (Para 19)
    - Regarding reported fraud or non-compliance (Para 19)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate effect on audit procedures (Para 19)
- Reporting by User Auditor
  - 🎯 The auditor shall modify the audit opinion (Para 20)
    - ⚠️ If unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence (Para 20)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall not refer to service auditor (Para 21)
    - ⚠️ Unless required by law or regulation (Para 21)
    - 🎯 If required, shall indicate responsibility not diminished (Para 21)
  - 🎯 If reference relevant to modified opinion, shall indicate (Para 22)
    - Reference does not diminish auditor's responsibility (Para 22)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Paras 9-11: Obtaining Understanding
  - Information available from multiple sources (Para A1)
  - Past experience with organisation provides knowledge (Para A2)
  - Significance depends on services provided (Para A3)
  - Smaller entities retain governance responsibilities (Para A5)
  - Transaction nature may be significant despite low materiality (Para A6)
  - Interaction degree dictates practical control implementation (Para A7)
  - Contract terms dictate auditor access rights (Para A8)
  - No direct relationship usually exists between auditors (Para A9)
  - ⚠️ If access unavailable, must assess jurisdictional laws (Para A10)
  - 🔗 May use another auditor per SA 600 (Para A11)
- Explains Paras 12-14: Further Procedures & Reports
  - User controls may mitigate risks regardless of service controls (Para A12)
  - Substantive procedures alone may not suffice for automated risks (Para A14)
  - Report availability depends on user-service contracts (Para A17)
  - Visiting may be most effective if function entirely outsourced (Para A18)
  - 🔗 Refer to SA 600 when using another auditor (Para A19)
  - Subservice impact expands entity interaction complexity (Para A20)
  - Type 1 reports do not evidence operating effectiveness (Para A22)
  - Out-of-period reports require supplementary updates (Para A23)
- Explains Paras 15-17: Responding to Risks
  - Service use might actually decrease misstatement risk (Para A24)
  - Direct access to electronic records provides valuable evidence (Para A25)
  - ⚠️ Confirmations alone are not reliable without independent records (Para A26)
  - Another auditor may perform substantive procedures (Para A27)
  - Service auditor report does not alter user auditor responsibility (Para A28)
  - ⚠️ Tests of controls required if substantive alone insufficient (Para A29)
  - Shorter test periods provide less audit evidence (Para A32)
  - ⚠️ Completely outside periods require additional audit procedures (Para A35)
  - Complementary controls must be evaluated if assumed (Para A36)
  - Exceptions in Type 2 report require auditor assessment (Para A38)
  - 🔗 Deficiencies must be communicated per SA 265 (Para A39)
- Explains Para 18: Subservice Organisations
  - Carve-out method excludes subservice controls (Para A40)
  - Inclusive method includes subservice controls (Para A40)
- Explains Para 19: Fraud & Non-Compliance
  - Contracts often require fraud disclosure to user entities (Para A41)
- Explains Paras 20-22: Reporting
  - Scope limitations cause modified or disclaimed opinions (Para A42)
  - Reference to service auditor never justifies unmodified opinion (Para A44)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 5 Exception: SA does not apply to transactions specifically authorised by entity and limited to processing by financial institutions.
- ⚠️ Para 13(a) Exception: Auditor must assess service auditor competence unless the service auditor is an ICAI member.
- ⚠️ Para 15(b) Condition: If evidence unavailable at user entity, auditor must perform procedures at service organisation or use another auditor.
- ⚠️ Para 20 Condition: Opinion modification is strictly required if sufficient appropriate audit evidence cannot be obtained regarding the services.
- ⚠️ Para 21 Exception: Auditor cannot refer to service auditor in unmodified report unless explicitly mandated by law.
- ⚠️ Para A10 Exception: If broad access rights are restricted by foreign jurisdiction, contractual rights of access must be negotiated.
- ⚠️ Para A26 Condition: Confirmations from service organisation are not reliable evidence if user entity lacks independent supporting records.
- ⚠️ Para A35 Condition: Type 2 report completely outside current audit period provides no current evidence unless updated via other procedures.

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para 3 — Examples of Services Affecting Information System
  - 📋 Classes of significant transactions
  - 📋 IT and manual procedures for transactions
  - 📋 Related accounting records and supporting information
  - 📋 Capture of other significant events and conditions
  - 📋 Financial reporting process and estimates
  - 📋 Controls surrounding non-standard journal entries
- Para A1 — Examples of Information Sources
  - 📋 User manuals
  - 📋 System overviews
  - 📋 Technical manuals
  - 📋 Contract or service level agreement
  - 📋 Reports by internal auditors or regulatory authorities
  - 📋 Reports by the service auditor
- Para A4 — Examples of Relevant Services
  - 📋 Maintenance of the user entity’s accounting records
  - 📋 Management of assets
  - 📋 Initiating, recording or processing transactions
- Para A8 — Examples of Contract Matters
  - 📋 Information provided and initiation responsibilities
  - 📋 Application of regulatory record requirements
  - 📋 Indemnification for performance failure
  - 📋 Whether service organisation provides control report
  - 📋 Whether user auditor has access rights
  - 📋 Allowance of direct auditor communication
- Para A12 — Examples of User Entity Controls
  - 📋 Comparing submitted data with received processed reports
  - 📋 Recomputing sample payroll for clerical accuracy
- Para A15 — Examples of Procedure Decision Factors
  - 📋 Size of both entities
  - 📋 Complexity of transactions and services
  - 📋 Location of the service organisation
  - 📋 Expected effectiveness of audit procedures
  - 📋 Nature of relationship between entities
- Para A22 — Examples of Understanding from Reports
  - 📋 Aspects of controls affecting user transactions
  - 📋 Flow of significant transactions to identify risk points
  - 📋 Control objectives relevant to assertions
  - 📋 Suitability of control design and implementation
- Para A23 — Examples of Updating Out-of-Period Reports
  - 📋 Discussing changes with user entity personnel
  - 📋 Reviewing current documentation and correspondence
  - 📋 Discussing changes with service organisation personnel
- Para A26 — Examples of Procedures for Balances
  - 📋 Inspecting records and documents held by user entity
  - 📋 Inspecting records held by service organisation
  - 📋 Obtaining confirmations from the service organisation
  - 📋 Performing analytical procedures on available records
- Para A33 — Examples of Factors for Additional Evidence
  - 📋 Significance of assessed risks of material misstatement
  - 📋 Specific controls tested and significant changes to them
  - 📋 Degree of audit evidence obtained previously
  - 📋 Length of the remaining period
  - 📋 Extent to which auditor reduces substantive procedures
  - 📋 Effectiveness of control environment and monitoring
- Para A39 — Examples of Reportable Deficiencies
  - 📋 Any monitoring of controls implemented by user entity
  - 📋 Instances of unimplemented complementary user controls
  - 📋 Needed controls not implemented at service organisation
- Para A42 — Examples of Scope Limitations
  - 📋 Unable to obtain sufficient understanding of services
  - 📋 Unable to obtain operating effectiveness evidence
  - 📋 Evidence only at service organisation without direct access

## Logical Flow Chains
- Evaluating Reliance on Service Organisation (Paras 9-12)
  - Obtain understanding of services at user entity (Para 9)
    - leads to: Evaluating relevant user entity controls (Para 10)
      - leads to: Determining if understanding is sufficient (Para 11)
        - if sufficient: Proceed to risk assessment (Para 15)
        - ⚠️ unless insufficient: Perform further specific procedures (Para 12)
          - leads to: Obtaining Type 1/2 report or visiting (Para 12)
- Using a Type 2 Report for Operating Effectiveness (Paras 16-17)
  - Expectation that service controls are effective (Para 16)
    - leads to: Obtaining Type 2 report (Para 16(a))
      - leads to: Evaluating report period appropriateness (Para 17(a))
        - and: Testing complementary user entity controls (Para 17(b))
          - and: Evaluating time elapsed since tests (Para 17(c))
            - results in: Sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 17)

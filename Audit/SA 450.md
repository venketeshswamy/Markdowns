# SA 450: Evaluation of Misstatements Identified during the Audit
## Objective
- Evaluate effect of identified misstatements on the audit (Para 3)
- Evaluate effect of uncorrected misstatements on financial statements (Para 3)

## Scope
- Applies to auditor's evaluation of identified and uncorrected misstatements (Para 1)
- Effective for audits from periods beginning on or after April 1, 2010 (Para 2)

## Definitions
- 📌 Misstatement (Para 4): Difference between reported item and required per framework
  - Includes errors or fraud
  - Includes adjustments for true and fair view
- 📌 Uncorrected misstatements (Para 4): Accumulated misstatements not corrected

## Requirements
- Accumulation of Identified Misstatements
  - 🎯 Accumulate misstatements identified during audit (Para 5)
    - ⚠️ Except those that are clearly trivial (Para 5)
- Consideration as Audit Progresses
  - 🎯 Determine if audit strategy/plan need revision if: (Para 6)
    - Nature/circumstances indicate other misstatements may exist (Para 6)
    - Aggregate approaches materiality per SA 320 (Para 6)
  - 🎯 If management examined and corrected, perform additional procedures (Para 7)
- Communication and Correction
  - 🎯 Communicate all accumulated misstatements to management (Para 8)
    - ⚠️ Unless prohibited by law or regulation (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Request management to correct misstatements (Para 8)
  - 🎯 If management refuses, understand reasons (Para 9)
- Evaluating Effect of Uncorrected Misstatements
  - 🎯 Reassess materiality per SA 320 before evaluation (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Determine if uncorrected misstatements are material (Para 11)
    - Consider size, nature, circumstances (Para 11)
    - Consider effect of prior period misstatements (Para 11)
- Communication with Those Charged with Governance
  - 🎯 Communicate uncorrected misstatements to TCWG (Para 12)
    - ⚠️ Unless prohibited by law or regulation (Para 12)
  - 🎯 Identify material uncorrected misstatements individually (Para 12)
  - 🎯 Request correction of uncorrected misstatements (Para 12)
  - 🎯 Communicate effect of prior period misstatements (Para 13)
- Written Representation
  - 🎯 Request written representation on immateriality of uncorrected misstatements (Para 14)
  - 🎯 Include summary of items in/with representation (Para 14)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 Document amount below clearly trivial (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Document all accumulated misstatements and correction status (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Document conclusion on materiality of uncorrected misstatements (Para 15)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 4(a) - Misstatements
  - 📋 May result from inaccuracy in data (A1)
  - 📋 May result from omission (A1)
  - 📋 May result from incorrect accounting estimate (A1)
  - 📋 May result from unreasonable judgments/policies (A1)
  - 🔗 Examples of fraud misstatements in SA 240 (A1)
- Explains Para 5 - Accumulation
  - Clearly trivial is wholly different order than materiality (A2)
  - If uncertainty, matter is not clearly trivial (A2)
  - Distinguish factual, judgmental, projected misstatements (A3)
    - Factual: no doubt (A3)
    - Judgmental: unreasonable estimates/inappropriate policies (A3)
    - Projected: best estimate from sampling (A3)
    - 🔗 Projected misstatement guidance in SA 530 (A3)
- Explains Para 6-7 - Consideration as Audit Progresses
  - 📋 Evidence other misstatements may exist: internal control breakdown (A4)
  - 📋 Evidence other misstatements may exist: inappropriate widespread assumptions (A4)
  - Aggregate approaching materiality increases risk (A5)
  - Management examination may be based on projection (A6)
- Explains Para 8-9 - Communication and Correction
  - Timely communication enables management evaluation (A7)
  - Appropriate level has authority to take action (A7)
  - Law/regulation may restrict communication (A8)
  - Correction maintains accurate records (A9)
  - Understanding refusal reasons affects evaluation (A10)
    - 🔗 Consideration per SA 700(Revised) (A10)
- Explains Para 10-11 - Evaluating Effect
  - May need to revise materiality based on actual results (A11)
  - Significant revision likely made earlier (A12)
    - If lower materiality, reconsider performance materiality/procedures (A12)
  - Evaluate individual misstatement effect on relevant classes (A13)
  - Material misstatement unlikely offset by others (A14)
    - May offset within same account/class with consideration of risk (A14)
  - Classification materiality involves qualitative evaluation (A15)
  - Circumstances may cause evaluation as material below overall materiality (A16)
    - 📋 Affects regulatory compliance (A16)
    - 📋 Affects debt covenants (A16)
    - 📋 Incorrect policy affecting future periods (A16)
    - 📋 Changes earnings trends (A16)
    - 📋 Affects key ratios (A16)
    - 📋 Affects segment information (A16)
    - 📋 Increases management compensation (A16)
    - 📋 Significant to previous communications (A16)
    - 📋 Relates to particular parties (A16)
    - 📋 Omission important to users' understanding (A16)
    - 📋 Affects other information in annual report (A16)
    - 🔗 Other information responsibilities in SA 720(Revised) (A16)
  - 🔗 Consider fraud implications per SA 240 (A17)
  - Cumulative effect of prior immaterial misstatements may be material (A18)
  - Evaluation for government entities affected by legislation (A19)
  - Public interest, accountability may affect materiality by nature (A20)
- Explains Para 12 - Communication with TCWG
  - If communicated to management with governance roles, no need to repeat (A21)
    - 🔗 Per SA 260(Revised) Para 13 (A21)
  - Large number: communicate number and overall effect (A22)
  - 🔗 Discuss reasons for not correcting per SA 260(Revised) (A23)
- Explains Para 14 - Written Representation
  - Management may add disagreement with reasons (A24)
  - Does not relieve auditor of conclusion responsibility (A24)
- Explains Para 15 - Documentation
  - Documentation may consider aggregate effect (A25)
  - Documentation may consider materiality levels for classes (A25)
  - Documentation may consider effect on ratios/trends/compliance (A25)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 5: Clearly trivial misstatements not accumulated
- ⚠️ Para 8: Communication not required if prohibited by law/regulation
- ⚠️ Para 12: Communication to TCWG not required if prohibited by law/regulation
- ⚠️ Para 21: No repeat communication if management also has governance role
- Material misstatement unlikely offset by other misstatements (A14)
- Classification misstatement materiality based on qualitative factors (A15)
- Circumstances can make smaller misstatements material (A16)
- Cumulative prior immaterial misstatements may become material (A18)

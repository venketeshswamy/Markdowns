# SA 530: Audit Sampling

## Objective
- 🎯 Provide reasonable basis to draw conclusions about the population (Para 4)
  - Based on the sample selected from that population (Para 4)

## Scope
- Applies to auditor's use of statistical and non-statistical sampling (Para 1)
- Applies when auditor decides to use audit sampling (Para 1)
- Complements means available for selecting items for testing (Para 2)
- Effective from: April 1, 2009 (Para 3)

## Definitions
- 📌 Audit sampling (sampling) (Para 5a): The application of audit procedures to less than 100% of items within a population of audit relevance such that all sampling units have a chance of selection in order to provide the auditor with a reasonable basis on which to draw conclusions about the entire population.
- 📌 Population (Para 5b): The entire set of data from which a sample is selected and about which the auditor wishes to draw conclusions.
- 📌 Sampling risk (Para 5c): The risk that the auditor’s conclusion based on a sample may be different from the conclusion if the entire population were subjected to the same audit procedure. Sampling risk can lead to two types of erroneous conclusions:
  - Affecting effectiveness: controls more effective, or misstatement does not exist (Para 5c.i)
  - Affecting efficiency: controls less effective, or misstatement exists (Para 5c.ii)
- 📌 Non-sampling risk (Para 5d): The risk that the auditor reaches an erroneous conclusion for any reason not related to sampling risk.
- 📌 Anomaly (Para 5e): A misstatement or deviation that is demonstrably not representative of misstatements or deviations in a population.
- 📌 Sampling unit (Para 5f): The individual items constituting a population.
- 📌 Statistical sampling (Para 5g): An approach to sampling that has the following characteristics: (i) Random selection of the sample items; and (ii) The use of probability theory to evaluate sample results, including measurement of sampling risk.
  - ⚠️ A sampling approach without both characteristics is non-statistical sampling (Para 5g)
- 📌 Stratification (Para 5h): The process of dividing a population into sub-populations, each of which is a group of sampling units which have similar characteristics (often monetary value).
- 📌 Tolerable misstatement (Para 5i): A monetary amount set by the auditor in respect of which the auditor seeks to obtain an appropriate level of assurance that the monetary amount set by the auditor is not exceeded by the actual misstatement in the population.
- 📌 Tolerable rate of deviation (Para 5j): A rate of deviation from prescribed internal control procedures set by the auditor in respect of which the auditor seeks to obtain an appropriate level of assurance that the rate of deviation set by the auditor is not exceeded by the actual rate of deviation in the population.

## Requirements
- Sample Design, Size and Selection
  - 🎯 The auditor shall consider the purpose of the audit procedure (Para 6)
    - And characteristics of the population for the sample (Para 6)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine a sample size sufficient (Para 7)
    - To reduce sampling risk to an acceptably low level (Para 7)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall select items for the sample ensuring (Para 8)
    - Each sampling unit in population has a chance of selection (Para 8)
- Performing Audit Procedures
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform appropriate audit procedures on each item (Para 9)
  - 🎯 If procedure is not applicable to the selected item (Para 10)
    - The auditor shall perform the procedure on a replacement item (Para 10)
  - 🎯 If auditor is unable to apply designed or alternative procedures (Para 11)
    - The auditor shall treat that item as a deviation for controls (Para 11)
    - Or as a misstatement for tests of details (Para 11)
- Nature and Cause of Deviations and Misstatements
  - 🎯 The auditor shall investigate the nature and cause of identified deviations (Para 12)
    - And evaluate their possible effect on the audit procedure purpose (Para 12)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain a high degree of certainty (Para 13)
    - ⚠️ If treating misstatement or deviation as an anomaly (Para 13)
    - By performing additional audit procedures to obtain sufficient evidence (Para 13)
- Projecting Misstatements
  - 🎯 The auditor shall project misstatements found in the sample (Para 14)
    - ⚠️ Applicable only for tests of details (Para 14)
- Evaluating Results of Audit Sampling
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate the results of the sample (Para 15a)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate whether sampling provided a reasonable basis (Para 15b)
    - For conclusions about the tested population (Para 15b)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Sampling Unit and Tolerable Misstatement
  - Sampling units might be physical items or monetary units (A2)
  - Tolerable misstatement is application of performance materiality (A3)
    - 🔗 See SA 320, Para 9 (A3)
- Explains Sample Design (Para 6)
  - Requires clear understanding of what constitutes a deviation (A6)
  - Expected misstatement rate dictates examination extent (A7)
    - High expected rate may warrant 100% examination (A7)
  - Stratification or value-weighted selection may be appropriate (A8)
  - Choice between statistical and non-statistical is professional judgment (A9)
- Explains Sample Size (Para 7)
  - Lower acceptable sampling risk requires greater sample size (A10)
  - Determined by statistical formula or professional judgment (A11)
- Explains Selection of Items (Para 8)
  - Important to select representative sample to avoid bias (A12)
- Explains Performing Audit Procedures (Paras 10-11)
  - Cancelled cheque selection requires testing a replacement item (A14)
  - Lost documentation means unable to apply designed procedures (A15)
  - Examining subsequent receipts is a suitable alternative procedure (A16)
- Explains Nature and Cause (Para 12)
  - Common features among deviations warrant extending audit procedures (A17)
  - Intentional misstatements may indicate possibility of fraud (A17)
- Explains Projecting Misstatements (Para 14)
  - Projection provides broad view of scale of misstatement (A18)
  - ⚠️ Established anomalies may be excluded from projection (A19)
    - But uncorrected effect still considered additionally (A19)
  - No explicit projection necessary for tests of controls (A20)
- Explains Evaluating Results (Para 15)
  - Unexpectedly high deviation rate increases assessed risk (A21)
  - Projected plus anomalous misstatement is the best estimate (A22)
  - If sampling lacks reasonable basis, auditor may request adjustments (A23)
    - Or tailor nature, timing, and extent of further procedures (A23)

## Appendices
- Stratification and Value-Weighted Selection (App 1)
  - Stratification reduces variability within each stratum (App 1.1)
    - Allows reduced sample size without increasing sampling risk (App 1.1)
  - Value-weighted selection uses monetary units as sampling unit (App 1.5)
    - Directs audit effort to larger value items efficiently (App 1.5)
- Factors Influencing Sample Size for Tests of Controls (App 2)
  - Increase in intended reliance on controls increases size (App 2.1)
  - Increase in tolerable rate of deviation decreases size (App 2.2)
  - Increase in expected rate of deviation increases size (App 2.3)
  - Increase in desired assurance level increases size (App 2.4)
  - Number of sampling units has negligible effect (App 2.5)
- Factors Influencing Sample Size for Tests of Details (App 3)
  - Increase in assessed risk of material misstatement increases size (App 3.1)
  - Increase in use of other substantive procedures decreases size (App 3.2)
  - Increase in desired assurance level increases size (App 3.3)
  - Increase in tolerable misstatement decreases size (App 3.4)
  - Increase in expected misstatement amount increases size (App 3.5)
  - Stratification of the population decreases size (App 3.6)
  - Number of sampling units has negligible effect (App 3.7)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ [Para 5g]: Absence of random selection or probability theory
  - If condition met: Approach is considered non-statistical sampling
- ⚠️ [Para 13]: Misstatement discovered in a sample is an anomaly
  - If condition met: Requires high degree of certainty via additional procedures
- ⚠️ [Para A19]: When misstatement is established as an anomaly
  - If condition met: May be excluded from projecting misstatements to population
  - Alternate outcome: Uncorrected effect still considered additionally
- ⚠️ [Para A22]: Projected plus anomalous misstatement exceeds tolerable limit
  - If condition met: Sample does not provide reasonable basis for conclusions
  - If condition not met: Sample results support the population conclusions

## Logical Flow Chains
- Evaluating Sample Results Process (Paras 14-15, A22-A23)
  - Calculate projected misstatement plus anomalous misstatement (A22)
    - leads to: Compare total to tolerable misstatement (A22)
      - if exceeds tolerable limit: Sample provides no reasonable basis (A22)
        - leads to: Request management to investigate and adjust (A23)
        - or: Tailor nature, timing, extent of further procedures (A23)
      - if close to tolerable limit: High risk of actual exceeding limit (A22)
        - leads to: Obtain additional audit evidence (A22)
- Managing Inapplicable Selected Items (Paras 10-11, A14-A15)
  - Item selected for testing (Para 9)
    - if procedure not applicable (e.g., cancelled cheque): Examine replacement (Para 10)
    - if unable to apply procedures (e.g., lost document): Attempt alternatives (Para 11)
      - if alternatives fail: Treat as deviation or misstatement (Para 11)

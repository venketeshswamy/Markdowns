# SA 540: Auditing Accounting Estimates, Including Fair Value Accounting Estimates, and Related Disclosures

## Objective
- 🎯 The auditor shall obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 6)
  - Accounting estimates are reasonable (Para 6(a))
  - Related disclosures are adequate (Para 6(b))

## Scope
- Applies to: Auditor responsibilities regarding accounting estimates (Para 1)
- Expands on: SA 315 and SA 330 application (Para 1)
- Effective from: April 1, 2009 (Para 5)

## Definitions
- 📌 Accounting estimate (Para 7(a)): An approximation of a monetary amount in the absence of a precise means of measurement. This term is used for an amount measured at fair value where there is estimation uncertainty, as well as for other amounts that require estimation. Where this SA addresses only accounting estimates involving measurement at fair value, the term “fair value accounting estimates” is used.
- 📌 Auditor’s point estimate or auditor’s range (Para 7(b)): The amount, or range of amounts, respectively, derived from audit evidence for use in evaluating management’s point estimate.
- 📌 Estimation uncertainty (Para 7(c)): The susceptibility of an accounting estimate and related disclosures to an inherent lack of precision in its measurement.
- 📌 Management bias (Para 7(d)): A lack of neutrality by management in the preparation and presentation of information.
- 📌 Management’s point estimate (Para 7(e)): The amount selected by management for recognition or disclosure in the financial statements as an accounting estimate.
- 📌 Outcome of an accounting estimate (Para 7(f)): The actual monetary amount which results from the resolution of the underlying transaction(s), event(s) or condition(s) addressed by the accounting estimate.

## Requirements
- Risk Assessment Procedures
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain an understanding of (Para 8)
    - Requirements of applicable financial reporting framework (Para 8(a))
    - How management identifies the TEC that arise for need for estimates (Para 8(b))
    - How management makes the accounting estimates (Para 8(c))
      - Method and model used (Para 8(c)(i))
      - Relevant controls (Para 8(c)(ii))
      - Whether management used an expert (Para 8(c)(iii))
      - Assumptions underlying the estimates (Para 8(c)(iv))
      - Changes in methods from prior period (Para 8(c)(v))
      - Management assessment of estimation uncertainty (Para 8(c)(vi))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall review outcome of prior period estimates (Para 9)
    - ⚠️ Exception: Not intended to call into question prior judgments (Para 9)
- Identifying and Assessing Risks
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate degree of estimation uncertainty (Para 10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine significant risks (Para 11)
- Responses to Assessed Risks
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine if FRF applied appropriately (Para 12(a))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine if methods are appropriate (Para 12(b))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall undertake one or more following responses (Para 13)
    - Determine if subsequent events provide audit evidence (Para 13(a))
    - Test how management made the accounting estimate (Para 13(b))
    - Test operating effectiveness of controls and substantive procedures (Para 13(c))
    - Develop a point estimate or a range (Para 13(d))
      - Understand management assumptions if methods differ (Para 13(d)(i))
      - Narrow range until all outcomes considered reasonable (Para 13(d)(ii))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall consider if specialised skills are required (Para 14)
- Further Substantive Procedures
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate for significant risks (Para 15)
    - How alternative assumptions were considered (Para 15(a))
    - Whether significant assumptions are reasonable (Para 15(b))
    - Management intent and ability to carry out actions (Para 15(c))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall develop a range if (Para 16)
    - ⚠️ Condition: Management has not adequately addressed estimation uncertainty (Para 16)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence whether (Para 17)
    - Management decision to recognise is per FRF (Para 17(a))
    - Selected measurement basis is per FRF (Para 17(b))
- Evaluating Reasonableness and Disclosures
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate whether estimates are reasonable (Para 18)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain evidence about disclosure adequacy (Para 19)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall evaluate disclosure of estimation uncertainty (Para 20)
- Bias, Representations, and Documentation
  - 🎯 The auditor shall review judgments for possible management bias (Para 21)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain written representations from management (Para 22)
  - 🎯 The audit documentation shall include (Para 23)
    - Basis for conclusions on significant risks (Para 23(a))
    - Indicators of possible management bias (Para 23(b))

## Application Guidance
- Explains Nature of Accounting Estimates (Para 2)
  - Estimation involves judgments based on available information (Para A8)
  - Susceptibility to management bias increases with subjectivity (Para A9)
  - Management bias is difficult to detect at account level (Para A10)
  - Specialised assets may lack reliable measurement sources (Para A11)
- Explains Risk Assessment (Para 8)
  - Understanding FRF assists discussion with management (Para A13)
  - Completeness of estimates is an important consideration (Para A17)
  - Smaller entities have less complex business activities (Para A21)
  - Method selection considers asset nature and industry norms (Para A25)
  - Expert engagement depends on specialized or technical nature (Para A29)
  - Assumptions are integral components of accounting estimates (Para A31)
  - Fair value assumptions reflect marketplace participant views (Para A34)
  - Method changes must be demonstrated as more appropriate (Para A37)
- Explains Prior Period Estimates Review (Para 9)
  - Obtains information regarding effectiveness of estimation process (Para A39)
  - Assists in identifying possible management bias indicators (Para A40)
  - Carried out with retrospective review required by SA 240 (Para A41)
- Explains Identifying Risks (Para 10 to 11)
  - Uncertainty depends on judgment, sensitivity, and data reliability (Para A45)
  - Immaterial estimates may have high estimation uncertainty (Para A48)
- Explains Responses to Assessed Risks (Para 12 to 14)
  - Arbitrary estimate changes result in inconsistent financial statements (Para A57)
  - Subsequent events response appropriate if expected to occur (Para A62)
  - Contradictory events may indicate ineffective management processes (Para A65)
  - Testing methods involve accuracy, relevance, and recalculation (Para A69)
  - Model validation requires evaluating theoretical soundness (Para A76)
  - Assumptions evaluation considers interdependency and consistency (Para A78)
  - Developing a range is effective if controls are deficient (Para A87)
- Explains Further Substantive Procedures (Para 15 to 17)
  - Sensitivity analysis used to evaluate alternative outcomes (Para A103)
  - Significant assumptions materially affect estimate measurement (Para A107)
  - Intent evaluation includes reviewing management history and plans (Para A80)
- Explains Evaluating Reasonableness (Para 18)
  - Misstatement is difference between auditor and management estimate (Para A116)
  - Subjective estimate changes may indicate management bias (Para A117)
- Explains Disclosures (Para 19 to 20)
  - Evaluation includes ensuring disclosures meet FRF requirements (Para A120)
  - Inadequate uncertainty disclosure may require audit report modification (Para A123)
- Explains Indicators of Possible Management Bias (Para 21)
  - Indicators affect conclusion on risk assessment appropriateness (Para A124)
- Explains Written Representations (Para 22)
  - Include appropriateness of measurement processes and assumptions (Para A126)
  - Include basis for not recognising or disclosing estimates (Para A127)

## Appendices
- Appendix: Fair Value Measurements (App 1)
  - Financial reporting frameworks prescribe varied guidance (App 1)
  - Definitions ordinarily assume a current arm's length transaction (App 2)
  - Changes in fair value measurements are treated differently (App 3)
  - Presumption that fair value can be measured reliably (App 5)
  - Quoted prices may require adjustment if market inactive (App 6)
  - Underlying concept presumes entity is a going concern (App 7)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 4: Difference between outcome and estimate — does not necessarily represent misstatement
- ⚠️ Para 9: Prior period estimate review — not intended to call into question prior judgments
- ⚠️ Para 16: Management efforts on uncertainty — if inadequate, auditor shall develop a range
- ⚠️ Para 21: Indicators of possible bias — do not themselves constitute misstatements
- ⚠️ Para A44: Difference from prior period — misstatement if available information ignored
- ⚠️ Para A49: Extremely high uncertainty — FRF may preclude recognition in financial statements
- ⚠️ Para A80: FRF limits — may not permit considering management intentions for fair value
- ⚠️ Para A94: Range narrowing limits — may not be possible to narrow below performance materiality

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A2 — Examples of low estimation uncertainty estimates
  - 📋 Accounting estimates arising in non-complex business activities
  - 📋 Accounting estimates frequently made and updated for routine transactions
  - 📋 Accounting estimates derived from readily available data
  - 📋 Fair value estimates where measurement method is simple
  - 📋 Fair value estimates using well-known models with observable inputs
- Para A3 — Examples of high estimation uncertainty estimates
  - 📋 Accounting estimates relating to the outcome of litigation
  - 📋 Fair value estimates for non-publicly traded derivative financial instruments
  - 📋 Fair value estimates using highly specialised entity-developed models
- Para A6 — Examples of situations requiring non-fair value accounting estimates
  - 📋 Allowance for doubtful accounts
  - 📋 Inventory obsolescence
  - 📋 Warranty obligations
  - 📋 Depreciation method or asset useful life
  - 📋 Provision against investment carrying amount where recoverability is uncertain
  - 📋 Outcome of long term contracts
  - 📋 Financial Obligations or Costs arising from litigation settlements
- Para A7 — Examples of situations requiring fair value accounting estimates
  - 📋 Complex financial instruments not traded in open market
  - 📋 Share-based payments
  - 📋 Property or equipment held for disposal
  - 📋 Certain assets or liabilities acquired in a business combination
  - 📋 Transactions involving exchange of assets between independent parties
- Para A19 — Examples of inquiries regarding changes in circumstances
  - 📋 The entity has engaged in new types of transactions
  - 📋 Terms of transactions that gave rise to estimates changed
  - 📋 Accounting policies relating to accounting estimates have changed
  - 📋 Regulatory or other external changes have occurred
  - 📋 New conditions or events have occurred
- Para A118 — Examples of types of misstatements
  - 📋 Misstatements about which there is no doubt
  - 📋 Differences arising from management judgments auditor considers unreasonable
  - 📋 The auditor best estimate of projected misstatements in populations
- Para A120 — Examples of related disclosures in notes
  - 📋 The assumptions used
  - 📋 The method of estimation used including any applicable model
  - 📋 The basis for the selection of the method of estimation
  - 📋 The effect of any changes to the method from prior period
  - 📋 The sources and implications of estimation uncertainty
- Para A125 — Examples of indicators of possible management bias
  - 📋 Changes in estimate based on subjective assessment of changed circumstances
  - 📋 Use of entity assumptions inconsistent with observable marketplace assumptions
  - 📋 Selection of significant assumptions yielding favorable point estimate
  - 📋 Selection of a point estimate indicating pattern of optimism or pessimism

## Logical Flow Chains
- Responding to Assessed Risks (Para 13)
  - Identify assessed risks of material misstatement (Para 13)
    - leads to: Determine testing approach (Para 13)
      - if events provide evidence: Review up to audit report date (Para 13(a))
      - if routine processing: Test how management made estimate (Para 13(b))
      - if controls are well-designed: Test operating effectiveness (Para 13(c))
      - if management process ineffective: Develop point estimate or range (Para 13(d))
        - requires: Narrowing range until outcomes are reasonable (Para 13(d)(ii))
- Evaluating Estimation Uncertainty for Significant Risks (Para 15 to 16)
  - Identify accounting estimate with significant risk (Para 15)
    - leads to: Evaluate management consideration of alternative assumptions (Para 15(a))
      - if adequately addressed: Accept approach and proceed (Para 16)
      - ⚠️ unless management inadequately addressed: Auditor develops a range (Para 16)

# SRS 4400: Engagements to Perform Agreed-upon Procedures regarding Financial Information

## Objective
- The objective of an agreed-upon procedures engagement is for the auditor to carry out procedures of an audit nature to which the auditor and the entity and any appropriate third parties have agreed and to report on factual findings. (Para 4)
  - carry out agreed procedures of audit nature (Para 4)
  - report on factual findings (Para 4)
  - express no assurance in report (Para 5)

## Scope
- Applies to agreed-upon procedures (Para 1)
  - regarding financial information elements or accounts (Para 2)
- Applies to non-financial information (Para 3)
  - ⚠️ provided auditor has adequate knowledge (Para 3)
  - ⚠️ provided reasonable criteria exist (Para 3)
- Effective for engagements after April 1, 2004 (Para 19)

## Definitions
- 📌 Agreed-upon procedures engagement (Para 4): carry out procedures of an audit nature to which the auditor and the entity and any appropriate third parties have agreed and to report on factual findings.

## Requirements
- General Principles
  - 🎯 Auditor should comply with Code of Ethics (Para 7)
  - ⚠️ Independence not required unless engagement terms demand it (Para 7)
  - 🎯 State lack of independence in report if applicable (Para 7)
  - 🎯 Conduct engagement in accordance with SRS (Para 8)
  - ⚠️ Report restricted to agreeing parties (Para 6)
- Engagement Terms
  - 🎯 Ensure clear understanding of procedures and conditions (Para 9)
  - ⚠️ Do not accept if distribution limits conflict with law (Para 9e)
  - ⚠️ If unable to discuss with all receiving parties (Para 10)
    - discuss with appropriate representatives (Para 10)
  - Send engagement letter to document key terms (Para 11)
- Planning and Documentation
  - 🎯 Plan work for effective engagement performance (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Document matters providing evidence for report (Para 14)
  - 🎯 Document evidence that SRS was followed (Para 14)
- Procedures and Evidence
  - 🎯 Carry out agreed-upon procedures (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Use evidence as basis for factual findings (Para 15)
- Reporting
  - 🎯 Describe purpose and procedures in sufficient detail (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Clearly mention no audit or review performed (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Report of factual findings should contain required elements (Para 18)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 3: Non-financial scope — applies only if auditor has knowledge and reasonable criteria exist
- ⚠️ Para 6: Restricted distribution — restricted unless required for wider entities like government
- ⚠️ Para 7: Independence — not required unless objective demands it
- ⚠️ Para 9e: Legal conflict — do not accept engagement if report limitation conflicts with law
- ⚠️ Para 10: Multi-party limits — if unable to discuss with all, discuss with representatives

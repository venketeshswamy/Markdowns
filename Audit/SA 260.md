# SA 260 (Revised): Communication with Those Charged with Governance

## Objective
- 🎯 To communicate clearly with TCWG (Para 9)
  - Auditor's responsibilities in relation to FS audit
  - Overview of planned scope and timing
- 🎯 To obtain information relevant to the audit (Para 9)
  - From TCWG
- 🎯 To provide timely observations (Para 9)
  - Significant findings arising from audit
  - Relevant to oversight of financial reporting
- 🎯 To promote effective two-way communication (Para 9)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audit of financial statements (Para 1)
- Applies irrespective of governance structure or size (Para 1)
- Particular considerations:
  - TCWG involved in managing the entity
  - Listed entities
- Does NOT apply to: Communication with management unless also TCWG (Para 1)
- Effective Date: Audits beginning on/after April 1, 2017 (Para 8)

## Definitions
- 📌 Those Charged with Governance (TCWG) (Para 10a):
  - Person(s) or organization(s) with responsibility for overseeing strategic direction
  - And obligations related to accountability of the entity
  - Includes overseeing financial reporting process
- 📌 Management (Para 10b):
  - Person(s) with executive responsibility for conduct of operations
  - May include some/all of TCWG in some entities

## Requirements
- Identifying TCWG
  - 🎯 Determine appropriate person(s) to communicate with (Para 11)
  - Communication with Subgroup (e.g., Audit Committee) (Para 12)
    - 🎯 Determine if communication with governing body also needed
  - TCWG involved in Management (Para 13)
    - ⚠️ If communicated with person with management role:
      - No need to communicate again in governance role
      - 🎯 Must be satisfied all TCWG are adequately informed

- Matters to be Communicated
  - Auditor's Responsibilities (Para 14)
    - 🎯 Auditor forms opinion on FS prepared by management
    - 🎯 Audit does not relieve management/TCWG of responsibilities
  - Planned Scope and Timing (Para 15)
    - 🎯 Overview of planned scope and timing
    - 🎯 Significant risks identified by auditor
  - Significant Findings from Audit (Para 16)
    - 🎯 Views on qualitative aspects of accounting practices
      - Accounting policies, estimates, disclosures
      - Explain why practice not most appropriate (if applicable)
    - 🎯 Significant difficulties encountered during audit
    - 🎯 Significant matters discussed with management
      - ⚠️ Unless all TCWG are involved in management
    - 🎯 Written representations requested
      - ⚠️ Unless all TCWG are involved in management
    - 🎯 Circumstances affecting auditor's report form/content
    - 🎯 Other significant matters relevant to oversight
  - Auditor Independence (Para 17)
    - ⚠️ Required for: Listed Entities
    - 🎯 Statement of compliance with ethical requirements
    - 🎯 Disclosure of all relationships/fees bearing on independence
      - Allocated to appropriate categories
    - 🎯 Related safeguards applied

- The Communication Process
  - Establishing Process (Para 18)
    - 🎯 Communicate form, timing, and expected content
  - Forms of Communication (Para 19-20)
    - 🎯 Written communication required for:
      - Significant findings (if oral inadequate) (Para 19)
      - Independence matters (Para 20)
    - Oral communication allowed for others
  - Timing (Para 21)
    - 🎯 Communicate on a timely basis
  - Adequacy (Para 22)
    - 🎯 Evaluate if two-way communication was adequate
    - ⚠️ If inadequate:
      - Evaluate effect on risk assessment
      - Evaluate ability to obtain evidence
      - Take appropriate action

- Documentation (Para 23)
  - 🎯 If oral: Document matters, when, and to whom
  - 🎯 If written: Retain copy of communication

## Application Guidance

  - Diversity of governance structures (A1-A8)

  - Law may restrict communication (A10)

  - Helps TCWG understand consequences of work (A11)
  - Does not change auditor's sole responsibility for strategy (A15)
  - ⚠️ Caution: Do not compromise audit effectiveness (A16)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ TCWG involved in Management (Para 13)
  - If communicated to person in management role
  - AND that person has governance role
  - THEN no need to repeat in governance role
  - BUT must ensure all TCWG informed
- ⚠️ Written vs Oral (Para 19-20)
  - Independence: Must be Written
  - Significant Findings: Written if oral "not adequate"
  - Others: Oral permitted (Documented in minutes)
- ⚠️ Predictability (A16)
  - Communicating nature/timing of detailed procedures
  - Risk: Reducing effectiveness by making audit predictable
- ⚠️ Third Parties (A43)
  - Communication prepared for TCWG only
  - Not for third party reliance
  - Disclosure restrictions may apply

## Logical Flow Chains
- Communication Process (Para 18-22)
  - Start: Establish form/timing/content
    - leads to: Two-way dialogue during audit
      - if Adequate: Document and conclude
      - ⚠️ unless Inadequate: Evaluate effect on Risk/Evidence
        - if Unresolved:
          - Modify Opinion (Scope limitation)
          - Communicate with Third Parties/Owners
          - Withdraw (if possible)

- Determining TCWG (Para 11-12)
  - Identify Governance Structure
    - if Subgroup (Audit Committee) exists:
      - Communicate with Subgroup
      - Determine if Governing Body needs info
    - if TCWG = Management:
      - Communicate with Management
      - Ensure all TCWG informed (No duplication needed)

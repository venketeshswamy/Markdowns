# SA 240: The Auditor’s Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements

## 🎯 Scope & Objectives
- **Scope** (Para 1)
    - Expands on SA 315 & SA 330 in relation to fraud.
    - Addresses the **Inherent Limitations** of an audit concerning fraud.
- **Objectives of the Auditor** (Para 10)
    - 1. **Identify & Assess** risks of material misstatement (RoMM) due to fraud (FS & Assertion level).
    - 2. **Obtain SAAE** (Sufficient Appropriate Audit Evidence) through designing & implementing responses.
    - 3. **Respond appropriately** to identified or suspected fraud.

## 📚 Key Concepts & Definitions
- **Fraud Definition** (Para 11)
    - An **intentional act** by one or more individuals (Mgmt, TCWG, Employees, 3rd Parties).
    - Involving the use of **deception** to obtain an **unjust or illegal advantage**.
- **Fraud vs. Error** (Para 2)
    - The distinguishing factor is **Intent**.
    - **Fraud** = Intentional.
    - **Error** = Unintentional.
- **Two Types of Fraud** (Para 3)
    - **1. Fraudulent Financial Reporting (FFR)**
        - *Goal:* Deceive FS users (e.g., Earnings Management).
        - *Techniques:*
            - **Manipulation/Falsification/Alteration** of accounting records/documents.
            - **Misrepresentation/Omission** of events/transactions.
            - **Intentional Misapplication** of accounting principles (classification, disclosure, amount).
    - **2. Misappropriation of Assets (MoA)**
        - *Goal:* Theft of entity assets (often by employees in small amounts, but can be Mgmt).
        - *Techniques:*
            - **Embezzling receipts** (e.g., diverting collections).
            - **Stealing physical assets/IP** (e.g., inventory for personal use).
            - **Causing entity to pay** for goods/services not received (fictitious vendors).
            - **Using entity assets** for personal use (e.g., collateral for personal loan).
- **Fraud Risk Factors (The Triangle)** (Para 11b & A25)
    - Events/conditions indicating:
        - 1. **Incentive / Pressure** (e.g., bonuses, debt covenants).
        - 2. **Opportunity** (e.g., poor internal controls, dominance of mgmt).
        - 3. **Rationalization / Attitude** (e.g., poor ethical values, "I deserve this").

## ⚖️ Responsibilities
- **Management & TCWG** (Para 4)
    - **Primary Responsibility** for Prevention & Detection.
    - Must create a **culture of honesty and ethical behavior**.
    - **Prevention:** Reduce opportunities.
    - **Deterrence:** Persuade individuals not to commit fraud (fear of punishment).
- **The Auditor** (Para 5-8)
    - Responsible for **Reasonable Assurance** that FS are free from material misstatement (Fraud or Error).
    - ⚠️ **Inherent Limitations (High Risk):**
        - Risk of not detecting **Fraud** > Risk of not detecting **Error** (due to concealment/collusion).
        - Risk of not detecting **Management Fraud** > **Employee Fraud** (Mgmt can override controls/manipulate records).

## ✅ Requirements: Professional Skepticism (Para 12-14)
- **Maintenance:**
    - Maintain **Professional Skepticism** throughout the audit.
    - Recognize possibility of fraud *notwithstanding* past honesty/integrity of Mgmt.
- **Documents:**
    - Unless reason to believe contrary, may accept records as genuine.
    - ⚠️ **If authenticity is doubted:**
        - Confirm directly with **Third Party**.
        - Use work of an **Expert** (Para A9).
- **Inquiries:**
    - If responses to inquiries are **inconsistent**, auditor **shall investigate**.

## 🔍 Risk Assessment Procedures (RAP)
- **Engagement Team Discussion** (Para 15)
    - **Mandatory** "Brainstorming" session.
    - **Focus:** How/Where FS is susceptible to fraud.
    - **Benefit:** Share insights from experienced members; set proper tone.
- **Inquiries of Management** (Para 17)
    - Mgmt's **assessment** of fraud risk.
    - Mgmt's **process** for identifying/responding to risks.
    - Communications to **TCWG** and **Employees** regarding ethics/fraud.
- **Inquiries of Others** (Para 18)
    - Employees, Internal Audit, Legal Counsel, Chief Ethics Officer.
    - *Goal:* Get a perspective different from Management.
- **Inquiries of Internal Audit** (Para 19)
    - Knowledge of fraud? Procedures performed?
- **Oversight by TCWG** (Para 20-21)
    - Understand how TCWG oversees Mgmt's fraud risk processes.
- **Analytical Procedures** (Para 22)
    - Evaluate **unusual/unexpected relationships** (e.g., Revenue growing while Cash Flow declines).
- **Evaluation of Fraud Risk Factors** (Para 24)
    - Check for presence of Incentive, Opportunity, Attitude.

## ⚠️ Identification & Assessment (Para 25-27)
- **Revenue Recognition Presumption** (Para 26)
    - **Rebuttable Presumption:** Auditor **shall presume** fraud risk exists in revenue recognition.
    - *Evaluate:* Which revenue types/transactions give rise to risk (e.g., premature recognition).
    - *Rebuttal:* If risk not applicable (e.g., simple leasehold revenue from single tenant), **document reasons** (Para 47).
- **Significant Risks** (Para 27)
    - Treat assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud as **Significant Risks**.
    - **Shall obtain understanding** of related controls (Design & Implementation).

## 🛡️ Responses to Assessed Risks (Para 28-33)
### 1. Overall Responses (FS Level) (Para 29)
- **Personnel:** Assign more experienced staff/specialists (forensic experts).
- **Supervision:** Increase level of supervision.
- **Accounting Policies:** Evaluate for bias (Earnings Management).
- **Unpredictability:** Incorporate **element of unpredictability** in nature, timing, and extent (NTE) of audit procedures (Para A36).
    - *Examples:* Visit unannounced locations, test seldom-used accounts, change timing.

### 2. Audit Procedures (Assertion Level) (Para 30)
- Change **N**ature, **T**iming, and **E**xtent of procedures.
- Seek more reliable evidence (physical observation vs. inquiry).
- Perform substantive testing at period-end rather than interim.

### 3. Response to Management Override of Controls (Para 32)
- *Note:* Risk of override is present in **all** entities (Significant Risk).
- **Required Procedure A: Journal Entries (JEs)**
    - Test appropriateness of JEs recorded in General Ledger & adjustments.
    - (i) **Inquire** about inappropriate/unusual activity.
    - (ii) Select JEs made at **end of reporting period**.
    - (iii) Consider testing JEs **throughout the period**.
- **Required Procedure B: Accounting Estimates**
    - Review for **biases** that represent fraud risk.
    - (i) Evaluate judgments/decisions (even if individually reasonable) for patterns of bias.
    - (ii) Perform **Retrospective Review** of significant estimates in prior year FS.
- **Required Procedure C: Significant Unusual Transactions**
    - Evaluate the **Business Rationale** (or lack thereof).
    - *Indicators:* Complex structures, non-consolidated related parties, transaction lacks commercial substance.

## ⚖️ Evaluation of Audit Evidence
- **Analytical Procedures** (Para 34): Perform near end of audit to corroborate conclusions.
- **Identified Misstatement** (Para 35-36):
    - Evaluate if indicative of fraud.
    - If **Mgmt is involved** (Senior Mgmt):
        - Re-evaluate assessment of RoMM.
        - Re-evaluate **reliability of evidence** and **written representations** (Risk of Collusion).
- **Unable to Continue Engagement** (Para 38):
    - If exceptional circumstances exist:
        - Consider **professional/legal responsibilities** (Report to Regulator?).
        - Consider **withdrawal** (if legally permitted).
        - If withdrawing: Discuss with Mgmt/TCWG and Report reasons to Appointing Authority/Regulator.

## 📝 Written Representations (Para 39)
- Obtain WR from Management/TCWG confirming:
    - 1. They acknowledge responsibility for Internal Control (prevention/detection).
    - 2. Disclosed their **assessment of fraud risk**.
    - 3. Disclosed knowledge of **actual/suspected fraud** involving Mgmt, Employees with IC roles, or others (if material).
    - 4. Disclosed any **allegations** of fraud (whistleblowers).

## 📢 Communication
- **To Management** (Para 40)
    - Communicate identified/suspected fraud on **timely basis** to appropriate level (at least one level above involvement).
- **To TCWG** (Para 41)
    - If fraud involves: (a) Management, (b) Employees with significant IC roles, or (c) Material misstatement.
- **To Regulatory/Enforcement Authorities** (Para 43)
    - Determine if legal duty to report exists (overrides confidentiality).
    - *Indian Context:*
        - **CARO 2020:** Report fraud noticed/reported.
        - **Companies Act (Sec 143(12)):** Report to Central Govt (if amount > threshold).
        - **Bank Audits:** Report to RBI.

## 🧠 Appendix: Fraud Risk Factors (Examples)
- **Incentives / Pressures:**
    - High competition / Declining margins.
    - Operating losses / Bankruptcy threat.
    - Need for additional financing / Debt covenants.
    - Mgmt personal financial guarantees.
    - **Personal Financial Obligations** (Pressure for Asset Misappropriation).
- **Opportunities:**
    - Significant **Related Party Transactions** (not ordinary course).
    - Domination of Mgmt by single person.
    - Complex organizational structure / Tax havens.
    - **High turnover** of Senior Mgmt/Counsel.
    - **Inadequate Internal Control** (Segregation of Duties).
    - Large cash on hand / Small high-value inventory (Diamonds/Chips).
- **Attitudes / Rationalizations:**
    - Poor communication of ethical standards.
    - Excessive interest in stock price.
    - Recurring attempts to justify inappropriate accounting on basis of materiality.
    - Strained relationship with Auditor (denial of access, time pressure).

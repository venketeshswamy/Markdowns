# SA 210: Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements

## 🎯 Objective & Scope
### 🌐 Scope (Para 1)
- Deals with auditor's responsibilities in **agreeing the terms** of the audit engagement with **Management** & **TCWG**.
- ⚠️ **Exclusion**: SA 220 deals with engagement acceptance aspects within auditor's control (e.g., ethics, competence). (Ref: Para A1)

### 🎯 Objective (Para 3)
- To **accept or continue** an engagement **ONLY** when the basis is agreed through:
    - 1️⃣ Establishing whether **Preconditions** are present.
    - 2️⃣ Confirming a **Common Understanding** of terms exists between Auditor and Management.

### 🗓️ Effective Date
- Audits of financial statements for periods beginning on/after **April 1, 2010**. (Para 2)

## 📚 Definitions (Para 4-5)
- **Preconditions for an Audit**:
    - The use by management of an **Acceptable Financial Reporting Framework (AFRF)**.
    - The agreement of management to the **Premise** on which audit is conducted.
- **Management**:
    - References to "management" should be read as "management and, where appropriate, **Those Charged With Governance (TCWG)**".

## ✅ Phase 1: Establishing Preconditions (The "Gatekeeper")

### 1️⃣ Acceptability of Financial Reporting Framework (Para 6a)
- Auditor **shall determine** if the framework to be applied is **acceptable**.
- **How to Determine Acceptability?** (Ref: Para A2-A10, App 2)
    - **Attributes of Acceptable Framework**:
        - Relevance, Completeness, Reliability, Neutrality, Understandability.
    - **Factors to Consider**:
        - Nature of Entity (Business vs NPO).
        - Purpose of FS (General vs Special Purpose).
        - Nature of FS (Complete set vs Single statement).
        - Whether Law/Regs prescribe the framework.
    - 💡 **Presumption**: Standards issued by authorized bodies (e.g., ICAI, IFRS, IPSAS) are presumed acceptable for General Purpose FS.

### 2️⃣ Agreement on Responsibilities (The "Premise") (Para 6b)
- Auditor **shall obtain agreement** that Management acknowledges & understands its responsibility for:
    - **(i) Preparation of FS**:
        - In accordance with the applicable framework (including **Fair Presentation**). (Ref: Para A14)
    - **(ii) Internal Control (IC)**:
        - Such IC as management determines necessary to enable FS prep free from **Material Misstatement** (fraud/error).
        - *Note*: Agreement does not imply auditor finds IC free of deficiency. (Ref: Para A15-A18)
    - **(iii) Access to Evidence**: Providing the auditor with:
        - **a.** Access to **all information** relevant to preparation of FS.
        - **b.** **Additional information** auditor may request. (Ref: Para A19)
        - **c.** **Unrestricted access** to persons within the entity.

### ⛔ Restrictions & Non-Acceptance
- **Scope Limitation (Para 7)**:
    - If management imposes a scope limitation in proposed terms such that auditor believes it will result in **disclaiming an opinion** -> **DO NOT ACCEPT** (unless required by law).
- **Other Factors (Para 8)**:
    - If Preconditions are **not present** -> Discuss with management.
    - **DO NOT ACCEPT** if:
        - Framework is unacceptable (except Para 19 scenario).
        - Agreement on responsibilities (Premise) is not obtained.

## 📝 Phase 2: Agreement on Terms (The "Contract")

### ✍️ Engagement Letter (Para 9-10)
- Auditor **shall agree** terms with Management/TCWG.
- Terms **shall be recorded** in an **Audit Engagement Letter** (or suitable written agreement).
- **Mandatory Contents** (Para 10):
    - 1. **Objective & Scope** of the audit.
    - 2. Responsibilities of the **Auditor**.
    - 3. Responsibilities of **Management**.
    - 4. Identification of applicable **Financial Reporting Framework**.
    - 5. Reference to **Expected Form/Content** of reports (and statement that actual report may differ).
- **Additional Considerations** (Ref: Para A23-A26):
    - Elaboration of scope (applicable laws/SAs).
    - Peer Review requirements.
    - Basis of Fees.
    - Planning arrangements/Audit Team.
    - **Components**: Factors to send separate letter (Who appoints, legal reqs, degree of independence).

### ⚖️ Special Case: Terms Prescribed by Law (Para 11-12)
- If law prescribes terms in **sufficient detail**:
    - Auditor **need not record** them in a new agreement.
    - **EXCEPTION**: Must still record:
        - Fact that Law applies.
        - Management's acknowledgement of responsibilities (Para 6b).
- If law prescribes responsibilities:
    - If **Equivalent** to Para 6b -> Use wording of law.
    - If **Not Equivalent** -> Use description in Para 6b.

## 🔄 Phase 3: Recurring Audits (Para 13)

### 🧠 Assessment
- Auditor **shall assess** whether circumstances require:
    - Revision of terms; OR
    - Reminding the entity of existing terms.

### 📋 Factors Requiring Revision/Reminder (Ref: Para A29)
- **Mnemonic: C-H-A-N-G-E-S**
    - **C**: **Change** in Senior Management.
    - **H**: **Huge** change in nature/size of business.
    - **A**: **Any** revised or special terms.
    - **N**: **New** legal or regulatory requirements.
    - **G**: **Governance** or ownership change (Significant).
    - **E**: **Entity** misunderstands objective/scope.
    - **S**: **Standard** (Framework) change or Reporting requirement change.

## ⚠️ Phase 4: Changes to Engagement Terms (Para 14-17)

### 🛑 Request to Change Terms
- Auditor **shall not agree** to a change where there is **no reasonable justification**. (Para 14)
- **Reasonable Justification** (Ref: Para A30-A31):
    - ✅ Change in circumstances affecting need for service.
    - ✅ Misunderstanding of nature of service originally requested.
- **NOT Reasonable** (Ref: Para A32):
    - ❌ Change relates to incorrect/incomplete information.
    - ❌ To avoid a **Qualified Opinion** or **Disclaimer**.

### 📉 Request to Change to Lower Assurance (Review/Related Service)
- Auditor **shall determine** if reasonable justification exists. (Para 15)
- **Reporting Implications** (Ref: Para A34):
    - Report on new service **shall NOT** refer to:
        - Original audit engagement.
        - Procedures performed (unless it's an Agreed-Upon Procedures engagement).

### 🏁 Outcome
- **If Justified**: Record new terms in engagement letter. (Para 16)
- **If Not Justified** & Management forbids continuing original audit: (Para 17)
    - 1. **Withdraw** (if legally permitted).
    - 2. Determine **Obligation to Report** (to TCWG, Owners, Regulators).

## 🏛️ Special Considerations: Law vs. Standards

### ⚔️ Standards Supplemented by Law (Para 18)
- If law imposes **additional** requirements:
    - Auditor **shall determine** if conflicts exist.
    - Discuss with management to agree on:
        - Additional disclosures; OR
        - Amended description of framework.

### 🚫 Framework Prescribed by Law is Unacceptable (Para 19-20)
- Auditor **shall accept ONLY IF**:
    - **Conditions**:
        - Management agrees to **additional disclosures** to avoid misleading FS.
        - Engagement terms recognize:
            - Auditor's Report will include **Emphasis of Matter (EOM)**.
            - Opinion will **NOT** use phrases "True and Fair" / "Present Fairly" (unless law mandates).
- **If Conditions NOT met + Law mandates audit**:
    - Evaluate effect of misleading nature on Auditor's Report.
    - Reference this matter in engagement terms.

### 📝 Auditor's Report Prescribed by Law (Para 21)
- If law prescribes layout/wording significantly different from SAs:
    - **Evaluate**:
        - Can users misunderstand assurance?
        - Can additional explanation mitigate this?
    - **Conclusion**:
        - If explanation **cannot mitigate** -> **DO NOT ACCEPT** (unless required by law).
        - If required to accept -> **DO NOT** state compliance with SAs in the report.

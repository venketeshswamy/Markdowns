# SRS 4410 (Revised): Compilation Engagements

## Objective
- 🎯 Apply accounting and financial reporting expertise (Para 16a)
  - Assist management in preparation and presentation (Para 16a)
  - Comply with applicable financial reporting framework (Para 16a)
- 🎯 Report in accordance with requirements (Para 16b)

## Scope
- Applies to: Compilation of historical financial information (Para 2)
- Adaptable for: Non-financial or other financial info (Para 2)
- Does NOT apply to: Assurance engagements (Para 6)
  - No requirement to verify accuracy (Para 6)
  - No expression of audit opinion (Para 6)
- Does NOT override: Management or governance responsibilities (Para 8)
- Effective from: Engagements after March 31, 2016 (Para 15)

## Definitions
- 📌 Applicable financial reporting framework (Para 17a): The financial reporting framework adopted by management and, where appropriate, those charged with governance in the preparation of the financial information that is acceptable in view of the nature of the entity and the objective of the financial information, or that is required by law or regulation.
- 📌 Compilation engagement (Para 17b): An engagement in which a practitioner applies accounting and financial reporting expertise to assist management in the preparation and presentation of financial information of an entity in accordance with an applicable financial reporting framework, and reports as required by this SRS.
- 📌 Engagement partner (Para 17c): The partner or other person in the firm who is responsible for the engagement and its performance, and for the report that is issued on behalf of the firm, and who, where required, has the appropriate authority from a professional, legal or regulatory body.
- 📌 Engagement team (Para 17d): All partners and staff performing the engagement, and any individuals engaged by the firm or a network firm who perform procedures on the engagement. This excludes external experts engaged by the firm or a network firm.
- 📌 Misstatement (Para 17e): A difference between the amount, classification, presentation, or disclosure of a reported item in the financial information, and the amount, classification, presentation, or disclosure that is required for the item to be in accordance with the applicable financial reporting framework. Misstatements can arise from error or fraud.
- 📌 Practitioner (Para 17f): A professional accountant in public practice who conducts the compilation engagement.
- 📌 Relevant ethical requirements (Para 17g): Ethical requirements the engagement team is subject to when undertaking compilation engagements. These requirements comprise the Code of Ethics issued by ICAI together with other relevant pronouncements issued by ICAI.
  - 🔗 See Code of Ethics (Para 17g)

## Requirements
- General Conduct
  - 🎯 Have understanding of entire SRS text (Para 18)
  - 🎯 Comply with each requirement (Para 19)
    - ⚠️ Unless requirement is not relevant (Para 19)
  - 🎯 Not represent compliance with SRS (Para 20)
    - ⚠️ Unless complied with all relevant requirements (Para 20)
  - 🎯 Comply with relevant ethical requirements (Para 21)
  - 🎯 Exercise professional judgment (Para 22)
- Engagement Quality Control
  - 🎯 Engagement partner shall take responsibility (Para 23)
    - Overall quality of engagement (Para 23a)
    - Performance per firm quality policies (Para 23b)
- Acceptance and Continuance
  - 🎯 Not accept engagement (Para 24)
    - ⚠️ Unless agreed terms with management (Para 24)
    - Include intended use and distribution (Para 24a)
    - Include applicable framework identification (Para 24b)
    - Include objective and scope (Para 24c)
    - Include practitioner responsibilities (Para 24d)
    - Include management responsibilities (Para 24e)
    - Include expected report form (Para 24f)
  - 🎯 Record agreed terms in engagement letter (Para 25)
  - 🎯 Evaluate need to revise terms (Para 26)
    - On recurring compilation engagements (Para 26)
- Communication
  - 🎯 Communicate with management or TCWG (Para 27)
    - On timely basis regarding important matters (Para 27)
- Performing the Engagement
  - 🎯 Obtain sufficient understanding of (Para 28)
    - Entity business and operations (Para 28a)
    - Applicable financial reporting framework (Para 28b)
  - 🎯 Compile financial information using provided records (Para 29)
  - 🎯 Discuss significant judgments with management (Para 30)
  - 🎯 Read compiled financial info prior to completion (Para 31)
  - 🎯 Request additional or corrected information (Para 32)
    - ⚠️ If aware records are incomplete or inaccurate (Para 32)
  - 🎯 Withdraw from engagement and inform management (Para 33)
    - ⚠️ If management fails to provide requested records (Para 33)
  - 🎯 Propose appropriate amendments to management (Para 34)
    - ⚠️ If info inadequately describes framework (Para 34a)
    - ⚠️ If amendments needed to avoid misstatement (Para 34b)
    - ⚠️ If compiled info is otherwise misleading (Para 34c)
  - 🎯 Withdraw from engagement and inform management (Para 35)
    - ⚠️ If management declines proposed amendments (Para 35)
  - 🎯 Determine professional and legal responsibilities (Para 36)
    - ⚠️ If withdrawal is not possible (Para 36)
  - 🎯 Obtain management acknowledgement of responsibility (Para 37)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 Include significant matters and how addressed (Para 38a)
  - 🎯 Include record of info reconciliation (Para 38b)
  - 🎯 Include copy of final version and report (Para 38c)
- Practitioner Report
  - 🎯 Issue report in writing with elements (Para 40)
    - Report title and addressee (Para 40a-b)
    - Statement practitioner compiled info based on management (Para 40c)
    - Description of management responsibilities (Para 40d)
    - Identification of applicable framework (Para 40e)
    - Identification of financial information elements (Para 40f)
    - Description of practitioner responsibilities (Para 40g)
    - Description of compilation entailments (Para 40h)
    - Explanation it is not assurance engagement (Para 40i)
    - Date, signature, and place (Para 40k-m)
  - 🎯 Include explanatory paragraph (Para 40j)
    - ⚠️ If prepared using special purpose framework (Para 40j)
  - 🎯 Date report on completion date (Para 41)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Scope and General Considerations
  - Practitioner association communicated through report (A2)
  - Mandatory application specified in national settings (A5)
  - Firm relies on internal quality control systems (A9)
- Explains The Compilation Engagement
  - Varies depending on engagement circumstances (A13)
  - May involve additional trial balance prep activities (A14)
  - Frameworks range from general to special purpose (A15)
- Explains Ethical Requirements and Judgment
  - Follow fundamental principles including integrity (A18)
  - Cannot associate with materially false info (A19)
  - Independence not required unless specified by law (A20)
  - Judgment required for framework acceptability (A21)
- Explains Acceptance and Continuance
  - Identify intended use via law or contracts (A27)
  - Ensure management agrees to responsibilities early (A33)
  - Rely on management for complete information (A35)
  - Use engagement letter to avoid misunderstandings (A36)
- Explains Performing the Engagement
  - Breadth of understanding less than management possesses (A42)
  - May assist management with accounting estimates (A44)
  - Reading info fulfills ethical requirements (A45)
  - Materiality considered per applicable framework (A47)
  - Withdrawal opportunity explains ethical obligations (A51)
- Explains Documentation and Reporting
  - Serves as record for future recurring engagements (A53)
  - May include schedule of ledger reconciliations (A55)
  - Special purpose report limits suitability for others (A59)
  - Distribution can be restricted to intended users (A61)

## Logical Flow Chains
- Acceptance and Continuance Process
  - Identify intended use and framework (Para 24)
    - leads to: Evaluate management agreement to responsibilities (Para 24e)
      - if agreed: Record terms in engagement letter (Para 25)
      - ⚠️ unless: Practitioner shall not accept engagement (Para 24)
- Compilation and Review Process
  - Compile financial information using records (Para 29)
    - leads to: Read compiled financial information (Para 31)
      - if incomplete: Request corrected information (Para 32)
        - ⚠️ unless management fails: Withdraw from engagement (Para 33)
      - if misstated or misleading: Propose amendments (Para 34)
        - if management declines: Withdraw from engagement (Para 35)
        - ⚠️ unless withdrawal impossible: Determine legal responsibilities (Para 36)

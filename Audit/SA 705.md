# SA 705(Revised): Modifications to the Opinion in the Independent Auditor's Report

## Objective
- 🎯 The auditor shall express appropriately modified opinion when: (Para 4)
  - Financial statements have material misstatement (Para 4(a))
  - Unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 4(b))

## Scope
- Applies to auditor's responsibility to issue modified report (Para 1)
- Establishes three types of modified opinions (Para 2)
  - Depends on nature of matter (Para 2(a))
  - Depends on pervasiveness of effects (Para 2(b))
- Effective for audits beginning April 1, 2018 (Para 3)

## Definitions
- 📌 Pervasive (Para 5(a)): A term used, in the context of misstatements, to describe the effects on the financial statements of misstatements or the possible effects on the financial statements of misstatements, if any, that are undetected due to an inability to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence. Pervasive effects on the financial statements are those that, in the auditor’s judgment: (i) Are not confined to specific elements, accounts or items of the financial statements; (ii) If so confined, represent or could represent a substantial proportion of the financial statements; or (iii) In relation to disclosures, are fundamental to users’ understanding of the financial statements.
- 📌 Modified opinion (Para 5(b)): A qualified opinion, an adverse opinion or a disclaimer of opinion on the financial statements.

## Requirements
- Circumstances Requiring Modification (Para 6)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall modify the opinion when: (Para 6)
    - Financial statements have material misstatement (Para 6(a))
    - Unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 6(b))
- Determining Modification Type (Paras 7-10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall express qualified opinion when: (Para 7)
    - Misstatements are material but not pervasive (Para 7(a))
    - Undetected effects could be material but not pervasive (Para 7(b))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall express adverse opinion when: (Para 8)
    - Misstatements are both material and pervasive (Para 8)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall disclaim an opinion when: (Para 9)
    - Undetected effects could be material and pervasive (Para 9)
    - ⚠️ Multiple uncertainties prevent forming an opinion (Para 10)
- Management-Imposed Scope Limitations (Paras 11-14)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall request management remove limitation (Para 11)
  - 🎯 If management refuses, communicate to TCWG (Para 12)
    - ⚠️ Unless all TCWG are involved in management (Para 12)
    - 🔗 See SA 260, Para 13 (Para 12)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine implications if evidence lacking (Para 13)
    - Qualify if effects are material but not pervasive (Para 13(a))
    - Withdraw or disclaim if material and pervasive (Para 13(b))
  - 🎯 If withdrawing, communicate identified misstatements to TCWG (Para 14)
- Other Considerations (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall not include unmodified opinion on single element (Para 15)
    - ⚠️ Contradicts adverse or disclaimer on whole statements (Para 15)
- Form and Content of Modified Report (Paras 16-29)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall use specific heading for opinion type (Para 16)
  - 🎯 Qualified opinion shall use "except for the effects" (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Adverse opinion shall use "do not present fairly" (Para 18)
  - 🎯 Disclaimer shall state auditor does not express opinion (Para 19)
  - 🎯 Basis for Opinion shall describe matter causing modification (Paras 20-21)
    - ⚠️ Unless impracticable to quantify financial effects (Para 21)
  - 🎯 Disclaimer omits standard audit evidence statement (Para 26)
  - 🎯 Disclaimer shall amend auditor's responsibilities description (Para 28)
  - 🎯 Disclaimer shall not include Key Audit Matters (Para 29)
    - ⚠️ Unless required by law or regulation (Para 29)
- Communication with TCWG (Para 30)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall communicate expected modification circumstances (Para 30)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Modification Types (A1)
  - Provides matrix of judgment on pervasiveness and nature (A1)
- Explains Material Misstatements (A2-A7)
  - Arise from appropriateness of accounting policies (A3-A5)
  - Arise from application of accounting policies (A6)
  - Arise from appropriateness or adequacy of disclosures (A7)
- Explains Scope Limitations (A8-A12)
  - Circumstances beyond control of entity (A8(a), A10)
  - Relating to nature or timing of work (A8(b), A11)
  - Limitations imposed by management (A8(c), A12)
  - Alternate procedures may resolve specific limitations (A9)
- Explains Consequence of Limitations (A13-A16)
  - Withdrawal practicality depends on engagement stage (A13)
  - ⚠️ Withdrawal may be prohibited by law or regulation (A14)
  - Does not contradict if diff framework or specific elements (A16)
- Explains Form and Content (A17-A26)
  - Consistent wording desirable but uniformity not always possible (A21)
  - Omitted disclosures not practicable if voluminous or unavailable (A23)
  - Disclaimer still requires disclosing other identified matters (A24)
  - Key Audit Matters in disclaimer may suggest credibility (A26)
- Explains Communication with TCWG (A27)
  - Enables seeking concurrence or confirming disagreements (A27)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 10: Multiple uncertainties — disclaimer required even if individual evidence obtained.
- ⚠️ Para 12: Communicate limitation refusal to TCWG — unless all TCWG manage entity.
- ⚠️ Para 13: Withdraw if pervasive — unless withdrawal not practicable, then disclaim.
- ⚠️ Para 15: Piecemeal opinion prohibited — adverse/disclaimer overrides specific item unmodified opinion.
- ⚠️ Para 21: Quantify financial effects in Basis section — unless impracticable.
- ⚠️ Para 23: Include omitted disclosures — unless prohibited by law or unduly voluminous.
- ⚠️ Para 29: Do not include KAM or Other Info — unless required by law.

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A10 — Examples of circumstances beyond entity control
  - 📋 The entity's accounting records have been destroyed
  - 📋 Records of a significant component seized by governmental authorities
- Para A11 — Examples of nature or timing of auditor's work
  - 📋 Unable to obtain evidence for equity method associate
  - 📋 Timing of appointment prevents observing physical inventory
  - 📋 Substantive procedures alone insufficient and controls ineffective
- Para A12 — Examples of management-imposed limitations
  - 📋 Management prevents observing physical inventory counting
  - 📋 Management prevents requesting external confirmation of specific balances
- Para A16 — Examples not contradicting adverse or disclaimer opinion
  - 📋 Unmodified opinion under given framework, adverse under different framework
  - 📋 Disclaimer on results of operations, unmodified on financial position
- Para A23 — Examples when disclosing omitted information is not practicable
  - 📋 Disclosures not prepared by management or not readily available
  - 📋 Disclosures would be unduly voluminous in relation to report

## Logical Flow Chains
- Management Limitation Process (Paras 11-14)
  - Limitation requires modified opinion (Para 11)
    - leads to: Request management remove limitation (Para 11)
      - if refused: Communicate TCWG and seek alt evidence (Para 12)
        - if still missing: Qualify, Disclaim, or Withdraw (Para 13)

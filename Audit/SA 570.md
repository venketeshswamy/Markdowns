# SA 570 (Revised): Going Concern

## Objective
- 🎯 Obtain evidence on appropriateness of going concern (GC) basis (Para 9a)
- 🎯 Conclude if material uncertainty exists (Para 9b)
  - Related to events or conditions
  - That may cast significant doubt on ability to continue
- 🎯 Report in accordance with this SA (Para 9c)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audit of financial statements (Para 1)
- Focus: Auditor responsibilities regarding GC and reporting implications
- Concept: GC basis assumes entity will continue for foreseeable future (Para 2)
  - ⚠️ Unless management intends to liquidate
  - ⚠️ Or cease operations
  - ⚠️ Or has no realistic alternative
- Effective Date: Audits beginning on/after April 1, 2017 (Para 8)

## Definitions
- 📌 Material Uncertainty (Para 18/A21):
  - Uncertainty related to events or conditions
  - May cast significant doubt on entity's ability to continue
  - Magnitude and likelihood require appropriate disclosure
  - Necessary for fair presentation or not misleading view

## Requirements

- Risk Assessment Procedures
  - 🎯 Consider if events/conditions exist casting significant doubt (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Determine if management performed preliminary assessment (Para 10)
    - If Yes: Discuss assessment and identified events (Para 10a)
    - If No: Discuss basis for GC use and inquire about events (Para 10b)
  - 🎯 Remain alert throughout audit for new evidence (Para 11)

- Evaluating Management's Assessment
  - 🎯 Evaluate management's assessment of ability to continue (Para 12)
  - 🎯 Cover same period as management (min 12 months) (Para 13)
    - ⚠️ If less than 12 months: Request extension to 12 months
  - 🎯 Consider all relevant information auditor is aware of (Para 14)

- Period Beyond Assessment
  - 🎯 Inquire regarding events beyond assessment period (Para 15)
    - Focus: Events casting significant doubt
    - ⚠️ No other procedures required unless events identified (A15)

- Additional Procedures (When Events Identified)
  - 🎯 Perform procedures to determine if Material Uncertainty exists (Para 16)
    - (a) Request assessment if not yet done
    - (b) Evaluate plans for future actions (feasibility/likely outcome)
    - (c) Evaluate cash flow forecast (if prepared/significant)
      - (i) Reliability of underlying data
      - (ii) Support for assumptions
    - (d) Consider additional facts since assessment date
    - (e) Request written representations regarding plans/feasibility

- Auditor Conclusions
  - 🎯 Conclude on appropriateness of GC basis use (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Conclude if Material Uncertainty (MU) exists (Para 18)
    - Based on audit evidence
    - Judgment on magnitude and likelihood
  - 🎯 Determine adequacy of disclosures if MU exists (Para 19)
    - (a) Principal events/conditions and plans
    - (b) Clearly disclose MU and potential inability to realize assets

- Implications for Auditor's Report
  - ⚠️ Use of GC Basis is Inappropriate (Para 21)
    - 🎯 Express Adverse Opinion
    - Regardless of disclosure
  - ⚠️ GC Basis Appropriate BUT Material Uncertainty Exists (Para 22-23)
    - Case A: Adequate Disclosure Made (Para 22)
      - 🎯 Express Unmodified Opinion
      - 🎯 Include separate section "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern"
      - Draw attention to note in FS
      - State that opinion is not modified
    - Case B: Adequate Disclosure NOT Made (Para 23)
      - 🎯 Express Qualified or Adverse Opinion (per SA 705)
      - State in Basis for Opinion that MU exists
      - State that FS do not adequately disclose

- Other Scenarios
  - 🎯 Events identified but NO Material Uncertainty (Para 20)
    - Evaluate if FS provide adequate disclosure per framework
  - 🎯 Management Unwilling to Assess (Para 24)
    - Consider implications for report (Qualified or Disclaimer)
  - 🎯 Communication with TCWG (Para 25)
    - Events/conditions identified
    - Whether they constitute MU
    - Appropriateness of GC use
    - Adequacy of disclosures
    - Report implications
  - 🎯 Significant Delay in Approval (Para 26)
    - Inquire reasons
    - If related to GC: Perform additional procedures

## Application Guidance
- Explains Scope
  - 🔗 SA 701: MU is by nature a Key Audit Matter (A1)
- Explains GC Basis
  - Public Sector: Funding/policy decisions affect GC (A2)
- Explains Risk Assessment
  - Significance mitigated by management plans (A4)
  - Small Entities: Ability to withstand shocks, bank support (A5-A6)
  - 🔗 SA 315: Revise risk assessment if new events found (A7)
- Explains Evaluation
  - History of profits may reduce need for detailed procedures (A9)
  - Process/assumptions evaluation (A10)
  - Small Entities: Owner-manager support, personal guarantees (A12-A13)
- Explains Additional Procedures
  - Reading minutes, legal counsel inquiry, borrowing terms (A16)
  - Assessing plans to liquidate/borrow/restructure (A17)
  - Comparing prospective info with historical results (A18)
  - Third party support: Confirmations of legality/ability (A19)
  - Written Reps: Focus on specific plans (A20)
- Explains Reporting
  - Adverse opinion if GC inappropriate (A26)
    - Even if prepared on liquidation basis (if acceptable) -> Emphasis of Matter (A27)
  - Disclaimer: Extremely rare cases of multiple uncertainties (A33)
  - Regulators: Duty to communicate (A34)
  - Refusal to assess: Lack of evidence -> Qualified/Disclaimer (A35)

## ICAI Listed Examples

- Para A3 — Events/Conditions Casting Doubt (Financial)
  - 📋 Net liability or net current liability position
  - 📋 Fixed-term borrowings approaching maturity without renewal
  - 📋 Excessive reliance on short-term borrowings for long-term assets
  - 📋 Indications of withdrawal of financial support by creditors
  - 📋 Negative operating cash flows (historical or prospective)
  - 📋 Adverse key financial ratios
  - 📋 Substantial operating losses
  - 📋 Significant deterioration in value of assets used to generate cash
  - 📋 Arrears or discontinuance of dividends
  - 📋 Inability to pay creditors on due dates
  - 📋 Inability to comply with loan agreement terms
  - 📋 Change from credit to cash-on-delivery by suppliers
  - 📋 Inability to obtain financing for essential development

- Para A3 — Events/Conditions Casting Doubt (Operating)
  - 📋 Management intentions to liquidate or cease operations
  - 📋 Loss of key management without replacement
  - 📋 Loss of major market, key customer, franchise, or license
  - 📋 Loss of principal supplier
  - 📋 Labor difficulties
  - 📋 Shortages of important supplies
  - 📋 Emergence of a highly successful competitor

- Para A3 — Events/Conditions Casting Doubt (Other)
  - 📋 Non-compliance with capital or statutory requirements
  - 📋 Pending legal/regulatory proceedings with likely unpayable claims
  - 📋 Changes in law/regulation adversely affecting entity
  - 📋 Uninsured or underinsured catastrophes

- Para A16 — Additional Audit Procedures
  - 📋 Analyzing and discussing cash flow/profit forecasts
  - 📋 Analyzing latest interim financial statements
  - 📋 Reading debenture/loan terms for breaches
  - 📋 Reading minutes of shareholder/TCWG meetings
  - 📋 Inquiring of legal counsel regarding litigation/claims
  - 📋 Confirming existence/legality of financial support arrangements
  - 📋 Assessing financial ability of supporting parties
  - 📋 Evaluating plans for unfilled customer orders
  - 📋 Performing subsequent events procedures
  - 📋 Confirming existence/adequacy of borrowing facilities
  - 📋 Reviewing regulatory reports
  - 📋 Determining support for planned asset disposals

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Small Entities (A13):
  - Reliance on owner-manager support
  - Need evidence of subordination of owner loans
  - Need evidence of personal assets supporting guarantees
- ⚠️ Period of Assessment (Para 13):
  - If < 12 months: Auditor MUST request extension
  - If management refuses: Consider Para 24 implications
- ⚠️ Public Sector (A2):
  - GC risks linked to government support/funding policy
  - Not limited to solvency issues
- ⚠️ Fair Presentation vs Compliance (Para 18):
  - Fair Presentation: Disclosure needed for fair view
  - Compliance: Disclosure needed so FS not misleading
- ⚠️ Disclaimer of Opinion (A33):
  - Only in extremely rare cases
  - Involving multiple significant uncertainties

## Logical Flow Chains
- Risk Assessment Flow
  - Start: Risk Assessment Procedures (Para 10)
    - leads to: Identification of Events/Conditions
      - if Yes: Perform Additional Procedures (Para 16)
        - leads to: Conclusion on Material Uncertainty (Para 18)
      - if No: Remain Alert (Para 11)

- Reporting Decision Tree
  - Step 1: Is GC Basis Appropriate? (Para 21)
    - if No: Adverse Opinion (Para 21)
    - if Yes: Proceed to Step 2
  - Step 2: Does Material Uncertainty (MU) Exist? (Para 18)
    - if No: Consider if disclosure of events required (Para 20) -> Unmodified
    - if Yes: Proceed to Step 3
  - Step 3: Is MU Adequately Disclosed? (Para 22-23)
    - if Yes: Unmodified Opinion + "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern" section (Para 22)
    - if No: Qualified or Adverse Opinion + State MU in Basis for Opinion (Para 23)

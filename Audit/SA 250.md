# ISA 250 (Revised): Consideration of Laws and Regulations in an Audit of Financial Statements

## 🎯 Overall Objectives of the Auditor (Para 11)
- To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence on compliance with laws and regulations that have a **direct effect** on the financial statements. (Para 11a)
- To perform specified procedures to identify non-compliance with **other laws and regulations** that may have a material effect. (Para 11b)
- To respond appropriately to identified or suspected non-compliance. (Para 11c)

## 📚 Definitions (Para 12, A9-A10)
- **Non-compliance**: Acts of omission or commission, intentional or unintentional, by the entity, management, or others, that are contrary to prevailing laws and regulations. (Para 12)
- ⚙️ **Includes** acts by those charged with governance or other individuals working for the entity. (Para A9)
- ⚙️ **Includes** personal misconduct related to business activities, e.g., a manager taking a bribe to award a contract. (Para A10)
- ⚠️ **Does not include** personal misconduct unrelated to the business. (Para 12)

## ✅ Core Requirements
### ⚙️ The Auditor's Consideration of Laws & Regulations (Paras 13-18)
- **General Understanding**: The auditor shall obtain a general understanding of the applicable legal and regulatory framework and how the entity is complying with it. (Para 13)
- **Procedures for Direct-Effect Laws**: The auditor **shall obtain** sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with laws and regulations that directly affect material amounts and disclosures in the financial statements (e.g., tax, pension laws). (Para 14, A12)
- **Procedures for Other Laws**: The auditor **shall perform** specific procedures to identify non-compliance with other laws that may have a material effect, including: (Para 15, A13-A14)
    - Inquiring of management and TCWG about compliance. (Para 15a)
    - Inspecting correspondence with regulatory authorities. (Para 15b)
- **Professional Skepticism**: The auditor **shall remain alert** during the audit to instances of non-compliance that may be brought to their attention by other audit procedures. (Para 16, A15)
- **Written Representations**: The auditor **shall request** management and TCWG to provide written representations that all known non-compliance has been disclosed. (Para 17, A16)
- **No Further Procedures**: The auditor is **not required** to perform other procedures on compliance unless non-compliance is identified or suspected. (Para 18)

### ⚠️ When Non-Compliance is Identified or Suspected (Paras 19-22)
- **Obtain Understanding**: The auditor **shall obtain** an understanding of the nature and circumstances of the act and evaluate its possible effect on the financial statements. (Para 19, A17-A19)
- **Discuss with Management/TCWG**: The auditor **shall discuss** the matter with the appropriate level of management or TCWG, unless prohibited by law or regulation. (Para 20, A20-A22)
    - If they don't provide sufficient information, the auditor **shall consider** obtaining legal advice. (Para 20)
- **Evaluate Lack of Evidence**: If sufficient information cannot be obtained, the auditor **shall evaluate** the effect of this scope limitation on the audit opinion. (Para 21)
- **Evaluate Implications**: The auditor **shall evaluate** the implications of the non-compliance on the audit, including risk assessment and the reliability of written representations. (Para 22, A23-A25)

### 📢 Communicating & Reporting (Paras 23-29)
- **To Those Charged with Governance (TCWG)**:
    - The auditor **shall communicate** non-compliance matters that come to their attention, unless clearly inconsequential. (Para 23)
    - If the non-compliance is believed to be intentional and material, the auditor **shall communicate** it as soon as practicable. (Para 24)
    - If management or TCWG are involved, the auditor **shall communicate** to the next higher level of authority, or consider legal advice if none exists. (Para 25)
- **In the Auditor's Report**:
    - **Material Misstatement**: If non-compliance has a material effect on the financial statements and is not reflected, the auditor **shall express** a qualified or adverse opinion. (Para 26, A26)
    - **Scope Limitation**: If precluded from obtaining sufficient evidence, the auditor **shall express** a qualified opinion or disclaim an opinion. (Para 27)
    - **Circumstance Limitation**: If unable to determine non-compliance due to circumstances, the auditor **shall evaluate** the effect on the opinion. (Para 28)
- **Outside the Entity**:
    - The auditor **shall determine** if law, regulation, or ethical requirements require or make it appropriate to report non-compliance to an outside authority. (Para 29, A28-A34)
    - ⚠️ **Confidentiality may apply**, and the auditor may need to seek legal advice. (Para A32, A33)

### 📝 Documentation (Para 30, A35-A36)
- The auditor **shall document** identified or suspected non-compliance, including: (Para 30)
    - Audit procedures performed, significant professional judgments, and conclusions reached. (Para 30a, A35)
    - Discussions with management, TCWG, and others, including their responses. (Para 30b, A35)
    - ⚙️ Law, regulation, or ethical requirements may have additional documentation requirements. (Para A36)

## ⚙️ Application & Explanatory Material
### ⛓️ Inherent Limitations of an Audit (Paras 5-7, A3-A5)
- **Responsibility**: The auditor's role is to identify material misstatement, not to prevent or detect all non-compliance. (Para 5)
- **Reasons for Limitations**:
    - Many laws relate to operating aspects and don't affect financial statements. (Para 60)
    - Non-compliance can be concealed through fraud, collusion, etc. (Para 61)
    - The determination of non-compliance is ultimately a legal judgment. (Para 62, A4)
    - Non-compliance further removed from financial transactions is less likely to be detected. (Para 63)
- **Materiality**: Detection of non-compliance, regardless of materiality, may affect the auditor's assessment of management's integrity. (Para A3)

### 📚 Management's Responsibility (Para 3, A1-A2)
- It is the responsibility of management, with TCWG oversight, to ensure the entity's operations comply with laws and regulations. (Para 3, A1)
- 💡 **Examples of control measures** include monitoring legal requirements, implementing internal controls, and having a code of conduct. (Para A2)
    - **Monitoring legal requirements** and designing operating procedures to meet them.
    - **Instituting and operating** appropriate systems of **internal control**.
    - **Developing, publicizing**, and following a **code of conduct**.
    - **Ensuring employees** are properly trained and **understand the code of conduct**.
    - **Monitoring compliance with the code of conduct** and appropriately **disciplining** employees who fail to comply.
    - **Engaging legal advisors** to assist in monitoring legal requirements.
    - **Maintaining a register of significant laws and regulations** and a record of complaints.
    - In larger entities, **assigning appropriate responsibilities to an internal audit function**, an audit committee, or a compliance function.

### ⚙️ Other Considerations
- **Categorization of Laws**: The distinction between direct-effect and other laws is key to the auditor's approach. (Para 6, A6)
    - ⟳ **Direct Effect (Para 6a)**: Laws that determine amounts/disclosures, e.g., tax and pension laws.
    - ⟳ **Other Laws (Para 6b)**: Compliance is fundamental to business, but non-compliance may lead to fines or penalties, e.g., licensing, environmental regulations.
- **Indications of Non-Compliance**: The auditor may be alerted to non-compliance by various indicators, such as investigations, excessive payments, or unusual transactions. (Para A17-A18)
    - ⚙️ **Regulatory and Governmental Actions**
        - Investigations by regulatory organizations and government departments.
        - Payment of fines or penalties.
    - ⚙️ **Financial and Transactional Red Flags**
        - Payments for unspecified services or loans to consultants, related parties, employees, or government employees.
        - Sales commissions or agent's fees that appear excessive in relation to those ordinarily paid by the entity or its industry.
        - Purchasing at prices significantly above or below market price.
        - Unusual payments in cash, purchases in the form of cashier's checks payable to bearer, or transfers to numbered bank accounts.
        - Unusual transactions with companies registered in tax havens.
        - Payments for goods or services made to a country other than the one from which the goods or services originated.
        - Payments without proper exchange control documentation.
    - ⚙️ **Internal System and Control Deficiencies**
        - Existence of an information system that fails, by design or by accident, to provide an adequate audit trail or sufficient evidence.
        - Unauthorized or improperly recorded transactions.
    - ⚙️ **External Information**
        - Adverse media comment.
- **Withdrawal from Engagement**: The auditor may consider withdrawing from the engagement if management/TCWG do not take appropriate remedial action, or if the non-compliance raises questions about their integrity. (Para A25)
- **Public Sector**: Public sector auditors may have additional reporting obligations regarding compliance with laws and regulations. (Para A7, A34)
- **Relationship with other ISAs**: This standard is to be read in conjunction with **ISA 200** (Overall Objectives), and links to **ISA 230** (Documentation), **ISA 315** (Risk Assessment), **ISA 570** (Going Concern), **ISA 580** (Written Representations), and various reporting standards (**ISA 700, 701, 705, 706, 800**). (Paras 5, 7, 13, 26, 27, 28, A5, A26, A31)

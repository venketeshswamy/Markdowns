# SA 701: Communicating Key Audit Matters in the Independent Auditor’s Report

## Objective
- 🎯 The objectives of the auditor are (Para 7)
  - to determine key audit matters
  - having formed an opinion, communicate those matters
  - by describing them in the auditor’s report

## Scope
- Applies to: (Para 5)
  - audits of complete sets of general purpose financial statements
  - of listed entities
  - circumstances when auditor otherwise decides to communicate KAM
  - when required by law or regulation to communicate KAM
- Does NOT apply to: (Para 4)
  - substitute for management financial statement disclosures
  - substitute for expressing modified opinion under SA 705
  - substitute for going concern material uncertainty reporting
  - separate opinion on individual matters
- ⚠️ Exceptions: (Para 5)
  - SA 705 prohibits KAM when auditor disclaims opinion
  - unless such reporting is required by law or regulation
- Effective from: (Para 6)
  - audits of financial statements beginning on or after April 1, 2018

## Definitions
- 📌 Key audit matters (Para 8): Those matters that, in the auditor’s professional judgment, were of most significance in the audit of the financial statements of the current period. Key audit matters are selected from matters communicated with those charged with governance.

## Requirements
- Determining Key Audit Matters
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine matters requiring significant auditor attention (Para 9)
    - from matters communicated with those charged with governance
  - 🎯 The auditor shall take into account the following: (Para 9)
    - Areas of higher assessed risk of material misstatement (Para 9a)
    - 🔗 or significant risks identified in accordance with SA 315 (Para 9a)
    - Significant auditor judgments relating to financial statement areas (Para 9b)
    - involving significant management judgment and high estimation uncertainty (Para 9b)
    - The effect on audit of significant events or transactions (Para 9c)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall determine which of determined matters (Para 10)
    - were of most significance in audit of current period
    - and therefore are the key audit matters
- Communicating Key Audit Matters
  - 🎯 The auditor shall describe each key audit matter (Para 11)
    - using appropriate subheading in separate Key Audit Matters section
    - ⚠️ unless circumstances in paragraphs 14 or 15 apply
  - 🎯 The introductory language in section shall state that: (Para 11)
    - KAM are matters of most significance in professional judgment (Para 11a)
    - matters addressed in context of audit of financial statements (Para 11b)
    - auditor does not provide separate opinion on these matters (Para 11b)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall not communicate matter in KAM section (Para 12)
    - when required to modify opinion under SA 705 for that matter
- Descriptions of Individual Key Audit Matters
  - 🎯 The description of each key audit matter shall include: (Para 13)
    - reference to the related disclosures in financial statements
    - why matter considered one of most significance in audit (Para 13a)
    - how the matter was addressed in the audit (Para 13b)
- Circumstances Not Communicated in Report
  - 🎯 The auditor shall describe each key audit matter unless: (Para 14)
    - ⚠️ law or regulation precludes public disclosure about matter (Para 14a)
    - ⚠️ extremely rare circumstances where adverse consequences outweigh public interest (Para 14b)
    - ⚠️ exception: does not apply if entity publicly disclosed information (Para 14b)
- Interaction with Other Required Elements
  - ⚠️ Matters giving rise to modified opinion or going concern uncertainty (Para 15)
    - are by their nature key audit matters
    - 🎯 shall not be described in Key Audit Matters section
    - 🎯 auditor shall report on these matters per applicable SAs (Para 15a)
    - 🎯 auditor shall include reference to Basis for Opinion sections (Para 15b)
- Form and Content in Other Circumstances
  - 🎯 The auditor shall include a statement to this effect (Para 16)
    - ⚠️ if auditor determines there are no KAM to communicate
    - ⚠️ if only KAM communicated are those addressed by paragraph 15
- Communication with Those Charged with Governance
  - 🎯 The auditor shall communicate with those charged with governance: (Para 17)
    - those matters auditor determined to be key audit matters (Para 17a)
    - ⚠️ if applicable, determination that there are no KAM (Para 17b)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 The auditor shall include in the audit documentation: (Para 18)
    - matters requiring significant attention and rationale for KAM determination (Para 18a)
    - rationale for determination that there are no KAM to communicate (Para 18b)
    - rationale for determining not to communicate a key audit matter (Para 18c)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Scope and General Purpose (A1-A8)
  - Significance involves objective analysis of quantitative and qualitative factors (A1)
  - Users expressed interest in robust dialogue with governance (A2)
  - 🔗 SA 320 explains reasonable financial statement user assumptions (A4)
  - 🔗 SA 706 Emphasis of Matter is not substitute for KAM (A8)
- Explains Determining Key Audit Matters (A9-A30)
  - Decision process selects smaller number from governance communications (A9)
  - Determination limited to current period even if comparatives presented (A10)
  - Prior period KAM may still be useful to consider (A11)
  - Higher assessed risk requires more persuasive audit evidence (A12)
  - Matters posing challenges in obtaining evidence are highly relevant (A13)
  - Complexity affects overall strategy and resource allocation (A14)
  - Preliminary view of KAM may develop at planning stage (A16)
  - More than one para 9 consideration may apply concurrently (A17)
  - ⚠️ Not all SA 315 significant risks require significant auditor attention (A21)
  - 🔗 SA 540 estimates with high uncertainty often require significant attention (A24)
  - Matters of most significance applicable in context of specific audit (A28)
  - Lengthy lists of KAM may contradict notion of most significance (A30)
- Explains Communicating Key Audit Matters (A31-A59)
  - Placing section near opinion gives prominence to engagement-specific info (A31)
  - Order of presentation is matter of professional judgment (A32)
  - 🎯 Auditor should avoid providing original information about entity (A36)
  - ⚠️ Auditor may provide info if necessary and law does not preclude (A36)
  - Reference to disclosures is not mere reiteration of financial statements (A40)
  - Relating matter to specific circumstances prevents overly standardized descriptions (A44)
  - Amount of detail in describing audit response is professional judgment (A46)
  - Challenges exist summarizing procedures in complex judgmental areas (A50)
  - Presumed public interest benefit in providing transparency (A53)
  - Legal advice may be appropriate regarding decision not to communicate (A56)
- Explains Communication with Governance (A60-A63)
  - Timely communication alleviates challenges of robust two-way dialogue (A60)
  - Providing draft report provides opportunity for further clarification (A61)
  - Discussion may cause auditor to re-evaluate determination of no KAM (A63)
- Explains Documentation (A64)
  - Judgments supported by communication records and significant matter documentation (A64)
  - Not required to document why other matters were not KAM (A64)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 5: Disclaimer exception — auditor prohibited from communicating KAM when disclaiming opinion, unless required by law.
- ⚠️ Para 11: Formatting exception — KAM subheading requirements waived if modifying matters under paras 14 or 15 apply instead.
- ⚠️ Para 14(a): Legal restriction — KAM description omitted if law or regulation specifically precludes public disclosure.
- ⚠️ Para 14(b): Extreme circumstances — KAM omitted if adverse consequences outweigh public interest benefits.
- ⚠️ Para 14(b): Public disclosure override — Extreme circumstance exception voided if entity has already publicly disclosed the information.
- ⚠️ Para 15: Structural displacement — Modified opinions and going concern uncertainties are inherently KAM but must be excluded from KAM section to retain separate reporting prominence.

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A15 — Examples of difficulties requiring specific communications
  - 📋 Related party transactions limitations on obtaining audit evidence
  - 📋 Limitations on group audit access to information
  - 📋 Consultation on difficult or contentious matters
- Para A18 — Examples of other matters requiring significant attention
  - 📋 Implementation of a new IT system during the period
  - 📋 Significant changes to an existing IT system
- Para A29 — Examples of considerations determining relative significance
  - 📋 Importance of matter to users understanding of financial statements
  - 📋 Nature of underlying accounting policy and complexity
  - 📋 Nature and materiality of corrected and uncorrected misstatements
  - 📋 Nature and extent of audit effort needed
  - 📋 Nature and severity of difficulties in applying audit procedures
  - 📋 Severity of any control deficiencies identified
  - 📋 Whether matter involved multiple separate but related auditing considerations
- Para A41 — Examples of drawing attention to specific disclosure aspects
  - 📋 Robust disclosure of key assumptions or possible outcomes range
  - 📋 Aspects of going concern events like substantial operating losses
- Para A45 — Examples of principal considerations making matter significant
  - 📋 Economic conditions affecting ability to obtain audit evidence
  - 📋 New or emerging accounting policies consulted within the firm
  - 📋 Changes in entity strategy or business model materially affecting financials
- Para A46 — Examples of describing how matter was addressed
  - 📋 Aspects of auditor response relevant to assessed risk
  - 📋 A brief overview of procedures performed
  - 📋 An indication of the outcome of auditor procedures
  - 📋 Key observations with respect to the matter
- Para A47 — Examples of care needed in descriptive language
  - 📋 Does not imply matter not appropriately resolved by auditor
  - 📋 Relates matter directly to specific entity circumstances avoiding generic language
  - 📋 Takes into account how matter is addressed in related disclosures
  - 📋 Does not contain or imply discrete opinions on separate elements
- Para A54 — Examples of governance communication informing auditor judgment
  - 📋 Assisting auditor in understanding why matter not publicly disclosed
  - 📋 Highlighting communications with regulatory or enforcement authorities
  - 📋 Enabling auditor to encourage management to make public disclosure
- Para A57 — Examples of circumstances requiring statement of no KAM
  - 📋 Auditor determines there are no key audit matters
  - 📋 Key audit matter will not be communicated and no others determined
  - 📋 Only matters determined to be KAM are communicated per paragraph 15

## Logical Flow Chains
- Determining KAM Process (Paras 9-10)
  - All matters communicated with those charged with governance (Para 9)
    - leads to: filter matters requiring significant auditor attention (Para 9)
      - requires: assessing higher risks, significant judgments, and events (Para 9)
        - leads to: select matters of most significance (Para 10)
          - results in: Key Audit Matters (Para 10)
- Decision to Omit KAM Description (Para 14)
  - Matter identified as Key Audit Matter (Para 14)
    - if law or regulation precludes public disclosure: omit description (Para 14a)
    - if extreme adverse consequences outweigh public interest: omit description (Para 14b)
      - ⚠️ unless: entity publicly disclosed information about the matter (Para 14b)




# SAE 3402: Assurance Reports on Controls at a Service Organisation

## Objective
- To obtain reasonable assurance about whether based on suitable criteria (Para 8a)
  - Description fairly presents system designed and implemented (Para 8a(i))
  - Controls relating to control objectives were suitably designed throughout the specified period (Para 8a(ii))
  - Controls operated effectively if included in scope (Para 8a(iii))
- To report on matters in accordance with auditor findings (Para 8b)

## Scope
- Applies to: Assurance engagements on controls relevant to financial reporting (Para 1)
- Does NOT apply to: Reporting only whether controls operated as described (Para 3a)
- Does NOT apply to: Controls affecting only production or quality (Para 3b)
- Effective from: April 1, 2011 (Para 7)

## Definitions
- 📌 Carve-out method (Para 9a): Method of dealing with the services provided by a subservice organization, whereby the service organization’s description of its system includes the nature of the services provided by a subservice organization, but that subservice organization’s relevant control objectives and related controls are excluded from the service organization’s description of its system and from the scope of the service auditor’s engagement. The service organization’s description of its system and the scope of the service auditor’s engagement include controls at the service organization to monitor the effectiveness of controls at the subservice organization, which may include the service organization’s review of an assurance report on controls at the subservice organization.
- 📌 Complementary user entity controls (Para 9b): Controls that the service organization assumes, in the design of its service, will be implemented by user entities, and which, if necessary to achieve control objectives stated in the service organization’s description of its system, are identified in that description.
- 📌 Control objective (Para 9c): The aim or purpose of a particular aspect of controls. Control objectives relate to risks that controls seek to mitigate.
- 📌 Controls at the service organization (Para 9d): Controls over the achievement of a control objective that is covered by the service auditor’s assurance report.
- 📌 Controls at a subservice organization (Para 9e): Controls at a subservice organization to provide reasonable assurance about the achievement of a control objective.
- 📌 Criteria (Para 9f): Benchmarks used to evaluate or measure a subject matter including, where relevant, benchmarks for presentation and disclosure.
- 📌 Inclusive method (Para 9g): Method of dealing with the services provided by a subservice organization, whereby the service organization’s description of its system includes the nature of the services provided by a subservice organization, and that subservice organization’s relevant control objectives and related controls are included in the service organization’s description of its system and in the scope of the service auditor’s engagement.
- 📌 Internal audit function (Para 9h): An appraisal activity established or provided as a service to the service organization. Its functions include, amongst other things, examining, evaluating and monitoring the adequacy and effectiveness of internal control.
- 📌 Internal auditors (Para 9i): Those individuals who perform the activities of the internal audit function. Internal auditors may belong to an internal audit department or equivalent function.
- 📌 Report on the description and design of controls at a service organization (referred to in this SAE as a “type 1 report”) (Para 9j): A report that comprises: The service organization’s description of its system; A written assertion by the service organization that, in all material respects, and based on suitable criteria: The description fairly presents the service organization’s system as designed and implemented as at the specified date; The controls related to the control objectives stated in the service organization’s description of its system were suitably designed as at the specified date; and A service auditor’s assurance report that conveys reasonable assurance about the matters in above.
- 📌 Report on the description, design and operating effectiveness of controls at a service organization (referred to in this SAE as a “type 2 report”) (Para 9k): A report that comprises: The service organization’s description of its system; A written assertion by the service organization that, in all material respects, and based on suitable criteria: The description fairly presents the service organization’s system as designed and implemented throughout the specified period; The controls related to the control objectives stated in the service organization’s description of its system were suitably designed throughout the specified period; and The controls related to the control objectives stated in the service organization’s description of its system operated effectively throughout the specified period; and A service auditor’s assurance report that: Conveys reasonable assurance about the matters above; and Includes a description of the tests of controls and the results thereof.
- 📌 Service auditor (Para 9l): A professional accountant in public practice who, at the request of the service organization, provides an assurance report on controls at a service organization.
- 📌 Service organization (Para 9m): A third-party organization (or segment of a third-party organization) that provides services to user entities that are likely to be relevant to user entities’ internal control as it relates to financial reporting.
- 📌 Service organization’s system (or the system) (Para 9n): The policies and procedures designed and implemented by the service organization to provide user entities with the services covered by the service auditor’s assurance report. The service organization’s description of its system includes identification of: the services covered; the period, or in the case of a type 1 report, the date, to which the description relates; control objectives; and related controls.
- 📌 Service organization’s assertion (Para 9o): The written assertion about the matters referred to in paragraph 9(k)(ii) (or paragraph 9(j)(ii) in the case of a type 1 report).
- 📌 Subservice organization (Para 9p): A service organization used by another service organization to perform some of the services provided to user entities that are likely to be relevant to user entities’ internal control as it relates to financial reporting.
- 📌 Test of controls (Para 9q): A procedure designed to evaluate the operating effectiveness of controls in achieving the control objectives stated in the service organization’s description of its system.
- 📌 User auditor (Para 9r): An auditor who audits and reports on the financial statements of a user entity.
- 📌 User entity (Para 9s): An entity that uses a service organization.

## Requirements
- Framework and Ethics
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall comply with Framework requirements (Para 10)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall comply with relevant ethical requirements (Para 11)
- Management and Governance
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine appropriate person to interact with (Para 12)
- Acceptance and Continuance
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine capabilities and criteria suitability (Para 13a)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall obtain agreement of service organization responsibility (Para 13b)
    - Preparation of description and accompanying assertion (Para 13b(i))
    - Reasonable basis for the assertion (Para 13b(ii))
    - Stating criteria and control objectives (Para 13b(iii-iv))
    - Identifying risks threatening achievement of objectives (Para 13b(v))
    - Providing unrestricted access to information and persons (Para 13b(vi))
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall be satisfied reasonable justification exists for scope change (Para 14)
- Assessing Suitability of Criteria
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall assess whether suitable criteria are used (Para 15)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine if criteria encompass system design elements (Para 16)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine if criteria evaluate design suitably (Para 17)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine if criteria evaluate operating effectiveness (Para 18)
- Materiality
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall consider materiality when planning and performing (Para 19)
- Understanding the System
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall obtain understanding of service organization system (Para 20)
- Evidence Regarding Description
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall obtain and read description and evaluate presentation (Para 21)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine whether system has been implemented (Para 22)
- Evidence Regarding Design
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine necessary controls and assess suitable design (Para 23)
- Evidence Regarding Operating Effectiveness
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall test necessary controls and assess operating effectiveness (Para 24)
    - ⚠️ Prior period evidence does not provide basis for reduction (Para 24)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall perform combined procedures regarding control application (Para 25)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall consider population characteristics when determining test extent (Para 26)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall use appropriate selection and replacement when sampling (Para 27)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall investigate nature and cause of any deviations (Para 28)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall obtain high degree of certainty for anomalies (Para 29)
- Using Internal Audit Function
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall obtain understanding of internal audit responsibilities (Para 30)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine adequacy and planned effect of work (Para 31)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall evaluate objectivity and technical competence (Para 32)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall consider specific work scope and subjectivity (Para 33)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall evaluate and perform procedures on specific work (Para 34)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall evaluate training, supervision, and appropriate conclusions (Para 35)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall make no reference to work in opinion (Para 36)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall describe internal audit work in Type 2 report (Para 37)
- Written Representations
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall request written representations from service organization (Para 38)
  - 🎯 Written representations shall be letter addressed near report date (Para 39)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall disclaim opinion if representations are refused (Para 40)
- Other Information
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall read other information to identify material inconsistencies (Para 41)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall discuss and take appropriate action if uncorrected (Para 42)
- Subsequent Events
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall inquire about significant subsequent events (Para 43)
    - ⚠️ No obligation to perform procedures after assurance report date (Para 44)
- Documentation
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall prepare sufficient documentation for experienced auditor (Para 45)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall record identifying characteristics and reviewer dates (Para 46)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall document conclusions regarding internal audit work (Para 47)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall document discussions of significant matters (Para 48)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall document how inconsistent information was addressed (Para 49)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall assemble documentation in final file timely (Para 50)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall not discard documentation before retention period ends (Para 51)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall document reasons for modifying existing documentation (Para 52)
- Preparing the Assurance Report
  - 🎯 The service auditor assurance report shall include specific basic elements (Para 53)
  - 🎯 Type 2 reports shall include separate section describing control tests (Para 54)
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall modify opinion if material misstatement or limitation exists (Para 55)
- Other Communication
  - 🎯 The service auditor shall determine appropriate communication of identified non-compliance (Para 56)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Scope
  - Controls over operations relevant to user entity financial reporting (Para A1)
  - May perform agreed-upon procedures if suitability assertion is absent (Para A2)
- Explains Definitions
  - Controls include environment and monitoring related to services provided (Para A3)
  - Inclusive method entails coordination between service and subservice organizations (Para A4)
- Explains Ethical Requirements
  - Auditor ordinarily subject to ICAI Code of Ethics independence rules (Para A5)
- Explains Governance
  - Identifying appropriate management structure requires exercise of professional judgment (Para A6)
- Explains Acceptance and Continuance
  - Refusal to provide assertion is scope limitation causing withdrawal (Para A8)
  - Monitoring activities do not substitute service auditor testing procedures (Para A9)
  - Formal and informal processes identify risks threatening control objectives (Para A10)
  - Request to exclude subservice organization may be reasonable justification (Para A12)
- Explains Assessing Criteria
  - Criteria must be available to intended users to understand basis (Para A13)
  - Elements may not be appropriate if system relates to general IT hosting (Para A15)
- Explains Materiality
  - Materiality relates to service organization system, not user financials (Para A16)
  - Materiality not applied when disclosing results of identified test deviations (Para A18)
- Explains Understanding System
  - Understanding boundaries assists in determining necessary controls and design (Para A19)
  - Assessing completeness ensures framework meets broad range user needs (Para A23)
  - Testing implementation may be performed with understanding procedures (Para A24)
- Explains Evidence on Design and Operating Effectiveness
  - Effective controls provide reasonable assurance material misstatements are prevented (Para A28)
  - Design understanding insufficient for operating effectiveness unless automation present (Para A29)
  - Type 2 report ordinarily covers a minimum period of six months (Para A30)
  - Inherent IT consistency allows application controls evidence via general controls (Para A34)
  - Selective examination does not constitute statistically valid sampling projection (Para A36)
- Explains Internal Audit Function
  - Less extensive procedures indicated if internal audit work is limited (Para A38)
  - Auditor holds sole responsibility for opinion despite using internal audit (Para A40)
- Explains Written Representations
  - Refusal of disclosures requires appropriate modification of auditor opinion (Para A43)
- Explains Other Information
  - Auditor must not be associated with materially false or misleading information (Para A44)
- Explains Documentation
  - Assembly of final file ordinarily not more than 60 days (Para A46)
- Explains Preparing the Report
  - Report criteria relevant only for users with system understanding (Para A48)
  - Description of tests should include causative factors for identified deviations (Para A49)
  - Identifying procedures in disclaimer of opinion might overshadow scope limitation (Para A52)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 14: Change in scope requested — Must be satisfied reasonable justification exists
  - If condition met: Accept change in engagement terms
  - If condition not met: Do not accept scope limitation
- ⚠️ Para 24: Satisfactory operation in prior periods — Does not provide reduction basis
  - If condition met: Perform full testing in current period
  - If condition not met: Testing extent remains unchanged
- ⚠️ Para 29: Deviation is an anomaly — Requires additional procedures for certainty
  - If condition met: Obtain evidence deviation does not affect population
  - If condition not met: Project deviation to population evaluation
- ⚠️ Para 40: Written representations refused — Auditor shall disclaim an opinion
  - If condition met: Issue disclaimer of opinion
  - If condition not met: Proceed with normal reporting procedures
- ⚠️ Para 44: Post-report events — No obligation to perform procedures
  - If condition met: No further testing required
  - If condition not met: Assess impact if independently made aware
- ⚠️ Para 55: Deficient evidence or design — Requires modified opinion
  - If condition met: Modify opinion with clear description of reasons
  - If condition not met: Issue unmodified assurance report
- ⚠️ Para A11: Exclude controls to avoid modification — Not a reasonable justification
  - If condition met: Reject change in terms
  - If condition not met: Assess alternative justification validity

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A7 — Examples of relevant capabilities and competence
  - 📋 Knowledge of the relevant industry
  - 📋 An understanding of information technology and systems
  - 📋 Experience in evaluating risks as they relate to suitable design
  - 📋 Experience in the design and execution of tests of controls
- Para A17 — Examples of qualitative materiality factors
  - 📋 Whether description includes significant aspects of processing significant transactions
  - 📋 Whether description omits or distorts relevant information
  - 📋 Ability of controls to provide reasonable assurance objectives achieved
- Para A20 — Examples of procedures to obtain understanding
  - 📋 Inquiring of those within the service organization
  - 📋 Observing operations and inspecting documents, reports, printed and electronic records
  - 📋 Inspecting a selection of agreements between service organization and users
  - 📋 Reperforming control procedures
- Para A21 — Examples of questions evaluating fair presentation of description
  - 📋 Does description address major aspects of service provided?
  - 📋 Is description prepared at sufficient level of detail?
  - 📋 Does description omit or distort information affecting users?
  - 📋 Does description clearly identify excluded objectives?
  - 📋 Have controls identified in the description been implemented?
  - 📋 Are complementary user entity controls described adequately?
  - 📋 Does description separately identify controls if inclusive method used?
- Para A22 — Examples of procedures to evaluate fair presentation
  - 📋 Considering nature of user entities and how services affect them
  - 📋 Reading standard contracts or standard terms with user entities
  - 📋 Observing procedures performed by service organization personnel
  - 📋 Reviewing service organization policy and procedure manuals
- Para A35 — Examples of means of selecting items for testing
  - 📋 Selecting all items (100% examination)
  - 📋 Selecting specific items
  - 📋 Sampling
- Para A39 — Examples of procedures on specific work of internal auditors
  - 📋 Examination of items already examined by the internal auditors
  - 📋 Examination of other similar items
  - 📋 Observation of procedures performed by the internal auditors
- Para A41 — Examples of presenting internal audit work in report
  - 📋 By including introductory material indicating certain work was used
  - 📋 Attribution of individual tests to internal audit
- Para A45 — Examples of further actions if refusal to restate other information
  - 📋 Requesting the service organization to consult with its legal counsel
  - 📋 Describing the material inconsistency in the assurance report
  - 📋 Withholding the assurance report until the matter is resolved
  - 📋 Withdrawing from the engagement
- Para A53 — Examples of appropriate actions regarding non-compliance
  - 📋 Obtaining legal advice about the consequences of different courses of action
  - 📋 Communicating with those charged with governance of the service organization
  - 📋 Communicating with third parties when required to do so
  - 📋 Modifying the service auditor's opinion, or adding an Other Matter paragraph
  - 📋 Withdrawing from the engagement

## Logical Flow Chains
- Accepting a Change in Scope (Para 14)
  - Service organization requests change before engagement completion (Para 14)
    - leads to: Assessing whether reasonable justification exists (Para 14)
      - if reasonable justification: Accept change and continue (Para A12)
      - ⚠️ unless: Request is to exclude controls to avoid modification (Para A11)
- Evaluating Sample Deviations (Para 28)
  - Auditor identifies deviation in sample testing (Para 28)
    - leads to: Investigating nature and cause of deviation (Para 28)
      - if within expected acceptable rate: Conclude control operates effectively (Para 28a)
      - if unacceptable: Determine if additional testing is necessary (Para 28b)
      - if definitive failure: Conclude control did not operate effectively (Para 28c)
- Using Internal Audit Work (Para 30)
  - Determine if internal audit function is relevant to engagement (Para 30)
    - leads to: Evaluating objectivity and technical competence (Para 32)
      - if adequate: Determine planned effect on auditor procedures (Para 31)
        - leads to: Performing procedures on specific internal audit work (Para 34)

# SA 501: Audit Evidence - Specific Considerations for Selected Items

## Objective
- 🎯 The auditor shall obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence (Para 3)
  - Regarding existence and condition of inventory (Para 3a)
  - Regarding completeness of litigation and claims (Para 3b)
  - Regarding presentation and disclosure of segment information (Para 3c)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audit evidence for selected items (Para 1)
- Effective from: April 1, 2010 (Para 2)

## Definitions
- No formal definitions are provided in this SA

## Requirements
- Inventory
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain evidence on existence and condition (Para 4)
    - ⚠️ When inventory is material to financial statements (Para 4)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall attend physical inventory counting (Para 4a)
    - ⚠️ Unless impracticable to do so (Para 4a)
    - Evaluate management instructions and procedures (Para 4a(i))
    - Observe performance of count procedures (Para 4a(ii))
    - Inspect the inventory (Para 4a(iii))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform test counts (Para 4a(iv))
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform procedures over final records (Para 4b)
    - To determine if they accurately reflect count results (Para 4b)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform procedures on inventory changes (Para 5)
    - ⚠️ If count is conducted at a date other than FS date (Para 5)
      - Para A11 — Examples of matters for inventory change procedures
        - 📋 Whether the perpetual inventory records are properly adjusted (A11)
        - 📋 Reliability of the entity's perpetual inventory records (A11)
        - 📋 Reasons for significant differences during the physical count (A11)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall make counts on alternative date (Para 6)
    - ⚠️ If unable to attend due to unforeseen circumstances (Para 6)
    - And perform procedures on intervening transactions (Para 6)
      - Explains Para 5 - Alternate Count Date
        - Appropriateness depends on controls over inventory changes (A9)
        - Differences may indicate controls not operating effectively (A10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform alternative audit procedures (Para 7)
    - ⚠️ If attendance at physical count is impracticable (Para 7)

      - Explains Para 7 - Impracticability
        - May be due to threats to safety of the auditor from nature and location of inventory (A12)
        - ⚠️ General inconvenience or cost is not valid basis (A12)


  - 🎯 The auditor shall modify the opinion (Para 7)
    - ⚠️ If alternative procedures are not possible (Para 7)
    - 🔗 See SA 705(Revised) (Para 7)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain evidence for third-party inventory (Para 8)
    - ⚠️ When third-party custody is material (Para 8)
    - Request confirmation from the third party (Para 8a)
    - Perform inspection or other appropriate audit procedures (Para 8b)


    - Para A16 — Examples of other procedures for third party
      - 📋 Attending physical counting of inventory if practicable (A16)
      - 📋 Obtaining another auditor or service auditor report (A16)
      - 📋 Inspecting documentation regarding inventory held by third parties (A16)
      - 📋 Requesting confirmation when inventory pledged as collateral (A16)


- Litigation and Claims
  - 🎯 The auditor shall design and perform identification procedures (Para 9)
    - Inquiry of management and in-house counsel (Para 9a)
    - Reviewing minutes and external counsel correspondence (Para 9b)
    - Reviewing legal expense accounts (Para 9c)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall seek direct communication with external counsel (Para 10)
    - ⚠️ If risk of material misstatement is assessed (Para 10)
    - ⚠️ Or if audit procedures indicate other material litigation (Para 10)
    - Through a letter of inquiry prepared by management (Para 10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall perform alternative audit procedures (Para 10)
    - ⚠️ If law prohibits external counsel from communicating directly (Para 10)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall modify the opinion (Para 11)
    - ⚠️ If management refuses permission to communicate (Para 11a)
    - ⚠️ Or external counsel refuses to respond appropriately (Para 11a)
    - ⚠️ And auditor is unable to obtain alternative evidence (Para 11b)
  - 🎯 The auditor shall request written representations (Para 12)
    - That all known actual or possible litigation is disclosed (Para 12)
- Segment Information
  - 🎯 The auditor shall obtain evidence regarding presentation and disclosure (Para 13)
    - Obtaining understanding of methods used by management (Para 13a)
    - Evaluating whether methods result in proper disclosure (Para 13a(i))
    - Testing the application of such methods where appropriate (Para 13a(ii))
    - Performing analytical or other appropriate procedures (Para 13b)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 4(a) - Physical Count
  - Counting ordinarily serves as basis for FS preparation (A1)
  - Procedures may serve as test of controls or substantive (A2)
  - Inspecting assists in ascertaining existence and condition (A6)
  - Test counts provide evidence about completeness and accuracy (A7)
  - Obtaining management count records assists subsequent procedures (A8)


  - Subsequent sale inspection may provide alternative evidence (A13)
- Explains Para 8 - Third Party Inventory
  - 🔗 SA 505 guides external confirmation procedures (A15)
  - Doubts about third-party integrity require other procedures (A16)
- Explains Para 9 - Identifying Litigations
  - Risk assessment procedures assist in becoming aware (A18)
  - Evidence may also relate to valuation and measurement (A19)
  - May appropriately examine source documents like invoices (A20)
- Explains Para 10-11 - External Counsel
  - Validates known claims and reasonableness of cost estimates (A21)
  - Letter of general inquiry requests known claims list (A22)
  - Meetings ordinarily require management permission and attendance (A24)
  - Report dated only after obtaining sufficient litigation evidence (A25)
- Explains Para 13 - Segment Information
  - Responsibility relates to financial statements as a whole (A26)
  - Opinion on standalone segment information is not required (A26)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 4: Materiality condition — Count attendance required only when inventory is material
- ⚠️ Para 4(a): Impracticability exception — Attendance excused if completely impracticable
- ⚠️ Para 6: Unforeseen circumstances — Auditor may count on alternative date
- ⚠️ Para 10: Legal prohibition — Requires alternative procedures if external counsel cannot respond
- ⚠️ Para 11: Scope limitation — Modify opinion if communication restricted and no alternatives exist
- ⚠️ Para A12: Impracticability limit — General inconvenience or cost are never valid excuses

## Logical Flow Chains
- Inventory Count Process (Paras 4-7)
  - Is inventory material? (Para 4)
    - leads to: Attend physical count (Para 4a)
      - ⚠️ unless impracticable: Perform alternative procedures (Para 7)
        - if not possible: Modify opinion (Para 7)
      - ⚠️ if unforeseen circumstances: Count on alternative date (Para 6)
- Third Party Inventory Process (Para 8)
  - Is third party inventory material? (Para 8)
    - leads to: Request confirmation (Para 8a)
    - or leads to: Perform inspection and other procedures (Para 8b)
- Litigation Communication Process (Paras 10-11)
  - Assess risk of material misstatement (Para 10)
    - leads to: Send letter of inquiry to external counsel (Para 10)
      - if law prohibits response: Perform alternative procedures (Para 10)
      - if management refuses permission: Attempt alternative procedures (Para 11)
        - if alternatives fail: Modify opinion (Para 11)

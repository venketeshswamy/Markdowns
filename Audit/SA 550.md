# SA 550: Related Parties

## 🎯 Objectives & Scope
- **Scope**: Auditor's responsibilities regarding **Related Party (RP)** relationships and transactions.
    - Expands on SA 315, SA 330, and SA 240. (Para 1)
- **Nature of RPs**:
    - Many are normal course of business (Low Risk).
    - Some carry **higher risks of material misstatement** due to complexity, lack of independence, or subjective terms. (Para 2)
- **Auditor's Objectives** (Para 9):
    - 1. Obtain understanding of RP relationships/transactions to recognize **fraud risk factors**.
    - 2. Conclude whether Financial Statements (FS) achieve **true and fair presentation** (or are not misleading).
    - 3. Obtain **Sufficient Appropriate Audit Evidence (SAAE)** regarding identification, accounting, and disclosure.

## 📖 Key Definitions (Para 10)
- **Arm's Length Transaction**
    - Terms/conditions between a **willing buyer** and **willing seller**.
    - Unrelated parties.
    - Acting independently.
    - Pursuing their own best interests. (Para 10(a))
- **Related Party**
    - (i) As defined in the applicable **Financial Reporting Framework** (e.g., AS 18 / Ind AS 24).
    - (ii) If framework is minimal/absent, a person/entity with:
        - **Control** or **Significant Influence** (Direct/Indirect).
        - **Common Control** (Ownership, Close family, Key management). (Para 10(b))
    - 💡 *Note: Entities under common state/govt control are usually excluded unless significant transactions occur.*

## ✅ Risk Assessment Procedures (Planning)
- **Engagement Team Discussion**
    - **Shall** include specific consideration of susceptibility of FS to material misstatement due to **fraud or error** via RPs. (Para 12)
    - 🧠 **Discussion Points** (Para A9):
        - Nature/extent of relationships.
        - Importance of **Professional Skepticism**.
        - Complex structures / Special Purpose Entities (SPEs).
        - Records indicating undisclosed RPs.
- **Inquiries of Management** (Para 13)
    - The **Identity** of RPs (and changes from prior period).
    - The **Nature** of relationships.
    - **Transactions** entered into (Type & Purpose).
    - ⚙️ *Application*: If framework has no RP requirements, auditor still inquires to understand ownership/governance structure. (Para A12)
- **Understanding Controls** (Para 14)
    - Inquire & perform procedures to understand controls over:
        - (a) **Identification, accounting, & disclosure**.
        - (b) **Authorization & approval** of significant transactions.
        - (c) Authorization of transactions **outside normal course**.
    - ⚠️ *Weak Controls*: Lack of oversight or intentional disregard by mgmt indicates Fraud Risk. (Para A18)
- **Remaining Alert (Inspection of Records)** (Para 15)
    - **Shall** remain alert when inspecting records for undisclosed RPs.
    - **Specific Documents to Inspect**:
        - Bank / Legal confirmations.
        - Minutes of Shareholders / TCWG meetings.
        - Other necessary records.
    - 🧠 **Examples of "Other Records"** (Para A22):
        - Income tax returns.
        - Regulatory filings (e.g., prospectuses).
        - Shareholder registers.
        - Conflicts of interest statements.
        - Life insurance policies.
        - Contracts with key management.
- **Significant Transactions Outside Normal Course** (Para 16)
    - **Shall** inquire regarding:
        - (a) The **Nature** of these transactions.
        - (b) Whether **Related Parties** could be involved.
        - ⟳ *Link*: See "Responses to Risk" for detailed testing (Para 23).
- **Sharing Information**
    - **Shall** share relevant RP info with the engagement team. (Para 17)

## ⚠️ Identification & Assessment of Risks
- **General Requirement**
    - Identify and assess risks of material misstatement (SA 315). (Para 18)
- **Significant Risks**
    - **MANDATORY**: Identified significant RP transactions **outside the entity's normal course of business** give rise to **Significant Risks**. (Para 18)
- **Fraud Risk Factors** (Para 19)
    - Consider **Dominant Influence** by a Related Party.
    - 🧠 **Indicators of Dominant Influence** (Para A29):
        - RP vetoes significant business decisions.
        - Significant transactions referred to RP for final approval.
        - No debate on RP proposals.
        - Transactions rarely independently reviewed.
    - *Fraud Implication*: Suggests mgmt override of controls (SA 240).

## 🛡️ Responses to Risks (Execution)
- **General Response**
    - Design and perform further audit procedures to obtain SAAE. (Para 20)
- **Procedures for Newly Identified RPs** (Para 21-22)
    - *Scenario: Auditor finds RP/Transaction not previously identified by Mgmt.*
    - 1. **Promptly communicate** to engagement team.
    - 2. **Request Management** to identify *all* transactions with this newly found RP.
    - 3. **Inquire** why entity's controls failed to identify it.
    - 4. **Perform Substantive Procedures** on the newly identified transactions.
    - 5. **Reconsider the risk** that other undisclosed RPs exist.
    - 6. **If Intentional Non-Disclosure**: Evaluate implications for audit integrity (**Fraud Risk**). (Para 22(e))
- **Testing Significant Transactions Outside Normal Course** (Para 23)
    - Inspect underlying contracts/agreements to evaluate:
        - (i) **Business Rationale**: Was it entered into to engage in fraudulent reporting or asset misappropriation? (Ref A38)
        - (ii) **Consistency**: Do terms match mgmt explanations?
        - (iii) **Accounting**: Appropriately disclosed?
    - Obtain evidence of **Authorization and Approval**.
- **Asserting "Arm's Length" Terms** (Para 24)
    - If mgmt asserts a transaction was "Arm's Length":
    - Auditor **shall** obtain **SAAE** to support this.
    - ⚠️ *Warning*: Merely comparing price is insufficient; consider all terms (credit, contingencies). (Para A42)
    - *Procedures*: Compare to external market data, engage expert, compare to unrelated third-party trades. (Para A43)

## 📝 Reporting, Conclusion & Documentation
- **Evaluation of Accounting & Disclosure** (Para 25)
    - Evaluate if RP relationships/transactions are:
        - Appropriately accounted for.
        - Appropriately disclosed (Understandability).
        - Preventing FS from being misleading / Achieving True & Fair view.
- **Written Representations** (Para 26)
    - Obtain WR from Mgmt (and TCWG) that:
        - (a) They have **disclosed** to auditor the identity of *all* RPs and transactions.
        - (b) They have **accounted for and disclosed** them in FS as per framework.
- **Communication with TCWG** (Para 27)
    - Communicate significant matters arising during audit regarding RPs.
    - 🧠 **Examples** (Para A50):
        - Intentional non-disclosure by mgmt.
        - Lack of authorization for significant transactions.
        - Disagreements on accounting/disclosure.
        - Non-compliance with laws prohibiting specific RP transactions.
- **Documentation** (Para 28)
    - Names of identified Related Parties.
    - Nature of the Related Party relationships.

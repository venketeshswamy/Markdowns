# SA 810 (Revised): Engagements to Report on Summary Financial Statements

## Objective
- To determine appropriateness of accepting the engagement (Para 3a)
- If engaged to report on summary financial statements: (Para 3b)
  - To form an opinion based on conclusions from evidence (Para 3b)
  - To express clearly that opinion through a written report (Para 3b)
## Scope
- Applies to reporting on summary financial statements (Para 1)
- Derived from financial statements audited by same auditor (Para 1)
- Effective for periods beginning on or after April 1, 2024 (Para 2)

## Definitions
- 📌 Applied criteria (Para 4a): The criteria applied by management in the preparation of the summary financial statements.
- 📌 Audited financial statements (Para 4b): Financial statements audited by the auditor in accordance with SAs, and from which the summary financial statements are derived.
  - 🔗 See SA 200
- 📌 Summary financial statements (Para 4c): Historical financial information that is derived from financial statements but that contains less detail than the financial statements, while still providing a structured representation consistent with that provided by the financial statements of the entity’s economic resources or obligations at a point in time or the changes therein for a period of time.

## Requirements
- Engagement Acceptance
  - 🎯 Auditor shall ordinarily accept an engagement (Para 5)
    - Only when engaged to audit the financial statements (Para 5)
  - 🎯 Before accepting the engagement, auditor shall: (Para 6)
    - Determine whether applied criteria are acceptable (Para 6a)
    - Obtain management agreement acknowledging its responsibility (Para 6b)
      - For preparation in accordance with applied criteria (Para 6b)
      - To make audited statements available without undue difficulty (Para 6b)
      - To include auditor report in documents containing summary statements (Para 6b)
    - Agree with management the form of opinion (Para 6c)
  - 🎯 If criteria unacceptable or agreement not obtained: (Para 7)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall not accept the engagement (Para 7)
    - ⚠️ Unless required by law or regulation to do so (Para 7)
    - 🎯 If law requires, report shall not indicate SA compliance (Para 7)
- Nature of Procedures
  - 🎯 Auditor shall perform the following procedures: (Para 8)
    - Evaluate adequate disclosure of summarised nature (Para 8a)
    - Evaluate disclosure of audited statements availability (Para 8b)
    - Evaluate adequate disclosure of applied criteria (Para 8c)
    - Compare summary statements with audited information (Para 8d)
    - Evaluate preparation in accordance with applied criteria (Para 8e)
    - Evaluate appropriate aggregation level so not misleading (Para 8f)
    - Evaluate availability of audited statements to users (Para 8g)
- Form of Opinion
  - 🎯 Opinion shall use one of following phrases: (Para 9)
    - ⚠️ Unless otherwise required by law or regulation (Para 9)
    - Are consistent, in all material respects, with audited statements (Para 9a)
    - Are a fair summary of the audited financial statements (Para 9b)
  - 🎯 If law prescribes different opinion wording, auditor shall: (Para 10)
    - Apply procedures and further necessary procedures (Para 10a)
    - Evaluate if users might misunderstand opinion (Para 10b)
    - Evaluate if additional explanation mitigates misunderstanding (Para 10b)
  - 🎯 If additional explanation cannot mitigate misunderstanding: (Para 11)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall not accept the engagement (Para 11)
    - ⚠️ Unless required by law or regulation to do so (Para 11)
- Timing and Subsequent Events
  - Report may be dated later than audited report (Para 12)
    - 🎯 Shall state it does not reflect subsequent events (Para 12)
  - 🎯 If aware of previously unknown facts at audited report date: (Para 13)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall not issue summary statements report until (Para 13)
    - Consideration in accordance with SA 560 is completed (Para 13)
    - 🔗 See SA 560
- Documents Containing Summary Financial Statements
  - 🎯 Auditor shall read information in document (Para 14)
    - Consider whether there is a material inconsistency (Para 14)
  - 🎯 If material inconsistency identified: (Para 15)
    - 🎯 Discuss matter with management (Para 15)
    - Determine if summary statements or information needs revision (Para 15)
  - 🎯 If information needs revision and management refuses: (Para 15)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall take appropriate action in circumstances (Para 15)
- Elements of Auditor's Report
  - 🎯 Report shall include following elements: (Para 16)
    - Title indicating independent auditor report (Para 16a)
    - An addressee (Para 16b)
    - Identification of summary financial statements (Para 16c)
    - Identification of audited financial statements (Para 16d)
    - Clear expression of an opinion (Para 16e)
    - Statement that reading summary is not substitute for audited statements (Para 16f)
    - Statement regarding subsequent events if applicable (Para 16g)
    - Reference to audited report, date, and opinion fact (Para 16h)
    - Description of management responsibility for summary statements (Para 16i)
    - Statement of auditor responsibility and procedures conducted (Para 16j)
    - Signature, date, and place of signature (Para 16k-m)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall evaluate appropriateness of different addressee (Para 17)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall date report no earlier than: (Para 18)
    - Date sufficient appropriate evidence obtained (Para 18a)
    - Date of auditor report on audited financial statements (Para 18b)
- Reference to Modified Audited Reports
  - 🎯 When audited report includes modifications or key matters: (Para 19)
    - 🎯 Report shall state audited report includes such matters (Para 19i)
    - 🎯 Report shall describe basis and effects of such matters (Para 19ii)
  - 🎯 When audited report contains adverse or disclaimer: (Para 20)
    - 🎯 State audited report contains adverse or disclaimer (Para 20a)
    - 🎯 Describe basis for adverse or disclaimer (Para 20b)
    - 🎯 State it is inappropriate to express opinion on summary (Para 20c)
- Modified Opinion on Summary Statements
  - 🎯 If summary statements not consistent and management refuses changes: (Para 21)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall express an adverse opinion on summary statements (Para 21)
- Restrictions and Comparatives
  - 🎯 Auditor shall include similar distribution restriction or alert (Para 22)
  - 🎯 If audited statements contain comparatives but summary omits: (Para 23)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall determine whether omission is reasonable (Para 23)
    - 🎯 Determine effect of unreasonable omission on report (Para 23)
  - 🎯 If summary contains comparatives reported by another auditor: (Para 24)
    - 🎯 Report shall contain matters SA 710 requires (Para 24)
    - 🔗 See SA 710
- Unaudited Supplementary Information
  - 🎯 Auditor shall evaluate if clearly differentiated from summary statements (Para 25)
  - 🎯 If not differentiated, ask management to change presentation (Para 25)
  - 🎯 If management refuses, explain in report information is not covered (Para 25)
- Other Considerations
  - 🎯 If entity plans to state auditor reported but omits report: (Para 26)
    - 🎯 Auditor shall request management to include report (Para 26)
    - 🎯 If refused, take actions to prevent inappropriate association (Para 26)
  - 🎯 If entity refers to auditor when not engaged for summary: (Para 27)
    - 🎯 Ensure reference context is in audited report (Para 27a)
    - 🎯 Ensure it does not imply summary reported on (Para 27b)
    - 🎯 If not met, request management to change or delete (Para 27)
    - 🎯 If refused, advise disagreement and take preventive action (Para 27)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Engagement Acceptance (Para 5-6)
  - Audit provides knowledge for summary reporting responsibilities (A1)
  - Management agreement evidenced by written acceptance terms (A2)
  - Aggregation increases risk of misleading summary statements (A3)
  - Criteria established by standards or law presumed acceptable (A5)
- Explains Developed Criteria (Para 6a)
  - Management may develop criteria based on industry practice (A6)
  - Adequate disclosure may be provided by specific title (A7)
- Explains Evaluating Availability (Para 8g)
  - Evaluate factors allowing users to obtain ready access (A8)
- Explains Form of Opinion (Para 9)
  - Jurisdiction practice affects which unmodified phrase is used (A9)
- Explains Timing and Subsequent Events (Para 12)
  - Auditor not required to obtain additional evidence on audited statements (A10)
- Explains Documents Containing SFS (Para 14-15)
  - Differs from SA 720 which deals with annual reports (A11-A12)
  - Auditor must avoid knowing association with false information (A13)
  - Work on annual report info may suffice for summary documents (A14)
  - Uncorrected misstatements signal potential material inconsistency (A15)
  - SA 720 helps determine action if management refuses revisions (A16)
- Explains Elements of Auditor's Report (Para 16-20)
  - Title affirms independent auditor ethical requirements met (A17)
  - Addressee evaluation depends on engagement terms and purpose (A18)
  - Page numbers identification helps readers locate summary statements (A19)
  - Recognised authority depends on terms and entity nature (A20)
  - Not required to describe individual key audit matters again (A21)
  - Summary descriptions do not substitute reading audited report (A22)
- Explains Comparatives (Para 23-24)
  - Presumption summary contains comparatives if audited statements do (A24)
  - Reasonableness of omission depends on statement objective and users (A25)
- Explains Unaudited Supplementary Information (Para 25)
  - SA 700 contains helpful differentiation requirements (A26)
- Explains Other Considerations (Para 26-27)
  - Appropriate actions include informing known third-party users (A27)
  - Course of action depends on legal rights and obligations (A27)

## Appendices
- Appendix: Illustrations of Independent Auditor’s Reports (App)
  - Includes various factual circumstances leading to unmodified or modified reports
  - Supports report structure requirements in Paras 16-21

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 7 Exception: Unacceptable criteria trigger refusal — unless required by law.
- ⚠️ Para 9 Exception: Opinion phrasing is strictly specified — unless otherwise required by law.
- ⚠️ Para 11 Exception: Prescribed wording causing misunderstanding triggers refusal — unless required by law.

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A4 — Factors affecting acceptability of applied criteria
  - 📋 The nature of the entity
  - 📋 The purpose of the summary financial statements
  - 📋 The information needs of the intended users
  - 📋 Whether the applied criteria will result in not misleading statements
- Para A6 — Acceptable developed criteria result in statements that:
  - 📋 Adequately disclose their summarised nature and identify audited statements
  - 📋 Clearly describe from whom or where statements are available
  - 📋 Adequately disclose the applied criteria
  - 📋 Agree with or can be re-calculated from audited information
  - 📋 Contain information necessary and appropriate level of aggregation
- Para A8 — Factors evaluating availability without undue difficulty:
  - 📋 The summary statements describe clearly from whom or where available
  - 📋 The audited financial statements are on public record
  - 📋 Management established a process for users to obtain ready access
- Para A12 — Information in document containing summary statements may include:
  - 📋 Some or all same matters dealt with in annual report
  - 📋 Matters that are not dealt with in annual report
- Para A23 — Appendix illustrations of auditor's reports that variously:
  - 📋 Contain unmodified opinions
  - 📋 Are derived from audited financial statements with modified opinions
  - 📋 Contain a modified opinion
  - 📋 Describe uncorrected material misstatement of other information
  - 📋 Include Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern

## Logical Flow Chains
- Engagement Acceptance Decision (Para 6-7)
  - Evaluate acceptability of applied criteria (Para 6a)
  - Obtain management agreement on responsibilities (Para 6b)
  - if criteria unacceptable or agreement not obtained:
    - leads to: Auditor shall not accept engagement (Para 7)
    - ⚠️ unless: Required by law or regulation (Para 7)
- Handling Material Inconsistencies (Para 15)
  - Identify material inconsistency in document
  - leads to: Discuss matter with management (Para 15)
  - if management refuses necessary revision:
    - results in: Auditor shall take appropriate action (Para 15)

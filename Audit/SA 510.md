# SA 510: Initial Audit Engagements - Opening Balances

## Objective
- 🎯 To obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether: (Para 3)
  - (a) Opening balances contain misstatements that materially affect current period's financial statements
  - (b) Appropriate accounting policies reflected in opening balances have been consistently applied
  - OR changes thereto are properly accounted for and adequately presented/disclosed

## Scope
- Applies to: Initial audit engagements (Para 1)
- Includes: Matters requiring disclosure (contingencies/commitments) (Para 1)
- Excludes: Specific activities prior to starting initial audit (See SA 300) (Para 1)
- Effective Date: Audits for periods beginning on/after April 1, 2010 (Para 2)

## Definitions
- 📌 Initial audit engagement (Para 4a): An engagement in which either:
  - (i) Financial statements for prior period were not audited
  - (ii) Financial statements for prior period were audited by predecessor auditor
- 📌 Opening balances (Para 4b): Account balances that exist at beginning of period
  - Based upon closing balances of prior period
  - Reflect effects of transactions/events of prior periods
  - Reflect accounting policies applied in prior period
  - Include matters requiring disclosure (contingencies/commitments)
- 📌 Predecessor auditor (Para 4c): Auditor from a different audit firm
  - Who audited financial statements of entity in prior period
  - And who has been replaced by current auditor

## Requirements
- 📂 Audit Procedures - General
  - 🎯 Read most recent financial statements and predecessor auditor's report (Para 5)
    - Purpose: Information relevant to opening balances including disclosures
- 📂 Audit Procedures - Opening Balances
  - 🎯 Obtain evidence if opening balances contain material misstatements by: (Para 6)
    - (a) Determining if prior closing balances correctly brought forward (Para 6a)
      - OR adjustments disclosed as prior period items
    - (b) Determining if opening balances reflect appropriate accounting policies (Para 6b)
    - (c) Performing one or more specific procedures: (Para 6c)
      - (i) Perusing copies of audited financial statements (if prior year audited)
      - (ii) Evaluating if current period audit procedures provide evidence
      - (iii) Performing specific audit procedures
  - 🎯 If evidence suggests material misstatements: (Para 7)
    - Perform additional procedures to determine effect on current statements
    - If misstatement exists: Communicate with management and TCWG (SA 450)
- 📂 Audit Procedures - Accounting Policies
  - 🎯 Obtain evidence on consistency of application (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Ensure changes are properly accounted for/presented/disclosed (Para 8)
- 📂 Predecessor Auditor's Report
  - 🎯 If prior opinion was modified: (Para 9)
    - Evaluate effect of that matter on current period risks (SA 315)
- 📂 Reporting Conclusions
  - 🎯 If unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence: (Para 10)
    - Express Qualified Opinion OR Disclaimer of Opinion (SA 705)
  - 🎯 If opening balances contain material misstatement: (Para 11)
    - And effect is not properly accounted for/disclosed
    - Express Qualified Opinion OR Adverse Opinion (SA 705)
  - 🎯 If consistency of policies failed: (Para 12)
    - Current policies not consistently applied relative to opening balances
    - OR change in policy not properly accounted/disclosed
    - Express Qualified Opinion OR Adverse Opinion (SA 705)
  - 🎯 If predecessor modification remains relevant/material: (Para 13)
    - Modify opinion on current financial statements

## Application Guidance
- 📂 Explains Para 6 (Procedures)
  - Factors affecting nature/extent of procedures (Para A1)
  - Reliance on prior period audited statements (Para A2)
    - ⚠️ Exception: If current procedures indicate possibility of misstatement
  - Evidence for Current Assets/Liabilities (Para A3)
    - Collection/payment provides some evidence
    - Inventory requires additional procedures (observation/valuation)
  - Evidence for Non-Current Assets/Liabilities (Para A4)
    - Examine accounting records/underlying information
    - Confirmation with third parties (debt/investments)
- 📂 Explains Para 10 (Reporting Limitation)
  - ⚠️ "Split Opinion" Concept (Para A5)
    - If unable to obtain evidence on opening balances
    - May express Qualified/Disclaimer on Results of Operations/Cash Flows
    - AND Unmodified on State of Affairs (Balance Sheet)
    - Condition: Unless prohibited by law or regulation
- 📂 Explains Para 13 (Predecessor Modification)
  - Modification may not be relevant if matter resolved (Para A6)
    - Example: Prior scope limitation resolved in current period

## ICAI Listed Examples
- 📋 Para A1 — Factors affecting audit procedures on opening balances:
  - Accounting policies followed by entity
  - Nature of account balances/classes of transactions
  - Risks of material misstatement in current period
  - Significance of opening balances relative to current period
  - Whether prior period audited and if opinion modified
- 📋 Para A3 — Additional procedures for Opening Inventory:
  - Observing current physical inventory count and reconciling to opening
  - Performing audit procedures on valuation of opening inventory items
  - Performing audit procedures on gross profit and cut-off
- 📋 Para A4 — Confirmations for non-current assets:
  - Long-term debt
  - Investments

## Logical Flow Chains
- ⚙️ Evaluation of Opening Balances (Para 6)
  - Start: Read prior FS (Para 5)
  - Action: Check closing balances brought forward (Para 6a)
  - Action: Check accounting policies (Para 6b)
  - Action: Perform specific procedure (Para 6c)
    - Option 1: Peruse audited prior FS
    - Option 2: Rely on current audit evidence (e.g., collection of receivables)
    - Option 3: Specific opening balance procedures (e.g., inventory rollback)
    - ⚠️ If misstatement found: Perform additional procedures (Para 7)
      - leads to: Communication with Management/TCWG

- ⚙️ Reporting Decision Tree (Para 10-12)
  - Branch 1: Inability to obtain evidence (Scope Limit)
    - Result: Qualified or Disclaimer (Para 10)
    - ⚠️ Nuance: Split opinion if allowed (Para A5)
  - Branch 2: Misstatement identified (Disagreement)
    - Result: Qualified or Adverse (Para 11)
  - Branch 3: Inconsistent Policies
    - Result: Qualified or Adverse (Para 12)

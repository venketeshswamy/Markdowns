# SA 265: Communicating Deficiencies in Internal Control to Those Charged with Governance and Management

## Objective
- 🎯 Communicate appropriately to TCWG and Management (Para 5)
  - Deficiencies in internal control identified during audit
  - Those of sufficient importance to merit attention

## Scope
- Applies to: Auditor's responsibility to communicate deficiencies (Para 1)
- Context: Audit of financial statements (Para 1)
- Does NOT impose: Additional responsibilities for understanding/testing controls (Para 1)
  - 🔗 See SA 315 and SA 330
- Preclusion: Does not preclude communicating other matters (Para 3)
- Effective Date: Audits beginning on/after April 1, 2010 (Para 4)

## Definitions
- 📌 Deficiency in internal control (Para 6a)
  - (i) Control designed/operated so it cannot prevent/detect/correct misstatements
  - (ii) Control necessary to prevent/detect/correct misstatements is missing
- 📌 Significant deficiency in internal control (Para 6b)
  - Deficiency or combination thereof
  - In auditor's professional judgment
  - Of sufficient importance to merit attention of TCWG

## Requirements
- 🎯 Identification of Deficiencies (Para 7)
  - Determine if deficiencies exist based on audit work
- 🎯 Assessment of Significance (Para 8)
  - Determine if deficiencies are significant (individually or combined)
- 🎯 Communication to TCWG (Para 9)
  - Communicate significant deficiencies in writing
  - On a timely basis
- 🎯 Communication to Management (Para 10)
  - 🎯 Significant deficiencies (Para 10a)
    - In writing on timely basis
    - ⚠️ Unless inappropriate to communicate directly
  - 🎯 Other deficiencies (Para 10b)
    - If sufficient importance to merit attention
    - And not communicated by other parties
- 🎯 Content of Written Communication (Para 11)
  - (a) Description of deficiencies and potential effects
  - (b) Sufficient context explanation:
      - (i) Purpose was audit opinion on financial statements
      - (ii) Controls considered for audit design, not control opinion
      - (iii) Matters limited to those identified and important

## Application Guidance
- Explains Para 7 (Identification)
  - May discuss facts with management (Para A1)
    - Clarifies understanding of causes (Para A2)
    - ⚠️ Exception: If findings question mgmt integrity (Para A1)
  - Smaller Entities (Para A3-A4)
    - Less formal controls
    - Mgmt oversight may compensate for lack of segregation
- Explains Para 8 (Significance Assessment)
  - Factors: Likelihood and potential magnitude (Para A5)
  - Misstatement need not actually occur (Para A5)
  - Legal requirements may define specific types (Para A9-A11)
- Explains Para 9 (Communication to TCWG)
  - Writing reflects importance (Para A12)
  - Timing: Before approval of FS for listed entities (Para A13)
  - Level of detail: Matters of professional judgment (Para A15)
  - Prior periods: Repeat if not remedied (Para A17)
- Explains Para 10 (Communication to Management)
  - Appropriate level: One with authority to act (Para A19)
  - "Other deficiencies" need not be in writing (Para A23)
  - ⚠️ Inappropriate to communicate (Para A20)
    - If evidence of fraud/incompetence by management
- Explains Para 11 (Content)
  - Potential effects: Need not quantify amounts (Para A28)
  - Suggestions for remedial action permitted (Para A28)
  - Mgmt responses may be included (Para A28)
  - Disclaimer on use for other purposes (Para A29)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Communication to Management Exception (Para 10a, A20)
  - Condition: Finds question mgmt integrity or competence
  - Result: Do NOT communicate to management directly
  - Action: Consult with TCWG or legal counsel
- ⚠️ "Other" Deficiencies Form (Para A23)
  - Condition: Deficiencies not significant but important
  - Result: Oral communication is sufficient
  - Note: Formal writing not mandated
- ⚠️ Prior Period Deficiencies (Para A17)
  - Condition: Significant deficiency remains unremedied
  - Result: Must repeat communication
  - Option: Can reference previous communication
- ⚠️ Written vs Oral for TCWG (Para 9, A12)
  - Requirement: Significant deficiencies MUST be written
  - Nuance: Oral discussion first is allowed but does not replace writing (A14)

## ICAI Listed Examples
- 📋 Para A6 — Matters to consider for "Significance"
  - Likelihood of leading to material misstatement
  - Susceptibility to loss or fraud
  - Subjectivity and complexity of amounts
  - Financial statement amounts exposed
  - Volume of activity in account/class
  - Importance of controls to reporting process
  - Cause and frequency of exceptions
  - Interaction with other deficiencies
- 📋 Para A7 — Indicators of Significant Deficiencies
  - Ineffective aspects of control environment
  - Absence of expected risk assessment process
  - Ineffective entity risk assessment process
  - Ineffective response to identified significant risks
  - Misstatements detected by auditor (not prevented by controls)
  - Disclosure of material misstatement as prior period item (AS 5 context)
  - Mgmt inability to oversee financial statement preparation
- 📋 Para A8 — Combination examples
  - Deficiencies affecting same account balance
  - Deficiencies affecting same assertion
  - Deficiencies affecting same component of internal control

## Logical Flow Chains
- Assessment Process (Para 7-8)
  - Audit work performed (Para 7)
    - leads to: Identification of deficiencies
      - leads to: Assessment of significance (Para 8)
        - if Significant: Communicate to TCWG (Written) + Mgmt (Written)
        - if Not Significant but Important: Communicate to Mgmt (Oral/Written)
        - if Minor: No specific requirement
- Communication Decision (Para 10a)
  - Significant Deficiency Identified
    - leads to: Intent to communicate to Mgmt
      - ⚠️ check: Is mgmt involved/incompetent? (A20)
        - If YES: Do not communicate to Mgmt; Go to TCWG/Legal
        - If NO: Communicate to Mgmt

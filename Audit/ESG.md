# CHAPTER 18: SDG & ESG ASSURANCE

## 1. INTRODUCTION TO SUSTAINABILITY
- **Definition (Brundtland Report)**
  - Development meeting present needs without compromising future generations.
- **ESG Framework (3 Pillars)**
  - **Environment (E)**: Climate policies, energy use, waste, natural resources.
    - *Elements*: Climate Change (Emissions, Footprints), Natural Resources (Water stress, Biodiversity), Pollution & Waste (E-waste, Toxics), Env. Opportunity (Clean Tech, Green Building).
  - **Social (S)**: Relationships, reputation, labor, diversity, inclusions.
    - *Elements*: Human Capital (Labor, Safety), Product Liability (Data Security), Stakeholder Opposition (Controversial Sourcing), Social Opportunity (Access to Finance/Healthcare).
  - **Governance (G)**: Internal practices, controls, legal compliance.
    - *Elements*: Corporate Governance (Diversity, Pay, Accounting), Corporate Behavior (Ethics, Anti-corruption, Tax Transparency).
- **Reporting Modalities**
  - **Qualitative**: Strategy/policy descriptions.
  - **Quantitative**: Metrics and KPIs.

## 2. SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS (SDGs)
- **Origin**: Adopted by UN Member States in **2015** (2030 Agenda).
- **Key Body**: Division for Sustainable Development Goals (DSDG) in UNDESA.
- **The 17 SDGs**
  - 1. No Poverty | 2. Zero Hunger | 3. Good Health & Well Being | 4. Quality Education | 5. Gender Equality | 6. Clean Water & Sanitization | 7. Affordable & Clean Energy | 8. Decent Work & Economic Growth | 9. Industry Innovation & Infrastructure | 10. Reduced Inequalities | 11. Sustainable Cities & Communities | 12. Responsible Consumption & Production | 13. Climate Action | 14. Life Below Water | 15. Life on Land | 16. Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions | 17. Partnership for the Goals

## 3. GLOBAL TRENDS IN REPORTING
- **Major Frameworks**
  - **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative)**: Used by 93% of top 250 global corps (Economic, Env, Social impacts).
  - **CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project)**: Focus on GHG, water, forests (Targeted at investors/buyers).
  - **IIRC (Intl Integrated Reporting Council)**: Connects financial & sustainability data.
  - **TCFD**: Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (created 2015 by FSB).
  - **CDSB**: Climate Disclosure Standards Board.

## 4. INTEGRATED REPORTING (IR)
- **The 6 Capitals (6 Cs)**
  - **Financial**: Funds available (debt, equity, grants).
  - **Manufactured**: Human-created equipment, infrastructure.
  - **Natural**: Env. inputs (water, land, biodiversity).
  - **Human**: Employee skills, experience, alignment with ethics.
  - **Social/Relationship**: Community trust, social license to operate.
  - **Intellectual**: R&D, innovation, competitive advantage.
- **Trend**: Asia Pacific dominates (~60% of companies reporting in 2022). 

## 5. GLOBAL SCENARIO
- **United States**: Mar 2022 SEC proposed TCFD-guided climate-risk disclosures (financial impact, mitigation, transition plans).
- **United Kingdom**: 2019 law for Net Zero GHG by 2050; Companies Act 2006 expanded in 2022 for TCFD-aligned reporting (cos >£500M turnover or >500 employees).

## 6. BRSR FRAMEWORK & ICAI INITIATIVES
- **ICAI Actions**: Constituted **SRSB** (Feb 2020); Issued **SAE 3410** (GHG Statements).
- **BRSR Applicability**: Voluntary FY 21-22; **Mandatory FY 22-23** (Top 1,000 listed entities).
- **BRSR Structure (3 Sections)**
  - **Section A**: General Disclosures (Company details, products, subsidiaries).
  - **Section B**: Management Process & disclosures (Governance, oversight).
  - **Section C**: Principle-wise performance disclosures (Essential = Mandatory; Leadership = Optional).

### 6.1 Nine Principles of BRSR (Core Elements)
- **Principle 1: Ethics, Transparency & Accountability**
  - Governing structure must develop policies/procedures ensuring uncompromised ethics.
  - Policy, procedure, and performance information must be available to stakeholders.
  - Transparent disclosures required for adverse effects.
  - Value chain entities must be encouraged to adopt these principles.
  - Proactive response required against violating outside entities (suppliers, distributors, sub-contractors, regulators).
- **Principle 2: Safe & Sustainable Goods/Services**
  - Design/production must minimize resource usage for sustainability.
  - Responsible for educating/making consumers aware of their rights.
  - Mitigate over-exploitation; encourage reduce, reuse, and recycling of resources.
- **Principle 3: Employee Well-Being (Including Value Chain)**
  - Ensure compliance with all employee regulatory requirements.
  - Respect human dignity; do not restrict freedom of association/collective bargaining.
  - Prevent child labor, bonded labor, and involuntary labor.
  - Maintain uncompromised work-life balance systems.
  - Ensure timely payment of wages/compensation.
  - Provide living wages (basic needs + economic security).
  - Create safe, hygienic, comfortable work environments.
  - Facilitate skill/career development and training.
  - Ensure workplace is completely free of harassment and violence.
- **Principle 4: Stakeholders' Interests & Responsiveness**
  - Transparently communicate operational impacts on people and nature.
  - Systematically determine operational context and identify interested parties.
  - Fairly share benefits / provide equitable opportunities to stakeholders.
- **Principle 5: Respect and Promote Human Rights**
  - Understand human rights via Constitution of India, national laws, Intl Bill of Human Rights.
  - Integrate human rights into policies, procedures, and management systems.
  - Recognize/respect rights of all stakeholders (workplace, communities, consumers, marginalized groups).
- **Principle 6: Protection & Restoration of Environment**
  - Assess/rectify environmental impacts covering the whole product life cycle.
  - Optimum use of natural/manmade resources using stakeholder feedback.
  - Measure performance: pollution prevention, forest destruction, waste, energy/land use.
  - Contribute to climate change resilience (Paris Agreement, National Action Plans).
  - Explore industry best practices for reduce/reuse/recycle/recover.
  - Proactively seek avenues to improve environmental responsibilities.
- **Principle 7: Influence on Public & Regulatory Policy**
  - Meet BRSR core elements holistically during policy formulation/advocacy.
  - Utilize collective associations (trade groups, industry chambers) for advocacy.
  - Ensure advocacy encourages fair competition and prevents human rights abuses.
- **Principle 8: Inclusive Growth & Equitable Development**
  - Identify/address socio-economic impacts of activities (land acquisition, construction).
  - Review, measure, track, and mitigate adverse societal/environmental impacts.
  - Innovate products/tech to improve quality of life for marginalized communities.
  - Review local/regional priorities when designing CSR activities.
  - Prevent business-induced displacement; if unavoidable, ensure mutually agreed, participative, fair compensation.
  - Respect IP and traditional knowledge; share derived benefits equitably.
- **Principle 9: Value to Consumers in a Responsible Manner**
  - Minimize negative product impacts on consumers, nature, and society.
  - Do not prevent freedom of choice or fair competition in design/marketing.
  - Transparently and accurately disclose all adverse impacts on user/planet/biodiversity.
  - Maintain strict right to privacy when handling customer data.
  - Inform customers on safe usage, reuse, recycling, disposal, and eliminating over-consumption.
  - Ensure advertising is not misleading or confusing.
  - Provide transparent/accessible grievance redressal and feedback systems.
  - Ensure universal, non-discriminatory access for essential goods/services (e.g., Utilities).

### 7.2 Assurance in BRSR (SSAE 3000)
- **Applicability (SSAE 3000 - Umbrella Standard)**
  - Voluntary: Periods ending on 31st March 2023.
  - **Mandatory**: Periods ending on or after **31st March 2024**.
- **Methodology to Provide Assurance (6 Sequential Steps)**
  - 1. Preliminary Review of ESG report and parameters.
  - 2. On-site Assessment / Verification of ESG Report.
  - 3. Issuance of Assessment Report and Assessment Statement.
  - 4. Review of responses and clarifications on the findings.
  - 5. Submission of findings of the on-site assessment and document review.
  - 6. Preparation of Assessment / Verification report including final results/recommendations.

## 8. ROLE OF AUDITOR (CLIMATE-RELATED RISKS IN FS AUDIT)
- **Risk Assessment Phase**
  - Consider climate-related risks relevant to the entity (high relevance in banks, insurance, energy, transport, agriculture, forestry).
- **Audit Reporting & Communication**
  - Must follow applicable auditing standards despite increased pressure for ESG transparency.
  - **Emphasis of Matter**: May be warranted to draw attention to fundamental climate-related disclosures.
- **Financial Statement Disclosures**
  - Determine if management appropriately disclosed climate info as per financial reporting framework (Ind AS / AS) before considering it in the auditor's report.
- **Consistency Verification (ISA 720 / SA 720)**
  - Auditor must read "Other Information" (management reports, press releases, investor updates) to ensure consistency with audited financial statements.
- **Regulatory Breaches (SA 250 & SA 570)**
  - Assess non-compliance with environmental laws (e.g., banned toxic products).
  - Material misstatements from fines, litigation, or Going Concern implications must be evaluated.

# SA 805(Revised): Special Considerations - Audits of Single Financial Statements and Specific Elements, Accounts or Items of a Financial Statement

## Objective
- 🎯 The objective of the auditor is to address appropriately the special considerations that are relevant to (Para 5)
  - The acceptance of the engagement (Para 5a)
  - The planning and performance of that engagement (Para 5b)
  - Forming an opinion and reporting on the single financial statement or specific element (Para 5c)

## Scope
- Applies to: Audit of single financial statement or specific element, account, or item (Para 1)
- Does NOT apply to: Report of a component auditor for consolidated financial statements (Para 2)
- Does not override other SA requirements (Para 3)
- Effective from: Periods beginning on or after April 1, 2024 (Para 4)

## Definitions
- 📌 Element of a financial statement (Para 6a): means an “element, account or item of a financial statement”
- 📌 Financial Reporting Standards (Para 6b): includes The Accounting Standards issued by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI); or Accounting Standards notified under the Companies Act, 2013; or The International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRSs) issued by the International Accounting Standards Board; or The International Public Sector Accounting Standards (IPSASs) issued by the International Public Sector Accounting Standards Board; or Any other Accounting Standards as may be applicable to the entity
- Single financial statement or specific element includes related notes (Para 6c)

## Requirements
- Considerations When Accepting the Engagement
  - 🎯 Auditor shall determine whether audit is practicable (Para 7)
    - If not also engaged to audit entity's complete financial statements (Para 7)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall determine acceptability of financial reporting framework (Para 8)
    - Must provide adequate disclosures for intended users to understand (Para 8)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall consider whether expected form of opinion is appropriate (Para 9)
- Considerations When Planning and Performing the Audit
  - 🎯 Auditor shall adapt all relevant SAs as necessary (Para 10)
- Forming an Opinion and Reporting Considerations
  - 🎯 Auditor shall apply SA 700(Revised) requirements (Para 11)
    - And SA 800(Revised) adapted as necessary when applicable (Para 11)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall express a separate opinion for each engagement (Para 12)
    - If reporting on single statement in conjunction with complete statements (Para 12)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall ask management to rectify presentation (Para 13)
    - If single statement not differentiated from complete statements (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall differentiate opinion from opinion on complete statements (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall not issue report until satisfied with differentiation (Para 13)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall consider implications of matters in complete statement report (Para 14)
    - Modified opinion in accordance with SA 705(Revised) (Para 14a)
    - Emphasis of Matter or Other Matter paragraph (Para 14b)
    - Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern (Para 14c)
    - Uncorrected material misstatement of other information (Para 14d)
- Adverse or Disclaimer on Complete Financial Statements
  - SA 705(Revised) prohibits unmodified opinion on included single statement (Para 15)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall only express unmodified opinion on specific element if: (Para 16)
    - Not prohibited by law or regulation (Para 16a)
    - Report is not published together with adverse or disclaimer report (Para 16b)
    - Element does not constitute major portion of complete statements (Para 16c)
  - 🎯 Auditor shall not express unmodified opinion on single financial statement (Para 17)
    - Even if published separately it constitutes a major portion (Para 17)

## Application Guidance
- Explains Scope and Definitions
  - Term historical financial information defined in SA 200 (A1)
  - Single financial statement includes related notes and accounting policies (A2)
  - Appendix 1 lists examples of other historical financial information (A3)
- Explains Considerations When Accepting the Engagement (Para 7-9)
  - Exceptional circumstances may justify departure from relevant SA requirement (A4)
  - Compliance with SAs may not be practicable if complete statements unaudited (A5)
  - Auditor lacks understanding of entity environment and internal control (A5)
  - Auditor may need corroborative evidence from accounting records (A5)
  - Acceptability involves checking if framework includes all relevant requirements (A6)
  - Form of opinion depends on applicable framework and laws (A7)
- Explains Considerations When Planning and Performing (Para 10)
  - Fraud, related party, and going concern SAs remain relevant (A9)
  - Communication with those charged with governance may need adaptation (A10)
  - Written representations replaced by representations about the single element (A11)
  - Must perform audit procedures on element to obtain sufficient evidence (A12)
  - Elements interrelated so auditor cannot consider in isolation (A13)
  - Materiality determined may be lower than complete statements materiality (A14)
- Explains Forming an Opinion and Reporting Considerations (Para 11-17)
  - Ensure element provides adequate disclosures under framework (A15)
  - Appendix 2 provides auditor report illustrations (A16)
  - Must apply reporting requirements in other SAs adapted as necessary (A17)
  - Going concern responsibilities description may need adaptation (A18)
  - Key Audit Matters apply for listed entity general purpose statements (A19)
  - SA 720(Revised) applies if reports considered to be annual reports (A20)
  - Report signed in personal name, firm name, membership number, and UDIN (A21)
  - Considering implications of complete statement auditor report matters (A22)
  - Refer to matters in complete statements report via Other Matter paragraph (A25)
  - Disclaimer on results but unmodified on state of affairs permitted (A26)

## Key Nuances and Exceptions
- ⚠️ Para 15: Adverse or Disclaimer on complete statements — Prohibits unmodified opinion
  - If condition met: SA 705 prohibits unmodified opinion on single statement
  - If condition not met: Unmodified opinion possible if evidence supports it
- ⚠️ Para 16: Exception to unmodified opinion prohibition — Specific element audit
  - If condition met: Unmodified opinion allowed if separate and not major portion
  - If condition not met: Unmodified opinion strictly prohibited
- ⚠️ Para A4: Requirement conditional — SA compliance exception
  - If condition exists: Must comply with all SA requirements
  - If condition not met: Requirement not relevant, need not comply

## ICAI Listed Examples
- Para A8 — Factors affecting form of expected opinion
  - 📋 Whether framework is explicitly or implicitly restricted to complete statements
  - 📋 Comply fully with each requirement of framework relevant to element
  - 📋 Provide disclosures beyond framework specifically required to achieve fair presentation
- Para A23 — Factors relevant in considering implications of complete statement report matters
  - 📋 The nature of the matters being described in complete statements report
  - 📋 The pervasiveness of the matters described in the complete statements report
  - 📋 The nature and extent of differences between applicable financial reporting frameworks
  - 📋 The extent of difference between periods covered by the financial statements
  - 📋 The time elapsed since date of complete statements report
- Appendix 1 — Examples of Specific Elements, Accounts or Items
  - 📋 Accounts receivable, allowance for doubtful accounts, inventory
  - 📋 Liability for accrued benefits of a private pension plan
  - 📋 Recorded value of identified intangible assets
  - 📋 Liability for incurred but not reported claims in insurance portfolio
  - 📋 A schedule of externally managed assets and income of pension plan
  - 📋 A schedule of net tangible assets including related notes
  - 📋 A schedule of disbursements in relation to a lease property
  - 📋 A schedule of profit participation or employee bonuses

## Logical Flow Chains
- Engagement Acceptance Assessment (Para 7-8)
  - Auditor requested to audit specific element (Para 7)
    - leads to: Check if complete financial statements are also audited (Para 7)
      - if not audited: Determine if SA application is practicable (Para 7)
      - ⚠️ unless: Management agrees to another type of engagement (A5)
    - leads to: Determine acceptability of financial reporting framework (Para 8)
- Differentiation of Reporting Process (Para 12-13)
  - Undertakes element audit and complete statements audit together (Para 12)
    - requires: Express separate opinion for each engagement (Para 12)
    - leads to: Review presentation for sufficient differentiation (Para 13)
      - if not differentiated: Ask management to rectify presentation (Para 13)
      - ⚠️ unless: Differentiated properly, do not issue report (Para 13)

# Ch.14 — Mergers, Acquisitions & Corporate Restructuring

## 🧠 Core Concepts & Theory

### 🏢 Structural Restructuring
#### Internal Restructuring
- Capital expenditure on new plant and machinery
- R&D investment for product development
- Hiving off non-core business divisions
- Business divestments and partial sell-offs
- Demergers of family-managed business units
#### External Restructuring
- Corporate mergers and strategic acquisitions
- Joint ventures with strategic partners
- Collaborative business alliances

### 🤝 Legal Combinations
#### Amalgamation
- Blending of two existing undertakings
- Governed under Companies Act 2013 Chapter XV
- Transferee company vests all transferor rights
#### Acquisition
- Purchase of controlling interest in share capital
- Both entities remain standing separate
#### Consolidation
- Legal dissolution of both combining parties
- Creates entirely new single entity

### 📈 M&A Motives
#### Operating Economies
- Synergy formula where VAB > VA + VB
- Complementary manufacturing and distribution channels
- Scale economies reducing average overhead costs
- Real input reduction vs pecuniary pricing discount
#### Business Diversification
- Merging statistically independent income streams
- Negatively correlated revenues reduce risk
- Lowers required rate of return
#### Other Key Drivers
- Set off and carry forward of tax losses
- Rapid market growth bypassing organic delays
- Monopolistic market power expansion

### 🛡️ Takeover Strategies
#### Street Sweep
- Accumulating shares before public announcement
- Target left with no choice but negotiation
#### Bear Hug
- Threatening hostile open offer to target board
- Forces friendly settlement or control change

### 🛡️ Defensive Tactics
#### Poison Pill
- Convertible debentures issued to inflate share count
- Dilutes raider voting control significantly
#### Poison Put
- Bonds cashable at premium to drain cash
- Makes target financially unattractive
#### Greenmail
- Buy back raider shares at premium price
- Avoids hostile takeover threat
#### White Knight
- Offer friendly takeover to escape hostile bidder
- Retains existing management structures
#### White Squire
- Sell minority stake to friendly entity
- Keeps control with current management
#### Pac-Man Defence
- Launch counter-takeover bid for bidder
- Forces bidder to defend itself
#### Golden Parachutes
- Large severance contracts for target executives
- Increases overall acquisition cost

## 📐 Formulas, Models & Theorems

### 💰 Synergy and Gains
#### Synergy Value
- V_AB > V_A + V_B
- Synergy = V_AB - (V_A + V_B)
#### Net Gain
- Net Gain = Synergy - Premium
#### Premium Paid
- Premium = Consideration Paid - V_B

### 📊 Exchange Ratios
#### Market Price Basis
- ER = MPS_Target / MPS_Acquirer
#### EPS Basis
- ER = EPS_Target / EPS_Acquirer
#### Book Value Basis
- ER = BV_Target / BV_Acquirer
#### Weighted Fair Value ER
- ER = W1*ER_EPS + W2*ER_BV + W3*ER_MPS
- Sum of weights = 1.0

### 📉 Post-Merger Performance
#### Post-Merger EPS
- EPS_AB = (Earnings_A + Earnings_B) / (Shares_A + Shares_issued)
- Shares_issued = Shares_B * ER
#### Post-Merger MPS
- MPS_AB = EPS_AB * PE_AB
#### Equivalent EPS
- Eq_EPS = EPS_AB * ER

### 💸 Valuation Models
#### True Cost of Merger (Stock Payment)
- Cost = (alpha * V_AB) - V_B
- alpha = Shares_issued / (Shares_A + Shares_issued)
#### Intrinsic Value of Target (Dividend Growth Model)
- Value = DPS_B * (1 + g_new) / (Ke - g_new)
#### Earning Capitalisation Method
- Value = Profit after tax / Capitalisation rate

## 🔢 Step-by-Step Calculation Procedures

### 🧮 Calculating Weighted Swap Ratio
#### Step 1: Compute Individual Ratios
- Calculate ER for EPS, Book Value, and MPS
#### Step 2: Apply Assigned Weights
- Multiply individual ERs by weights
#### Step 3: Sum Weighted Values
- Add weighted results to get final ER
#### Step 4: Calculate Issued Shares
- Shares issued = Target Shares * Final ER

### 🔎 Calculating Post-Merger Shareholder Gain/Loss
#### Step 1: Pre-Merger Value
- Acquirer pre-merger value = Shares_A * MPS_A
- Target pre-merger value = Shares_B * MPS_B
#### Step 2: Post-Merger Value
- Total Value = (Earnings_A + Earnings_B + Synergy) * Combined_PE
#### Step 3: Allocate Value
- Acquirer post value = Total Value * (Shares_A / Total_Shares)
- Target post value = Total Value * (Shares_issued / Total_Shares)
#### Step 4: Compare Gains
- Gain = Post Value - Pre Value

### 🛠️ Financial Restructuring / Internal Reconstruction
#### Step 1: Identify Total Liabilities Reduction
- Sum capital reduction, waived interest, waived trade credit
#### Step 2: Identify Asset Revaluation Surplus
- Add asset appreciation amounts
#### Step 3: Aggregate Reconstruction Profits
- Total profit = Liability reductions + Revaluation surplus
#### Step 4: Write off Accumulated Losses
- Write off P&L debit and fictitious assets
- Write down overvalued assets and create provisions

## 📋 ICAI Adjustment Checklist

### 🪙 Equity & Debt Reconstruction Adjustments
- Reduce equity share nominal value to specified level
- Change preference dividend rate if stated in scheme
- Eliminate accrued debenture interest if waived
- Convert waived creditor balance to equity shares

### 🏢 Balance Sheet Post-Demerger
- Allocate assets and liabilities to demerged entity
- Keep remaining net assets in parent company
- Adjust reserves to balance the demerger entry

### 🏦 Bank Mergers Special Adjustments
- Calculate post-merger Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)
- Calculate post-merger Gross NPA percentage
- Deduct preliminary expenses from Weak Bank net worth

## ⚠️ Pitfalls, Traps & Examiner Tricks

### 🕳️ Synergy Valuation Pitfalls
#### ⚠️ Double Counting Synergy
- Do not add synergy to pre-merger standalone value
#### ⚠️ Overestimating Growth Rates
- Check if post-merger growth applies indefinitely

### 🕳️ Swap Ratio Calculation Traps
#### ⚠️ Inverse Swap Ratio Error
- Ensure ratio is Target/Acquirer, not Acquirer/Target
#### ⚠️ Outstanding Shares Base
- Check if shares are fully paid or partly paid
- Convert partly paid to equivalent fully paid first

### 🕳️ Cash vs Stock True Cost Trap
#### ⚠️ Cash Cost vs Stock Cost
- Cash cost is fixed: Cash Paid - V_B
- Stock cost varies: alpha * V_AB - V_B

## 📝 Key ICAI Definitions (verbatim)

### 📝 Amalgamation
- Amalgamation is a blending of two or more existing undertakings
- Shareholders of each company become substantially shareholders in transferee

### 📝 Acquisition
- Purchase of controlling interest by one company in share capital
- Both acquiring and acquired companies left standing as separate entities

### 📝 Slump Sale
- Sale of business division for lumpsum consideration without individual assets valued

### 📝 Leveraged Buyout
- Acquisition financed 50% or more using borrowed funds
- Deal secured by acquired firm physical assets

## 🎯 Exam-Focused High-Yield Points

### 🎯 Maximum Exchange Ratio
- Set by Acquirer to prevent dilution of own EPS
- Solve: Post-merger EPS >= Pre-merger EPS of Acquirer

### 🎯 Minimum Exchange Ratio
- Set by Target to prevent dilution of own EPS
- Solve: Equivalent post-merger EPS >= Pre-merger EPS of Target

### 🎯 Reverse Merger 3-Test Verification
- Essential to verify all three tests are met

### 🎯 Free Float Market Capitalisation
- Exclude promoter holdings from total market capitalisation

## 🧩 Hidden Logic & Non-Obvious Reasoning

### 💡 Limited Liability Zero-Floor Logic
- Under slow growth/insolvency, equity cannot be negative
- If Assets - Debt < 0, equity value is zero
- Debt value is restricted to remaining total asset value

### 💡 P/E Ratio Expansion Effect
- High P/E acquirer buying low P/E target boosts EPS
- Called boot-strapping earnings without actual synergy

### 💡 Contingent Liability Treatment
- Deduct full contingent liability from NAV if materialisation is assumed

## 📊 Decision Rules, Shortcuts & Method Selection Trees

### 🌲 Method Selection Tree: Target Valuation
#### Given: Valuation Problem
- Are cash flows and growth rates given? -> Use DCF Valuation
- Are sector P/E multiples given? -> Use Relative P/E Valuation
- Is asset and liability book value given? -> Use Net Asset Value
- Are earnings and capitalization rate given? -> Use Earnings Capitalisation Method

### 🌲 Swap Ratio Decision Tree
#### Objective: Set Swap Ratio
- Is the goal to satisfy both companies? -> Use Weighted Fair Value
- Is the goal to avoid EPS dilution? -> Use EPS swap ratio
- Is the goal to reflect market values? -> Use MPS swap ratio

## 🔄 "What If" Scenarios & Edge Cases

### 🔄 What if a Target Share is Partly Paid?
- Convert partly paid to equivalent fully paid shares
- Apply the swap ratio only to fully paid equivalent

### 🔄 What if Tax Rate Changes Mid-Project?
- Apply year-specific tax rates to each year cash flow

### 🔄 What if P/E Ratio Declines Post-Merger?
- Combined MPS will fall even if earnings increase
- Negative synergy on share price due to increased risk

## 🆚 Comparison Nodes (X vs Y)

### 🆚 Spin-off vs Split-up
- Spin-off: Part of business separated; parent company continues to exist
- Split-up: Entire firm broken; parent company ceases to exist

### 🆚 Spin-off vs Equity Carve-out
- Spin-off: Shares given to existing shareholders; no fresh cash
- Equity Carve-out: Shares sold via IPO to public; brings cash

### 🆚 Merger vs Takeover
- Merger: Friendly co-equal combination; legal dissolution of one firm
- Takeover: Often hostile; target company continues as a subsidiary

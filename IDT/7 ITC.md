# CHAPTER 7 — INPUT TAX CREDIT [CGST Act Sec. 16-21 & Rules 36-45]

## 🗺️ CHAPTER OVERVIEW
### What This Chapter Covers
#### Rules, conditions, restrictions, and formulas for availing, apportioning, reversing, and utilizing Input Tax Credit (ITC)
### Governing Law
#### CGST Act: Sections 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 41, 49, 49A, 49B
#### CGST Rules: Rules 36, 37, 37A, 38, 39, 40, 41, 41A, 42, 43, 44, 86A, 86B, 88A
#### IGST Act: Section 20 (applicability of CGST provisions), Section 16 (Zero-Rated Supply)
### Why This Chapter Matters
#### High-weightage area: Tested in compulsory 14-mark computation questions, MCQs, and independent case-study scenarios u/s 17(5) and Rules 42/43
### Logical Flow of ITC
#### Eligibility [Sec 16(1)] → Conditions [Sec 16(2)] → Blocked Credit Filter [Sec 17(5)] → Apportionment [Sec 17(1)/(2) & Rules 42/43] → Special Circumstances [Sec 18] → Distribution [Sec 20] → Utilization [Sec 49, 49A, 49B]

## 📖 APPLICABLE DEFINITIONS
### Input [Sec. 2(59)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Any goods other than capital goods used or intended to be used by a supplier in the course or furtherance of business
### Input Service [Sec. 2(60)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Any service used or intended to be used by a supplier in the course or furtherance of business
### Capital Goods [Sec. 2(19)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Goods, the value of which is capitalized in the books of account of the person claiming the ITC and which are used or intended to be used in the course or furtherance of business
### Inward Supply [Sec. 2(67)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Receipt of goods or services or both, whether by purchase, acquisition, or any other means, with or without consideration
### Input Tax [Sec. 2(62)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Central tax, State tax, Integrated tax, or Union Territory tax charged on any supply of goods or services or both made to a registered person
#### Includes
##### Integrated tax (IGST) charged on import of goods u/s 3(7) of Customs Tariff Act
##### Tax payable under reverse charge mechanism (RCM) u/s 9(3) and 9(4) of CGST Act
##### Tax payable under RCM u/s 5(3) and 5(4) of IGST Act
##### Tax payable under RCM u/s of SGST/UTGST Acts
#### Excludes: Tax paid under the composition levy [Sec. 10]
### Exempt Supply [Sec. 2(47)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Supply of any goods or services or both which attracts nil rate of tax, or which may be wholly exempt u/s 11 of CGST or u/s 6 of IGST Act, and includes non-taxable supply
### Zero-Rated Supply [Sec. 16(1) of IGST Act]
#### Statutory Meaning: Any of the following supplies of goods or services or both:
##### (a) Export of goods or services or both
##### (b) Supply of goods or services or both to an SEZ developer or SEZ unit for authorized operations

## ⚖️ CORE PROVISIONS — SECTIONS
### Sec. 16 — Eligibility and Conditions for Taking ITC
#### Sec. 16(1) — Basic Eligibility
##### 🔴 SHALL: Every registered person is entitled to take credit of input tax charged on inward supplies used or intended to be used in the course or furtherance of business
##### Amount is credited to the electronic credit ledger (ECrL) of such person
#### Sec. 16(2) — Six Core Conditions (Non-Obstante Clause)
##### (a) Possession of tax paying document [Rule 36(1)]
##### (aa) Details of invoice/debit note furnished by supplier in GSTR-1/IFF and communicated to recipient in GSTR-2B [Rule 36(4)]
##### (b) Actual receipt of goods or services or both
###### 🏛️ Deeming Fiction 1 (Bill-to-Ship-To Goods): Deemed received by registered person if delivered by supplier to a recipient/third-party on directions of such registered person [Sec. 16(2)(b)(i)]
###### 🏛️ Deeming Fiction 2 (Bill-to-Ship-To Services): Deemed received by registered person if services provided by supplier to any person on directions and account of such registered person [Sec. 16(2)(b)(ii)]
###### ❌ Proviso 1 (Goods in Lots/Instalments): Entitled to take credit only upon receipt of the last lot or instalment
##### (ba) Details of ITC communicated under Section 38 have not been restricted
##### (c) Tax charged has been actually paid to Government (either in cash or through utilization of eligible ITC)
##### (d) Recipient has furnished return under Section 39 (GSTR-3B)
##### ❌ Proviso 2 (180-Day Payment Failure Reversal):
###### Where a recipient fails to pay the supplier the value of supply plus tax within ⏱️ 180 days from invoice date
###### 🔴 SHALL: Pay/reverse an amount equal to ITC availed, proportionate to unpaid amount, along with interest u/s 50
###### Reversal to be done in GSTR-3B for the tax period immediately following the ⏱️ 180 days period
###### Exceptions to 180-Day Rule
####### 🔁 RCM supplies u/s 9(3) / 9(4)
####### Deemed supplies without consideration under Schedule I
####### Additions made to value u/s 15(2)(b) (recipient incurring supplier's liability)
##### ❌ Proviso 3 (Re-availment): Recipient entitled to re-avail ITC upon subsequent payment of value plus tax
#### Sec. 16(3) — Depreciation vs ITC Double Benefit Bar
##### If depreciation is claimed on the tax component of the cost of capital goods/plant and machinery under Income-tax Act 1961, GST ITC on said tax component is NOT allowed
#### Sec. 16(4) — Statutory Time Limit for Availing ITC
##### ⏱️ Deadline: 30th November following the end of the financial year (FY) to which the invoice/debit note pertains, OR the date of furnishing of the relevant annual return (GSTR-9), whichever is earlier
##### Debit Notes: Time limit is determined based on the date of issuance of the debit note itself, not the date of the underlying original invoice
#### Sec. 16(6) — Relaxation for Revoked Cancellation of Registration
##### If registration is cancelled and subsequently revoked, the registered person can claim ITC on invoices/debit notes during the cancellation period in a return u/s 39 filed within ⏱️ 30 days of the revocation order, even if the 16(4) time limit lapsed during cancellation (provided it had not expired on the cancellation date)
### Sec. 17 — Apportionment of Credit & Blocked Credits
#### Sec. 17(1) — Business vs Other Purposes Apportionment
##### If goods/services are used partly for business and partly for other (personal/non-business) purposes, ITC is restricted to the amount attributable to business purposes
#### Sec. 17(2) — Taxable vs Exempt Supplies Apportionment
##### If goods/services are used partly for taxable (including zero-rated) supplies and partly for exempt supplies, ITC is restricted to the amount attributable to taxable and zero-rated supplies
#### Sec. 17(3) — Valuation of Exempt Supplies for ITC Apportionment
##### Value of exempt supply includes:
###### Supplies liable to tax under RCM u/s 9(3) / 9(4)
###### Transactions in securities (valued at 1% of the sale value of such securities)
###### Sale of land
###### Sale of building (subject to Clause (b) of Para 5 of Schedule II)
##### Value of exempt supply excludes:
###### Schedule III activities/transactions (except Para 5 - land/building sale)
###### Value of services by way of accepting deposits, extending loans/advances where consideration is interest/discount (except for Banks/FIs/NBFCs u/s 17(4)) [Rule 42 Explanation 1]
###### Supply of Duty Credit Scrips u/s Notification 35/2017-CT(R)
#### Sec. 17(4) — Special Option for Banks & Financial Institutions (FIs)
##### 🟡 OPTION: Banking companies/FIs/NBFCs engaged in accepting deposits/extending loans have a monthly choice:
###### Option A: Comply with proportionate apportionment u/s 17(2) [Rules 42 & 43]
###### Option B: Avail 50% of the eligible ITC on inputs, capital goods, and input services in that month; the remaining 50% lapses
###### Irrevocability: Once exercised, Option B cannot be withdrawn during the remaining part of the financial year
###### Exception: The 50% restriction does not apply to tax paid on inward supplies procured from another registered distinct person having the same PAN (100% ITC allowed)
#### Sec. 17(5) — Blocked Credits (Statutory Overriding Clause)
##### Notwithstanding anything in 16(1) or 18(1), ITC is NOT available for:
##### (a) Motor vehicles for transportation of persons with approved seating capacity <= 13 persons (including the driver)
###### Exceptions (ITC allowed): Used for making taxable supplies of:
####### (A) Further supply of such motor vehicles (e.g., dealers)
####### (B) Transportation of passengers
####### (C) Imparting training on driving such motor vehicles
##### (aa) Vessels and aircraft
###### Exceptions (ITC allowed): Used for making taxable supplies of:
####### (A) Further supply of such vessels/aircraft
####### (B) Transportation of passengers
####### (C) Imparting training on navigating (vessels) or flying (aircraft)
####### (ii) Transportation of goods
##### (ab) Services of general insurance, servicing, repair, and maintenance relating to motor vehicles, vessels, or aircraft on which ITC is blocked u/s 17(5)(a)/(aa)
###### Exceptions (ITC allowed):
####### (i) Used for eligible purposes specified in (a)/(aa)
####### (ii) Received by a manufacturer of such motor vehicles, vessels, or aircraft
####### (iii) Received by a general insurance service provider insuring such vehicles, vessels, or aircraft
##### (b)(i) Food and beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services, cosmetic and plastic surgery, leasing, renting or hiring of motor vehicles, vessels or aircraft on which ITC is blocked
###### Exception 1: Inward supply used for making outward taxable supply of the same category, or as an element of a taxable composite or mixed supply
###### Exception 2: Obligatory for an employer to provide to its employees under any law for the time being in force (Applies to all of Sec. 17(5)(b)) [Circular No. 172/04/2022-GST]
##### (b)(ii) Membership of a club, health and fitness centre
##### (b)(iii) Travel benefits extended to employees on vacation (leave travel concession / home travel concession)
##### (c) Works contract services supplied for construction of an immovable property (other than plant and machinery)
###### Exceptions (ITC allowed):
####### (i) Input service for further supply of works contract service (sub-contracting)
####### (ii) Construction of eligible "plant and machinery" (as defined in Explanation)
####### (iii) Value of works contract service is not capitalized in the books
##### (d) Goods or services or both received by a taxable person for construction of an immovable property (other than plant and machinery) on his own account (self-construction)
###### Note: Applies even if used in the course or furtherance of business
###### Exceptions (ITC allowed): Construction of plant and machinery; or expenses not capitalized
##### (e) Goods or services or both on which tax has been paid under Section 10 (Composition Scheme)
##### (f) Goods or services or both received by a Non-Resident Taxable Person (NRTP)
###### Exception: ITC is allowed on goods imported by the NRTP u/s 2(62)(a)
##### (g) Goods or services or both used for personal consumption
##### (h) Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, or disposed of by way of gift or free samples
##### (i) Any tax paid in accordance with the provisions of Section 74 (pertaining to period up to FY 2023-24), Section 129 (detention/seizure), and Section 130 (confiscation)
### Sec. 18 — Credit in Special Circumstances
#### Sec. 18(1) — Entitlement to ITC
##### (a) Applied for registration within ⏱️ 30 days of becoming liable, and has been granted registration:
###### Entitled to ITC on inputs held in stock, inputs in semi-finished/finished goods held in stock on the day immediately preceding the date from which he became liable to pay tax
###### Capital Goods: No ITC allowed
##### (b) Obtains voluntary registration u/s 25(3):
###### Entitled to ITC on inputs held in stock, inputs in semi-finished/finished goods held in stock on the day immediately preceding the date of grant of registration
###### Capital Goods: No ITC allowed
##### (c) Ceases to pay composition tax and switches to regular scheme u/s 9:
###### Entitled to ITC on inputs held in stock, semi-finished/finished goods in stock, AND capital goods on the day immediately preceding the date he becomes liable to pay tax u/s 9
###### Capital Goods ITC: Reduced by 5% per quarter or part thereof from invoice date [Rule 40(1)(a)]
##### (d) Exempt supply of goods/services becomes a taxable supply:
###### Entitled to ITC on inputs in stock, semi-finished/finished goods in stock relatable to such exempt supply, AND capital goods exclusively used for such exempt supply on the day immediately preceding the date the supply becomes taxable
###### Capital Goods ITC: Reduced by 5% per quarter or part thereof from invoice date [Rule 40(1)(a)]
#### Sec. 18(2) — Absolute One-Year Invoice Limit
##### No ITC under Sec. 18(1) can be claimed after the expiry of ⏱️ 1 year from the date of issue of tax invoice relating to such supply
#### Sec. 18(3) — Transfer of ITC on Change in Constitution
##### In case of sale, merger, demerger, amalgamation, lease, or transfer of business with specific provisions for transfer of liabilities
##### 🔴 SHALL: Allow transfer of unutilized ITC in electronic credit ledger of transferor to transferee [Rule 41]
#### Sec. 18(4) — Reversal of ITC on Switching to Composition or Becoming Wholly Exempt
##### 🔴 SHALL: Pay/reverse an amount by debiting electronic credit ledger / cash ledger equivalent to ITC on inputs in stock, semi-finished/finished goods in stock, and capital goods (reduced on pro-rata basis) on the day immediately preceding the date of option/exemption
##### Balance ITC lying in electronic credit ledger, if any, shall lapse
#### Sec. 18(6) — Supply of Capital Goods on which ITC was claimed
##### Registered person must pay higher of:
###### (a) ITC taken on such capital goods/plant & machinery, reduced by 5% per quarter or part thereof from invoice date (Rule 40(2)), or
###### (b) Tax on the transaction value u/s 15
##### ❌ Proviso: If refractory bricks, moulds, dies, jigs, and fixtures are supplied as scrap, tax may be paid on transaction value u/s 15 only
### Sec. 20 — Input Service Distributor (ISD)
#### 🔴 SHALL: Register as ISD u/s 24(viii) if receiving invoices for common input services on behalf of distinct persons
#### 🔴 SHALL: Distribute CGST/SGST/UTGST/IGST as prescribed in Section 20 and Rule 39
### Sec. 21 — Recovery of Credit Distributed in Excess by ISD
#### If ISD distributes credit in contravention of Sec. 20, excess credit shall be recovered from the recipients u/s 73, 74, or 74A (along with interest and penalties)

## 📏 ITC RULES [Rules 36-44]
### Rule 36 — Documentary Requirements and Conditions
#### Rule 36(1) — Eligible Documents for Availing ITC:
##### (a) Tax invoice issued by registered supplier u/s 31
##### (b) Invoice issued by recipient u/s 31(3)(f) in RCM supplies, subject to tax payment
##### (c) Debit note issued by supplier u/s 34
##### (d) Bill of Entry or equivalent under Customs Act 1962 for imports
##### (e) ISD invoice, credit note, or distribution document u/s Rule 54(1)
#### Rule 36(2) — Document Quality / Minimum Details:
##### If invoice does not contain all details, ITC can be availed if it has:
###### Amount of tax charged
###### Description of goods/services
###### Total value of supply
###### GSTIN of supplier and recipient
###### Place of supply (for inter-State supplies)
#### Rule 36(3) — No ITC on fraud-related tax payments u/s 74 (for periods up to FY 2023-24)
#### Rule 36(4) — GSTR-2B Matching Requirement (100% Match Rule):
##### ITC on GSTR-1 invoices can only be availed if details are furnished by supplier in GSTR-1/IFF and communicated to recipient in GSTR-2B
### Rule 37 — Reversal for Non-Payment within 180 Days
#### 🔴 SHALL: Reverse ITC proportionate to unpaid amount along with interest u/s 50 in the GSTR-3B for the tax period following the ⏱️ 180 days from invoice date
### Rule 37A — Reversal of ITC on Non-Payment of Tax by Supplier
#### Scenario: Recipient availed ITC in GSTR-3B based on GSTR-2B, but supplier failed to file GSTR-3B for the corresponding period
#### ⏱️ Deadline for Supplier: If supplier does not file GSTR-3B till 30th September following the end of the FY in which ITC was availed
#### ⏱️ Reversal Deadline for Recipient: Recipient must reverse such ITC in GSTR-3B on or before 30th November of the succeeding FY
#### Interest Impact: No interest u/s 50 if reversed on or before 30th November; interest @ 18% applies if reversed after 30th November
#### Re-availment: Recipient can re-avail the reversed ITC in GSTR-3B of any tax period after the supplier subsequently files GSTR-3B
### Rule 38 — Bank and FI Option Procedure
#### 🔴 SHALL: Do not avail credit of tax paid on inputs/input services used for non-business purposes and blocked items u/s 17(5)
#### 🔴 SHALL: Avail 50% of remaining eligible ITC; reverse the balance 50% in Form GSTR-3B
### Rule 39 — ISD Distribution Procedure
#### 🔴 SHALL: Distribute credit in the same month u/s Form GSTR-6
#### 🔴 SHALL: Distribute credit attributable to a specific recipient only to that recipient
#### 🔴 SHALL: Distribute credit attributable to more than one recipient pro-rata based on State/UT turnover of the recipient in the relevant period to aggregate turnover of all operational recipients u/s Formula: `C1 = (t1 / T) * C`
#### 🔴 SHALL: Distribute CGST/SGST/UTGST/IGST separately:
##### Intrastate: CGST as CGST, SGST as SGST
##### Interstate: CGST and SGST as IGST; IGST as IGST
### Rule 40 — Manner of Claiming Credit under Special Circumstances (u/s 18)
#### 🔴 SHALL: File Form GST ITC-01 u/s ⏱️ 30 days of becoming eligible to claim ITC
#### 🔴 SHALL: Obtain CA or Cost Accountant certificate if the aggregate claim of CGST + SGST/UTGST + IGST exceeds ₹ 2 lakh [Rule 40(1)(d)]
### Rule 41 — Transfer of Credit on Constitution Change (u/s 18(3))
#### 🔴 SHALL: File Form GST ITC-02 on common portal along with CA/Cost Accountant certificate certifying provision for transfer of liabilities
#### Demerger Apportionment: ITC is apportioned in the ratio of the "value of assets" of new units to total assets (irrespective of whether ITC was availed on those assets) [Rule 41(1) Proviso]
### Rule 41A — Transfer of Credit on Separate Registration in State
#### 🔴 SHALL: File Form GST ITC-02A within ⏱️ 30 days of obtaining separate registration to transfer unutilized ITC in the ratio of "value of assets" held by newly registered places at the time of registration
### Rule 42 — Apportionment of ITC on Inputs and Input Services
#### Step 1: Credit to ECrL (C1)
##### Formula: `C1 = T - (T1 + T2 + T3)`
##### T = Total input tax involved in the tax period
##### T1 = Input tax attributable to non-business purposes
##### T2 = Input tax attributable to exempt supplies
##### T3 = Blocked credits u/s 17(5)
#### Step 2: Compute Common Credit (C2)
##### Formula: `C2 = C1 - T4`
##### T4 = Input tax attributable exclusively to taxable (including zero-rated) supplies
#### Step 3: Compute Ineligible Credit to be Reversed (D1 and D2)
##### D1 (Exempt Proportion) u/s Formula: `D1 = (E / F) * C2`
###### E = Aggregate value of exempt supplies during the tax period
###### F = Total turnover in the State of the registered person during the tax period
###### Note: If no turnover in tax period, use previous month's E and F values
##### D2 (Non-Business Proportion) u/s Formula: `D2 = 5% * C2`
#### Step 4: Compute Eligible Common Credit (C3)
##### Formula: `C3 = C2 - (D1 + D2)`
#### Step 5: Year-End Reconciliation [Rule 42(2)]
##### Calculate finally for the entire FY before 30th September of the succeeding FY
##### If Final (D1 + D2) > Monthly (D1 + D2) paid: Reverse the difference in GSTR-3B/DRC-03 u/s interest @ 18% from 1st April of the succeeding FY till payment date
##### If Final (D1 + D2) < Monthly (D1 + D2) paid: Re-avail the excess reversed amount in GSTR-3B
### Rule 43 — Apportionment of ITC on Capital Goods
#### (a) Capital goods used exclusively for non-business or exempt supplies:
##### 🔴 SHALL: Indicated in GSTR-3B; do NOT credit to electronic credit ledger [Rule 43(1)(a)]
#### (b) Capital goods used exclusively for taxable/zero-rated supplies:
##### 🔴 SHALL: Indicated in GSTR-3B; credit directly to electronic credit ledger [Rule 43(1)(b)]
#### (c) Capital goods commonly used (taxable + exempt + personal), denoted as 'A':
##### 🔴 SHALL: Credit 'A' to electronic credit ledger; useful life is taken as ⏱️ 5 years (60 months) from invoice date [Rule 43(1)(c)]
##### Proviso (Exempt to Common Change): If capital goods under (a) subsequently get covered under common use:
###### Credit full tax 'A' to electronic credit ledger
###### Calculate ineligible credit 'Tie' u/s 5% for every quarter or part thereof during which capital goods were exclusively exempt/personal
###### 🔴 SHALL: Add 'Tie' to output tax liability of the tax period of claim
##### Proviso (Taxable to Common Change): If capital goods under (b) subsequently get covered under common use:
###### Add invoice tax u/s Tc
#### (d) Aggregate value of common capital goods: `Tc`
##### Sum of 'A' of all common capital goods whose useful life remains
#### (e) Monthly common credit: `Tm`
##### Formula: `Tm = Tc / 60`
#### (g) Monthly exempt credit to be added to output tax liability: `Te`
##### Formula: `Te = (E / F) * Tr`
###### Tr = Sum of Tm for all common capital goods
###### E = Exempt supplies value during the tax period
###### F = Total turnover in the State during the tax period
### Rule 44 — Reversal under Special Circumstances (u/s 18(4) or 29(5))
#### Inputs in Stock (Inputs as such, contained in semi-finished/finished goods):
##### Reversal amount calculated proportionately based on corresponding invoices
##### If invoices not available: Estimate u/s market price on the date of event, certified by a practicing CA/Cost Accountant [Rule 44(3)]
#### Capital Goods:
##### Reversal amount is computed on pro-rata basis for the remaining useful life in months (taking useful life as ⏱️ 5 years/60 months)
##### Formula: `Reversal = ITC taken * (Remaining useful life in months / 60)`

## 📋 SCHEDULES
### Schedule I — Transactions Deemed Supply Without Consideration
#### Para 2: Supplies between related persons or distinct persons u/s 25
##### ITC Relevance: Inward supplies for such transactions are eligible for ITC even if there is no invoice/payment between them (exempt from the 180-day reversal rule u/s Rule 37 Proviso)
#### Para 3: Principal-Agent transactions (Goods)
##### ITC Relevance: Agent is entitled to ITC on goods received from principal if invoice is issued in Agent's name
### Schedule II — Classification as Supply of Goods or Services
#### Para 5(b): Construction of building where part consideration is received before completion certificate
##### Classification: Supply of Services
#### Para 6(a): Works contract composite supply
##### Classification: Supply of Services
### Schedule III — Neither Supply of Goods nor Services (Negative List)
#### Para 1: Services by an employee to the employer in the course of or in relation to employment
##### ITC Relevance: Non-supply, no output GST, but does NOT trigger ITC reversal on inputs/input services u/s Rule 42/43 (specifically excluded from "exempt supply" definition u/s 17(3))
#### Para 5: Sale of land and sale of building (subject to clause (b) of paragraph 5 of Schedule II)
##### ITC Relevance: Though neither supply, it is specifically INCLUDED in "exempt supply" u/s 17(3) for Rule 42/43 ITC reversal

## 🔗 INTERLINKED SECTIONS & RULES
### [Sec. 16(1)] ↔ [Sec. 17(5)]
#### Entitlement is primary [16(1)], but completely blocked by the specific exclusions [17(5)] which act as an overriding filter
### [Sec. 16(2) Proviso 2] ↔ [Rule 37]
#### Section 16(2) provides the substantive obligation to pay within 180 days; Rule 37 provides the procedural machinery for proportionate reversal and interest calculation u/s 50
### [Sec. 18(4)] ↔ [Rule 44]
#### Section 18(4) directs reversal on switching to composition/exempt; Rule 44 provides the exact pro-rata month-based calculation for capital goods and invoice/market-value-based calculation for inputs
### [Sec. 17(4)] ↔ [Rule 38]
#### Section 17(4) grants the 50% option to banks; Rule 38 provides the operational steps to exclude 17(5) and non-business items first, then split the rest 50:50

## 🚫 EXCLUSIONS, NON-SUPPLIES & NEGATIVE LIST

#### Motor vehicles for passenger transport with capacity <= 13 (except for dealership, passenger transport, driving school)
#### Vessels & aircraft (except for dealership, passenger transport, flying school, goods transport)
#### General insurance, repair, servicing of blocked vehicles/vessels/aircraft
#### Food & beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, cosmetic surgery (except when obligatory u/s law or same-category outward supply)
#### Membership of club, health & fitness centre

#### Works contract for immovable property construction (except plant & machinery, sub-contracting, or if charged to P&L)

#### Inward supplies on which tax is paid under Composition Scheme [Sec. 10]
#### Inward supplies of NRTP (except imported goods)
#### Goods/services used for personal consumption
#### Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, or given as gift/free sample
#### Tax paid due to fraud detection/seizure/confiscation [Sec. 74, 129, 130]

## 🔄 COMPOSITE & MIXED SUPPLY
### In-flight Dining / Catering [Sec. 8(a)]
#### Nature: Composite supply where Passenger Transportation by Air is Principal Supply
#### ITC Treatment: Since catering service is used as an element of taxable composite supply (transportation), airline can claim ITC on catering bills u/s Proviso to Sec. 17(5)(b)(i)
### Buy One Get One Free Offers [Circular No. 92/11/2019-GST]
#### Nature: Supply of two or more goods at a single price; to be classified as composite or mixed supply based on natural bundling test u/s Sec. 8
#### ITC Treatment: Not a supply of free goods; ITC on inputs/input services used for such offers is fully ALLOWED (no reversal u/s 17(5)(h))

## 💡 CIRCULARS, NOTIFICATIONS & AAR CLARIFICATIONS
### 📜 Circular No. 47/21/2018-GST (OEM Moulds)
#### Issue: OEM provides moulds/dies free of cost (FOC) to component manufacturer
#### Clarification: Not a supply (no consideration, unrelated); no requirement for OEM to reverse ITC on moulds as they are used in the course of business
### 📜 Circular No. 195/07/2023-GST & Circular No. 216/10/2024-GST (Warranty Claims)
#### Issue: Manufacturer/distributor provides FOC replacement of parts/repair under warranty
#### Clarification: Original price includes warranty cost; no GST on replacement/repair; NO ITC reversal required on such parts
#### Distributor-Manufacturer Scenarios:
##### (1) Distributor replaces from stock & invoices manufacturer: GST payable, manufacturer takes ITC; no reversal by distributor
##### (2) Distributor raises requisition, manufacturer replenishes FOC: No GST on replenishment, no reversal by manufacturer
##### (3) Distributor replaces from stock, manufacturer issues credit note u/s 34(2): Manufacturer adjusts tax liability ONLY IF distributor reverses ITC
##### (4) Distributor replaces from stock, manufacturer replenishes via delivery challan FOC: No GST on replenishment, no reversal by manufacturer
### 📜 Circular No. 214/8/2024-GST (Life Insurance Premium)
#### Issue: Reversal of ITC on non-taxable premium portion u/s Rule 32(4)
#### Clarification: Premium portion not included in taxable value is neither nil-rated nor wholly exempt; NO reversal of ITC required under Rule 42/43
### 📜 Circular No. 211/5/2024-GST (RCM Invoices u/s 16(4))
#### Issue: Time limit for claiming ITC on RCM supplies from unregistered suppliers
#### Clarification: The relevant FY is the year in which the recipient issues self-invoice u/s 31(3)(f); recipient can claim ITC u/s 16(4) within ⏱️ 30th Nov of succeeding FY of invoice issuance (subject to tax payment)
### 📜 Circular No. 231/25/2024-GST (Demo Vehicles)
#### Issue: Eligibility of ITC on demo vehicles for car dealers
#### Clarification: Demo vehicles promote sales; hence used for "further supply of such vehicles"; ITC is NOT blocked u/s 17(5)(a)
#### Exceptions: If used for staff transportation or capitalized and u/s 16(3) depreciation is claimed on tax component -> ITC barred
### 📜 Circular No. 219/13/2024-GST (Ducts & Manholes)
#### Issue: ITC on OFC ducts and manholes
#### Clarification: OFC ducts and manholes are basic components for telecommunication network; covered under "plant and machinery" (not civil structures); ITC is NOT restricted u/s 17(5)(c)/(d)
### 📜 Circular No. 217/11/2024-GST (Insurance Claims Repair)
#### Issue: Cashless vs Reimbursement mode of MV repair claim settlement
#### Clarification: Insurance company is "recipient" of repair service to the extent of claim cost; ITC on repair expenses is ALLOWED to insurer if:
##### (a) Invoices are issued in the name of the insurance company
##### (b) ITC is claimed solely for the amount reimbursed to the insured

## 🔢 FORMULAS, SLABS & COMPUTATIONS
### Rule 42 Apportionment (Inputs & Input Services)
#### Total Input Tax: `T`
#### Credited to ECrL: `C1 = T - (T1 + T2 + T3)`
#### Common Credit: `C2 = C1 - T4`
#### Exempt Reversal: `D1 = (E / F) * C2`
#### Non-Business Reversal: `D2 = 5% * C2`
#### Net Eligible Common Credit: `C3 = C2 - (D1 + D2)`
### Rule 43 Apportionment (Capital Goods)
#### Monthly Common Credit: `Tm = Tc / 60` (Useful life = 60 months)
#### Monthly Exempt Reversal: `Te = (E / F) * Tr` (Tr = Sum of Tm for all common capital goods)
### Sec. 18(6) Capital Goods Disposal
#### Pay higher of:
##### Method 1 (Quarterly Reduction): `ITC taken − (ITC taken * 5% per quarter or part thereof from invoice date)`
##### Method 2 (Transaction Value Tax): `Transaction Value * GST Rate`
##### Rule 44(6) pro-rata check: In remaining months of useful life u/s Formula: `ITC taken * (Remaining useful life in months / 60)`

### 💬 Illustration 3: Rule 42 — Hawai Slippers (Exempt) vs Shoes (Taxable)
#### Facts: Common dye credit C2 = ₹ 1,15,000. Exempt Turnover E = ₹ 3 cr; Taxable Turnover = ₹ 2 cr; Total F = ₹ 5 cr.
#### Computation:
##### D1 = (3 cr / 5 cr) * 1,15,000 = ₹ 69,000 (To be reversed)
##### D2 = 5% * 1,15,000 = ₹ 5,750 (To be reversed)
##### Net eligible credit C3 = 1,15,000 - (69,000 + 5,750) = ₹ 40,250.
### 💬 Illustration 4: Sec 18(1)(c) — Exit from Composition
#### Facts: Retailer exits composition on 15th July. Capital goods purchased on 1st April (1 year ago) for ₹ 1,00,,000 + ₹ 18,000 GST.

##### Eligible capital goods ITC = 18,000 - (25% * 18,000) = ₹ 13,500.

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NUANCES & EXAM TRAPS
### ⚠️ Original vs Revoked Registration Gap
#### The Trap: Assuming that since the registration was cancelled, the taxpayer loses all ITC on invoices received during the cancellation period
#### The Reality: Extended relaxation u/s Sec 16(6) allows claiming ITC on such invoices in returns filed within ⏱️ 30 days of the revocation order, provided the original 16(4) limit had not expired on the cancellation date
### ⚠️ Debit Note Time Limits
#### The Trap: Determining the 16(4) time limit for a Debit Note using the date of its original underlying invoice
#### The Reality: The Debit Note has an independent time limit u/s Sec 16(4). The date of the Debit Note itself is the sole determinant for the relevant FY
### ⚠️ SC Safari Retreats vs Finance Act 2025 Amendment
#### The Trap: Relying on the Safari Retreats SC ruling to claim ITC on shopping malls/buildings built for renting out u/s "plant or machinery"
#### The Reality: Finance Act 2025 explicitly replaces "plant or machinery" with "plant and machinery" in Section 17(5)(d), retrospectively shutting the loophole and restricting Safari Retreats' benefit
### ⚠️ Employee Travel Benefits — The Statutory Law Hook
#### The Trap: Blocking ITC on Leave Travel Concession (LTC) simply because it relates to employees' personal travel
#### The Reality: u/s Proviso to Sec 17(5)(b), if it is obligatory for the employer to provide LTC under any law (e.g., Factories Act), ITC is fully ALLOWED

## 🧪 CONCEPT TESTS & BOUNDARY CASES
### Situation — Obligatory Canteen Service
#### Fact Pattern: A software firm has 1,200 employees. The Factories Act 1948 does not apply to it. However, under local Karnataka Shops & Establishments Act, a canteen is mandatory for firms with > 100 staff. The firm avails catering services.
#### Question: Can the firm claim ITC on the outdoor catering service?
#### Answer: YES.
#### Reasoning: The proviso to Sec 17(5)(b) allows ITC where it is obligatory for an employer to provide the service under "any law for the time being in force". It is not restricted to the Factories Act only.
### Boundary Case — RCM Self-Invoice Delayed
#### Fact Pattern: Recipient receives goods from an unregistered supplier on 10th March 2024 (Time of Supply is March 2024, FY 2023-24). Recipient pays tax in cash under RCM in GSTR-3B for March 2024 but forgets to issue self-invoice. Recipient issues self-invoice u/s 31(3)(f) on 15th April 2025 (FY 2025-26).
#### Question: What is the last date to claim ITC?
#### Answer: 30th November 2026.
#### Reasoning: As per Circular No. 211/5/2024-GST, the relevant FY is the year in which the invoice is issued. Since self-invoice was issued in FY 2025-26, the 16(4) time limit is 30th November 2026. Note: Taxpayer may face Section 122 penalty for delayed invoice issuance, but ITC is legally preserved.

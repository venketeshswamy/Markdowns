## 📖 APPLICABLE DEFINITIONS
### 2(52) Goods
#### Statutory Meaning: Every kind of movable property
#### Includes:
##### Actionable claims
##### Growing crops, grass, things attached to land agreed to be severed
#### Excludes:
##### Money
##### Securities
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "movable property"
#### ⚡ Common Mistake: Forgetting that actionable claims are goods (though only specified ones are taxed).
### 2(102) Services
#### Statutory Meaning: Anything other than goods, money, and securities
#### Includes:
##### Activities relating to use of money or its conversion for a separate consideration
##### Facilitating or arranging transactions in securities
#### Excludes:
##### Goods, Money, Securities
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "anything other than"
### 2(31) Consideration
#### Statutory Meaning: Payment made/to be made, or monetary value of act/forbearance
#### Includes:
##### Payments in money or otherwise (barter/exchange)
##### By recipient or any other person
#### Excludes:
##### Subsidy given by Central/State Government
##### Deposit (unless applied as consideration by supplier)
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "in respect of, in response to, or for the inducement of"
### 2(17) Business
#### Statutory Meaning: Any trade, commerce, manufacture, profession, vocation, adventure, wager
#### Includes:
##### Any activity whether or not for a pecuniary benefit
##### Incidental/ancillary activities
##### Activities regardless of volume, frequency, continuity, or regularity
##### Supply/acquisition of capital assets on commencement/closure
##### Club/association facilities for members
##### Admission to premises
##### Services as holder of an office accepted in course of trade/profession
##### Race club activities / totalisator / book maker
##### Government / local authority acting as public authorities
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "whether or not for a pecuniary benefit"
### 2(84) Person
#### Statutory Meaning: Exhaustive list of legal entities
#### Includes:
##### Individual, HUF, Company, Firm, LLP, Trust
##### AOP/BOI (incorporated or not, India or outside)
##### Co-operative Society, Society, Local Authority, CG/SG
##### Every artificial juridical person
### Sec. 15 Explanation — Related Persons
#### Statutory Meaning: Persons legally deemed to influence each other
#### Includes:
##### Officers/directors of one another's business
##### Legally recognised partners
##### Employer & Employee
##### Person controlling/owning ≥ 25% voting stock of both
##### One controls the other, or third person controls both, or both control third person
##### Members of the same family
##### Sole agent / sole distributor
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "control" and "family"
### 2(49) Family
#### Statutory Meaning: Relatives of a person
#### Includes:
##### Spouse and children (unconditional)
##### Parents, grandparents, brothers, sisters (ONLY IF wholly or mainly dependent)
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "wholly or mainly dependent"
### 2(69) Local Authority [FA 2025 Amendment]
#### Statutory Meaning: Panchayat, Municipality, Municipal Committee, Zilla Parishad, Cantonment Board, Regional/District Council, Development Board
#### Includes (FA 2025 Addition):
##### 📌 Statutory Explanation: "local fund" or "municipal fund" means any fund under control/management of local self-government for civic functions, vested with powers to levy tax/duty/toll/cess/fee.
### 2(1) Actionable Claim [read with Sec. 3 TPA 1882]
#### Statutory Meaning: Claim to any debt (unsecured) or beneficial interest in movable property not in possession.
#### ⚡ Common Mistake: Equating all actionable claims to supply. Only "Specified Actionable Claims" are supply.
### 2(102A) Specified Actionable Claim
#### Statutory Meaning: Actionable claim involved in betting, casinos, gambling, horse racing, lottery, or online money gaming.

## ⚖️ CORE PROVISIONS [Section-wise]
### Sec. 7 — Meaning and Scope of Supply
#### Sec. 7(1)(a) — General Supply
##### Supply includes all forms of supply of goods or services (sale, transfer, barter, exchange, license, rental, lease, disposal)
##### ✅ Condition 1: Made or agreed to be made for a consideration
##### ✅ Condition 2: In the course or furtherance of business
##### 🔴 SHALL apply to all commercial transactions.
#### Sec. 7(1)(aa) — Supply by Club/Association
##### Activities/transactions by a person (other than individual) to its members/constituents or vice-versa for cash/deferred payment/valuable consideration.
##### 🏛️ Sec. 7(1)(aa) deeming fiction: Person and members deemed as two separate persons. (Overrides Doctrine of Mutuality).
#### Sec. 7(1)(b) — Import of Services
##### Importation of services for a consideration
##### ✅ Condition: Whether or not in the course or furtherance of business.
##### ⚡ Exam Trap: Import of service for personal use IS a supply if made for consideration.
#### Sec. 7(1)(c) — Deemed Supply
##### The activities specified in Schedule I, made or agreed to be made without a consideration.
#### Sec. 7(1A) — Classification
##### 🔴 SHALL classify activities constituting supply as either Goods or Services as per Schedule II.
#### Sec. 7(2) — Non-Supplies (Negative List)
##### 7(2)(a): Activities specified in Schedule III.
##### 7(2)(b): Activities by CG/SG/Local Authority notified by Government.
##### 🏛️ Deeming Fiction: SHALL be treated neither as supply of goods nor services.
#### Sec. 7(3) — Government Power
##### 🟡 MAY notify transactions to be treated as supply of goods (not services) or supply of services (not goods).

## 📋 SCHEDULES 
### Schedule I — Deemed Supply Without Consideration
#### Purpose of This Schedule
##### Deems specific activities as supply even if no consideration exists, provided they are in course/furtherance of business.
#### Para 1 — Permanent Transfer/Disposal of Business Assets
##### Provision: Permanent transfer/disposal of business assets where ITC has been availed.
##### ✅ Condition: ITC must have been availed on such assets.
##### ❌ Exception: If ITC is blocked or not availed, no supply.
##### 💬 Example: Donating old office laptops to charity (ITC was taken) = Supply.
#### Para 2 — Supply between Related / Distinct Persons
##### Provision: Supply between related persons or distinct persons [Sec. 25], made in course/furtherance of business.
##### ✅ Condition: Must be related (Sec 15) or distinct (same PAN, diff registrations).
##### ❌ Proviso: Gifts `₹ 50,000 threshold` per FY from employer to employee are 🟢 EXEMPT (not treated as supply).
##### ⚡ Nuance: Stock transfers between branches in different states are taxable. Branches in same state under single registration are not distinct persons.
#### Para 3 — Supply between Principal and Agent
##### Provision: Supply of goods by principal to agent (where agent undertakes to supply on behalf of principal) OR by agent to principal (where agent receives on behalf of principal).
##### 🔑 Operative Condition (Invoice Test): Does the agent issue the invoice to the customer in his own name?
##### 💬 Example: If agent issues invoice in Principal's name → NOT Schedule I agent. If in Agent's name → IS Schedule I agent.
##### ⚡ Exam Trap: Applies ONLY to supply of GOODS, not services.
#### Para 4 — Import of Services from Related Person
##### Provision: Import of services by a person from a related person or from any of his other establishments outside India.
##### ✅ Condition: Must be in the course or furtherance of business.
##### ❌ Exception: Import from related person for personal use without consideration = NOT a supply.

### Schedule II — Classification as Goods or Services
#### Purpose of This Schedule
##### Resolves classification disputes (Goods vs Services). Does NOT determine if an activity is a supply.
#### Para 1 — Transfer
##### Title in goods → Goods
##### Right in goods / undivided share without transfer of title → Services
##### Title in goods under agreement passing property at future date (Hire Purchase) → Goods
#### Para 2 — Land and Building
##### Lease, tenancy, easement, licence to occupy land → Services
##### Letting out building (commercial/industrial/residential) → Services
#### Para 3 — Treatment or Process
##### Treatment/process applied to another person's goods (Job work) → Services
#### Para 4 — Transfer of Business Assets
##### Transferred/disposed so as no longer forming part of business → Goods
##### Put to private use / made available for non-business purpose → Services
##### Assets of person ceasing to be taxable person → Goods (Deemed supply immediately before ceasing).
##### ❌ Exception: Business transferred as a going concern, or carried on by personal representative.
#### Para 5 — Specific Services
##### Renting of immovable property → Services
##### Construction of complex/building intended for sale (before completion) → Services
##### ❌ Proviso: If entire consideration received AFTER completion certificate/first occupation → Schedule III (Not a supply).
##### Temporary transfer/permitting use of IPR → Services
##### Development, design, programming of IT software → Services
##### Agreeing to obligation to refrain from act, tolerate an act/situation, or do an act → Services
##### Transfer of right to use any goods for any purpose → Services
#### Para 6 — Composite Supplies
##### Works contract [Sec. 2(119)] → Services
##### Supply of food/beverages for human consumption (Restaurant) → Services

### Schedule III — Negative List (Neither Goods Nor Services)
#### Purpose of This Schedule
##### Activities strictly kept outside the GST net.
#### Para 1 — Employment
##### Provision: Services by employee to employer in the course of or in relation to employment.
##### ⚡ Nuance: Contract for service (Principal-to-Principal) is taxable. Contract of service (Employer-Employee) is not.
#### Para 2 — Court/Tribunal
##### Provision: Services by Court or Tribunal established under any law.
##### 📜 Circular No. 32/06/2018: Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions are tribunals. Fee/penalty not taxable.
#### Para 3 — Constitutional / Govt Posts
##### Provision: Functions performed by MPs, MLAs, Panchayat/Municipality members. Duties by persons holding constitutional posts. Duties of Chairperson/Member/Director of Govt body (not deemed employee).
#### Para 4 — Deceased
##### Provision: Services of funeral, burial, crematorium, mortuary including transportation of deceased.
#### Para 5 — Land and Building
##### Provision: Sale of land and, subject to Sch II Para 5(b), sale of building (completed building).
#### Para 6 — Actionable Claims
##### Provision: Actionable claims, OTHER THAN specified actionable claims (betting, casinos, gambling, horse racing, lottery, online money gaming).
#### Para 7 — Merchant Trading
##### Provision: Supply of goods from a place in non-taxable territory to another place in non-taxable territory without entering India.
#### Para 8 — Warehoused Goods & High Sea Sales
##### Provision: Supply of warehoused goods before clearance for home consumption. High sea sales (endorsement of documents of title before clearance).
#### Para 9 & 10 — Insurance specific
##### Provision: Apportionment of co-insurance premium by lead insurer to co-insurer. Services by insurer to reinsurer (ceding commission).

## 🔗 INTERLINKED SECTIONS
### Sec. 7(1)(c) ↔ Schedule I Para 2 ↔ Sec. 15 ↔ Sec. 25
#### Combined Effect
##### Supply without consideration is taxable if between related/distinct persons. Sec 15 defines related (includes employer-employee). Sec 25 defines distinct (same PAN, diff states). Thus, branch transfers are taxable, and employer gifts > ₹50,000 are taxable.
### Sec. 7(1)(b) ↔ Schedule I Para 4
#### Combined Effect
##### Import of service WITH consideration = Supply (Personal or Business).
##### Import of service WITHOUT consideration = Supply ONLY IF from related person/estb AND for business.

## 🚫 NEGATIVE LIST / NON-SUPPLIES (Notified)
### Sec. 7(2)(b) — Notified Non-Supplies
#### Panchayat / Municipality Functions
##### Statutory Basis: Notification No. 14/2017 CT(R)
##### Why Not a Supply: Activities entrusted under Art 243G (Panchayat) or 243W (Municipality).
#### Grant of Alcoholic Liquor Licence
##### Statutory Basis: Notification No. 25/2019 CT(R)
##### Why Not a Supply: Special dispensation only for liquor licenses granted by State Govt against fee. (Note: Grant of mining rights/spectrum IS taxable).

## 🔄 COMPOSITE & MIXED SUPPLY
### Composite Supply [Sec. 8(a)]
#### Definition[Sec. 2(30)]
##### Consists of 2 or more taxable supplies.
##### Naturally bundled and supplied in conjunction in ordinary course of business.
##### One is a principal supply [Sec. 2(90)].
#### Tax Rate → 🔴 SHALL be treated as supply of Principal Supply (rate of principal applies to total value).
#### 💬 Examples
##### ✅ Is Composite: Laptop + Bag (Laptop is principal).
##### ✅ Is Composite: Poshaak shirts in designer boxes with transit insurance.
### Mixed Supply [Sec. 8(b)]
#### Definition [Sec. 2(74)]
##### 2 or more individual supplies made in conjunction for a single price.
##### Not naturally bundled.
#### Tax Rate → 🔴 SHALL be treated as supply of that particular supply which attracts HIGHEST rate of tax.
#### 💬 Examples
##### ✅ Is Mixed: Gift pack of chocolates, juices, sweets.
##### ✅ Is Mixed: House rented for residential + printing press usage for a single rent.
### ⚡ Single Invoice ≠ Automatically Composite/Mixed
#### If multiple items are sold, individually priced, and not bundled naturally, they are independent supplies taxed at individual rates (e.g., Car servicing: separate charge for parts and labour).

## 💡 ANALYSIS, CLARIFICATIONS & CIRCULARS
### Sales Promotion, BOGO & Free Samples — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 92/11/2019
#### Issue: Are free samples or "Buy 1 Get 1" taxable?
#### GST Treatment:
##### Free samples: NOT a supply (unless Sch I applies). ITC must be reversed.
##### BOGO: Treated as supplying two goods for the price of one. It is a composite or mixed supply based on bundling. ITC is fully available.
### Liquidated Damages & Penalties — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 178/10/2022
#### Issue: Are damages, cheque bounce fees, or forfeiture of earnest money taxable under Sch II Para 5(e) (tolerating an act)?
#### GST Treatment:
##### ❌ NOT a Supply: Liquidated damages for breach, Cheque dishonor fine, Penalty for law violation, Forfeiture of salary for early quitting, Forfeiture of earnest money. (These are to deter breach, not consideration for tolerating it).
##### ✅ IS a Supply (Ancillary): Late payment surcharge (bundled with main supply of electricity/loan). Cancellation charges for tickets/hotels (ancillary to main service).
### Vouchers (PPIs) — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 243/37/2024
#### Issue: Are vouchers actionable claims or goods/services? What about unredeemed vouchers (breakage)?
#### GST Treatment:
##### Vouchers recognized by RBI (PPIs) = Money. (Not supply).
##### Vouchers not recognized by RBI = Actionable claims. (Not specified, hence Sch III - Not supply).
##### Distribution P2P margin = Trading of vouchers (Not supply).
##### Distribution on commission = Agency service (Commission is Taxable).
##### Unredeemed vouchers (Breakage) = No underlying supply, hence NOT TAXABLE.
### Warranty & Extended Warranty — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 245/02/2025 & 195/07/2023
#### Issue: GST on parts replaced during warranty.
#### GST Treatment:
##### Original Warranty: Value already included in main product. No GST on replacement parts/labour (unless additional consideration charged).
##### Extended Warranty (at time of sale): Composite supply (Principal = Goods).
##### Extended Warranty (after sale): Separate supply of services.
### ESOPs / RSUs by Overseas Holding — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 213/07/2024
#### Issue: Indian subsidiary reimburses foreign holding for ESOPs given to Indian employees.
#### GST Treatment: Securities are neither G nor S. Compensation to employee is Sch III. Cost-to-cost reimbursement is NOT import of service. 
##### ⚡ Boundary Case: If foreign holding charges a mark-up/fee, that fee is taxable as import of service (RCM applies).
### Tenancy Rights (Pagadi System) — Clarification
#### Source: 📜 Circular No. 44/2018
#### Issue: Is transfer of tenancy rights against premium a sale of land (Sch III)?
#### GST Treatment: NO. It is a supply of service (leasing/tenancy). Taxable.
##### 🟢 EXEMPT: Renting of residential dwelling for residence is exempt.

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NUANCES & TRAPS
### ⚠️ Consideration vs Deposit
#### The Trap
##### Assuming all security deposits are consideration upfront.
#### The Reality
##### Sec 2(31) Proviso states a deposit is NOT consideration unless the supplier applies it towards the supply.
### ⚠️ Inter-State Movement of Conveyances
#### The Trap
##### Moving trucks/buses/cranes across states for deployment is a taxable branch transfer.
#### The Reality
##### 📜 Circular 1/1/2017 & 21/21/2017: Inter-state movement of conveyances, rigs, tools, spares (on wheels) carrying goods/passengers or for repairs is NEITHER supply of goods nor services (except if moved for further supply of that very item).
### ⚠️ Securities Lending Scheme (SLS)
#### The Trap
##### Securities are not goods/services, so lending them is not taxable.
#### The Reality
##### 📜 Circular 119/38/2019: Disposal of securities is not supply. But LENDING of securities (lending fee) is a taxable service.
### ⚠️ Cost Petroleum in PSC
#### The Trap
##### Oil contractor extracting oil and keeping a share = receiving consideration from Govt.
#### The Reality
##### 📜 Circular 32/06/2018: Contractor mines for himself. Cost petroleum is NOT consideration for service to Govt. Not taxable.

## 🧪 CONCEPT TESTS & EXAMPLES
### Schedule I Para 3 (Agent) — Concept Test
#### Situation
##### Anmol appoints Bholu to procure goods. Bholu asks supplier to invoice directly to Anmol.
#### Question
##### Is Bholu an agent under Schedule I?
#### Answer
##### NO.
#### Reasoning
##### The invoice is issued directly in the principal's name (Anmol). Bholu does not take title. Bholu is just providing procurement service, not supplying the goods under Sch I.
### Del-Credere Agent (DCA) — Concept Test
#### Situation
##### DCA extends short-term loan to buyer to pay principal. DCA charges interest. DCA did NOT issue invoice in own name.
#### Question
##### Is the interest part of the value of goods?
#### Answer
##### NO. 
#### Reasoning
##### Since DCA didn't issue invoice, DCA is not Sch I agent. Loan is an independent service (Exempt under Notification 12/2017).

## 🎯 LDR (LAST DAY REVISION) SNAPSHOT
### ⚡ One-Liners — Definitions
#### Goods: Movable property, actionable claims, growing crops. Excludes money/securities.
#### Services: Anything other than goods, money, securities.
#### Business: Activity with/without pecuniary benefit, volume, frequency irrelevant.
#### Consideration: Payment/act/forbearance. Excludes CG/SG subsidy and unapplied deposit.
### ⚡ One-Liners — Key Provisions
#### Sec 7(1)(a): Supply = Goods/Services + Consideration + Business.
#### Sec 7(1)(b): Import Service = Consideration + Business or Personal.
#### Sec 7(1)(c): Sch I = No Consideration + Business.
#### Sec 8(a): Composite Supply = Naturally bundled, Principal rate.
#### Sec 8(b): Mixed Supply = Unnaturally bundled, Highest rate.
### ⚡ One-Liners — Schedule Triggers
#### Sch I Para 1: Business Asset Disposal (ITC must be availed).
#### Sch I Para 2: Related/Distinct Persons.
#### Sch I Para 3: Principal & Agent (Invoice name test).
#### Sch II: Goods vs Services classification.
#### Sch III: Negative List (Neither G nor S).
### 🔴 SHALL-based Rules (Mandatory)
#### Sec 7(1A): SHALL be treated as supply of goods or services as per Sch II.
#### Sec 8(a): Composite supply SHALL be treated as supply of principal supply.
### 🟡 MAY-based Rules (Discretionary)
#### Sec 7(3): Govt MAY notify transactions as supply of goods/services.
### ₹ Thresholds Quick-Ref
#### ₹ 50,000: Max tax-free gift value per FY from Employer to Employee[Sch I Para 2].
### ✅ IS a Supply vs ❌ NOT a Supply — Flash Cards
#### ✅ BOGO Offers (Composite/Mixed)
#### ✅ Cancellation charges / Late fees
#### ✅ Corporate giving ESOPs with mark-up (Import of Service)
#### ❌ Liquidated damages for contract breach
#### ❌ Employee to Employer services
#### ❌ Free samples (No ITC)
#### ❌ Breakage (Unredeemed vouchers)
### 🏛️ Deeming Fictions
#### Sec 7(1)(aa): Club and members deemed two separate persons.
#### Sec 7(2): Sch III items deemed neither goods nor services.
#### Sch I: Activities deemed supply without consideration.

## ⚖️ CORE PROVISIONS [Section-wise]
### Sec. 9 — Levy and Collection (CGST)
#### Sec. 9(1) — Forward Charge
##### 🔴 SHALL be levied a tax called CGST on all intra-State supplies
##### Excludes: Alcoholic liquor for human consumption
##### Value: Computed under Sec. 15
##### Max Rate: 20% (CGST) + 20% (SGST) = 40%
##### Paid by: Taxable person
#### Sec. 9(2) — Deferred Levy on 5 Petroleum Products
##### Petroleum crude, High speed diesel, Motor spirit (petrol), Natural gas, Aviation turbine fuel
##### 🟡 MAY be levied from a date notified by Govt on Council recommendation
#### Sec. 9(3) — 🔁 RCM on Notified Supplies
##### 🟡 MAY notify categories of goods/services
##### Tax 🔴 SHALL be paid by recipient
##### All provisions apply to recipient as if he is the person liable
#### Sec. 9(4) — 🔁 RCM on Unregistered to Registered Supplies
##### 🟡 MAY notify a class of registered persons
##### If they receive specified goods/services from unregistered supplier → 🔁 RCM applies
##### Example: Promoters receiving shortfall from unregistered persons
#### Sec. 9(5) — Tax Payable by ECO
##### 🟡 MAY notify specific services supplied through ECO
##### ECO 🔴 SHALL pay the tax as if it is the supplier
##### E.g., Passenger transport, Housekeeping, Restaurant services, Accommodation

### Sec. 5 of IGST Act — Levy and Collection (IGST)
#### Sec. 5(1) — Inter-State Levy
##### 🔴 SHALL be levied IGST on all inter-State supplies (except alcoholic liquor)
##### Max Rate: 40%
##### ⚡ Proviso on Import of Goods: Levied as per Sec. 3 of Customs Tariff Act (at point of customs duties levy)
##### 📋 Notfn. 03/2023-IT: Import of specified actionable claim (online money gaming) taxed under IGST without customs duty applicability
#### Sec. 5(2), 5(3), 5(4) — Mirror provisions of Sec. 9(2), 9(3), 9(4)

### Sec. 10 — Composition Levy
#### Sec. 10(1) — Option for Goods & Restaurant Services
##### ⚡ Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Act
##### 🟡 MAY opt to pay concessional tax if Aggregate Turnover in preceding FY ≤ Threshold
##### Threshold: ₹ 1.5 Crore (Normal) / ₹ 75 Lakh (8 Special Category States)
##### Rates: 1% for Manufacturers, 5% for Restaurant Service, 1% for Traders
##### ❌ Second Proviso: Marginal Supply of Services Permitted
###### Can supply services up to 10% of turnover in State/UT in preceding FY OR ₹ 5 lakh, whichever is higher
###### 📌 Statutory Explanation: Value of exempt supply of extending deposits/loans (interest) NOT taken into account for this limit
#### Sec. 10(2) — Ineligibility Conditions for Sec. 10(1)
##### (a) Engaged in supply of services (save as provided in 10(1) proviso)
##### (b) Making supply of goods/services not leviable to tax
##### (c) Making inter-State outward supplies
##### (d) Making supplies through ECO required to collect TCS under Sec. 52
##### (e) Manufacturer of notified goods (Ice cream, Pan masala, Tobacco, Aerated waters, Bricks/Tiles)
##### (f) Casual taxable person or Non-resident taxable person
##### ❌ Proviso: Must be opted uniformly by all registered persons under same PAN
#### Sec. 10(2A) — Alternative Composition for Services
##### ⚡ Notwithstanding anything to the contrary
##### For registered persons NOT eligible under 10(1) and 10(2)
##### Threshold: Aggregate Turnover in preceding FY ≤ ₹ 50 Lakh
##### Rate: 6% (3% CGST + 3% SGST)
##### Ineligibility conditions mirror 10(2) (Non-taxable, Inter-state, ECO, Notified goods/services, CTP/NRTP)
#### Sec. 10(3) — Lapse of Option
##### Option 🔴 SHALL lapse on the day aggregate turnover exceeds threshold in current FY
#### Sec. 10(4) — Restrictions
##### Taxable person 🔴 SHALL NOT collect any tax from recipient
##### Taxable person 🔴 SHALL NOT be entitled to any ITC
#### Sec. 10(5) — Penalties for Wrong Availment
##### If proper officer believes person was not eligible, person 🔴 SHALL be liable to penalty
##### Sec. 73, 74, or 74A SHALL mutatis mutandis apply
#### 📌 Statutory Explanations to Sec. 10
##### Explanation 1 (For Aggregate Turnover Eligibility)
###### Includes supplies from 1st April to date of becoming liable for registration
###### Excludes exempt supply of services (interest/discount on deposits/loans)
##### Explanation 2 (For Turnover in State for Tax Payment)
###### Excludes supplies from 1st April to date of becoming liable for registration
###### Excludes exempt supply of services (interest/discount on deposits/loans)

## 🔗 INTERLINKED SECTIONS

### 📜 Circular No. 130/49/2019-GST & 177/09/2022-GST — Renting of Motor Vehicle vs Passenger Transport
#### Issue: Distinction between renting of motor vehicle and passenger transport service
#### Conclusion: If renter determines schedules, routes, and vehicle is at their disposal for a period → "Renting of Motor Vehicle" (🔁 RCM applies). If service provider runs on pre-determined route/schedule → "Passenger Transport" (Forward Charge).

### 📜 Circular No. 140/10/2020-GST — Director Remuneration
#### Issue: Is director's remuneration subject to RCM?
#### Conclusion: If declared as 'Salaries' and TDS deducted u/s 192 → Covered by Sch III (Not a supply). If declared as professional fees and TDS deducted u/s 194J → Taxable under 🔁 RCM.

### 📜 Circular No. 201/13/2023-GST — Director supplying service in personal capacity
#### Issue: Renting of property by director to the company
#### Conclusion: Services supplied in private/personal capacity (e.g., renting immovable property) are NOT taxable under RCM. RCM applies only when acting in the capacity of a director.

### 📜 Circular No. 245/02/2025-GST — DDA not a local authority
#### Issue: Can Delhi Development Authority (DDA) be treated as a local authority?
#### Conclusion: DDA is not entrusted with the control/management of a municipal fund. Hence, NOT a local authority under GST.

### 📜 Circular No. 115/34/2019-GST — Airport Dues (PSF and UDF)
#### Issue: Are Passenger Service Fee (PSF) and User Development Fee (UDF) taxable? Who pays?
#### Conclusion: Airport operator is the supplier. Airlines act as pure agents collecting it. Airlines do not pay GST on it. Airport operator pays GST.

### 📜 Circular No. 77/51/2018-GST — Composition Withdrawal / Denial
#### Issue: Effective date of withdrawal/denial
#### Conclusion: Taxpayer voluntary withdrawal → Date chosen by taxpayer (not before FY start). Denial by officer → Effective from date of contravention (can be retrospective).

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NUANCES & TRAPS
### ⚠️ GTA Forward vs Reverse Charge
#### The Trap: Assuming all GTA services are under RCM.
#### The Reality: GTA has an option to pay @ 12% (with ITC) or @ 5% (without ITC) under Forward Charge. 🔁 RCM @ 5% applies ONLY if GTA does not exercise this option AND recipient is a specified entity.
### ⚠️ Motor Vehicle Renting RCM
#### The Trap: Assuming all car rentals to companies are RCM.
#### The Reality: RCM applies ONLY IF supplier is NON-body corporate AND recipient IS a body corporate. If a Company rents to a Company → Forward charge.
### ⚠️ Promoter RCM (Real Estate)
#### The Reality: Promoters MUST procure 80% of inputs/services from registered persons. Shortfall attracts 18% 🔁 RCM. If cement is bought unregistered, it attracts 28% 🔁 RCM regardless of 80% limit.
### ⚠️ Composition Threshold Calculation (Interest)
#### 🔑 The Operative Rule: Interest on deposits/loans is EXCLUDED when checking the 10% / ₹ 5 Lakh marginal service limit AND when calculating the tax payable. However, it was historically tricky for the main ₹ 1.5 Cr threshold, but Explanation 1 clarifies it is excluded there too.
### ⚠️ Turnover Before Registration
#### The Reality: Turnover from April 1st to the date of liability for registration is INCLUDED for checking the ₹ 1.5 Cr / ₹ 50 L eligibility threshold, but EXCLUDED when calculating the actual tax payable under composition.

## 🧪 CONCEPT TESTS & EXAMPLES
### Composition Eligibility — Multiple Branches
#### Situation: 'Prithviraj' has branches in Maharashtra & Goa.
#### Question: Can he opt for composition for one and normal for another?
#### Answer: ❌ No. PAN-based. Both must be under composition. Aggregate turnover includes both.
### Composition Limit — Service Provider (Billoo Barber)
#### Situation: Billoo operates salons in Delhi and Haryana.
#### Question: What scheme applies?
#### Answer: Sec. 10(2A). Threshold is ₹ 50 Lakh total across both. Tax rate 6%.
### First Year Registration — Turnover Exclusion
#### Situation: Champak starts business in April. Q1 turnover ₹ 20L (unregistered). Q2 turnover ₹ 30L (registered under composition).
#### Question: What is tax payable?
#### Answer: Q1 is excluded from tax computation. Tax is 6% of ₹ 30L (Q2) = ₹ 1,80,000. Option lapses at end of Q2 as total crosses ₹ 50L.
### Director Remuneration Split
#### Situation: Arpan gets ₹ 25k sitting fee, ₹ 1.5L salary (TDS u/s 192), and ₹ 2L professional fee via his partnership firm.
#### Answer: Sitting fee = 🔁 RCM by Company. Salary = Sch III (Not taxable). Partnership fee = Forward charge by partnership firm.

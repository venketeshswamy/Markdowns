# Chapter 6: Value of Supply [Section 15 & CGST Rules 27-35]

## 🗺️ CHAPTER OVERVIEW
### What This Chapter Covers
#### Core principles of determining the value of taxable supplies of goods and services
#### Section 15(1): Transaction Value as the default assessable value
#### Section 15(2): Mandatory additions to the transaction value
#### Section 15(3): Permissible exclusions (discounts) from value
#### CGST Rules 27-31: Valuation rules when transaction value cannot be adopted (related parties / non-monetary consideration)
#### CGST Rules 31A-31C: Specific valuation rules for lottery, betting, horse racing, online gaming, and casinos
#### CGST Rule 32: Special optional valuation schemes for specific supplies (forex, air travel, life insurance, second-hand goods, vouchers)
#### CGST Rule 33: Exclusion of expenses incurred as a pure agent
#### CGST Rule 34: Determination of rate of exchange for foreign currencies
#### CGST Rule 35: Value of supply inclusive of integrated, central, state, or union territory tax
### Governing Law
#### CGST Act, 2017: Section 15, Section 25(4)/(5), Section 2(5), 2(31), 2(49), 2(52), 2(73), 2(75), 2(84), 2(87), 2(88), 2(93), 2(102), 2(105), 2(118)
#### CGST Rules, 2017: Rules 27 to 35 (Chapter IV: Determination of Value of Supply)
#### IGST Act, 2017: Section 20 (provisions of value of supply made applicable)
### Why This Chapter Matters
#### Weightage: High frequency in CA Final and Inter examinations (typically 5 to 12 marks)
#### Question Types: Practical computation of taxable value, MCQ traps on inclusions/exclusions, case studies on related party transactions and optional rule selections
### Logical Flow
#### Is price the sole consideration and parties unrelated?
##### Yes: Value is Transaction Value [Sec. 15(1)] → Add Sec. 15(2) Inclusions → Subtract Sec. 15(3) Exclusions
##### No: Is it a notified supply under Section 15(5)?
###### Yes: Value determined under Rule 31A, 31B, 31C, or 32
###### No: Value determined under Rules 27-31 in sequential order

## 📖 APPLICABLE DEFINITIONS
### Agent [Sec. 2(5)]
#### Statutory Meaning: A person who carries on the business of supply or receipt of goods or services or both on behalf of another
#### Type of Definition: Exhaustive and Enlarging ("means... including...")
#### Includes
##### Factor
##### Broker
##### Commission agent
##### Arhatia
##### Del credere agent
##### Auctioneer
##### Any other mercantile agent, by whatever name called
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "on behalf of another" (establishes the principal-agent relationship under GST)
#### ⚡ Common Mistake: Treating a mere facilitator as an agent without verifying if they have the authority to supply or receive on behalf of the principal
#### 🔗 Linked To: Rule 29 (Valuation of supply between principal and agent)
### Consideration [Sec. 2(31)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Payment in money or otherwise, or monetary value of any act or forbearance for the supply
#### Type of Definition: Inclusive
#### Includes
##### (a) Payment made or to be made, in money or otherwise, by recipient or any other person
##### (b) Monetary value of any act or forbearance, by recipient or any other person
#### Excludes / Shall Not Include
##### ❌ Subsidy given by the Central Government or a State Government
#### ❌ Proviso: Deposit given in respect of supply shall not be considered as payment unless the supplier applies such deposit as consideration
#### 💬 Example: Security deposit for renting a hall is not consideration until it is forfeited/adjusted against unpaid rent or damages
### Family [Sec. 2(49)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Spouse and children of the person, and parents, grandparents, brothers, and sisters if wholly or mainly dependent
#### Type of Definition: Exhaustive ("means")
#### Category 1 (Always Family — No Dependency Test)
##### Spouse
##### Children
#### Category 2 (Family Only If Wholly/Mainly Dependent)
##### Parents
##### Grand-parents
##### Brothers
##### Sisters
#### ⚠️ TRAP: Brothers/sisters/parents who are financially independent are NOT "family" for GST related party determinations
### Goods [Sec. 2(52)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Every kind of movable property other than money and securities
#### Includes
##### Actionable claims
##### Growing crops, grass
##### Things attached to or forming part of the land agreed to be severed before supply or under a contract of supply
#### Excludes
##### ❌ Money
##### ❌ Securities
### Market Value [Sec. 2(73)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Full amount a recipient is required to pay to obtain goods/services of like kind & quality at the same time and commercial level from an unrelated supplier
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "unrelated" and "same commercial level"
### Person [Sec. 2(84)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Broad definition covering all legal entity types
#### Includes
##### Individual, HUF, Company, Firm, LLP, AOP, BOI, Corporation, Govt Company, Local Authority, Central/State Govt, Trust, Artificial Juridical Person
### Money [Sec. 2(75)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Indian legal tender, foreign currency, cheques, promissory notes, bills of exchange, letters of credit, drafts, etc. used as consideration to settle obligations
#### Excludes
##### ❌ Currency held for its numismatic value (collectible old coins are treated as goods, not money)
### Services [Sec. 2(102)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Anything other than goods, money, and securities
#### Includes
##### Activities relating to the use of money or its conversion by cash or any other mode from one form/currency/denomination to another for which a separate consideration is charged
##### 📌 Statutory Explanation: Facilitating or arranging transactions in securities is included in services
### Supplier [Sec. 2(105)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Person supplying goods/services, including an agent acting on behalf of such supplier
##### 🏛️ Deeming Fiction: A person organizing or arranging supply of specified actionable claims (e.g., online gaming, casinos) shall be deemed to be the supplier
### Voucher [Sec. 2(118)]
#### Statutory Meaning: Instrument with an obligation to accept as consideration or part consideration for a supply of goods or services
#### 🔑 Key Operative Word: "obligation to accept"

## ⚖️ CORE PROVISIONS — SECTIONS
### Sec. 15(1) — Transaction Value
#### Provision: The value of a supply of goods or services or both shall be the transaction value, which is the price actually paid or payable for the said supply
#### ✅ Conditions for Applicability (Must be satisfied simultaneously)
##### (1) Supplier and recipient are not related persons
##### (2) Price is the sole consideration for the supply
#### 💬 Illustration 5: Sold cement to unrelated customer Y for ₹ 6,700 (wholesale price ₹ 7,000). Value is ₹ 6,700, as price is the sole consideration and parties are unrelated.
### Sec. 15(2) — Mandatory Inclusions in Value
#### Sec. 15(2)(a) — Taxes other than GST
##### Provision: Any taxes, duties, cesses, fees, and charges levied under any law for the time being in force other than CGST, SGST, UTGST, and GST Compensation Cess, if charged separately
##### 💬 Illustration: Municipal taxes charged separately by a landlord on commercial property renting must be added to the taxable rent value
##### 📜 Circular No. 76/50/2018 GST: Tax Collected at Source (TCS) under Income Tax Act, 1961 is NOT includible in taxable value (it is an interim levy, not a tax)
#### Sec. 15(2)(b) — Third-Party Payments
##### Provision: Any amount that the supplier is liable to pay in relation to such supply but which has been incurred by the recipient and not included in the price
##### ✅ Conditions: Supplier must be contractually/legally liable to pay, and recipient pays it in relation to the supply
##### 💬 Illustration 6: Grand Biz contracts ABC Co. for a dealers' meet. Grand Biz is liable to pay soft drink vendors. ABC Co. pays the vendor directly. This amount must be added to the value of supply by Grand Biz to ABC Co.
##### 📜 Circular No. 47/21/2018 GST: Moulds/dies provided Free of Cost (FOC) by OEM to a component manufacturer are NOT included in value of components unless the contract placed the liability to provide/incur them on the component manufacturer
#### Sec. 15(2)(c) — Incidental Expenses
##### Provision: Incidental expenses, including commission and packing, charged by the supplier to the recipient, and any amount charged for anything done by the supplier in respect of the supply at the time of, or before delivery
##### Includes
###### Commission paid to agents and recovered from buyer
###### Packing, loading, and design charges
###### Weighment and inspection charges
###### Installation, testing, and commissioning charges at recipient's site
###### Outward freight and transit insurance (if supplier contracts to deliver to buyer's premises — forms a Composite Supply under Sec. 8(a))
##### ❌ Excludes: Transport/insurance paid directly by buyer under an ex-factory contract
#### Sec. 15(2)(d) — Interest, Late Fee, or Penalty
##### Provision: Interest, late fee, or penalty for delayed payment of any consideration
##### ⏱️ Time of Supply: Receives the addition in value (actual receipt date per Sec. 12(6)/13(6))
##### 🔢 Treatment: Amount received is always deemed inclusive of tax; back-calculation is mandatory
#### Sec. 15(2)(e) — Price-Linked Subsidies
##### Provision: Subsidies directly linked to the price, excluding subsidies provided by Central and State Governments
##### 📌 Statutory Explanation: Amount of subsidy shall be included in the value of supply of the supplier who receives the subsidy
##### 💬 Illustration 13: Notebook price is ₹ 50. NGO provides price-linked subsidy of ₹ 30. Student pays ₹ 20. Taxable Value = ₹ 50 (since NGO subsidy is added). If Government subsidy: Taxable Value = ₹ 20.
### Sec. 15(3) — Mandatory Exclusions (Discounts)
#### Sec. 15(3)(a) — Pre-supply / At-the-time Discounts
##### Provision: Excluded if given before/at the time of supply and duly recorded in the tax invoice
##### 💬 Illustration 14: Trade discount of 30% shown in the invoice itself. Excluded; GST charged on net price.
##### 📜 Circular No. 186/18/2022 GST: No Claim Bonus (NCB) allowed by insurance companies to insured is a permissible discount under Sec. 15(3)(a) if disclosed in policy and invoice
#### Sec. 15(3)(b) — Post-supply Discounts
##### Provision: Excluded if given after the supply has been effected, subject to strict conditions
##### ✅ Conditions for Exclusion
###### (i) Discount established in terms of an agreement entered into at or before the time of supply - Note: The agreement requirement is gone away as per 2026 Finance Act Amendment
###### (ii) Specifically linked to relevant invoices
###### (iii) Proportionate Input Tax Credit (ITC) reversed by the recipient on the basis of document (credit note) issued by supplier
##### 📜 Circular No. 92/11/2019 GST - Discount Types
###### Staggered Discounts ("Buy more, Save more"): Shown on invoice or credit notes. Excluded if conditions of Sec. 15(3)(b) met.
###### Periodic / Volume / Year-end Discounts: Passed via credit notes under pre-existing agreement. Excluded if recipient reverses ITC.
###### Secondary Discounts (re-valuation after supply / commercial negotiation): NOT known at time of supply. Cannot be excluded. Financial/commercial credit notes (without GST) can be issued; no ITC reversal required by buyer.
##### ⏱️ Reversal Certification [Footnote 5]
###### If GST involved in discount in FY is ≤ ₹ 5 lakh: Procure an undertaking/certificate from recipient of ITC reversal
###### If GST > ₹ 5 lakh: Procure a certificate from CA or CMA certifying recipient reversed proportionate ITC
### Sec. 15(4) — Non-applicability of Sec. 15(1)
#### Provision: Value shall be determined in the manner prescribed by CGST Rules (Rules 27 to 31) when price is not sole consideration or parties are related
### Sec. 15(5) — Notified Supplies
#### Provision: Value of notified supplies shall be determined in the manner prescribed (Rules 31A, 31B, 31C, 32)
### Related Persons [Explanation to Sec. 15]
#### 🏛️ Deeming Fiction: Persons deemed related if they satisfy any of:
##### (i) Officers or directors of one another's businesses
##### (ii) Legally recognized partners in business
##### (iii) Employer and employee
##### (iv) Any person directly/indirectly owns, controls, or holds ≥ 25% of outstanding voting stock/shares of both
##### (v) One of them directly/indirectly controls the other
##### (vi) Both of them directly/indirectly controlled by a third person
##### (vii) Together they directly/indirectly control a third person
##### (viii) Members of the same family
##### (c) Sole agent, sole distributor, or sole concessionaire

## 📏 VALUATION RULES
### Rule 27 — Consideration Not Wholly in Money
#### Purpose: Valuation for barter, exchange, or asset disposals with ITC
#### 🔄 Sequence of Application (Mandatory Order)
##### (a) Open Market Value (OMV) of such supply
##### (b) If OMV not available: Sum of monetary consideration + money equivalent of non-monetary consideration known at time of supply
##### (c) If (a) or (b) not determinable: Value of supply of goods/services of like kind and quality
##### (d) If (a), (b), or (c) not determinable: Money consideration + money equivalent computed per Rule 30 (Cost + 10%) or Rule 31 (Residual) in that order
#### 💬 Illustration: New phone sold for ₹ 20,000 along with exchange of old phone. OMV of new phone is ₹ 24,000. Taxable Value = ₹ 24,000.
#### 💬 Illustration: Laptop supplied for ₹ 40,000 along with barter of printer (printer value known as ₹ 4,000 at time of supply, laptop OMV not known). Taxable Value = ₹ 40,000 + ₹ 4,000 = ₹ 44,000.
### Rule 28 — Distinct or Related Persons (excluding Agent)
#### Purpose: Valuation of stock transfers and related party transactions
#### 🔄 Sequence of Application (Rule 28(1))
##### (a) Open Market Value (OMV)
##### (b) Value of like kind and quality
##### (c) Cost plus 10% (Rule 30) or Residual (Rule 31) in that order
#### ❌ Proviso 1 (Option): If goods are intended for further supply *as such* by the recipient, supplier can value at 90% of price charged by recipient to unrelated customer
#### ❌ Proviso 2 (Eligible for Full ITC): If recipient is eligible for full ITC, the value declared in the invoice shall be deemed to be the OMV (can be declared as NIL or cost)
#### Rule 28(2) — Corporate Guarantee
##### Provision: Services provided by related supplier to related recipient located in India by way of corporate guarantee to any bank/FI
##### Value: Deemed to be 1% per annum of the guarantee amount, or the actual consideration, whichever is higher
##### ❌ Proviso: If recipient is eligible for full ITC, the value declared in the invoice shall be deemed to be the value of service (NIL can be declared)
### Rule 29 — Principal and Agent
#### Purpose: Valuation of goods supplied/received through an agent
#### 🔄 Sequence of Application
##### (a) Open Market Value (OMV) of goods, OR (at supplier's option) 90% of price charged for like kind/quality by recipient to unrelated customer (where goods are intended for further supply)
##### (b) Rule 30 or Rule 31 in that order
#### 💬 Illustration 25: Principal supplies groundnuts to agent. Agent sells like kind to unrelated customer for ₹ 1,000/unit. Independent supplier sells to agent at ₹ 950/unit. Value can be ₹ 950 (OMV) or 90% of ₹ 1,000 = ₹ 900 (Supplier's Option).
### Rule 30 — Cost-Based Valuation
#### Purpose: Valuation based on cost when previous rules fail
#### Formula: Value = 110% of cost of production, manufacture, acquisition, or provision of services
### Rule 31 — Residual Method (Best Judgement)
#### Purpose: Final fallback valuation rule using reasonable means consistent with Sec. 15 principles
#### 🟡 OPTION: For supply of services, supplier can skip Rule 30 and apply Rule 31 directly
### Rule 31A — Lottery, Betting, Gambling, Horse Racing
#### (2) Lottery Value Formula: Value = 100/128 × Higher of [Face value of ticket] or [Price notified in Official Gazette by organizing State]
#### (3) Betting, Gambling, Horse Racing Value: 100% of the face value of the bet or the amount paid into the totalisator
### Rule 31B — Online Gaming and Online Money Gaming
#### Valuation: Total amount of money paid/payable/deposited with the supplier by/on behalf of player (including virtual digital assets)
#### ❌ No Deduction: Refunded/returned amounts do not reduce taxable value
#### 📌 Statutory Explanation: Re-used winnings without withdrawal in further events are NOT considered as new deposits/payments
### Rule 31C — Actionable Claims in Casino
#### Valuation: Total amount paid/payable for purchase of tokens, chips, coins, or tickets, or participating in games where tokens are not required
#### ❌ No Deduction: Refunds/returns on return of tokens/chips do not reduce taxable value
#### 📌 Statutory Explanation: Re-used winnings without withdrawal in further events are NOT considered as new deposits/payments
### Rule 32 — Optional Valuation Schemes
#### Rule 32(1): Overrides other valuation rules; optional to the supplier
#### Rule 32(2) — Purchase or Sale of Foreign Currency
##### 🟡 Method-1 (RBI Reference Rate Based)
###### Case 1 (One currency exchanged is INR): Difference between buying/selling rate and RBI reference rate × total units. If RBI reference rate not available: Value = 1% of gross INR amount provided/received.
###### Case 2 (Neither currency is INR): 1% of the lesser of the two amounts the person would have received if converting either currency into INR at RBI reference rate on that day.
##### 🟡 Method-2 (Slab Rate Option — Irrevocable for the FY)
###### Slab 1 (Up to ₹ 1,00,000): 1% of gross amount or ₹ 250, whichever is higher
###### Slab 2 (₹ 1,00,001 to ₹ 10,00,000): ₹ 1,000 + 0.50% of (gross amount − ₹ 1,00,000)
###### Slab 3 (Exceeding ₹ 10,00,000): ₹ 5,500 + 0.10% of (gross amount − ₹ 10,00,000), subject to maximum cap of ₹ 60,000
#### Rule 32(3) — Air Travel Agent
##### Domestic Bookings: 5% of Basic Fare
##### International Bookings: 10% of Basic Fare
##### 📌 Explanation: Basic Fare is the part of airfare on which commission is normally paid by airlines to agents
#### Rule 32(4) — Life Insurance Business
##### Dual Benefit (Investment + Risk) with Savings allocation intimated: Gross Premium − Amount allocated for investment/savings
##### Single Premium Annuity (savings not intimated): 10% of single premium
##### Other General Cases: 25% of premium in Year 1; 12.5% in subsequent years
##### ❌ Proviso: Policy only towards risk cover (term insurance) → 100% of premium
#### Rule 32(5) — Second-Hand Goods (Margin Scheme)
##### Valuation: Value = Selling Price − Purchase Price (ignore if negative)
##### ✅ Condition: No ITC availed on purchase of the used goods
##### ❌ Proviso (Repossessed Goods from Unregistered Defaulting Borrower): Purchase price is reduced by 5% per quarter or part thereof between the date of purchase and date of disposal
##### 📋 Notification No. 8/2018 CT(R) — Old & Used Motor Vehicles
###### (i) Registered Person (claimed depreciation u/s 32 of Income Tax Act): Value = Consideration received − Depreciated value on date of supply (ignore if negative)
###### (ii) Other cases: Value = Selling price − Purchase price (ignore if negative)
#### Rule 32(6) — Vouchers/Coupons
##### Value: Equal to the money value of goods/services redeemable against such voucher
#### Rule 32(7) — Services between Distinct Persons
##### Value: Deemed to be NIL if ITC is available (cross-ref Schedule I Para 2)
### Rule 33 — Pure Agent
#### Purpose: Exclude reimbursement of actual third-party costs from value of supply
#### ✅ Conditions to satisfy (Must be met simultaneously)
##### (i) Supplier acts as pure agent of recipient, makes payment on authorization
##### (ii) Payment indicated separately in invoice
##### (iii) Supplies procured from third party are in addition to services supplied on own account
#### 📌 Definition of Pure Agent (4 statutory criteria)
##### (a) Contractual agreement with recipient to act as pure agent to incur costs
##### (b) Holds no title to goods/services so procured
##### (c) Does not use goods/services for own interest
##### (d) Receives only actual amount incurred
### Rule 34 — Rate of Exchange
#### Goods: Exchange rate notified by CBIC under Sec. 14 of Customs Act, 1962 on the date of Time of Supply of goods (Sec. 12)
#### Services: Exchange rate determined per GAAP on the date of Time of Supply of services (Sec. 13)
### Rule 35 — Value Inclusive of Tax
#### Formula: Tax Amount = (Value inclusive of taxes × GST rate %) ÷ (100 + Sum of applicable GST rates %)

## 📋 SCHEDULES
### Schedule I Para 2 — Supply between Related/Distinct Persons
#### Provision: Supply of goods or services or both between related persons or distinct persons (Sec. 25(4)/(5)) without consideration is a supply, if made in course/furtherance of business
#### Valuation: Rule 28 applies (value declared is deemed OMV if recipient eligible for full ITC)
### Schedule I Para 3 — Principal-Agent Transactions
#### Provision: Supply of goods between principal and agent without consideration is a supply
#### Valuation: Rule 29 applies

## 🔗 INTERLINKED SECTIONS & RULES
### Sec. 15(1) ↔ Rule 28
#### Consequence: Related party transactions cannot use Sec. 15(1) transaction value. They must use Rule 28 even if consideration is charged.
### Rule 28 ↔ Sec. 25(4)/(5)
#### Consequence: Stock transfers between branches with different registrations under same PAN (distinct persons) are valued under Rule 28.
### Sec. 15(2)(d) ↔ Sec. 12(6) / Sec. 13(6)
#### Consequence: Interest for late payment is taxed only on receipt. It is added to the value of main supply and taxed at the same rate, treated as tax-inclusive.
### Rule 32(7) ↔ Schedule I Para 2
#### Consequence: Head Office providing internally generated services to Branches without consideration → Value is NIL if Branch is eligible for full ITC.

## 🚫 EXCLUSIONS, NON-SUPPLIES & NEGATIVE LIST
### TCS under Income Tax Act, 1961
#### Exclusion: Excluded from value of supply under GST (Circular No. 76/50/2018 GST)
### Subsidies from Central/State Government
#### Exclusion: Excluded from value of supply even if linked to price (Sec. 15(2)(e))
### Refundable Deposits
#### Exclusion: Excluded from consideration under Sec. 2(31) unless applied as consideration
### Reused Online Gaming Winnings
#### Exclusion: Winnings used to play further games without withdrawal are excluded from "amounts deposited" under Rule 31B/31C Explanation

## 🔄 COMPOSITE & MIXED SUPPLY
### Renting + Electricity charges (Malls / RWAs)
#### Status: Composite supply. Principal supply is Renting of Immovable Property. Rate of rent applies to electricity charges if billed together.
#### ❌ Exception: Excluded from value if electricity is supplied as a pure agent (recharged at actual DISCOM rates) per Circular 206/18/2023.
### Outward Freight & Transit Insurance
#### Status: Composite supply where supplier contracts delivery to buyer's premises. Principal supply is goods. Freight/insurance are included in value of goods.

## 💡 CIRCULARS, NOTIFICATIONS & AAR CLARIFICATIONS
### Circular No. 76/50/2018 GST
#### Issue: Includibility of Income Tax TCS in GST value
#### Conclusion: TCS not includible; it is an interim tax collection, not a tax on goods.
### Circular No. 47/21/2018 GST
#### Issue: Moulds and dies provided FOC by OEM to component manufacturer
#### Conclusion: Amortised cost of moulds/dies added to component value ONLY if contract required the component manufacturer to provide them. If OEM was responsible, not added.
### Circular No. 92/11/2019 GST
#### Issue: Post-supply discounts, secondary discounts
#### Conclusion: Secondary discounts (negotiated post-sale) do not satisfy Sec. 15(3)(b) and cannot be excluded. Financial credit notes can be issued without GST adjustments.
### Circular No. 186/18/2022 GST
#### Issue: No Claim Bonus (NCB) in motor insurance
#### Conclusion: NCB is a pre-disclosure discount and is deductible under Sec. 15(3)(a) if shown in invoice.
### Circular No. 199/11/2023 GST / 210/4/2024 GST
#### Issue: HO services to BOs (cross-charge vs ISD)
#### Conclusion: (1) If BO eligible for full ITC, HO can issue invoice with any value (including NIL) under Rule 28 Proviso 2. (2) If BO not eligible for full ITC, HO employee salary costs are NOT mandatorily included in valuation.
### Circular No. 204/16/2023 GST
#### Issue: Personal guarantee by directors vs Corporate Guarantees
#### Conclusion: Personal guarantee OMV is NIL (due to RBI mandate prohibiting commission payment to directors). Corporate guarantee valued under Rule 28(2) at 1% p.a. or actual whichever higher.
### Circular No. 115/34/2019 GST
#### Issue: UDF and PSF collected by airlines
#### Conclusion: Airlines act as pure agents of passengers for airport operator levies. Excluded from airline value of supply. Collection charges paid to airlines are taxable.
### Circular No. 206/18/2023 GST
#### Issue: Electricity recharge by landlords/RWAs
#### Conclusion: Forms part of composite renting service. Excluded only if landlord acts as pure agent charging actual DISCOM rates.

## 🔢 FORMULAS, SLABS & COMPUTATIONS
### Transaction Value
#### Formula: Value = Base Price + Sec. 15(2) Inclusions − Sec. 15(3) Exclusions
### Rule 32(2)(b) Forex Slabs
#### Slab 1 (≤ ₹ 1,00,000): Value = Gross amount × 1%, subject to minimum ₹ 250
#### Slab 2 (₹ 1,00,001 to ₹ 10,00,000): Value = ₹ 1,000 + (Gross amount − ₹ 1,00,000) × 0.5%
#### Slab 3 (> ₹ 10,00,000): Value = ₹ 5,500 + (Gross amount − ₹ 10,00,000) × 0.1%, subject to maximum ₹ 60,000
### Rule 32(5) Repossessed Goods
#### Formula: Deemed Purchase Price = Original Purchase Price × [100% − (5% × Number of quarters or part thereof from purchase to disposal)]
### Rule 31A Lottery
#### Formula: Taxable Value = 100/128 × Higher of (Face Value, Gazette Price)
### Rule 35 Back-calculation
#### Formula: Tax = (Tax Inclusive Amount × Tax Rate) ÷ (100 + Tax Rate)

## 📝 ILLUSTRATIONS & WORKED EXAMPLES
### 💬 Illustration 1 — Excise & Packing (BW Ltd)
#### Key Facts: Price ₹ 60,000 (excl. tax/discount), Excise duty ₹ 6,000, Packing ₹ 2,000, Freight arranged by supplier ₹ 1,600, Discount 2% of price on invoice.
#### Computation
##### Base Price: ₹ 60,000
##### Add: Excise duty [Sec. 15(2)(a)]: ₹ 6,000
##### Add: Packing [Sec. 15(2)(c)]: ₹ 2,000
##### Add: Freight [Sec. 15(2)(c) Composite]: ₹ 1,600
##### Less: Discount [Sec. 15(3)(a)] (2% of ₹ 60,000): −₹ 1,200
#### Taxable Value: ₹ 68,400
### 💬 Illustration 2 — CSR Subsidy & Delayed Interest (SA Ltd)
#### Key Facts: Selling price ₹ 25/pack, NGO subsidy ₹ 5/pack, 1,000 packs sold. Loading ₹ 1,200 charged separately. Delayed interest ₹ 5,000 received (tax rate 18%).
#### Computation
##### Price (1,000 × ₹ 25): ₹ 25,000
##### Add: NGO Subsidy [Sec. 15(2)(e)] (1,000 × ₹ 5): ₹ 5,000
##### Add: Loading [Sec. 15(2)(c)]: ₹ 1,200
##### Add: Interest [Sec. 15(2)(d) back-calc] (₹ 5,000 × 100/118): ₹ 4,237
#### Taxable Value: ₹ 35,437
### 💬 Illustration 3 — Forex Method-2 Slabs (X Pvt Ltd)
#### Key Facts: Money changer exchanges USD 10,000 to INR @ ₹ 74 (Gross INR ₹ 7,40,000). Wants to value under Rule 32(2)(b).
#### Computation
##### Up to ₹ 1,00,000: ₹ 1,000
##### Next ₹ 6,40,000 (0.5% × ₹ 6,40,000): ₹ 3,200
#### Taxable Value: ₹ 4,200
### 💬 Illustration 4 — Air Travel Agent (UB & Sons)
#### Key Facts: Domestic bookings basic fare ₹ 1,00,900; International bookings basic fare ₹ 3,16,880.
#### Computation
##### Domestic [Rule 32(3)] (5% of ₹ 1,00,900): ₹ 5,045
##### International [Rule 32(3)] (10% of ₹ 3,16,880): ₹ 31,688
#### Taxable Value: ₹ 36,733
### 💬 Illustration 5 — Life Insurance (ALICL)
#### Key Facts: Gross premium ₹ 180 lakh, investment portion ₹ 100 lakh.
#### Computation (i) Intimated: ₹ 180 lakh − ₹ 100 lakh = ₹ 80,00,000
#### Computation (ii) Not intimated: 25% of ₹ 180 lakh = ₹ 45,00,000
#### Computation (iii) Risk cover only: 100% of ₹ 180 lakh = ₹ 1,80,00,000

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NUANCES & EXAM TRAPS
### ⚠️ Trap 1: "Solely" Dependent Family Members
#### The Trap: Assuming all siblings, parents, and grandparents are related persons automatically.
#### The Reality: They are only "family" (and thus related) if they are wholly or mainly dependent on the taxpayer. Spouse and children are always family.
### ⚠️ Trap 2: TCS under IT Act vs Other Local Taxes
#### The Trap: Including TCS in the GST value of supply because municipal taxes are included.
#### The Reality: Municipal taxes and local authority duties are included [Sec. 15(2)(a)], but Income Tax TCS is specifically excluded as an interim levy (Circular 76/50/2018).
### ⚠️ Trap 3: NGO Subsidy vs Government Subsidy
#### The Trap: Excluding all subsidies from the value of supply.
#### The Reality: NGO/Private subsidies are added to the price [Sec. 15(2)(e)]. Only Central or State Government subsidies are excluded.
### ⚠️ Trap 4: Delayed Payment Interest Back-calculation
#### The Trap: Adding the raw interest amount directly to the transaction value.
#### The Reality: Interest is always considered inclusive of GST. You must back-calculate (Interest × 100 ÷ (100 + Rate)) before adding.
### ⚠️ Trap 5: Post-Supply Discount Agreement Timing
#### The Trap: Excluding a post-supply discount because the buyer reversed the ITC.
#### The Reality: Both conditions must be met. If the agreement did not exist at or before the time of supply, the discount cannot be excluded, even if ITC is reversed.
### ⚠️ Trap 6: Goods "As Such" under Rule 28/29
#### The Trap: Applying the 90% optional valuation method when the recipient processes the goods.
#### The Reality: The 90% option is strictly available only if the goods are sold *as such* by the recipient. If they are processed, this option is lost.
### ⚠️ Trap 7: HO-BO Services with Exempt / Nil-rated Outward Supplies
#### The Trap: HO declaring NIL invoice value for services provided to an exempt branch, assuming Rule 28 Proviso 2 always applies.
#### The Reality: Proviso 2 (invoice value = OMV) is only applicable if the recipient is eligible for "full input tax credit". If the branch makes exempt supplies, it is not eligible for full ITC; HO must include costs plus 10% [Rule 30].

## 🧪 CONCEPT TESTS & BOUNDARY CASES
### Concept Test 1: Post-Supply Volume Discount
#### Situation: BEL offers 10% discount on televisions if distributor sells 500 units in a quarter. Shah Electronics purchases 750 units in April-June quarter, but fails to reverse ITC. In July-September quarter, Shah purchases 1,000 units and reverses ITC.
#### Analysis
##### April-June Quarter: Post-supply discount of 10% on ₹ 8,400 per unit is disallowed because Shah Electronics did not reverse ITC. Taxable value = ₹ 8,400 + ₹ 1,200 (packing) = ₹ 9,600 per unit.
##### July-September Quarter: Discount of 10% on ₹ 8,400 = ₹ 840 is allowed because Shah reversed ITC. Taxable value = ₹ 8,400 + ₹ 1,200 (packing) − ₹ 840 = ₹ 8,760 per unit.
### Concept Test 2: Repossessed Car Sale by Bank
#### Situation: Axis Bank repossesses a car from an unregistered borrower. Axis sells it for ₹ 3,50,000 after 5 quarters from the borrower's original purchase date (original purchase price ₹ 5,00,000). Axis Bank did not take ITC.
#### Computation
##### Deemed Purchase Price: ₹ 5,00,000 − (5 quarters × 5% per quarter = 25%) = ₹ 3,75,000.
##### Margin: ₹ 3,50,000 (Selling Price) − ₹ 3,75,000 (Deemed Purchase Price) = −₹ 25,000.
##### Value of Supply: NIL (negative margin ignored per Rule 32(5)). No GST is payable.
